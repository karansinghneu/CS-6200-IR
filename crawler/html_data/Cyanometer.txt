title="Cyan">cyan</a> and <a class="external text" href="http://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/-meter">-meter</a>) is an <a href="/wiki/Measuring_instrument" title="Measuring instrument">instrument for measuring</a> \'blueness\', specifically the colour intensity of blue <a href="/wiki/Sky" title="Sky">sky</a>. It is attributed to  <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Horace-B%C3%A9n%C3%A9dict_de_Saussure" title="Horace-B&#233;n&#233;dict de Saussure">Horace-B&#233;n&#233;dict de Saussure</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alexander_von_Humboldt" title="Alexander von Humboldt">Alexander von Humboldt</a>. It consists of squares of paper dyed in graduated shades of blue and arranged in a color circle or square that can be held up and compared to the color of the sky.\n</p><p>De Saussure is credited with inventing the cyanometer in 1789.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> De Saussure\'s cyanometer had 53 sections, ranging from white to varying shades of blue (dyed with <a href="/wiki/Prussian_blue" title="Prussian blue">Prussian blue</a>) and then to black,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> arranged in a circle; he used the device to measure the color of the sky at <a href="/wiki/Geneva" title="Geneva">Geneva</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chamonix" title="Chamonix">Chamonix</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mont_Blanc" title="Mont Blanc">Mont Blanc</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> De Saussure concluded, correctly, that the color of the sky was dependent on the amount of suspended particles in the atmosphere.\n</p><p>Humboldt was also an eager user of the cyanometer on his voyages and explorations in South America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>\n</p><p>The blueness of the <a href="/wiki/Atmosphere" title="Atmosphere">atmosphere</a> indicates transparency and the amount of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Water_vapour" title="Water vapour">water vapour</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc"><input class="toctogglecheckbox" id="toctogglecheckbox" role="button" style="display:none"
