For the astrosphere of the Sun, see <a href="/wiki/Heliosphere" title="Heliosphere">Heliosphere</a>.</div>\n<p>A <b>stellar-wind bubble</b> is a cavity light years across filled with hot gas blown into the <a href="/wiki/Interstellar_medium" title="Interstellar medium">interstellar medium</a> by the high-velocity (several thousand km/s) <a href="/wiki/Stellar_wind" title="Stellar wind">stellar wind</a> from a single massive <a href="/wiki/Star" title="Star">star</a> of <a href="/wiki/Stellar_classification" title="Stellar classification">type</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/O_star" title="O star">O</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B_V_star" title="B V star">B</a>. Weaker <a href="/wiki/Stellar_wind" title="Stellar wind">stellar winds</a> also blow bubble structures, which are also called <b>astrospheres</b>. The <a href="/wiki/Heliosphere" title="Heliosphere">heliosphere</a> blown by the <a href="/wiki/Solar_wind" title="Solar wind">solar wind</a>, within which all the major <a href="/wiki/Planet" title="Planet">planets</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Solar_System" title="Solar System">Solar System</a> are embedded, is a small example of a stellar-wind bubble.\n</p><p>Stellar-wind bubbles have a two-shock structure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>  The freely-expanding <a href="/wiki/Stellar_wind" title="Stellar wind">stellar wind</a> hits an inner termination shock, where its kinetic energy is thermalized, producing 10<sup>6</sup>&#160;<a href="/wiki/Kelvin" title="Kelvin">K</a>, <a href="/wiki/X-ray" title="X-ray">X-ray</a> emitting <a href="/wiki/Plasma_(physics)" title="Plasma (physics)">plasma</a>.  The hot, high-pressure, shocked wind expands, driving a shock into the surrounding interstellar gas.  If the surrounding gas is dense enough (number densities <span class="mwe-math-element"><span class="mwe-math-mathml-inline mwe-math-mathml-a11y" style="display: none;"><math alttext="{\\displaystyle n&gt;0.1{\\mbox{ cm}}^{-3}}" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">\n<semantics>\n<mrow class="MJX-TeXAtom-ORD">\n<mstyle displaystyle="true" scriptlevel="0">\n<mi>n</mi>\n<mo>&gt;</mo>\n<mn>0.1</mn>\n<msup>\n<mrow class="MJX-TeXAtom-ORD">\n<mstyle displaystyle="false" scriptlevel="0">\n<mtext>&#160;cm</mtext>\n</mstyle>\n</mrow>\n<mrow class="MJX-TeXAtom-ORD">\n<mo>&#8722;<!-- &#8722; --></mo>\n<mn>3</mn>\n</mrow>\n</msup>\n</mstyle>\n</mrow>\n<annotation encoding="application/x-tex">{\\displaystyle n&gt;0.1{\\mbox{ cm}}^{-3}}</annotation>\n</semantics>\n</math></span><img alt="n&gt;0.1{\\mbox{ cm}}^{{-3}}" aria-hidden="true" class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline" src="https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/8805944b658e91d11108368533e7cbc2496cb66b" style="vertical-align: -0.338ex; width:13.347ex; height:2.676ex;"/></span> or so), the swept-up gas radiatively cools far faster than the hot interior, forming a thin, relatively dense shell around the hot, shocked wind.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stellar-wind_bubble&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wolf-Rayet_nebula" title="Wolf-Rayet nebula">Wolf-Rayet nebula</a>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stellar-wind_bubble&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation journal">Castor, J.;
