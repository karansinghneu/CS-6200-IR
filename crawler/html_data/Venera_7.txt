links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Venera_7&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The lander was designed to be able to survive pressure of up to 180 bars (18,000&#160;kPa) and temperatures of 580&#160;&#176;C (1,076&#160;&#176;F).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Huntress235_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Huntress235-2">[2]</a></sup> This was significantly greater than what was expected to be encountered but significant uncertainties as to the surface temperatures and pressure of Venus resulted in the designers opting for a large margin of error.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Huntress235_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Huntress235-2">[2]</a></sup> The degree of hardening added mass to the probe which limited the amount of mass available for scientific instruments both on the probe itself and the <a href="/wiki/Satellite_bus" title="Satellite bus">interplanetary bus</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Huntress235_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Huntress235-2">[2]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Launch">Launch</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Venera_7&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Launch">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The probe was launched from Earth on 17 August 1970, at 05:38 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UTC" title="UTC">UTC</a>. It consisted of an interplanetary bus based on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3MV_(spacecraft)" title="3MV (spacecraft)">3MV</a> system and a lander.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reeves211_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-reeves211-3">[3]</a></sup> During the flight to Venus two in-course corrections were made using the bus\'s on-board <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=KDU-414&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="KDU-414 (page does not exist)">KDU-414</a> engine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reeves211_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-reeves211-3">[3]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Landing">Landing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Venera_7&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Landing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Venera 7 entered the <a href="/wiki/Atmosphere_of_Venus" title="Atmosphere of Venus">atmosphere of Venus</a> on 15 December 1970.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reeves211_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-reeves211-3">[3]</a></sup> The lander remained attached to the interplanetary bus during the initial stages of atmospheric entry<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reeves211_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-reeves211-3">[3]</a></sup> to allow the bus to cool the lander to &#8722;8&#160;&#176;C for as long as possible.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reeves211_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-reeves211-3">[3]</a></sup> The lander was ejected once atmospheric buffeting broke the interplanetary bus\'s lock-on with Earth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reeves211_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-reeves211-3">[3]</a></sup> The parachute opened at a height of 60&#160;km and atmospheric testing began with results showing the atmosphere to be 97% carbon dioxide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reeves211_3-6"><a href="#cite_note-reeves211-3">[3]</a></sup> The parachute appeared to fail during the descent, resulting in a descent more rapid than planned.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reeves211_3-7"><a href="#cite_note-reeves211-3">[3]</a></sup> As a result, the lander struck the surface of Venus at about 16.5&#160;m/s (37&#160;mph) at 05:37:10 UTC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reeves211_3-8"><a href="#cite_note-reeves211-3">[3]</a></sup> The landing coordinates are <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Venera_7&amp;params=5_S_351_E_globe:Venus"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">5&#176;S</span> <span class="longitude">351&#176;E</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">&#65279; / &#65279;</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">5&#176;S 351&#176;E</span><span style="display:none">&#65279; / <span class="geo">-5; 351</span></span></span></a></span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>\n</p><p>The probe appeared to go silent on impact<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reeves211_3-9"><a href="#cite_note-reeves211-3">[3]</a></sup> but recording tapes kept rolling.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Klaes_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Klaes-5">[5]</a></sup> A few weeks later, upon a review of the tapes by the radio astronomer Oleg Rzhiga another 23 minutes of very weak signals were found on them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Klaes_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Klaes-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Harvey_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Harvey-6">[6]</a></sup> The spacecraft had landed on Venus and probably bounced onto its side, leaving the medium gain antenna not aimed correctly for strong signal transmission to Earth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Klaes_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Klaes-5">[5]</a></sup>\n</p><p>The probe transmitted information to Earth for 53 minutes, which included 20 minutes from the surface.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="seems to disagree with above (cited) statement of 23 minutes of transmission after landing (February 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> It was found that the temperature at the surface of Venus was 475&#160;&#176;C (887&#160;&#176;F) &#177; 20&#160;&#176;C<sup class="reference"
