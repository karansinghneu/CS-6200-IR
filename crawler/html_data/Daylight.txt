combination of all direct and indirect <a href="/wiki/Sunlight" title="Sunlight">sunlight</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Daytime" title="Daytime">daytime</a>. This includes direct <a href="/wiki/Sunlight" title="Sunlight">sunlight</a>, <a href="/wiki/Diffuse_sky_radiation" title="Diffuse sky radiation">diffuse sky radiation</a>, and (often) both of these reflected by the <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a> and terrestrial objects, like <a href="/wiki/Landform" title="Landform">landforms</a> and buildings. Sunlight <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Light_scattering" title="Light scattering">scattered</a> or reflected by <a href="/wiki/Astronomical_object" title="Astronomical object">objects</a> in <a href="/wiki/Outer_space" title="Outer space">outer space</a> (that is, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Karman_line" title="Karman line">beyond</a> the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Earth%27s_atmosphere" title="Earth\'s atmosphere">Earth\'s atmosphere</a>) is generally not considered daylight. Thus, daylight excludes <a href="/wiki/Moonlight" title="Moonlight">moonlight</a>, despite it being indirect sunlight. <i>Daytime</i> is the period of time each day when daylight occurs. Daylight happens as <a href="/wiki/Earth%27s_rotation" title="Earth\'s rotation">Earth rotates</a>, and either side on which the <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">Sun</a> shines is considered daylight.\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc"><input class="toctogglecheckbox" id="toctogglecheckbox" role="button" style="display:none" type="checkbox"/><div class="toctitle" dir="ltr" lang="en"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Definition"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Definition</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Intensity_in_different_conditions"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Intensity in different conditions</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#Effects"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">Effects</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#See_also"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">See also</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#Notes"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">Notes</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-6"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">6</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Definition">Definition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daylight&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Definition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Daylight is present at a particular location, to some degree, whenever the <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">Sun</a> is above the local <a href="/wiki/Horizon" title="Horizon">horizon</a>. (This is true for slightly more than 50% of the <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a> at any given time. For an explanation of why it is not exactly half, see <a href="/wiki/Day#Introduction" title="Day">here</a>).  However, the outdoor <a href="/wiki/Illuminance" title="Illuminance">illuminance</a> can vary from 120,000 <a href="/wiki/Lux" title="Lux">lux</a> for direct <a href="/wiki/Sunlight" title="Sunlight">sunlight</a> at <a href="/wiki/Noon" title="Noon">noon</a>, which may cause eye <a href="/wiki/Pain" title="Pain">pain</a>, to less than 5 <a href="/wiki/Lux" title="Lux">lux</a> for thick storm <a href="/wiki/Cloud" title="Cloud">clouds</a> with the Sun at the horizon (even &lt;1 lux for the most extreme case), which may make shadows from distant <a href="/wiki/Street_light" title="Street light">street lights</a> visible. It may be darker under unusual circumstances like a <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse" title="Solar eclipse">solar eclipse</a> or very high levels of atmospheric <a href="/wiki/Particulates" title="Particulates">particulates</a>, which include smoke (see <a href="/wiki/New_England%27s_Dark_Day" title="New England\'s Dark Day">New England\'s Dark Day</a>), dust,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and volcanic ash.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daylight&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Effects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Daylighting" title="Daylighting">Daylighting</a> is lighting an indoor space with openings such as <a href="/wiki/Window" title="Window">windows</a> and <a href="/wiki/Skylight" title="Skylight">skylights</a> that allow daylight into the building. This type of lighting is chosen to save <a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">energy</a>, to avoid hypothesized adverse health effects of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Over-illumination" title="Over-illumination">over-illumination</a> by artificial light, and also for <a href="/wiki/Aesthetics" title="Aesthetics">aesthetics</a>. The amount of daylight received into an indoor space or room is defined as a <a href="/wiki/Daylight_factor" title="Daylight factor">daylight factor</a>, being the ratio between the measured internal and external light levels. Artificial lighting energy use can be reduced by simply installing fewer electric lights because daylight is present, or by dimming/switching electric lights automatically in response to the presence of daylight, a process known as <a href="/wiki/Daylight_harvesting" title="Daylight harvesting">daylight harvesting</a>.\n</p><p>In recent years, work has taken place to recreate the effects of daylight artificially. This is however expensive in terms of both equipment and energy consumption and is applied almost exclusively in specialist areas such as <a href="/wiki/Filmmaking" title="Filmmaking">filmmaking</a>, where light of such intensity is required anyway. In some filmmaking locations, such as Sweden, there is too much light due to long summer days. As a result, in location films like <a href="/wiki/Marianne_(2011_film)" title="Marianne (2011 film)">Marianne</a>, night scenes have to be shot during the daylight hours and digitally altered later.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daylight&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Twilight" title="Twilight">Twilight</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Moonlight" title="Moonlight">Moonlight</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Daylight_saving_time" title="Daylight saving time">Daylight saving time</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Daylighting" title="Daylighting">Daylighting</a></li>\n<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daytime_(astronomy)" title="Daytime (astronomy)">Daytime (astronomy)</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Right_to_light" title="Right to light">Right to light</a></li>\n<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Day_length" title="Day length">Day length</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Color_temperature" title="Color temperature">Color temperature</a></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daylight&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web">Cox, Clifford. <a class="external text" href="http://www.perryton.com/black.htm" rel="nofollow">"Dust Bowl"</a>. perryton.com<span class="reference-accessdate">.
