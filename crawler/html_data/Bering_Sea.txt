/ <span class="geo">58.000; -178.000</span></span></span></a></span></span></span></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Bering Sea</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" title="Russian language text">&#1041;&#1077;&#769;&#1088;&#1080;&#1085;&#1075;&#1086;&#1074;&#1086; &#1084;&#1086;&#769;&#1088;&#1077;</span>, <small><a href="/wiki/Romanization_of_Russian" title="Romanization of Russian">tr.</a></small> <i lang="ru-Latn" title="Russian language text">B&#233;ringovo m&#243;re</i>) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marginal_sea" title="Marginal sea">marginal sea</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fasham2003_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fasham2003-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-McColl2005_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-McColl2005-2">[2]</a></sup> It comprises a deep water basin, which then rises through a narrow slope into the shallower water above the <a href="/wiki/Continental_shelf" title="Continental shelf">continental shelves</a>.\n</p><p>The Bering Sea is separated from the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Alaska" title="Gulf of Alaska">Gulf of Alaska</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Alaska_Peninsula" title="Alaska Peninsula">Alaska Peninsula</a>. It covers over 2,000,000 square kilometers (770,000&#160;sq&#160;mi) and is bordered on the east and northeast by <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a>, on the west by <a href="/wiki/Russian_Far_East" title="Russian Far East">Russian Far East</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Kamchatka_Peninsula" title="Kamchatka Peninsula">Kamchatka Peninsula</a>, on the south by the Alaska Peninsula and the <a href="/wiki/Aleutian_Islands" title="Aleutian Islands">Aleutian Islands</a> and on the far north by the <a href="/wiki/Bering_Strait" title="Bering Strait">Bering Strait</a>, which connects the Bering Sea to the <a href="/wiki/Arctic_Ocean" title="Arctic Ocean">Arctic Ocean</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Chukchi_Sea" title="Chukchi Sea">Chukchi Sea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bristol_Bay" title="Bristol Bay">Bristol Bay</a> is the portion of the Bering Sea which separates the Alaska Peninsula from mainland Alaska.  The Bering Sea is named for <a href="/wiki/Vitus_Bering" title="Vitus Bering">Vitus Bering</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Danish</a> navigator in Russian service, who in 1728 was the first European to systematically explore it, sailing from the Pacific Ocean northward to the Arctic Ocean.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>\n</p><p>\nThe Bering Sea ecosystem includes resources within the jurisdiction of the United States and Russia, as well as <a href="/wiki/International_waters" title="International waters">international waters</a> in the middle of the sea (known as the "Donut Hole"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>). class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bering_Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Most scientists believe that during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Last_glacial_period" title="Last glacial period">most recent ice age</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sea_level" title="Sea level">sea level</a> was low enough to allow humans to migrate east on foot from <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> to <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> across what is now the Bering Strait. Other animals including megafauna migrated in both directions. This is commonly referred to as the "<a href="/wiki/Beringia" title="Beringia">Bering land bridge</a>" and is believed by most, though not all scientists, to be the first point of entry of humans into the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a>.\n</p><p>There is a small portion of the <a href="/wiki/Kula_Plate" title="Kula Plate">Kula Plate</a> in the Bering Sea. The Kula Plate is an ancient <a href="/wiki/Plate_tectonics" title="Plate tectonics">tectonic plate</a> that used to <a href="/wiki/Subduction" title="Subduction">subduct</a> under Alaska.<sup id="Ecosystem">Ecosystem</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bering_Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Ecosystem">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Bering Sea <a href="/wiki/Continental_shelf" title="Continental shelf">shelf break</a> is the dominant driver of <a href="/wiki/Primary_production" title="Primary production">primary productivity</a> in the Bering Sea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Springer_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Springer-11">[11]</a></sup> This zone, where the shallower <a href="/wiki/Continental_shelf" title="Continental shelf">continental shelf</a> drops off into the <a href="/wiki/North_Aleutians_Basin" title="North Aleutians Basin">North Aleutians Basin</a> is also known as the "Greenbelt". Nutrient upwelling from the cold waters of the Aleutian basin flowing up the slope and mixing with shallower waters of the shelf provide for constant production of <a href="/wiki/Phytoplankton" title="Phytoplankton">phytoplankton</a>.\n</p><p>The second driver of productivity in the Bering Sea is seasonal <a href="/wiki/Sea_ice" title="Sea ice">sea ice</a> that, in part, triggers the spring phytoplankton bloom.    Seasonal melting of sea ice causes an influx of lower salinity water into the middle and other shelf areas, causing stratification and hydrographic effects which influence productivity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Schumacher_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Schumacher-12">[12]</a></sup> In addition to the hydrographic and productivity influence of melting sea ice, the ice itself also provides an attachment substrate for the growth of algae as well as interstitial ice algae.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p><p>Some evidence suggests that great changes to the Bering Sea ecosystem have already occurred.  Warm water conditions in the summer of 1997 resulted in a massive bloom of low energy <a href="/wiki/Coccolithophore" title="Coccolithophore">coccolithophorid</a> phytoplankton (Stockwell et al. 2001).  A long record of <a href="/wiki/Isotopic_signature" title="Isotopic signature">carbon isotopes</a>, which is reflective of primary production trends of the Bering Sea, exists from historical samples of bowhead whale <a href="/wiki/Baleen" title="Baleen">baleen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Schell_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Schell-13">[13]</a></sup> Trends in carbon isotope ratios in whale baleen samples suggest that a 30&#8211;40% decline in average seasonal primary productivity has occurred over the last 50 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Schell_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-Schell-13">[13]</a></sup> The implication is that the <a href="/wiki/Carrying_capacity" title="Carrying capacity">carrying capacity</a> of the Bering Sea is much lower now than it has been in the past.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biodiversity">Biodiversity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bering_Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Biodiversity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The sea supports many whale species including the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beluga_(whale)" title="Beluga (whale)">beluga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Humpback_whale" title="Humpback whale">humpback whale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bowhead_whale" title="Bowhead whale">bowhead whale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gray_whale" title="Gray whale">gray whale</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blue_whale" title="Blue whale">blue whale</a>, the vulnerable <a href="/wiki/Sperm_whale" title="Sperm whale">sperm whale</a>, and the endangered <a href="/wiki/Fin_whale" title="Fin whale">fin whale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sei_whale" title="Sei whale">sei whale</a> and the rarest in the world, the <a href="/wiki/North_Pacific_right_whale" title="North Pacific right whale">North Pacific right whale</a>.  Other marine mammals include <a href="/wiki/Walrus" title="Walrus">walrus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steller_sea_lion" title="Steller sea lion">Steller sea lion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northern_fur_seal" title="Northern fur seal">northern fur seal</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orca" title="Orca">orca</a> and <a href="/wiki/Polar_bear" title="Polar bear">polar bear</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Bering Sea is very important to the seabirds of the world. Over 30 species of seabirds and approximately 20 million individuals breed in the Bering Sea region.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Seabird species include <a href="/wiki/Tufted_puffin" title="Tufted puffin">tufted puffins</a>, the endangered <a href="/wiki/Short-tailed_albatross" title="Short-tailed albatross">short-tailed albatross</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spectacled_eider" title="Spectacled eider">spectacled eider</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Red-legged_kittiwake" title="Red-legged kittiwake">red-legged kittiwakes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Many of these species are unique to the area, which provides highly productive foraging habitat, particularly along the shelf edge and in other nutrient-rich upwelling regions, such as the Pribilof, <a href="/wiki/Zhemchug_Canyon" title="Zhemchug Canyon">Zhemchug</a>, and Pervenets canyons. The Bering Sea is also home to colonies of <a href="/wiki/Crested_auklet" title="Crested auklet">crested auklets</a>, with upwards of a million individuals.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p><p>Two Bering Sea species, the <a href="/wiki/Steller%27s_sea_cow" title="Steller\'s sea cow">Steller\'s sea cow</a> (<i>Hydrodamalis gigas</i>) and <a href="/wiki/Spectacled_cormorant" title="Spectacled cormorant">spectacled cormorant</a> (<i>Phalacrocorax perspicillatus</i>), are extinct because of <a href="/wiki/Overexploitation" title="Overexploitation">overexploitation</a> by man.  In addition, a small subspecies of Canada goose, the Bering Canada goose (<i>Branta canadensis asiatica</i>) is extinct due to overhunting and introduction of rats to their breeding islands.\n</p><p>The Bering Sea supports many species of fish.  Some species of fish support large and valuable commercial fisheries.  Commercial fish species include 6 species<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> of Pacific <a href="/wiki/Salmon" title="Salmon">salmon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alaska_pollock" title="Alaska pollock">Alaska pollock</a>,  <a href="/wiki/Pacific_cod" title="Pacific cod">Pacific cod</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pacific_halibut" title="Pacific halibut">Pacific halibut</a>, yellowfin sole, <a href="/wiki/Pacific_ocean_perch" title="Pacific ocean perch">Pacific ocean perch</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sablefish" title="Sablefish">sablefish</a>. Shellfish include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paralithodes_camtschaticus" title="Paralithodes camtschaticus">red king crab</a> and <i><a href="/wiki/Chionoecetes" title="Chionoecetes">Chionoecetes</a></i>.\n</p><p>Fish biodiversity is high, and at least 419 species of fish have been reported from the Bering Sea.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fisheries">Fisheries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bering_Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Fisheries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Bering Sea is world-renowned for its productive and profitable fisheries, such as <a href="/wiki/Alaskan_king_crab_fishing" title="Alaskan king crab fishing">king crab</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> opilio and tanner crabs, Bristol Bay salmon, pollock and other groundfish.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> These fisheries rely on the productivity of the Bering Sea via a complicated and little understood food web. The continued existence of these fisheries requires an intact, healthy, and productive ecosystem.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p><p>Commercial fishing is big business in the Bering Sea, which is relied upon by the largest seafood companies in the world to produce fish and shellfish.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>  On the U.S. side, commercial fisheries catch approximately $1 billion worth of seafood annually, while Russian Bering Sea fisheries are worth approximately $600 million annually.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p><p>The Bering Sea also serves as the central location of the Alaskan <a href="/wiki/King_crab" title="King crab">king crab</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chionoecetes" title="Chionoecetes">opilio crab</a> seasons, which are chronicled on the <a href="/wiki/Discovery_Channel" title="Discovery Channel">Discovery Channel</a> television program <i><a href="/wiki/Deadliest_Catch" title="Deadliest Catch">Deadliest Catch</a></i>. Landings from Alaskan waters represents half the U.S. catch of fish and shellfish.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Change">Change</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bering_Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Change">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Because of the  changes going on in the Arctic, future evolution of the Bering Sea climate/ecosystem is uncertain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Between 1979 and 2012 the region experienced small growth in sea ice extent, standing in contrast to the substantial loss of summer sea ice in the Arctic Ocean to the north.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_media">In media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bering_Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: In media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The film <i><a
