class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek_language" title="Ancient Greek language">Ancient Greek</a>: <span lang="grc">&#935;&#961;&#965;&#963;&#8134; &#935;&#949;&#961;&#963;&#972;&#957;&#951;&#963;&#959;&#962;</span>, <i>Chrys&#7703; Chers&#243;n&#275;sos</i>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_language" title="Latin language">Latin</a>: <i class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Golden_Chersonese&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The earliest references to a fabulous land of gold that could be interpreted as places in South East Asia may be found in Indian literature.  In <i><a href="/wiki/Ramayana" title="Ramayana">Ramayana</a></i>, there are mentions of <i><a href="/wiki/Suvarnabhumi" title="Suvarnabhumi">Suvarnabhumi</a></i> (Land of Gold) and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suvarnadvipa" title="Suvarnadvipa">Suvarnadvipa</a></i> (the Golden Island or Peninsula, where <i>dvipa</i> might refer to either a <a href="/wiki/Peninsula" title="Peninsula">peninsula</a> or an <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">island</a>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheatley1961177&#8211;184_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheatley1961177&#8211;184-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Greek knowledge of lands further to their east improved after the conquests of <a href="/wiki/Alexander_the_Great" title="Alexander the Great">Alexander the Great</a>, but specific references to places in South East Asia did not appear until after the rise of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a>. Greek and Roman geographers <a href="/wiki/Eratosthenes" title="Eratosthenes">Eratosthenes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dionysius_Periegetes" title="Dionysius Periegetes">Dionysius Periegetes</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pomponius_Mela" title="Pomponius Mela">Pomponius Mela</a> had written about a <b>Golden Isle</b> (<i>Khrys&#275;</i>, <i>Chryse Insula</i>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGerini190978&#8211;79_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGerini190978&#8211;79-5">[5]</a></sup> which some in modern times argued to mean <a href="/wiki/Sumatra" title="Sumatra">Sumatra</a> while excluding the Malay Peninsula.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Pliny_the_Elder" title="Pliny the Elder">Pliny</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Natural_History_(Pliny)" title="Natural History (Pliny)">Natural History</a></i>, however, referred to <i><a href="/wiki/Chryse_and_Argyre" title="Chryse and Argyre">Chryse</a></i> as both a <a href="/wiki/Promontory" title="Promontory">promontory</a> and an island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheatley1961128&#8211;129_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheatley1961128&#8211;129-8">[8]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Ptolemy\'s <i><a href="/wiki/Geography_(Ptolemy)" title="Geography (Ptolemy)">Geography</a></i>, based on the work by <a href="/wiki/Marinus_of_Tyre" title="Marinus of Tyre">Marinus</a> of <a href="/wiki/Tyre,_Lebanon" title="Tyre, Lebanon">Tyre</a>, contains the best-known and perhaps the earliest reference to the Golden Chersonese.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGerini190978_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGerini190978-9">[9]</a></sup> However, <i>Geography</i> includes information added by later geographers, and the first specific mention of the Golden Chersonese may be in the work of <a href="/wiki/Marcian_of_Heraclea" title="Marcian of Heraclea">Marcian of Heraclea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheatley1961131&#8211;136_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheatley1961131&#8211;136-10">[10]</a></sup> Chersonese means peninsula in Greek, and although a few early scholars had attempted to link the Golden Chersonese with Lower Burma, the term is now generally accepted to mean the Malay Peninsula.  The Malay Peninsula is thought to have been a producer of gold in ancient times, and gold mines in <a href="/wiki/Patani" title="Patani">Patani</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pahang" title="Pahang">Pahang</a> were still mentioned in the 17th century by the Malay-Portuguese writer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Godinho_de_Er%C3%A9dia" title="Godinho de Er&#233;dia">Godinho de Er&#233;dia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheatley1961138&#8211;159_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheatley1961138&#8211;159-1">[1]</a></sup>  Although gold is now not a major product of modern-day Malaysia, it is still being <a href="/wiki/Mining_in_Malaysia#Gold" title="Mining in Malaysia">mined</a>, for example in <a href="/wiki/Raub_District" title="Raub District">Raub</a> in Pahang.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cartographic_references">Cartographic references</span><span title="Erdapfel">Erdapfel</a></div></div></div>\n<p>The Golden Chersonese appears in the map of <a href="/wiki/Ptolemy" title="Ptolemy">Ptolemy</a>, which gives some geographical locations on the peninsula. The earliest surviving maps of Ptolemy however came from the end of the 13th century. Note that Ptolemy, like many early geographers, believed the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a> to be a closed sea, and maps based on Ptolemy\'s work show Golden Chersonese to be located within a closed basin. To the east of the Golden Peninsula is the <a href="/wiki/Magnus_Sinus" title="Magnus Sinus">Great Gulf</a>, which is enclosed further east by the border of <i><a href="/wiki/Terra_incognita" title="Terra incognita">terra incognita</a></i> (unknown lands). <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medieval_Arab_geographers" title="Medieval Arab geographers">Arab geographers</a> were aware that the idea of the Indian Ocean as a closed basin was mistaken by the 8th century, for example in the work of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Al-Khw%C4%81rizm%C4%AB" title="Al-Khw&#257;rizm&#299;">al-Khw&#257;rizm&#299;</a>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Book_of_the_Description_of_the_Earth" title="Book of the Description of the Earth">Book of the Description of the Earth</a></i>.  They showed that the Indian Ocean might be linked to the <a href="/wiki/World_Ocean" title="World Ocean">World Oceans</a>, with the eastern limit of the <a href="/wiki/Ecumene" title="Ecumene">inhabited world</a> beyond the Malay Peninsula being the <a href="/wiki/Island_of_the_Jewel" title="Island of the Jewel">Island of the Jewel</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sea_of_Darkness" title="Sea of Darkness">Sea of Darkness</a>. The Ptolemaic eastern shore became the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dragon%27s_Tail_peninsula" title="Dragon\'s Tail peninsula">Dragon\'s Tail peninsula</a>.\n</p><p>The Golden Chersonese is shown on the <i><a href="/wiki/Mappa_mundi" title="Mappa mundi">mappa mundi</a></i> of <a href="/wiki/Andreas_Walsperger" title="Andreas Walsperger">Andreas Walsperger</a>, made in Constance around 1448, bearing the inscription, <i>hic rex caspar habitavit</i> (here lived King Caspar). <a href="/wiki/Saint_Caspar" title="Saint Caspar"> Caspar</a> was one of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Three_Magi" title="Three Magi">Three Magi</a> who worshipped the newborn Christ at Bethlehem.\n</p><p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Martin_of_Bohemia" title="Martin of Bohemia">Martin of Bohemia</a>, on his 1492 <a href="/wiki/Globe" title="Globe">geographical globe</a>, located the islands of <a href="/wiki/Chryse_and_Argyre" title="Chryse and Argyre">Chryse and Argyre</a> ("Gold" and "Silver") in the vicinity of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zipangu" title="Zipangu">Zipangu</a> (<a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>), which was said to be "rich in gold" by <a href="/wiki/Marco_Polo" title="Marco Polo">Marco Polo</a>. An expedition was sent to find the purported islands in this location under the command of <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_Unamuno" title="Pedro de Unamuno">Pedro de Unamuno</a> in 1587.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geographical_locations">Geographical locations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Golden_Chersonese&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geographical locations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The names of various geographical features and settlements of the Golden Chersonese are given in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ptolemy%27s_Geography" title="Ptolemy\'s Geography">Ptolemy\'s Geography</a></i>, including towns and rivers.  Different identities however have suggested by different scholars for these names.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTELinehan195186&#8211;98_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTELinehan195186&#8211;98-14">[14]</a></sup> Although coordinates are given for many of these places, they are not considered reliable for places so far away from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean">Mediterranean</a> as they may not be based on astronomical observation, and therefore cannot be reliably used for identification.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheatley1961138&#8211;159_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheatley1961138&#8211;159-1">[1]</a></sup>  Ptolemy\'s work was also copied and translated over many hundreds of years, with the oldest surviving copy copied over a thousand years after it was written, and errors may have been introduced.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-miksic_goh_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-miksic_goh-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rivers">Rivers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Golden_Chersonese&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Rivers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The Ptolemy map shows three rivers which are joined together to form as their source a single river which does not actually exist.  It has however been suggested that this might be an indication of the existence of an ancient transpeninsular route that linked Perak and Pahang, a short cut between the east and west coasts of the Malay Peninsula.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li><i><b>Khrysoanas</b></i> river, meaning "River of Gold", proposed to be various rivers on the west coast, from the Trang River (in southern Thailand) or Lungu River, to the <a href="/wiki/Perak_River" title="Perak River">Perak River</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Bernam_River" title="Bernam River">Bernam River</a>, or further south the <a href="/wiki/Muar_River" title="Muar River">Muar River</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTELinehan195186,_88&#8211;91_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTELinehan195186,_88&#8211;91-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-miksic_goh_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-miksic_goh-15">[15]</a></sup></li>\n<li><i><b>Palandas</b></i> river, proposed to be <a href="/wiki/Johor_River" title="Johor River">Johor River</a> by a number of scholars.</li>\n<li><i><b>Attabas</b></i> river, which most authors agreed to be the <a href="/wiki/Pahang_River" title="Pahang River">Pahang River</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTELinehan195188_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTELinehan195188-19">[19]</a></sup></li></ul>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Settlements">Settlements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Golden_Chersonese&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Settlements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<ul><li><i><b>Takola</b></i>  &#8211; a trading <a href="/wiki/Emporium_(antiquity)" title="Emporium (antiquity)">emporion</a>, located at <a href="/wiki/Trang_Province" title="Trang Province">Trang</a> in southern Thailand according to most authors. <a href="/wiki/Takua_Pa_District" title="Takua Pa District">Takua Pa</a> is another suggestion.  Takola was known to the Indians in ancient times; a place named <i>Takkola</i> is mentioned in the 2nd or 3rd century Indian texts <i><a href="/wiki/Niddesa" title="Niddesa">Maha Niddesa</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Milinda_Panha" title="Milinda Panha">Milinda Panha</a></i>, and it is also considered to be the same as the <i>Talaittakk&#333;lam</i> mentioned in the 1030 <a href="/wiki/Tamil_inscriptions_in_the_Malay_world#Tanjore_Inscription" title="Tamil inscriptions in the Malay world">Tanjore inscription</a> as one of the places conquered by <a href="/wiki/Rajendra_Chola_I" title="Rajendra Chola I">Rajendra Chola</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Chola_invasion_of_Srivijaya" title="Chola invasion of Srivijaya">invasion of Srivijaya</a>. These Indian sources indicate the existence of <i>Takola</i> from the 3rd to 11th century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheatley1961268&#8211;272_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheatley1961268&#8211;272-20">[20]</a></sup></li>\n<li><i><b>Konkonagara</b></i> &#8211; a place near the <i>Khrysoanas</i>, with many scholars placing it in <a href="/wiki/Perak" title="Perak">Perak</a> (e.g. at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kuala_Kangsar" title="Kuala Kangsar">Kuala Kangsar</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Kinta_District" title="Kinta District">Kinta District</a>, or along the Bernam river), but some placed it further north at the <a href="/wiki/Muda_River" title="Muda River">Muda River</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kedah" title="Kedah">Kedah</a>. or <a href="/wiki/Krabi" title="Krabi">Krabi</a> opposite <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phuket" title="Phuket">Phuket</a> in Thailand. It is thought to be an Indian name, but it has also been proposed to be a hybrid of Malay and Sanskrit &#8211; <i>kolong-kolong</i> (or <i>kekolong</i>) and <i>negara</i>, meaning "the country of mines".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTELinehan195191&#8211;92_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTELinehan195191&#8211;92-21">[21]</a></sup></li>\n<li><i><b>Sabara</b></i> or <i><b>Sabana</b></i> &#8211; the second emporion, variously proposed to be in <a href="/wiki/Selangor" title="Selangor">Selangor</a> or near <a href="/wiki/Klang_(city)" title="Klang (city)">Klang</a>, or just south of <a href="/wiki/Malacca" title="Malacca">Malacca</a>, or south <a href="/wiki/Johor" title="Johor">Johor</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheatley1961151&#8211;152_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheatley1961151&#8211;152-22">[22]</a></sup></li>\n<li><i><b>Tharra</b></i> &#8211; at least 10 different sets of coordinates have been given in different texts for this site, it is therefore difficult to pinpoint and there is no agreement in its identification.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTELinehan195192&#8211;93_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTELinehan195192&#8211;93-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheatley1961156_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheatley1961156-24">[24]</a></sup></li>\n<li><i><b>Palanda</b></i> &#8211; suggested to be <a href="/wiki/Kota_Tinggi" title="Kota Tinggi">Kota Tinggi</a> by a few authors.</li>\n<li><i><b>Kalonka</b></i> &#8211;  various sites ranging from <a href="/wiki/Chumphon_Province" title="Chumphon Province">Chumphon Province</a> in southern Thailand to the Pahang River basin have been proposed.</li>\n<li><i><b>Kole polis</b></i> &#8211; thought to be located on the north east coast of the Malay Peninsula, and suggested to be in <a href="/wiki/Kelantan" title="Kelantan">Kelantan</a>, or somewhere between <a href="/wiki/Kemaman_River" title="Kemaman River">Kemaman River</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kuantan" title="Kuantan">Kuantan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheatley1961152&#8211;153_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheatley1961152&#8211;153-25">[25]</a></sup></li>\n<li><i><b>Perimula</b></i> - placed on the north east coast of the Malay Peninsula, suggestions include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ligor" title="Ligor">Ligor</a>, the deltas of <a href="/wiki/Kelantan_River" title="Kelantan River">Kelantan</a> or <a href="/wiki/Terengganu_River" title="Terengganu River">Trengganu River</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Redang_Island" title="Redang Island">Redang Island</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheatley1961154&#8211;155_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheatley1961154&#8211;155-26">[26]</a></sup></li></ul>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_features">Other features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Golden_Chersonese&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Other features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<ul><li>Cape <i><b>Maleou-kolon</b></i> &#8211; the name appears to refer to a "Malay Point", perhaps the south east corner of the Malay Peninsula.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-miksic_goh_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-miksic_goh-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheatley1961153&#8211;154_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheatley1961153&#8211;154-27">[27]</a></sup></li>\n<li>The <i><b>Perimulikos</b></i> gulf &#8211; possibly the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Siam" title="Gulf of Siam">Gulf of Siam</a>, although alternative proposals ranging from  the Bay of <a href="/wiki/Patani" title="Patani">Patani</a> to the lake of <a href="/wiki/Songkhla_Lake" title="Songkhla Lake">Tale Sap</a> in <a href="/wiki/Songkhla_Province" title="Songkhla Province">Songkhla</a> have been suggested.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheatley1961154&#8211;155_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheatley1961154&#8211;155-26">[26]</a></sup></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Golden_Chersonese&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Cathay" title="Cathay">Cathay</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Daqin" title="Daqin">Daqin</a></li>\n<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kattigara" title="Kattigara">Kattigara</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Suvarnabhumi" title="Suvarnabhumi">Suvarnabhumi</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Sino-Roman_relations" title="Sino-Roman relations">Sino-Roman relations</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Serica" title="Serica">Serica</a></li>\n<li>"<a href="/wiki/Nanyang_(region)" title="Nanyang (region)">Great Golden Peninsula</a>", another gold-related name used for Southeast Asia by Chinese emigrants in later times</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Golden_Chersonese&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em;
