(<b>FAI</b>; English: <span lang="en">World Air Sports Federation</span>), is the world governing body for <a href="/wiki/Air_sports" title="Air sports">air sports</a>.  It was founded on 14 October 1905, and is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Lausanne" title="Lausanne">Lausanne</a>, Switzerland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It maintains world records for <a href="/wiki/Aeronautics" title="Aeronautics">aeronautical</a> activities including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Balloon_(aircraft)" title="Balloon (aircraft)">ballooning</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aeromodeling" title="Aeromodeling">aeromodeling</a>, <a href="/wiki/Unmanned_aerial_vehicle" title="Unmanned aerial vehicle">unmanned aerial vehicles</a> (drones) and also for <a href="/wiki/Human_spaceflight" title="Human spaceflight">human spaceflight</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc"><input class="toctogglecheckbox" id="toctogglecheckbox" role="button" style="display:none" type="checkbox"/><div class="toctitle" dir="ltr" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_A%C3%A9ronautique_Internationale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The FAI was founded at a conference held in Paris 12&#8211;14 October 1905, which was organised following a resolution passed by the Olympic Congress held in Brussels on 10 June 1905 calling for the creation of an Association "to regulate the sport of flying, ... the various aviation meetings and advance the science and sport of Aeronautics."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The conference was attended by representatives from 8 countries: Belgium (<i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Aero_Club_Royal_de_Belgique&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Aero Club Royal de Belgique (page does not exist)">Aero Club Royal de Belgique</a></i>, founded 1901), France (<i><a href="/wiki/A%C3%A9ro-Club_de_France" title="A&#233;ro-Club de France">A&#233;ro-Club de France</a></i>, 1898), Germany (<a class="extiw" href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deutscher_Aero_Club" title="de:Deutscher Aero Club">Deutscher Aero Club</a> e.V.<i>), Great Britain (<a href="/wiki/Royal_Aero_Club" title="Royal Aero Club">Royal Aero Club</a>, 1901), Italy (</i><a class="extiw" href="https://it.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aero_Club_d%27Italia" title="it:Aero Club d\'Italia">Aero Club d\'Italia</a><i>, 1904), Spain (</i><a class="extiw" href="https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_Aero_Club_de_Espa%C3%B1a" title="es:Real Aero Club de Espa&#241;a">Real Aero Club de Espa&#241;a</a><i>, 1905), Switzerland (</i><a href="/wiki/Aero-Club_der_Schweiz" title="Aero-Club der Schweiz">Aero-Club der Schweiz</a><i>, 1900) and the United States (<a href="/wiki/Aero_Club_of_America" title="Aero Club of America">Aero Club of America</a>, 1905).</i>\n</p><p>On 2 February 2017 the FAI announced its new strategic partnership with international asset management firm <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Noosphere_Ventures&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Noosphere Ventures (page does not exist)">Noosphere Ventures</a>. FAI Secretary General Susanne Sch&#246;del, FAI President Frits Brink and Noosphere Ventures Managing Partner <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Max_Polyakov&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Max Polyakov (page does not exist)">Max Polyakov</a> signed the agreement, making Noosphere Ventures FAI&#8217;s Global Technical Partner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Activities">Activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_A%C3%A9ronautique_Internationale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The FAI is the international governing body for the following activities: \n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Aerobatics" title="Aerobatics">Aerobatics</a> through the FAI Aerobatics Commission ("Commission Internationale de Voltige Aerienne" &#8211; CIVA)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CIVA_website_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-CIVA_website-7">[7]</a></sup></li>\n<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aeromodeling" title="Aeromodeling">Aeromodeling</a> and <a href="/wiki/Unmanned_aerial_vehicle" title="Unmanned aerial vehicle">drones</a> through the FAI Aeromodelling Commission ("Commission Internationale d\'Aero-Modelisme" &#8211; CIAM)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CIAM_website_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-CIAM_website-8">[8]</a></sup></li>\n<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Balloon_(aircraft)" title="Balloon (aircraft)">Ballooning</a> through the FAI Ballooning Commission ("Commission Internationale de l\'A&#233;rostation" &#8211; CIA)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CIA_website_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-CIA_website-9">[9]</a></sup></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/General_aviation" title="General aviation">General aviation</a> through the FAI General Aviation Commission ("General Aviation Commission" &#8211; GAC)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GAC_website_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-GAC_website-10">[10]</a></sup></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Gliding" title="Gliding">Gliding</a> through the <a href="/wiki/FAI_Gliding_Commission" title="FAI Gliding Commission">FAI Gliding Commission</a> ("International Gliding Commission &#8211; IGC")<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGC_website_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-IGC_website-11">[11]</a></sup></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Hang_gliding" title="Hang gliding">Hang gliding</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Paragliding" title="Paragliding">Paragliding</a> through the FAI Hang Gliding &amp; Paragliding Commission ("Commission Internationale de Vol Libre &#8211; CIVL)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CIVL_website_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-CIVL_website-12">[12]</a></sup></li>\n<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Human-powered_aircraft" title="Human-powered aircraft">Human-powered aircraft</a> through the FAI Amateur-Built and Experimental Aircraft Commission ("Commission Internationale des A&#233;ronefs de Construction Amateur &#8211; CIACA")<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CIACA_website_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-CIACA_website-13">[13]</a></sup></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Ultralight_aviation" title="Ultralight aviation">Microlighting</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paramotor" title="Paramotor">Paramotoring</a> through the FAI Microlight &amp; Paramotor Commission ("Commission Internationale de Microaviation &#8211; CIMA)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CIMA_website_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-CIMA_website-14">[14]</a></sup></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Parachuting" title="Parachuting">Parachuting</a> through the FAI Parachuting Commission ("<a href="/wiki/International_Parachuting_Commission" title="International Parachuting Commission">International Parachuting Commission</a> &#8211; IPC )<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IPC_website_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-IPC_website-15">[15]</a></sup></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Rotorcraft" title="Rotorcraft">Rotorcraft</a> through the FAI Rotorcraft Commission ("Commission Internationale de giraviation &#8211; CIG)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CIG_website_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-CIG_website-16">[16]</a></sup></li></ul>\n<p>The FAI establishes the standards for records in the activities.  Where these are <a href="/wiki/Air_sports" title="Air sports">air sports</a>, the FAI also oversees international competitions at world and continental levels, and also organizes the <a href="/wiki/World_Air_Games" title="World Air Games">World Air Games</a> and <a href="/wiki/FAI_World_Grand_Prix" title="FAI World Grand Prix">FAI World Grand Prix</a>.\n</p><p>The FAI keeps records set in <a href="/wiki/Human_spaceflight" title="Human spaceflight">human spaceflight</a>, through the FAI Astronautic Records Commission ("International Astronautic Records Commission &#8211; ICARE)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ICARE_website_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-ICARE_website-17">[17]</a></sup>\n</p><p>The FAI also organises the FAI International Drones Conference and Expo. This event offers a platform for organisations, businesses and individuals to discuss how drones are used today and to create a framework for how they will be used and impact on our lives in the future.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Records">Records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_A%C3%A9ronautique_Internationale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:The_Soviet_Union_1971_CPA_3992_stamp_(Yuri_Gagarin_Medal,_Spaceships_and_Planets).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="680" data-file-width="965" decoding="async" height="155" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/The_Soviet_Union_1971_CPA_3992_stamp_%28Yuri_Gagarin_Medal%2C_Spaceships_and_Planets%29.jpg/220px-The_Soviet_Union_1971_CPA_3992_stamp_%28Yuri_Gagarin_Medal%2C_Spaceships_and_Planets%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/The_Soviet_Union_1971_CPA_3992_stamp_%28Yuri_Gagarin_Medal%2C_Spaceships_and_Planets%29.jpg/330px-The_Soviet_Union_1971_CPA_3992_stamp_%28Yuri_Gagarin_Medal%2C_Spaceships_and_Planets%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/The_Soviet_Union_1971_CPA_3992_stamp_%28Yuri_Gagarin_Medal%2C_Spaceships_and_Planets%29.jpg/440px-The_Soviet_Union_1971_CPA_3992_stamp_%28Yuri_Gagarin_Medal%2C_Spaceships_and_Planets%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:The_Soviet_Union_1971_CPA_3992_stamp_(Yuri_Gagarin_Medal,_Spaceships_and_Planets).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>1971 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USSR" title="USSR">USSR</a> commemorative stamp depicting <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yuri_A._Gagarin_Gold_Medal" title="Yuri A. Gagarin Gold Medal">Yuri A. Gagarin Gold Medal</a> established by FAI</div></div></div>\n<p>Among the FAI\'s responsibilities are the verification of record-breaking flights. For a flight to be registered as a "World Record," it has to comply with the FAI\'s strict rules, which include a proviso that the record must exceed the previous record by a certain percentage. Since the late 1930s, military aircraft have dominated some classes of record for powered aircraft such as speed, distance, payload, and height, though other classes are regularly claimed by civilians.\n</p><p>Some records are claimed by countries as their own, even though their achievements fail to meet FAI standards.  These claims are not typically granted the status of official records. For example, <a href="/wiki/Yuri_Gagarin" title="Yuri Gagarin">Yuri Gagarin</a> earned recognition for the first manned spaceflight, despite failing to meet FAI requirements. The FAI initially did not recognize the achievement because he did not land in his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vostok_spacecraft" title="Vostok spacecraft">Vostok spacecraft</a> (he ejected from it), but later it recognized that Gagarin was the first human to fly into space.  The FAI then established <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yuri_A._Gagarin_Gold_Medal" title="Yuri A. Gagarin Gold Medal">"The Yuri A. Gagarin Gold Medal"</a>, which has been awarded since 1968.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto-18">[18]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Classes">Classes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_A%C3%A9ronautique_Internationale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Classes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<table class="box-Unreferenced_section plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Unreferenced" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg"><img alt="" data-file-height="399" data-file-width="512" decoding="async" height="39" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" width="50"/></a></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>does not <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">cite</a> any <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">sources</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_A%C3%A9ronautique_Internationale&amp;action=edit">improve this section</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Introduction_to_referencing_with_Wiki_Markup/1" title="Help:Introduction to referencing with Wiki Markup/1">adding citations to reliable sources</a>.
