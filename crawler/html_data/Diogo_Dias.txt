He was the brother of <a href="/wiki/Bartolomeu_Dias" title="Bartolomeu Dias">Bartolomeu Dias</a> and discovered some of the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde" title="Cape Verde">Cape Verde</a> islands together with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ant%C3%B3nio_Noli" title="Ant&#243;nio Noli">Ant&#243;nio Noli</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Serving_with_da_Gama">Serving with da Gama</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diogo_Dias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Serving with da Gama">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In 1497 on the first <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_India_Armadas" title="Portuguese India Armadas">Portuguese India Armadas</a> expedition to <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, Diogo Dias served as <i>escriv&#227;o</i> (<a href="/wiki/Captain%27s_clerk" title="Captain\'s clerk">clerk</a>) aboard <a href="/wiki/Vasco_da_Gama" title="Vasco da Gama">Vasco da Gama</a>\'s flagship <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Gabriel_(ship)" title="S&#227;o Gabriel (ship)"><i>S&#227;o Gabriel</i></a>. Dias was one of the main conduits between Gama and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zamorin" title="Zamorin">Zamorin</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kozhikode" title="Kozhikode">Calicut</a>, and was briefly taken prisoner by the Zamorin when negotiations became contentious.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:182px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Detail_of_Diogo_Dias%27s_ship_(Cabral_Armada).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="592" data-file-width="587" decoding="async" height="182" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Detail_of_Diogo_Dias%27s_ship_%28Cabral_Armada%29.jpg/180px-Detail_of_Diogo_Dias%27s_ship_%28Cabral_Armada%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Detail_of_Diogo_Dias%27s_ship_%28Cabral_Armada%29.jpg/270px-Detail_of_Diogo_Dias%27s_ship_%28Cabral_Armada%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Detail_of_Diogo_Dias%27s_ship_%28Cabral_Armada%29.jpg/360px-Detail_of_Diogo_Dias%27s_ship_%28Cabral_Armada%29.jpg 2x" width="180"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Detail_of_Diogo_Dias%27s_ship_(Cabral_Armada).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Ship of Diogo Dias, detail from the <i>Mem&#243;ria das Armadas</i></div></div></div>\n<p>In 1500, Diogo Dias accompanied the <a href="/wiki/2nd_Portuguese_India_Armada_(Cabral,_1500)" title="2nd Portuguese India Armada (Cabral, 1500)">2nd armada</a> of <a href="/wiki/Pedro_%C3%81lvares_Cabral" title="Pedro &#193;lvares Cabral">Pedro &#193;lvares Cabral</a> as one of the captains of the fleet, with a commission to open trade at <a href="/wiki/Sofala" title="Sofala">Sofala</a>.  Diogo Dias was one of the first to go ashore in the discovery of <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> in April 1500. Famously, Dias is credited for breaking the ice with the wary <a href="/wiki/Tupiniquim" title="Tupiniquim">Tupiniquim</a> on the beach by jumping into an impromptu joyful dance to the accompaniment of Tupi pipes.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="New_Waters">New Waters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diogo_Dias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: New Waters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>During an expedition along the <a href="/wiki/Cape_of_Good_Hope" title="Cape of Good Hope">Cape of Good Hope</a> (Diogo may have originally called it the Cape of Storms) with <a href="/wiki/Pedro_%C3%81lvares_Cabral" title="Pedro &#193;lvares Cabral">Pedro &#193;lvares Cabral</a> and his brother, Dias\'s ship got separated from the main fleet during the crossing of <a href="/wiki/Cape_of_Good_Hope" title="Cape of Good Hope">Cape of Good Hope</a> (his brother\'s ship was lost during that crossing).  Having struck a route too far east, Dias was the first European to sight the island of <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a> and is often credited with naming the island of <i>S&#227;o Louren&#231;o</i>, on account of it being found on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Lawrence" title="St. Lawrence">St. Lawrence</a>\'s day (August 10, 1500). Although the island was not unknown; its existence and Arabic name, "Island of the Moon", was already reported by <a href="/wiki/P%C3%AAro_da_Covilh%C3%A3" title="P&#234;ro da Covilh&#227;">P&#234;ro da Covilh&#227;</a> back in 1490.\n</p><p>Dias\'s subsequent attempts to find the main fleet ended with him mistakenly sailing past <a href="/wiki/Cape_Guardafui" title="Cape Guardafui">Cape Guardafui</a> and into the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Aden" title="Gulf of Aden">Gulf of Aden</a>, waters as yet unsailed by Portuguese ships.  Trapped by contrary winds, Dias spent several harrowing months in the area.  Battered by tempests, attacked by pirates and finally forced aground on the <a href="/wiki/Eritrea" title="Eritrea">Eritrean</a> coast, in a desperate search for water and food for his rapidly dying crew.  By the time he left there, Dias had only six crewmen left.\n</p><p>Unable to find Cabral, Diogo Dias\' ship hobbled back home alone. <a href="/wiki/Nicolau_Coelho" title="Nicolau Coelho">Nicolau Coelho</a>, leading the vanguard of Cabral\'s returning armada, finally stumbled upon the weary Dias at the watering stop of Bezeguiche (Bay of <a href="/wiki/Dakar" title="Dakar">Dakar</a>, Senegal) in June, 1501.  They returned to Lisbon together.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diogo_Dias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/2nd_Portuguese_India_Armada_(Cabral,_1500)" title="2nd Portuguese India Armada (Cabral, 1500)">2nd Portuguese India Armada (Cabral, 1500)</a></li>\n<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exploration_of_Asia" title="Exploration of Asia">Exploration of Asia</a></li></ul>\n<p><br/>\n</p>\n<table class="metadata plainlinks stub" role="presentation" style="background:transparent"><tbody><tr><td><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Vasco_da_Gama_-_1838.png"><img alt="Stub icon" data-file-height="1754" data-file-width="1275" decoding="async" height="41" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Vasco_da_Gama_-_1838.png/30px-Vasco_da_Gama_-_1838.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Vasco_da_Gama_-_1838.png/45px-Vasco_da_Gama_-_1838.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Vasco_da_Gama_-_1838.png/60px-Vasco_da_Gama_-_1838.png 2x" width="30"/></a></td><td><i>This article about a Portuguese explorer is a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Stub" title="Wikipedia:Stub">stub</a>.
