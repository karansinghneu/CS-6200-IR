<b>Marquesas Islands</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="\'m\' href="/wiki/Overseas_collectivity" title="Overseas collectivity">overseas collectivity</a> of <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> in the southern Pacific Ocean. The Marquesas are located at 9.7812&#176; S, 139.0817&#176; W. The highest point is the peak of <a href="/wiki/Mont_Oave" title="Mont Oave">Mount Oave</a> (French: <i lang="fr">Mont Oave</i>) on <a href="/wiki/Ua_Pou" title="Ua Pou">Ua Pou</a> island at 1,230 m (4,035&#160;ft) above sea level.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Research based on 2010 studies suggests the islands were colonized rapidly in two successive waves by indigenous colonists from West Polynesia, beginning <abbr title="circa">c.</abbr><span style="white-space:nowrap;">&#8201;1025&#8211;1120 AD</span>, leading to the development of a "remarkably uniform culture, human biology and language."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fast_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-fast-4">[4]</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Marquesas Islands form one of the <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_French_Polynesia" title="Administrative divisions of French Polynesia">five administrative divisions</a> (<i><i lang="fr" title="French language text">subdivisions administratives</i></i>) of French Polynesia. The capital of the Marquesas Islands administrative subdivision is the settlement of <a href="/wiki/Taiohae" title="Taiohae">Taiohae</a> on the island of <a href="/wiki/Nuku_Hiva" title="Nuku Hiva">Nuku Hiva</a>. Pacific Ocean</div></div></div>\n<p>The Marquesas Islands group is one of the most remote in the world, lying about 852 mi (1,370 km) northeast of <a href="/wiki/Tahiti" title="Tahiti">Tahiti</a> and about 3,000 mi (4,800 km) away from the west coast of Mexico, the nearest continental land mass. They fall naturally into two geographical divisions: the northern group, consisting of <a href="/wiki/Eiao" title="Eiao">Eiao</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hatutu" title="Hatutu">Hatutu</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hatutaa" title="Hatutaa">Hatutaa</a>), <a href="/wiki/Motu_One_(Marquesas_Islands)" title="Motu One (Marquesas Islands)">Motu One</a>, and the islands centered on the large island of <a href="/wiki/Nuku_Hiva" title="Nuku Hiva">Nuku Hiva</a>:  <a href="/wiki/Motu_Iti_(Marquesas_Islands)" title="Motu Iti (Marquesas Islands)">Motu Iti</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hatu_Iti" title="Hatu Iti">Hatu Iti</a>), <a href="/wiki/Ua_Pou" title="Ua Pou">Ua Pou</a>, <a href="/wiki/Motu_Oa" title="Motu Oa">Motu Oa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ua_Huka" title="Ua Huka">Ua Huka</a>, and the southern group of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fatu_Uku" title="Fatu Uku">Fatu Uku</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tahuata" title="Tahuata">Tahuata</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moho_Tani" title="Moho Tani">Moho Tani</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Motane" title="Motane">Motane</a>), <a href="/wiki/Terihi" title="Terihi">Terihi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fatu_Hiva" title="Fatu Hiva">Fatu Hiva</a> and <a href="/wiki/Motu_Nao" title="Motu Nao">Motu Nao</a> (Thomasset Rock), clustered around the main island of <a href="/wiki/Hiva_Oa" title="Hiva Oa">Hiva &#699;Oa</a>. With a combined land area of 1,049 square kilometres (405&#160;sq&#160;mi), the Marquesas are among the largest island groups of French Polynesia originally discovered by Spanish galleons fleets en route to Manila, Nuku Hiva being the second largest island in the entire territory, after <a href="/wiki/Tahiti" title="Tahiti">Tahiti</a>. With the exception of Motu One, all the islands of the Marquesas are of volcanic origin.\n</p><p>In contrast to the tendency to associate Polynesia with lush tropical vegetation, the Marquesas are remarkably dry islands. Though the islands lie within the tropics, they are the first major break in the prevailing easterly winds that spawn from the extraordinarily dry (from an atmospheric perspective) <a href="/wiki/Humboldt_Current" title="Humboldt Current">Humboldt Current</a>. Because of this, the islands are subject to frequent drought conditions, and only those that reach highest into the clouds (generally, above about 750&#160;m&#160;/ 2,500&#160; ft above sea level) have reliable precipitation. This has led to historical fluctuations in water supply, which have played a crucial role in the sustainability of human populations in certain sections of the various islands throughout the archipelago. This is especially evident in the low historical population of Ua Huka (maximum elevation 857 m&#160;m&#160;/ 2,812&#160;ft.) and the intermittent inhabitability of Eiao (maximum elevation 576 m&#160;m&#160;/ 1,890&#160;ft.). The Marquesas Islands are thought to have formed by a center of upwelling <a href="/wiki/Magma" title="Magma">magma</a> called the <a href="/wiki/Marquesas_hotspot" title="Marquesas hotspot">Marquesas hotspot</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Islands_of_the_Marquesas">Islands of the Marquesas</span><span of the Marquesas Islands are of volcanic origin, created by the <a href="/wiki/Marquesas_hotspot" title="Marquesas hotspot">Marquesas hotspot</a> that underlies the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Plate" title="Pacific Plate">Pacific Plate</a>. The Marquesas Islands lie above a submarine volcanic <a href="/wiki/Plateau" title="Plateau">plateau</a> of the same name. The plateau, like the islands, is generally believed to be less than 5 million years old, though one hypothesis has the plateau (not the islands) as significantly older and having a mirror image, the <a href="/wiki/Andean_Volcanic_Belt#Volcanic_gaps" title="Andean Volcanic Belt">Inca Plateau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Subduction" title="Subduction">subducting</a> under northern <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Except for <a href="/wiki/Motu_One_(Marquesas_Islands)" title="Motu One (Marquesas Islands)">Motu One</a>, all the Marquesas are <a href="/wiki/High_island" title="High island">high islands</a>. Motu One is a <a href="/wiki/Low_island" title="Low island">low island</a>, comprising two small <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sand_bank" title="Sand bank">sand banks</a> awash on a <a href="/wiki/Coral" title="Coral">coral</a> <a href="/wiki/Reef" title="Reef">reef</a>. Unlike the majority of French Polynesian islands, the Marquesas are not surrounded by protective fringing reefs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tp_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-tp-7">[7]</a></sup> Except for Motu One, and in bays and other protected areas, the only other coral in the Marquesas is found in a rather strange place:  on the top of the island of <a href="/wiki/Fatu_Huku" title="Fatu Huku">Fatu Huku</a>. The <a href="/wiki/South_Equatorial_Current" title="South Equatorial Current">South Equatorial Current</a> lashes the islands mercilessly, which has led to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sea-cave" title="Sea-cave">sea-caves</a> dotting the islands\' shores. Except for where the valleys empty into the small bays, the islands are remarkable for their mountain ridges, which end abruptly as cliffs where they meet the sea. The islands are estimated to range in age from the youngest, Fatu Hiva (1.3 million years) to the oldest, Eiao (6 million years).\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marquesas_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Rainforest_Fatu_Hiva.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="812" data-file-width="1020" decoding="async" height="175" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/Rainforest_Fatu_Hiva.jpg/220px-Rainforest_Fatu_Hiva.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/Rainforest_Fatu_Hiva.jpg/330px-Rainforest_Fatu_Hiva.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/Rainforest_Fatu_Hiva.jpg/440px-Rainforest_Fatu_Hiva.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Rainforest_Fatu_Hiva.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Breadfruit" title="Breadfruit">Breadfruit</a> tree on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fatu-Hiva" title="Fatu-Hiva">Fatu-Hiva</a>.</div></div></div>\n<p>Temperatures in the Marquesas are stable year around, but precipitation is highly variable.  Precipitation is much greater on the north and east (windward) parts of the islands than on the western (leeward) parts.  Average annual precipitation can vary from more than 100 inches (2,500&#160;mm) on windward shores and mountains to a low as 20 inches (510&#160;mm) in the "desert" region of Nuku Hiva. Droughts, sometimes lasting several years, are frequent and seem to be associated with the <i lang="es" title="Spanish language text"><a href="/wiki/El_Ni%C3%B1o" title="El Ni&#241;o">El Ni&#241;o</a></i> phenomena.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>  The statistics from the weather station at <a href="/wiki/Atuona" title="Atuona">Atuona</a> on Hiva &#699;Oa is representative of the average sea-level climate of the Marquesas.  Illustrating the variability of precipitation, the highest annual rainfall recorded in Atuona is 148.2 inches (3,760&#160;mm); the lowest is 22 inches (560&#160;mm).<sup class="reference" class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Kaimoko_Family._Headdress_(Peue_%27Ei),_19th_century..jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1202" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Kaimoko_Family._Headdress_(Peue_%27Ei),_19th_century..jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Kaimoko From the collection of the <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Museum" title="Brooklyn Museum">Brooklyn Museum</a></div></div></div>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Marquesas" title="History of the Marquesas">History of the Marquesas</a></div>\n<p>The first recorded settlers of the Marquesas were <a href="/wiki/Polynesians" title="Polynesians">Polynesians</a>, who, from <a href="/wiki/Archaeology" title="Archaeology">archaeological</a> evidence, was long believed by scholars to have arrived from West Polynesia before 100&#160; AD; other estimates were a settlement from 600&#160;AD.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p><p>However, a 2010 study using revised, high-precision radiocarbon dating based on more reliable samples has established that the period of eastern Polynesian colonization took place much later, in a shorter time frame of two waves: the "earliest in the Society Islands A.D. &#8764;1025&#8211;1120, four centuries later than previously assumed; then after 70&#8211;265 years, dispersal continued in one major pulse to all remaining islands A.D. &#8764;1190&#8211;1290."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fast_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-fast-4">[4]</a></sup> This rapid colonization is believed to account for the "remarkable uniformity of East Polynesia culture, biology and language."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fast_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-fast-4">[4]</a></sup> The new information will require major reworking of scholarship about the development of linguistics and culture in the islands. <a href="/wiki/Ethnology" title="Ethnology">Ethnological</a> and <a href="/wiki/Historical_linguistics" title="Historical linguistics">linguistic</a> evidence suggests that they likely migrated from the Western regions of Polynesia.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_culture">Historical culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marquesas_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Historical culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The rich environment of the islands supported a large population. They lived by fishing, eating both fish and shellfish. They used <a href="/wiki/Breadfruit" title="Breadfruit">breadfruit</a> and raised other foods.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="European_contact">European contact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marquesas_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: European contact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Chef_de_guerre_marquisien-Mus%C3%A9e_d%27histoire_naturelle_et_d%27ethnographie_de_Colmar.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3648" data-file-width="2736" decoding="async" height="293" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/Chef_de_guerre_marquisien-Mus%C3%A9e_d%27histoire_naturelle_et_d%27ethnographie_de_Colmar.jpg/220px-Chef_de_guerre_marquisien-Mus%C3%A9e_d%27histoire_naturelle_et_d%27ethnographie_de_Colmar.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/Chef_de_guerre_marquisien-Mus%C3%A9e_d%27histoire_naturelle_et_d%27ethnographie_de_Colmar.jpg/330px-Chef_de_guerre_marquisien-Mus%C3%A9e_d%27histoire_naturelle_et_d%27ethnographie_de_Colmar.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/Chef_de_guerre_marquisien-Mus%C3%A9e_d%27histoire_naturelle_et_d%27ethnographie_de_Colmar.jpg/440px-Chef_de_guerre_marquisien-Mus%C3%A9e_d%27histoire_naturelle_et_d%27ethnographie_de_Colmar.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Chef_de_guerre_marquisien-Mus%C3%A9e_d%27histoire_naturelle_et_d%27ethnographie_de_Colmar.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Traditional Marquesan warlord\'s headgear, ceremonial clothes, insignia, and weapon.</div></div></div>\n<p>The first Europeans to reach the Marquesas may have been the crew of <i>San Lesmes</i>, a Spanish vessel which disappeared in a storm in June 1526; it was part of an expedition headed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Garc%C3%ADa_Jofre_de_Loa%C3%ADsa" title="Garc&#237;a Jofre de Loa&#237;sa">Garc&#237;a Jofre de Loa&#237;sa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The Spanish explorer <span lang="es" title="Spanish language text"><a href="/wiki/%C3%81lvaro_de_Menda%C3%B1a_de_Neira" title="&#193;lvaro de Menda&#241;a de Neira">&#193;lvaro de Menda&#241;a</a></span> reached them nearly seventy years later on 21 July 1595. He named them after his patron, <a href="/wiki/Garc%C3%ADa_Hurtado_de_Mendoza,_5th_Marquis_of_Ca%C3%B1ete" title="Garc&#237;a Hurtado de Mendoza, 5th Marquis of Ca&#241;ete"><span lang="es" title="Spanish language text">Garc&#237;a Hurtado de Mendoza</span>, 5th Marquis of <span lang="es" title="Spanish language text">Ca&#241;ete</span></a> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es">Marqu&#233;s de Ca&#241;ete</span>), who served as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Viceroy_of_Peru" title="Viceroy of Peru">Viceroy of Peru</a> from 1590 to 1596. Menda&#241;a visited first <a href="/wiki/Fatu_Hiva" title="Fatu Hiva">Fatu Hiva</a> and then <a href="/wiki/Tahuata" title="Tahuata">Tahuata</a> before continuing on to the <a href="/wiki/Solomon_Islands" title="Solomon Islands">Solomon Islands</a>. His expedition charted the four southernmost Marquesas as <i><i lang="es" title="Spanish language text">Magdalena</i></i> (Fatu Hiva), <i><i lang="es" title="Spanish language text">Dominica</i></i> (<a href="/wiki/Hiva_Oa" title="Hiva Oa">Hiva &#699;Oa</a>), <i><i lang="es" title="Spanish language text">San Pedro</i></i> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moho_Tani" title="Moho Tani">Moho Tani</a>), and <i><i lang="es" title="Spanish language text">Santa Cristina</i></i> (Tahuata).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>\n</p><p>In the late 16th century European explorers estimated the population at more than 100,000.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Europeans and Americans were impressed with how easy life appeared to be in the islands, which had a rich habitat and environment. In 1791 the American <a href="/wiki/Maritime_fur_trade" title="Maritime fur trade">maritime fur trader</a> <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Ingraham" title="Joseph Ingraham">Joseph Ingraham</a> first visited the northern Marquesas while commanding the <a href="/wiki/Brig" title="Brig">brig</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Hope_(ship)" title="Hope (ship)">Hope</a>.</i> He named them the Washington Islands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WDL_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-WDL-13">[13]</a></sup> In 1813 <a href="/wiki/Commodore_(United_States)" title="Commodore (United States)">Commodore</a> <a href="/wiki/David_Porter_(naval_officer)" title="David Porter (naval officer)">David Porter</a> claimed <a href="/wiki/Nuku_Hiva" title="Nuku Hiva">Nuku Hiva</a> for the United States, but the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">United States Congress</a> never ratified that claim.\n</p><p>In 1842 France conducted a successful military operation on behalf of the native chief <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Iotete&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Iotete (page does not exist)">Iotete</a>, who claimed he was king of the whole island of Tahuata. The government laid claim to the whole group and established a settlement on Nuku Hiva. That settlement was abandoned in 1857, but France re-established control over the group in 1870. It later incorporated the Marquesas into <a href="/wiki/French_Polynesia" title="French Polynesia">French Polynesia</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Pareu_haka.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="599" data-file-width="892" decoding="async" height="148" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/Pareu_haka.jpg/220px-Pareu_haka.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/Pareu_haka.jpg/330px-Pareu_haka.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/Pareu_haka.jpg/440px-Pareu_haka.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Pareu_haka.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Marquesans dressed in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pareu" title="Pareu">pareu</a> demonstrating traditional dance, 1909</div></div></div>\n<p>Of all major island groups in the Pacific, the Marquesas suffered the greatest population decline in Polynesia from endemic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Infectious_disease" title="Infectious disease">diseases</a> carried by Western explorers. The indigenous people suffered high rates of mortality, as they had no <a href="/wiki/Immunity_(medical)" title="Immunity (medical)">immunity</a> to the new diseases. Such infectious diseases as <a href="/wiki/Smallpox" title="Smallpox">smallpox</a>, <a href="/wiki/Measles" title="Measles">measles</a> and others reduced the eighteenth-century population of over 78,000 inhabitants to about 20,000 by the middle of the nineteenth century.\nBy the turn of the 20th century, the islands\' population had reduced to just over 4,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-history-14">[14]</a></sup> During the course of the twentieth century, after reaching a nadir of 2,255 in 1926, the population finally started to increase, reaching 8,548 at the November 2002 census,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RP71_02_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-RP71_02-15">[15]</a></sup> not including the Marquesan community residing on <a href="/wiki/Tahiti" title="Tahiti">Tahiti</a>. It has continued to increase, reaching 9,346 inhabitants at the August 2017 census.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-census_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-census-2">[2]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government_and_politics">Government and politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marquesas_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Government and politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Marquesas Islands form one of the <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_French_Polynesia" title="Administrative divisions of French Polynesia">five administrative divisions</a> (<i><i lang="fr" title="French language text">subdivisions administratives</i></i>) of French Polynesia. French and Tahitian are the official languages of government. The capital of the Marquesas Islands administrative subdivision is the settlement of <a href="/wiki/Taiohae" title="Taiohae">Taiohae</a> on the island of <a href="/wiki/Nuku_Hiva" title="Nuku Hiva">Nuku Hiva</a>.\n</p><p>The sparsely populated Marquesas Islands are located 1,371&#160;km (852&#160;mi) from <a href="/wiki/Tahiti" title="Tahiti">Tahiti</a>. With 183,645 inhabitants (2012 census), Tahiti is the most populous island of French Polynesia, containing 68.5% of the total population of the grouping.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Residents of the Marquesas have chafed at Tahiti\'s overwhelming dominance, complaining of neglect by politicians based in Tahiti, and leaders have suggested developing a direct relationship with the metropole, the government in Paris, instead of depending on <a href="/wiki/Papeete" title="Papeete">Papeete</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-secession_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-secession-17">[17]</a></sup> As sentiment was rising in Tahiti in the 21st century for independence from France, several prominent Marquesan political leaders in 2007 floated the idea of the Marquesas Islands separating from French Polynesia but remaining within the French Republic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-secession_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-secession-17">[17]</a></sup> This has generated controversies in Tahiti, where pro-independence Tahitian leaders have accused the French central government of encouraging the separation of the Marquesas Islands from French Polynesia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-secession_17-2"><a href="#cite_note-secession-17">[17]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administration">Administration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marquesas_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Administration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flags_of_the_Marquesas_Islands" title="Flags of the Marquesas Islands">Flags of the Marquesas Islands</a></div>\n<p>The Marquesas Islands do not have a provincial or regional assembly. Administratively, they form a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Decentralisation" title="Decentralisation">deconcentrated</a> subdivision of both the French central State and the government of French Polynesia. As a deconcentrated subdivision of the French central State, the Marquesas Islands form the administrative subdivision of the Marquesas (French: <i lang="fr">subdivision administrative des Marquises</i>), one of <a href="/wiki/French_Polynesia" title="French Polynesia">French Polynesia</a>\'s five <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_French_Polynesia" title="Administrative divisions of French Polynesia">administrative subdivisions</a>. The head of the administrative subdivision of the Marquesas is the <i><i lang="fr" title="French language text">administrateur d\'&#201;tat</i></i> ("State administrator"), generally simply known as <i><i lang="fr" title="French language text">administrateur</i></i>, also sometimes called <i><i lang="fr" title="French language text">chef de la subdivision administrative</i></i> ("head of the administrative subdivision"). The <i><i lang="fr" title="French language text">administrateur</i></i> is a civil servant under the authority of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=High_Commissioner_of_the_French_Republic_in_French_Polynesia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="High Commissioner of the French Republic in French Polynesia (page does not exist)">High Commissioner of the French Republic in French Polynesia</a> in <a href="/wiki/Papeete" title="Papeete">Papeete</a>. The <i><i lang="fr" title="French language text">administrateur</i></i> and his staff sit in <a href="/wiki/Taiohae" title="Taiohae">Taiohae</a>, on the island of <a href="/wiki/Nuku_Hiva" title="Nuku Hiva">Nuku Hiva</a>, which has become the administrative capital of the Marquesas Islands, having replaced <a href="/wiki/Atuona" title="Atuona">Atuona</a> on the island of <a href="/wiki/Hiva_Oa" title="Hiva Oa">Hiva &#699;Oa</a>, which was previously the capital.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:FatuIva_Hanavave_Port_20061111.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2553" data-file-width="5527" decoding="async" height="139" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/FatuIva_Hanavave_Port_20061111.jpg/300px-FatuIva_Hanavave_Port_20061111.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/FatuIva_Hanavave_Port_20061111.jpg/450px-FatuIva_Hanavave_Port_20061111.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/FatuIva_Hanavave_Port_20061111.jpg/600px-FatuIva_Hanavave_Port_20061111.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:FatuIva_Hanavave_Port_20061111.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hanavave" title="Hanavave">Hanavave</a></div></div></div>\n<p>Acting as the representative of the French central State and delegate of Papeete\'s High Commissioner, the <i><i lang="fr" title="French language text">administrateur</i></i> of the Marquesas is in charge of:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Offering legal advice to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commune_in_France" title="Commune in France">communes</a> (municipalities) of the Marquesas and verifying the legality of decisions made by the communes</li>\n<li>Issuing official documents (<a href="/wiki/National_identity_card_(France)" title="National identity card (France)">ID cards</a>, <a href="/wiki/Driving_licence_in_France" title="Driving licence in France">driving licences</a>, etc.), applying immigration rules, organising elections</li>\n<li>Managing security (coordination of <a href="/wiki/National_Gendarmerie" title="National Gendarmerie">gendarmerie</a> forces, handling of major crises such as natural disasters, etc.)</li>\n<li>Overseeing public services of the French central State in the Marquesas Islands (such as the correctional facility on Nuku Hiva)</li></ul>\n<p>As a deconcentrated subdivision of the government of French Polynesia, the Marquesas Islands form the <i><i lang="fr" title="French language text">circonscription des Marquises</i></i> ("district of the Marquesas"), one of French Polynesia\'s four <i><i lang="fr" title="French language text">circonscriptions</i></i> ("districts") created in 2000 by the <a href="/wiki/Assembly_of_French_Polynesia" title="Assembly of French Polynesia">Assembly of French Polynesia</a> to serve as deconcentrated subdivisions of the government of French Polynesia in the islands away from <a href="/wiki/Tahiti" title="Tahiti">Tahiti</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moorea" title="Moorea">Moorea</a>. The head of the <i><i lang="fr" title="French language text">circonscription des Marquises</i></i> is the <i><i lang="ty" title="Tahitian language text">tavana hau</i></i>, known as <i><i lang="fr" title="French language text">administrateur territorial</i></i> in French ("territorial administrator"), but the <a href="/wiki/Tahitian_language" title="Tahitian language">Tahitian</a> title <i><i lang="ty" title="Tahitian language text">tavana hau</i></i> is most often used. The <i><i lang="ty" title="Tahitian language text">tavana hau</i></i> is the direct representative of the <a href="/wiki/President_of_French_Polynesia" title="President of French Polynesia">president of French Polynesia\'s government</a> who appoints him. The <i><i lang="ty" title="Tahitian language text">tavana hau</i></i> and his staff sit in Taiohae on Nuku Hiva, same as the State administrator.\n</p><p>The <i><i lang="ty" title="Tahitian language text">tavana hau</i></i> is in charge of:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Coordinating the work of French Polynesian administrations in the Marquesas Islands (such as the French Polynesian administrations in charge of roads, fisheries, etc.)</li>\n<li>Ensuring the enforcement of acts passed by the Assembly of French Polynesia and decisions taken by the government of French Polynesia</li>\n<li>Evaluating the performance of French Polynesian civil servants and sending the evaluations to the responsible ministries in Papeete</li>\n<li>Acting as a liaison between the local population and the government of French Polynesia in Papeete</li></ul>\n<p>The Marquesas Islands also form the electoral district of the Marquesas Islands, one of <a href="/wiki/French_Polynesia" title="French Polynesia">French Polynesia</a>\'s six electoral districts for the <a href="/wiki/Assembly_of_French_Polynesia" title="Assembly of French Polynesia">Assembly of French Polynesia</a> (see also <a href="/wiki/Politics_of_French_Polynesia" title="Politics of French Polynesia">Politics of French Polynesia</a>).\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Marquesas-administrative.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1593" data-file-width="1281" decoding="async" height="274" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Marquesas-administrative.png/220px-Marquesas-administrative.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Marquesas-administrative.png/330px-Marquesas-administrative.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Marquesas-administrative.png/440px-Marquesas-administrative.png 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Marquesas-administrative.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Communes of the Marquesas Islands</div></div></div>\n<p>The Marquesas Islands are subdivided in six <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commune_in_France" title="Commune in France">communes</a> (municipalities). In each of the six communes the local residents elect a municipal council and a mayor in charge of managing local affairs within the commune. Three communes (<a href="/wiki/Nuku-Hiva" title="Nuku-Hiva">Nuku-Hiva</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ua_Pu" title="Ua Pu">Ua-Pou</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hiva-Oa" title="Hiva-Oa">Hiva-Oa</a>) are further subdivided into <a href="/wiki/Associated_communes_of_France" title="Associated communes of France">associated communes</a> due to their larger population. The communes and associated communes are the only elected councils in the Marquesas since there does not exist a provincial or regional assembly for the entire archipelago. Municipal elections are held every six years on the same day as municipal elections in the rest of France (see <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/French_municipal_elections,_2014" title="French municipal elections, 2014">French municipal elections, 2014</a> for the last municipal elections).\n</p><p>The areas and populations of the communes at the 2012 Census were as title="Marquesan language">Marquesan</a></b> is a collection of <a href="/wiki/Polynesian_languages" title="Polynesian languages">East-Central Polynesian</a> dialects, of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marquesic_languages" title="Marquesic languages">Marquesic</a> group, spoken in the Marquesas Islands of <a href="/wiki/French_Polynesia" title="French Polynesia">French Polynesia</a>. They are usually classified into two groups, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Marquesan_language" title="North Marquesan language">North Marquesan</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Marquesan_language" title="South Marquesan language">South Marquesan</a>, corresponding roughly along geographic lines.\n</p><p>The North Marquesan dialects are spoken on the islands of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ua_Pu" title="Ua Pu">Ua Pu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nuku_Hiva" title="Nuku Hiva">Nuku Hiva</a>, and South Marquesan dialects on the islands of <a href="/wiki/Hiva_Oa" title="Hiva Oa">Hiva &#699;Oa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tahuata" title="Tahuata">Tahuata</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fatu_Hiva" title="Fatu Hiva">Fatu Hiva</a>. The dialects of <a href="/wiki/Ua_Huka" title="Ua Huka">Ua Huka</a> are often incorrectly classified as North Marquesan; they are instead transitional. While the island is in the northern Marquesas group, the dialects show more morphological and phonological affinities with South Marquesan. The North Marquesan dialects are sometimes considered to be two separate languages: North Marquesan and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tai_Pi_Marquesan_language&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tai Pi Marquesan language (page does not exist)">Tai Pi Marquesan</a>, the latter being spoken in the valleys of the eastern third of the island of Nuku Hiva, in the ancient province of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tai_Pi_(province)" title="Tai Pi (province)">Tai Pi</a>.\n</p><p>The most striking feature of the Marquesan languages is their almost universal replacement of the <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/r/</span> or <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/l/</span> of other Polynesian languages by a <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/&#660;/</span> (glottal stop).\n</p><p>Like other Polynesian languages, the phonology of Marquesan languages is characterised by a paucity of consonants and a comparative abundance of vowels.\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2007_language_data_in_census">2007 language data in census</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marquesas_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: 2007 language data in census">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>At the 2007 census, 94.1% of the population whose age was 15 and older reported that they could speak French. 90.2% reported that they could also read and write it.
