<i><b>crore</b></i> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="\'k\' in notation">scientific notation</a>) and is equal to 100 <i><a href="/wiki/Lakh" title="Lakh">lakh</a></i> in the <a href="/wiki/Indian_numbering_system" title="Indian numbering system">Indian numbering system</a> as <b>1,00,00,000</b> with the local style of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Digit_group_separator" title="Digit group separator">digit group separators</a> (a <i>lakh</i> is equal to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/100000_(number)" title="100000 (number)">one hundred thousand</a> and is written as 1,00,000).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nroer_1-0"><a id="Money">Money</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Money">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Large amounts of money in India, Pakistan, Bangladesh and Nepal are often written in terms of <i>crores</i>.\nFor example, 150,000,000 (one hundred and fifty million) is written as \'fifteen <i>crore</i> <a href="/wiki/Rupee" title="Rupee">rupees</a>\', \'15crore\' or \'Rs&#160;15 crore\'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nroer_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-nroer-1">[1]</a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Orders_of_magnitude_(numbers)#1012" title="Orders of magnitude (numbers)">Trillions</a> (in the <a href="/wiki/Long_and_short_scales" title="Long and short scales">short scale</a>) of money are often written or spoken of in terms of <i>lakh crore</i>. For example, <i>one <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trillion_(short_scale)" title="Trillion (short scale)">trillion</a> rupees</i> is:<br/>\n</p>\n<dl><dd>= <i>One lakh crore rupees</i></dd>\n<dd>= <span style="white-space: nowrap">&#8377;</span>1&#160;lakh crore</dd>\n<dd>= Rs&#160;1&#160;lakh crore</dd>\n<dd>= Rs&#160;10<sup>5+7</sup></dd>\n<dd>= Rs&#160;10<sup>12</sup></dd>\n<dd>= Rs&#160;10,00,00,00,00,000 in Indian notation</dd>\n<dd>= Rs&#160;1,000,000,000,000 in Western notation</dd></dl>\n<p><i>Lakh</i> is also used in Sri Lanka; however, most Sri Lankans use the term <i>koatiya</i> (<span lang="si" title="Sinhalese language text">&#3482;&#3549;&#3495;&#3538;&#3514;</span>) or <i>koti</i> (<span lang="ta" title="Tamil language text">&#2965;&#3019;&#2975;&#3007;</span>) for <i>crore</i> when referring to money.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="Is the word \'crore\' used otherwise, for example, in demographics? (November 2016)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\nThe word <i>crore</i> is a borrowing from the <a href="/wiki/Prakrit" title="Prakrit">Prakrit</a> word <i>kro&#7693;i</i>, is in turn from the <a href="/wiki/Sanskrit" title="Sanskrit">Sanskrit</a> <i>ko&#7789;i</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> denoting ten million in the Indian numbering system, which has separate terms for most powers of ten from 10<sup>0</sup> up to 10<sup>19</sup>.
