to navigation</a>\n<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>\n<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Jiaozhou</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh"><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E4%BA%A4%E5%B7%9E" title="wikt:&#20132;&#24030;">&#20132;&#24030;</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_language" title="Vietnamese language">Vietnamese</a>: <i lang="vi">Giao Ch&#226;u</i>) was an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imperial_China" title="Imperial China">imperial Chinese</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zhou_(administrative_division)" title="Zhou (administrative division)">province</a> under the <a href="/wiki/Han_dynasty" title="Han dynasty">Han</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jin_dynasty_(265%E2%80%93420)" title="Jin dynasty (265&#8211;420)">Jin</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Chinese_dynasties" title="List of Chinese dynasties">dynasties</a>. Under the Han, the area included <a href="/wiki/Liangguang" title="Liangguang">Liangguang</a> and <a href="/wiki/Northern_Vietnam" title="Northern Vietnam">northern Vietnam</a> but <a href="/wiki/Guangdong" title="Guangdong">Guangdong</a> was later separated to form the province of <a href="/wiki/Gu%C7%8Eng_Prefecture" title="Gu&#462;ng Prefecture">Guangzhou</a> by <a href="/wiki/Sun_Quan" title="Sun Quan">Sun Quan</a> following the death of <a href="/wiki/Shi_Xie" title="Shi Xie">Shi Xie</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc"><input class="toctogglecheckbox" id="Han_Dynasty">Han Dynasty</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jiaozhou_(region)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Han Dynasty">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In 111&#160;<span class="smallcaps"><span style="font-variant: small-caps; text-transform: lowercase;">BC</span></span>, the armies of <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Wu_of_Han" title="Emperor Wu of Han">Emperor Wu</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Han%E2%80%93Nanyue_War" title="Han&#8211;Nanyue War">conquered</a> the rebel state of <a href="/wiki/Nanyue" title="Nanyue">Nanyue</a> and organized the area as the circuit (<a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E9%83%A8" title="wikt:&#37096;">&#37096;</a> <i>b&#249;</i>) of Ji&#257;ozh&#464;, under the rule of a <i>c&#236;sh&#464;</i> (<a class="extiw" href="https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%88%BA%E5%8F%B2" title="zh:&#21050;&#21490;">zh:&#21050;&#21490;</a>, <a class="extiw" href="https://vi.wikipedia.org/wiki/th%E1%BB%A9_s%E1%BB%AD" title="vi:th&#7913; s&#7917;">vi:th&#7913; s&#7917;</a>). In addition to six original <a href="/wiki/Jun_(country_subdivision)" title="Jun (country subdivision)">commanderies</a> (<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nanhai_Commandery&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nanhai Commandery (page does not exist)">Nanhai</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hepu_Commandery" title="Hepu Commandery">Hepu</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cangwu_Commandery&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cangwu Commandery (page does not exist)">Cangwu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yulin,_Guangxi" title="Yulin, Guangxi">Yulin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jiaozhi" title="Jiaozhi">Jiaozhi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jiuzhen" title="Jiuzhen">Jiuzhen</a>), the Han Empire conquered new territories on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hainan_Island" title="Hainan Island">Hainan Island</a> as well as in the area south of the <a href="/wiki/Ngang_Pass" title="Ngang Pass">Ngang Pass</a> and established them as the commanderies of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zhuya_Commandery" title="Zhuya Commandery">Zhuya</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dan%27er_Commandery" title="Dan\'er Commandery">Dan\'er</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Rinan" title="Rinan">Rinan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Han28_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Han28-1">[1]</a></sup> In 203 CE, <i><b>Ji&#257;ozh&#464; b&#249;</b></i> was renamed to <i><b>Ji&#257;ozh&#333;u</b></i>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Eastern_Wu">Eastern Wu</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jiaozhou_(region)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Eastern Wu">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Following the death of <a href="/wiki/Shi_Xie" title="Shi Xie">Shi Xie</a> in 226 CE, <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Wu" title="Eastern Wu">Eastern Wu</a> divided Jiaozhou into <a href="/wiki/Gu%C7%8Eng_Prefecture" title="Gu&#462;ng Prefecture">Guangzhou</a> and the new Jiaozhou. However, after suppressing Shi Hui (<a class="extiw" href="https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A3%AB%E5%BE%BD" title="zh:&#22763;&#24509;">&#22763;&#24509;</a>), son of Shi Xie, Eastern Wu re-annexed Guangzhou into Jiaozhou. It was only in 264 CE that Jiaozhou was re-divided: Guangzhou was composed of three commanderies of Nanhai, Cangwu and Yulin while the new Jiaozhou was composed of four commanderies of Hepu, Jiaozhi, Jiuzhen and Rinan.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Jin_Dynasty">Jin Dynasty</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
