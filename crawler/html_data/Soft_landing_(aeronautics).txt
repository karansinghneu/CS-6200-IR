(Contradictory to <a href="/wiki/Hard_landing" title="Hard landing">hard landing</a>.)\n</p><p>This can be achieved by\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Parachute" title="Parachute">parachute</a>&#8212;often this is <a href="/wiki/Splashdown" title="Splashdown">into water</a>.</li>\n<li>vertical rocket powered landing, often referred to as <a href="/wiki/VTVL" title="VTVL">VTVL</a> (vertical landing referred to as <a href="/wiki/VTOL" title="VTOL">VTOL</a>, is usually for aircraft landing in a "level" attitude, rather than rockets &#8212; first achieved with an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Falcon_9_Flight_20" title="Falcon 9 Flight 20">orbital rocket vehicle stage on December 22, 2015</a> by a <a href="/wiki/SpaceX" title="SpaceX">SpaceX</a> <a href="/wiki/Falcon_9" title="Falcon 9">Falcon 9</a>.</li>\n<li>horizontal landing, most aircraft and some spacecraft, such as the <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle" title="Space Shuttle">Space Shuttle</a>, land this way.</li>\n<li>being caught, as attempted with <a href="/wiki/Genesis_(spacecraft)" title="Genesis (spacecraft)">Genesis (spacecraft)</a> and followed by some other form of <a href="/wiki/Takeoff_and_landing" title="Takeoff and landing">landing</a>.</li></ul>\n<p>Soft landing can be contrasted with the term <a href="/wiki/Hard_landing" title="Hard landing">hard landing</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
