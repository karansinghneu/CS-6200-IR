class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Lady_Emmeline_Stuart_Wortley_ne_Manners_mw38710_(cropped).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div></div></div></div>\n<p><b>Lady Emmeline Charlotte Elizabeth Stuart-Wortley</b> (n&#233;e <b>Manners</b>; 1806 &#8211; 20 October 1855) was an English poet and writer, best known for her <i>Travels in the United States, etc. During 1849 and 1850</i>. She was editor of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Keepsake" title="The Keepsake">The Keepsake</a></i> in 1837 and 1840.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>\n</p><p>She was a daughter of <a href="/wiki/John_Manners,_5th_Duke_of_Rutland" title="John Manners, 5th Duke of Rutland">John Manners, 5th Duke of Rutland</a> and his wife <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Manners,_Duchess_of_Rutland" title="Elizabeth Manners, Duchess of Rutland">Elizabeth Manners, Duchess of Rutland</a> and on 17 February 1831, married Hon. <a href="/wiki/Charles_Stuart-Wortley-Mackenzie" title="Charles Stuart-Wortley-Mackenzie">Charles Stuart-Wortley-Mackenzie</a>, a son of <a href="/wiki/James_Stuart-Wortley,_1st_Baron_Wharncliffe" title="James Stuart-Wortley, 1st Baron Wharncliffe">James Stuart-Wortley-Mackenzie, 1st Baron Wharncliffe</a>. They had a daughter, <a href="/wiki/Victoria,_Lady_Welby" title="Victoria, Lady Welby">Lady Victoria Welby</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-schmitz1985_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-schmitz1985-2">[2]</a></sup>\n</p><p>After the death of her husband in 1844, she began to travel as a wealthy Victorian widow, along with her daughter, Victoria.  In October 1855, she died of <a href="/wiki/Dysentery" title="Dysentery">dysentery</a> in the area between Antioch and Beirut, while travelling through Ottoman Empire regions following the history of the early Christians.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-significs_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-significs-3">[3]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc"><input class="toctogglecheckbox" id="toctogglecheckbox" role="button" style="display:none" type="checkbox"/><div class="toctitle" dir="ltr" lang="en"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Bibliography"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Bibliography</span></a>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-2 tocsection-2"><a href="#On_Lady_Emmeline_Stuart-Wortley"><span class="tocnumber">1.1</span> <span class="toctext">On Lady Emmeline Stuart-Wortley</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bibliography">Bibliography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lady_Emmeline_Stuart-Wortley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Bibliography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><i><a class="external text" href="https://babel.hathitrust.org/cgi/pt?id=uc1.aa0003675527;view=1up;seq=14" rel="nofollow">Poems</a></i>, London: John Murray 1833</li>\n<li><i>London at Night, and other Poems</i>, London: Longman, Rees, Orme, Brown, Green, &amp;
