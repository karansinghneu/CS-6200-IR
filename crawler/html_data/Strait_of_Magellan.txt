width</th><td>2&#160;km (1.2&#160;mi)</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Strait of Magellan</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">Estrecho de Magallanes</i>), also called the <i>Straits of Magellan</i>, is a navigable sea route in southern <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a> separating mainland <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a> to the north and <a href="/wiki/Tierra_del_Fuego" title="Tierra del Fuego">Tierra del Fuego</a> to the south. The <a href="/wiki/Strait" title="Strait">strait</a> is the most important natural passage between the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific</a> oceans.\n</p><p>The route is considered difficult to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Navigate" title="Navigate">navigate</a> due to frequent narrows and unpredictable winds and currents. <a href="/wiki/Maritime_pilot" title="Maritime pilot">Maritime piloting</a> is now compulsory. The strait is shorter and more sheltered than the <a href="/wiki/Drake_Passage" title="Drake Passage">Drake Passage</a>, the often stormy open sea route around <a href="/wiki/Cape_Horn" title="Cape Horn">Cape Horn</a>. Along with the narrow and sometimes treacherous <a href="/wiki/Beagle_Channel" title="Beagle Channel">Beagle Channel</a> and the seasonal and historically treacherous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_West_Passage" title="North West Passage">North West Passage</a>, these were the only sea routes between the Atlantic and Pacific until the construction of the <a href="/wiki/Panama_Canal" title="Panama Canal">Panama Canal</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc"><input class="toctogglecheckbox" id="toctogglecheckbox" role="button" style="display:none" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Strait_of_Magellan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Indigenous peoples">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Selk%27nam_people" title="Selk\'nam people">Selk\'nam people</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alacalufe_people" title="Alacalufe people">Alacalufe people</a></div><p>The Strait of Magellan has been inhabited by indigenous Americans for thousands of years. On the western part of its northern coast lived the <a href="/wiki/Alacalufe" title="Alacalufe">Alacalufe</a>, also known as the Kaw&#233;sqar. To the east of the Kaw&#233;sqar lived the <a href="/wiki/Tehuelche_people" title="Tehuelche people">Tehuelche</a>, whose territory extended to the north in <a href="/wiki/Patagonia" title="Patagonia">Patagonia</a>. South of the Tehuelche, across the Strait of Magellan, lived the <a href="/wiki/Selk%27nam_people" title="Selk\'nam people">Selk\'nam</a>, who inhabited the majority of the eastern portion of <a href="/wiki/Tierra_del_Fuego" title="Tierra del Fuego">Tierra del Fuego</a>. To the west of the Selk\'nam were the <a href="/wiki/Yaghan_people" title="Yaghan people">Yaghan</a>, who inhabited the southernmost part of <a href="/wiki/Tierra_del_Fuego" title="Tierra del Fuego">Tierra del Fuego</a>.\n</p><p>All tribes in the Strait of Magellan area were nomadic hunter-gatherers. The only non-maritime culture in the area were the Tehuelche, who fished and gathered shellfish along the coast during the winter and moved into the southern Andes in the summer to hunt. In the other three maritime-based tribes, each family had a canoe, around 7 to 9 feet long, that held the entire family and a dog. The canoe often contained a fire the family would use for warmth. Their sustenance came almost entirely from the sea: the men typically hunted seals and fished, while the women dove into the frigid waters to gather shellfish. They wore little to no clothing, and made extensive use of fires to keep warm in the bitterly-cold climate.\n</p><p>The tribes of the region faced little European interference until the late 19th century. Then, European diseases wiped out large portions of the indigenous population, and extermination campaigns sponsored by the governments of Chile and Argentina, like the <a href="/wiki/Selk%27nam_genocide" title="Selk\'nam genocide">Selk\'nam genocide</a>, eliminated the rest. Some were taken in by missionaries, but today, almost no full-blooded descendants of these peoples remain, and almost all of their culture is lost. Only two languages formerly spoken along the Strait of Magellan are extant, and both are spoken by less than ten people, although today there are hundreds of people who self-identify as descendants of indigenous peoples from the Strait of Magellan.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Discovery_by_Europeans">Discovery by Europeans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Strait_of_Magellan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Discovery by Europeans">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Accounts_before_Magellan">Accounts before Magellan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Strait_of_Magellan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Accounts before Magellan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>It was reported by <a href="/wiki/Ant%C3%B3nio_Galv%C3%A3o" title="Ant&#243;nio Galv&#227;o">Ant&#243;nio Galv&#227;o</a> in 1563 that the position of the Strait of Magellan was previously mentioned in old charts as <i>Dragon\'s Tail</i> (Draco Cola):\n<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r856303468">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>he [<a href="/wiki/Peter,_Duke_of_Coimbra" title="Peter, Duke of Coimbra">Pedro</a>] brought a map which had all the circuit of the world described. The Strait of Magellan was called the Dragon\'s Tail; and there were also the Cape of Good Hope and the coast of Africa. ... Francisco de Sousa Tavares told me that in the year 1528, the Infant D. Fernando showed him a map which had been found in the Cartorio of Alcoba&#231;a, which had been made more than 120 years before, the which contained all the navigation of India with the Cape of Good Hope.</p><div class="templatequotecite">&#8212;&#8201;<cite>Galvano, <a class="external text" href="https://books.google.com/books?id=XivHTiZoMycC" rel="nofollow">Discovery of the World</a>, sub ann. 1428.</cite></div>\n</blockquote>\n<p>This, however, would suggest that the Strait was mentioned in maps before the Americas were discovered, and consequently the claim has to be considered dubious.\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Magellan">Magellan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Strait_of_Magellan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Magellan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:NaoVictoria.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="4000" data-file-width="3000" decoding="async" height="200" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/NaoVictoria.JPG/150px-NaoVictoria.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/NaoVictoria.JPG/225px-NaoVictoria.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/NaoVictoria.JPG/300px-NaoVictoria.JPG 2x" width="150"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:NaoVictoria.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A replica of <a href="/wiki/Victoria_(ship)" title="Victoria (ship)"><i>Victoria</i></a>, one of Magellan\'s ships, in the <a href="/wiki/Museo_Nao_Victoria" title="Museo Nao Victoria">Museo Nao Victoria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Punta_Arenas" title="Punta Arenas">Punta Arenas</a>. Chile</div></div></div>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_Magellan" title="Ferdinand Magellan">Ferdinand Magellan</a> (<a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>: <i lang="pt">Fern&#227;o de Magalh&#227;es</i>), a Portuguese explorer and navigator in the service of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charles_I_of_Spain" title="Charles I of Spain">Charles I of Spain</a>, became the first European to navigate the strait in 1520 during his global circumnavigation voyage.\n</p><p>On March 22, 1518, the expedition was organized in <a href="/wiki/Valladolid" title="Valladolid">Valladolid</a>, naming Magellan captain general of the fleet and governor of all the lands discovered, and establishing the privileges of Magellan and his business associate <a href="/wiki/Rui_Faleiro" title="Rui Faleiro">Rui Faleiro</a>. The fleet would become known as the "Armada de las Molucas" or "Fleet of the Moluccas". The expeditionary fleet of five ships set sail from <a href="/wiki/Sanl%C3%BAcar_de_Barrameda" title="Sanl&#250;car de Barrameda">Sanl&#250;car de Barrameda</a> on September 20, 1519.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-murphy_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-murphy-2">[2]</a></sup>\n</p><p>The five ships included <i><a href="/wiki/Trinidad_(ship)" title="Trinidad (ship)">La Trinidad</a></i> (110 tons, crew 55), under the command of Magellan; <i>La San Antonio</i> (120 tons, crew&#160;60) under the command of <a href="/wiki/Juan_de_Cartagena" title="Juan de Cartagena">Juan de Cartagena</a>; <i>La Concepci&#243;n</i> (90 tons, crew&#160;45) under the command of Gaspar de Quezada (<a href="/wiki/Juan_Sebasti%C3%A1n_Elcano" title="Juan Sebasti&#225;n Elcano">Juan Sebasti&#225;n Elcano</a> served as boatswain); <a href="/wiki/Victoria_(ship)" title="Victoria (ship)"><i>La Victoria</i></a> (85 tons, crew 42) under the command of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luis_de_Mendoza" title="Luis de Mendoza">Luis de Mendoza</a>; and <i>La Santiago</i> (75 tons, crew&#160;32), under command of Juan Rodr&#237;guez Serrano (<a href="/wiki/Jo%C3%A3o_Serr%C3%A3o" title="Jo&#227;o Serr&#227;o">Jo&#227;o Rodrigues Serr&#227;o</a>). Before the passage of the Strait (and after the mutiny in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_San_Juli%C3%A1n" title="Puerto San Juli&#225;n">Puerto San Juli&#225;n</a>), &#193;lvaro de Mesquita became captain of the <i>San Antonio</i>, and <a href="/wiki/Duarte_Barbosa" title="Duarte Barbosa">Duarte Barbosa</a> of <i>Victoria</i>. Later, Serr&#227;o became captain of <i>Concepcion</i> (his <i>Santiago</i>, sent on a mission to find the passage, was caught in a storm and wrecked). <i>San Antonio</i>, charged to explore Magdalen Sound, failed to return to the fleet, instead sailing back to Spain under <a href="/wiki/Est%C3%AAv%C3%A3o_Gomes" title="Est&#234;v&#227;o Gomes">Est&#234;v&#227;o Gomes</a> who imprisoned the captain Mesquita.\n</p><p>Magellan\'s ships entered the strait on November 1, 1520, <a href="/wiki/All_Saints%27_Day" title="All Saints\' Day">All Saints\' Day</a>, and it was initially called <i>Estrecho de Todos los Santos</i> (Strait of All Saints). Magellan\'s chronicler, <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Pigafetta" title="Antonio Pigafetta">Antonio Pigafetta</a>, called it the <i>Patagonian Strait</i>, and others <i>Victoria Strait</i>, commemorating the first ship entering it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-murphy_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-murphy-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bergreen_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-bergreen-3">[3]</a></sup> Within seven years it was being called <i>Estrecho de Magallanes</i> in honor of Magellan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-murphy_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-murphy-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bergreen_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-bergreen-3">[3]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spanish Empire</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Captaincy_General_of_Chile" title="Captaincy General of Chile">Captaincy General of Chile</a> used it as the southern boundary of their territory.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_explorers">Other explorers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Strait_of_Magellan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Other explorers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>The first Spanish colony was established in 1584 by <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Sarmiento_de_Gamboa" title="Pedro Sarmiento de Gamboa">Pedro Sarmiento de Gamboa</a>, who founded <a href="/wiki/Nombre_de_Jes%C3%BAs_(Patagonia)" title="Nombre de Jes&#250;s (Patagonia)">Nombre de Jes&#250;s</a> and <a href="/wiki/Puerto_del_Hambre" title="Puerto del Hambre">Rey Don Felipe</a> on the northern shore of the strait. These towns suffered severe food shortages, and when the English navigator Sir <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Cavendish" title="Thomas Cavendish">Thomas Cavendish</a> landed at the site of Rey Don Felipe in 1587, he found only ruins of the settlement. He renamed the place <i><a href="/wiki/Puerto_del_Hambre" title="Puerto del Hambre">Port Famine</a></i>.\n</p><p>Other early explorers included <a href="/wiki/Francis_Drake" title="Francis Drake">Francis Drake</a> (1578). In February 1696 the first French expedition, under the command of M. de Gennes reached the Strait of Magellan. The expedition is described by the young French explorer, engineer and hydrographer <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fran%C3%A7ois_Froger&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fran&#231;ois Froger (page does not exist)">Fran&#231;ois Froger</a> in his <i><a href="/wiki/A_Relation_of_a_Voyage" title="A Relation of a Voyage">A Relation of a Voyage</a></i> (1699). The first map of the Pacific Ocean, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Descriptio_Maris_Pacifici" title="Descriptio Maris Pacifici">Descriptio Maris Pacifici</a> from 1589, depicts the strait as the only route between the Atlantic and Pacific oceans. The much wider <a href="/wiki/Drake_Passage" title="Drake Passage">Drake Passage</a> was discovered in 1616.\n</p><p>The strait was first carefully explored and thoroughly charted by <a href="/wiki/Phillip_Parker_King" title="Phillip Parker King">Phillip Parker King</a>, who commanded the British survey vessel <a href="/wiki/HMS_Aid_(1809)" title="HMS Aid (1809)">HMS <i>Adventure</i></a>, and in consort with <a href="/wiki/HMS_Beagle" title="HMS Beagle">HMS <i>Beagle</i></a> spent five years surveying the complex coasts around the strait (1826&#8211;1830). A report on the survey was presented at two meetings of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Geographical_Society" title="Royal Geographical Society">Geographical Society of London</a> in 1831.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Incorporation_into_Chile">Incorporation into Chile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Strait_of_Magellan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Incorporation into Chile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Patagonian_sheep_farming_boom" title="Patagonian sheep farming boom">Patagonian sheep farming boom</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tierra_del_Fuego_gold_rush" title="Tierra del Fuego gold rush">Tierra del Fuego gold rush</a></div>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a> took possession of the Strait of Magellan on May 23, 1843. President Bulnes of Chile ordered this expedition after consulting the Chilean <a href="/wiki/Libertadores" title="Libertadores">libertador</a> <a href="/wiki/Bernardo_O%27Higgins" title="Bernardo O\'Higgins">Bernardo O\'Higgins</a> (1778&#8211;1842), who feared an occupation by Great Britain or France. The first Chilean settlement was <a href="/wiki/Fuerte_Bulnes" title="Fuerte Bulnes">Fuerte Bulnes</a>, situated in a forested zone on the north side of the strait. Fuerte Bulnes was later abandoned, and in 1848 the city of <a href="/wiki/Punta_Arenas" title="Punta Arenas">Punta Arenas</a> was founded farther north where the <a href="/wiki/Magellanic_subpolar_forests" title="Magellanic subpolar forests">Magellanic forests</a> meets the <a href="/wiki/Patagonia" title="Patagonia">Patagonian</a> plains. In Tierra del Fuego, across the strait from Punta Arenas, the village of <a href="/wiki/Porvenir,_Chile" title="Porvenir, Chile">Porvenir</a> emerged during the <a href="/wiki/Tierra_del_Fuego_gold_rush" title="Tierra del Fuego gold rush">Tierra del Fuego gold rush</a> in the late 19th century.\n</p><p>Argentina effectively recognized Chilean <a href="/wiki/Sovereignty" title="Sovereignty">sovereignty</a> over the Strait of Magellan in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boundary_treaty_of_1881_between_Chile_and_Argentina" title="Boundary treaty of 1881 between Chile and Argentina">Boundary treaty of 1881 between Chile and Argentina</a>. Argentina had previously claimed all of the strait, or at least the eastern third of it.\n</p><p>In 1840 the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Steam_Navigation_Company" title="Pacific Steam Navigation Company">Pacific Steam Navigation Company</a> was the first to use <a href="/wiki/Steamship" title="Steamship">steamships</a> for commercial traffic in the strait.\n</p><p>Until the <a href="/wiki/Panama_Canal" title="Panama Canal">Panama Canal</a> opened in 1914, the Strait of Magellan was the main route for steamships traveling from the Atlantic Ocean to the Pacific. It was often considered the only safe way to move between the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans, as the <a href="/wiki/Drake_Passage" title="Drake Passage">Drake Passage</a> separating <a href="/wiki/Cape_Horn" title="Cape Horn">Cape Horn</a> (the southern tip of South America) from Antarctica is notorious for turbulent and unpredictable weather, and is frequented by <a href="/wiki/Iceberg" title="Iceberg">icebergs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sea_ice" title="Sea ice">sea ice</a>. Ships in the strait, protected by Tierra del Fuego to the south and the coast of continental South America to the north crossed with relative ease, and Punta Arenas became a primary refueling port providing coal for steam ships in transit. Sailing ships, however, partly because of variable winds and currents in the strait, generally preferred the Drake Passage, as they had more room to maneuver there.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Features">Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Strait_of_Magellan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Magellanglaciacion.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="546" data-file-width="702" decoding="async" height="171" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Magellanglaciacion.jpg/220px-Magellanglaciacion.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Magellanglaciacion.jpg/330px-Magellanglaciacion.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Magellanglaciacion.jpg/440px-Magellanglaciacion.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Magellanglaciacion.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Map showing the extent of the <a href="/wiki/Patagonian_Ice_Sheet" title="Patagonian Ice Sheet">Patagonian Ice Sheet</a> in the Strait of Magellan area during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Last_glacial_period" title="Last glacial period">last glacial period</a>. Selected modern settlements are shown with yellow dots.</div></div></div>\n<p>The strait is approximately 570 kilometres (310&#160;nmi; 350&#160;mi) long and about 2 kilometres (1.1&#160;nmi; 1.2&#160;mi) wide at its narrowest point (<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Carlos_III_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Carlos III Island (page does not exist)">Carlos III Island</a>, west of <a href="/wiki/Cape_Froward" title="Cape Froward">Cape Froward</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnr_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-cnr-5">[5]</a></sup> The northwestern portion of the strait is connected with other sheltered waterways via the <a href="/wiki/Smyth_Channel" title="Smyth Channel">Smyth Channel</a>. This area is similar to the <a href="/wiki/Inside_Passage" title="Inside Passage">Inside Passage</a> of Alaska. South of Cape Froward, the principal shipping route follows the <a href="/wiki/Magdalena_Channel" title="Magdalena Channel">Magdalena Channel</a>.\n</p><p>The eastern opening is a wide bay on the border of Chile and <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a> between <a href="/wiki/Punta_D%C3%BAngeness" title="Punta D&#250;ngeness">Punta D&#250;ngeness</a> on the mainland and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cabo_del_Esp%C3%ADritu_Santo" title="Cabo del Esp&#237;ritu Santo">Cabo del Esp&#237;ritu Santo</a> (Cape of the Holy Spirit) on Tierra del Fuego, the border as defined in the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Peace_and_Friendship_of_1984_between_Chile_and_Argentina" title="Treaty of Peace and Friendship of 1984 between Chile and Argentina">Treaty of Peace and Friendship of 1984 between Chile and Argentina</a>. Immediately west are <a href="/wiki/Primera_Angostura" title="Primera Angostura">Primera Angostura</a> and <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Angostura" title="Segunda Angostura">Segunda Angostura</a>, narrows formed by two <a href="/wiki/Terminal_moraine" title="Terminal moraine">terminal moraines</a> of different ages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usgs_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-usgs-6">[6]</a></sup> The Primera Angostura is the closest approach of Isla Grande de Tierra del Fuego to the mainland of South America. Farther west lies <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Magdalena_Island,_Magallanes,_Chile" title="Magdalena Island, Magallanes, Chile">Magdalena Island</a>, part of <a href="/wiki/Los_Ping%C3%BCinos_Natural_Monument" title="Los Ping&#252;inos Natural Monument">Los Ping&#252;inos Natural Monument</a>. The strait\'s southern boundary in the east follows first the shoreline of the Isla Grande de Tierra del Fuego, then the northern end of the <a href="/wiki/Canal_Whiteside" title="Canal Whiteside">Canal Whiteside</a> and the shoreline of <a href="/wiki/Dawson_Island" title="Dawson Island">Dawson Island</a>.\n</p><p>The western part of the strait leads northwest from the northern end of the Magdalena Channel to the strait\'s Pacific entrance. This portion of the strait is flanked on the south by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Capit%C3%A1n_Aracena_Island" title="Capit&#225;n Aracena Island">Capit&#225;n Aracena Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Clarence_Island,_Chile" title="Clarence Island, Chile">Clarence Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Santa_In%C3%A9s_Island" title="Santa In&#233;s Island">Santa In&#233;s Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Desolaci%C3%B3n_Island" title="Desolaci&#243;n Island">Desolaci&#243;n Island</a> (Cabo Pilar) and other smaller islands, and on the north by <a href="/wiki/Brunswick_Peninsula" title="Brunswick Peninsula">Brunswick Peninsula</a>, <a href="/wiki/Riesco_Island" title="Riesco Island">Riesco Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mu%C3%B1oz_Gamero_Peninsula" title="Mu&#241;oz Gamero Peninsula">Mu&#241;oz Gamero Peninsula</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manuel_Rodriguez_Island" title="Manuel Rodriguez Island">Manuel Rodriguez Island</a> and other minor islands of the <a href="/wiki/Queen_Adelaide_Archipelago" title="Queen Adelaide Archipelago">Queen Adelaide Archipelago</a>. Two narrow channels connect the strait with <a href="/wiki/Seno_Otway" title="Seno Otway">Seno Otway</a> and <a href="/wiki/Seno_Skyring" title="Seno Skyring">Seno Skyring</a>. A broader channel, <a href="/wiki/Smyth_Channel" title="Smyth Channel">Smyth Channel</a>, leads north from the strait between Mu&#241;oz Gamero Peninsula and Manuel Rodriguez Island. <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Coloane_Coastal_and_Marine_Protected_Area" title="Francisco Coloane Coastal and Marine Protected Area">Francisco Coloane Coastal and Marine Protected Area</a>, a sanctuary for <a href="/wiki/Humpback_whale" title="Humpback whale">humpback whales</a>, is located in this area. This part of the strait lies on the elongated <a href="/wiki/Magallanes-Fagnano_Fault" title="Magallanes-Fagnano Fault">Magallanes-Fagnano Fault</a>, which marks a plate boundary between the <a href="/wiki/South_American_Plate" title="South American Plate">South American Plate</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Scotia_Plate" title="Scotia Plate">Scotia Plate</a>. This fault continues southward under <a href="/wiki/Almirantazgo_Fjord" title="Almirantazgo Fjord">Almirantazgo Fjord</a> and then below <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fagnano_Lake" title="Fagnano Lake">Fagnano Lake</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lod_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lod-7">[7]</a></sup> Possibly, new tourism industries could be established in the eastern part of the strait for watching <a href="/wiki/Southern_right_whale" title="Southern right whale">southern right whales</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> as the number of observations in the area has increased in recent years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Toponym_of_the_zone">Toponym of the zone</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Strait_of_Magellan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Toponym of the zone">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The place names of the zone come from a variety of origins, many from Spanish and English, and from at least one native language, that is adaptations of the <a href="/wiki/Ona_language" title="Ona language">Ona language</a> to the Spanish language phonology and spelling<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> like <i><a href="/wiki/Timaukel" title="Timaukel">Timaukel</a></i> (hamlet at the east side of Tierra del Fuego), <i>Carukinka</i> (the end of the Almirantazgo Fjord), <i>Anika</i> (a channel located at 54&#176; 7&#8217; S and 70&#176; 30&#8217; W) and <i>Arska</i> (the north side of the Dawson Island).\n</p><p>Magellan named initially the strait <i>Todos los Santos</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> as he began the voyage through the west on 1 November 1520. <a href="/wiki/Charles_V,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor">Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor</a> renamed it as <i>Estrecho de Magallanes</i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="The citation for">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Magellan named the island on the south side as <i>Tierra del Fuego</i>, which the <a href="/wiki/Yaghan_people" title="Yaghan people">Yaghan people</a> called <i>Onais&#237;n</i> in the <a href="/wiki/Yaghan_language" title="Yaghan language">Yaghan language</a>. Magellan also gave the name <i>Patagones</i> (Big Feet) to the mainland Indians, their land was subsequently known as <i>Patagonia</i>.\n</p><p><i>Bah&#237;a Cordes</i> is named after the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a> pirate <a href="/wiki/Baltazar_de_Cordes" title="Baltazar de Cordes">Baltazar de Cordes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lighthouses_in_the_Strait">Lighthouses in the Strait</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Geospatial-Intelligence_Agency" title="National Geospatial-Intelligence Agency">National Geospatial-Intelligence Agency</a> lists 41 lighthouses in the waterway. Some of them are more than a century old, and some are declared <i>Monumento Nacional</i>. Among the most impressive lighthouses are the <i><a href="/wiki/County_of_Peebles_(ship)" title="County of Peebles (ship)">County of Peebles</a></i> hulk, the world\'s first four-masted, iron-hulled "full-rig ship", used now as a <a href="/wiki/Breakwater_(structure)" title="Breakwater (structure)">breakwater</a> for the harbour at <a href="/wiki/Punta_Arenas" title="Punta Arenas">Punta Arenas</a>, the <i>San Isidro</i> lighthouse, restored in 2004 and which is now a museum and lodge,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Evangelistas_Lighthouse" title="Evangelistas Lighthouse">Evangelistas Lighthouse</a>, located at the western mouth of the Strait and built by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/George_Slight" title="George Slight">George Slight</a>, who wrote on his arrival in 1934:\n</p>\n<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r856303468" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>I never imagined seeing something so wild and desolate as those emerging dark rocks in the middle of the raging waves. To see these stormy craggy rocks was frightening. With a dim light on the horizon we could see large waves crashing heavily in the western part of the islands: a vision that hardly anyone can imagine ...<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>\n</p></blockquote>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tidal_characteristics_of_the_Strait_of_Magellan">Tidal characteristics of the Strait of Magellan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Strait_of_Magellan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Tidal characteristics of the Strait of Magellan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>On the Atlantic side, the Strait is characterized by semidiurnal macrotides with mean and spring tide ranges of 7.1 and 9.0 m, respectively. On the Pacific side, tides are mixed, mainly semidiurnal, with mean and spring tide ranges of 1.1 and 1.2 m, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>\n</p><p>There is enormous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tidal_energy" title="Tidal energy">tidal energy</a> potential in the Strait.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Environment">Environment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Strait_of_Magellan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Environment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/VLCC_Metula_oil_spill" title="VLCC Metula oil spill">VLCC Metula oil spill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Los_Ping%C3%BCinos_Natural_Monument" title="Los Ping&#252;inos Natural Monument">Los Ping&#252;inos Natural Monument</a>, <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Coloane_Coastal_and_Marine_Protected_Area" title="Francisco Coloane Coastal and Marine Protected Area">Francisco Coloane Coastal and Marine Protected Area</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alacalufes_National_Reserve" title="Alacalufes National Reserve">Alacalufes National Reserve</a>, <a href="/wiki/Magallanes_National_Reserve" title="Magallanes National Reserve">Magallanes National Reserve</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Magellanic_subpolar_forests" title="Magellanic subpolar forests">Magellanic subpolar forests</a></div>\n<p>Located around the strait are protected systems (S.P.=Sistema Protegido &amp; B.N.P=Bienes Nacionales Protegidos) listed below:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li>S.P. Isla Carlos III</li>\n<li>B.N.P. Islote Rupert</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Traffic">Traffic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Strait_of_Magellan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Traffic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>As the strait provides a well protected inland water way for safe navigation, sheltered from rough weather and high seas, ships sail through the strait\n</p>\n<ul><li>from Pacific to Atlantic and back,</li>\n<li>from the oceans to the <a href="/wiki/Beagle_Channel" title="Beagle Channel">Beagle Channel</a> through the route <a href="/wiki/Magdalena_Channel" title="Magdalena Channel">Magdalena</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cockburn_Channel" title="Cockburn Channel">Cockburn Channel</a>, Paso Brecknock or Canal Ocasi&#243;n, <a href="/wiki/Ballenero_Channel" title="Ballenero Channel">Ballenero Channel</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=O%27Brien_Channel&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="O\'Brien Channel (page does not exist)">O\'Brien Channel</a>, Paso Timbales, northwest arm of the Beagle Channel and the Beagle Channel and back, and also</li>\n<li>to cross the strait from north to south and back. This is the case of all traffic between the Chilean and Argentine mainland and the cities in Tierra del Fuego: <a href="/wiki/Porvenir,_Chile" title="Porvenir, Chile">Porvenir</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cerro_Sombrero" title="Cerro Sombrero">Cerro Sombrero</a>, <a href="/wiki/Timaukel" title="Timaukel">Timaukel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ushuaia" title="Ushuaia">Ushuaia</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Grande,_Argentina" title="R&#237;o Grande, Argentina">R&#237;o Grande</a></li></ul>\n<p>571 Chilean ships and 1,681 non-Chilean ships sailed through the strait in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Maritime_pilot" title="Maritime pilot">Piloting</a> is compulsory for sailing the strait.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Navigation_status">Navigation status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Strait_of_Magellan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Navigation status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Peace_and_Friendship_of_1984_between_Chile_and_Argentina" title="Treaty of Peace and Friendship of 1984 between Chile and Argentina">Treaty of Peace and Friendship of 1984 between Chile and Argentina</a></div>\n<p>Article 35 of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Convention_on_the_Law_of_the_Sea" title="United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea">United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea</a> states that "Nothing in this Part affects: ... (c) the legal regime in straits in which passage is regulated in whole or in part by long-standing international conventions in force specifically relating to such straits". Article V of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boundary_treaty_of_1881_between_Chile_and_Argentina" title="Boundary treaty of 1881 between Chile and Argentina">Boundary treaty of 1881 between Chile and Argentina</a> established a legal regime for the Strait of Magellan, and in a diplomatic letter to major shipping nations in 1873 Chile promised freedom of navigation through and neutrality within the strait.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span
