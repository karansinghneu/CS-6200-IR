rel="nofollow">ITU.int/ITU-R</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>ITU Radiocommunication Sector</b> (<b>ITU-R</b>) is one of the three sectors (divisions or units) of the <a href="/wiki/International_Telecommunication_Union" title="International Telecommunication Union">International Telecommunication Union</a> (ITU) and is responsible for <a href="/wiki/Radio" title="Radio">radio</a> communication. \n</p><p>Its role is to manage the international <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radio-frequency_spectrum" title="Radio-frequency spectrum">radio-frequency spectrum</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Satellite_orbit" title="Satellite orbit">satellite orbit</a> resources and to develop standards for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radiocommunication" title="Radiocommunication">radiocommunication</a> systems with the objective of ensuring the effective use of the <a href="/wiki/Spectrum" title="Spectrum">spectrum</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>\n</p><p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ITU" title="ITU">ITU</a> is required, according to its Constitution, to allocate spectrum and register <a href="/wiki/Frequency_allocation" title="Frequency allocation">frequency allocation</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Orbital_positions&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Orbital positions (page does not exist)">orbital positions</a> and other parameters of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Satellites" title="Satellites">satellites</a>, &#8220;in order to avoid harmful interference between radio stations of different countries&#8221;. The international <a href="/wiki/Spectrum_management" title="Spectrum management">spectrum management</a> system is therefore based on regulatory procedures for <a href="/wiki/Frequency_coordination" title="Frequency coordination">frequency coordination</a>, notification and registration.\n</p><p>ITU-R has a permanent secretariat, the Radiocommunication Bureau, based at the ITU HQ in <a href="/wiki/Geneva" title="Geneva">Geneva</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>. The elected <a class="external text" href="http://www.itu.int/ITU-R/index.asp?category=information&amp;rlink=director&amp;lang=en" rel="nofollow">Director of the Bureau</a> is Mr. <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Rancy" title="Fran&#231;ois Rancy">Fran&#231;ois Rancy</a> of <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>; he was first elected by the ITU Membership to the Directorship in 2010.\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc"><input class="toctogglecheckbox" id="toctogglecheckbox" role="button" style="display:none" type="checkbox"/><div class="toctitle" dir="ltr" lang="en"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#History"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">History</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#See_also"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">See also</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ITU-R&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The CCIR&#8212;<b>Comit&#233; consultatif international pour la radio</b>, <b>Consultative Committee on International Radio</b> or <b>International Radio Consultative Committee</b>&#8212;was founded in 1927.\n</p><p>In 1932 the CCIR and several other organizations (including the original <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ITU" title="ITU">ITU</a>, which had been founded as the International Telegraph Union in 1865) merged to form what would in 1934 become known as the International Telecommunication Union.
