</small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Dutch" title="Help:IPA/Dutch">[&#712;&#651;&#618;l&#601;m &#712;j&#593;nso&#720;n]</a></span>; <abbr title="circa">c.</abbr><span style="white-space:nowrap;">&#8201;1570</span>&#160;&#8211; c.<span style="white-space:nowrap;">&#8201;1630</span>), sometimes abbreviated to <b>Willem Jansz.</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Republic" title="Dutch Republic">Dutch</a> navigator and colonial governor. Janszoon served in the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_Indies" title="Dutch East Indies">Netherlands East Indies</a> in the periods 1603&#8211;1611 and 1612&#8211;1616, including as governor of Fort Henricus on the island of <a href="/wiki/Solor" title="Solor">Solor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He is the first European known to have seen the coast of <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> during <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Janszoon_voyage_of_1605-6" title="Janszoon voyage of 1605-6">his voyage of 1605&#8211;1606</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc"><input class="toctogglecheckbox" id="toctogglecheckbox" role="button" style="display:none" type="checkbox"/><div class="toctitle" dir="ltr" href="/w/index.php?title=Willem_Janszoon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Willem Janszoon was born around 1570, but nothing is known of his early life.\n</p><p>Janszoon is first recorded as entering into the service of the <i>Oude compagnie</i>, one of the predecessors of the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_India_Company" title="Dutch East India Company">Dutch East India Company</a> (VOC), in 1598 as a mate aboard the <i>Hollandia</i>, part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_Dutch_Expedition_to_Indonesia_(1598-1600)" title="Second Dutch Expedition to Indonesia (1598-1600)">second fleet</a> under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jacob_Cornelisz._van_Neck" title="Jacob Cornelisz. van Neck">Jacob Cornelisz. van Neck</a>, dispatched by the Dutch to the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_Indies" title="Dutch East Indies">Dutch East Indies</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mutch_1942,_p13_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mutch_1942,_p13-3">[3]</a></sup> On 5 May 1601, he again sailed for the <a href="/wiki/East_Indies" title="East Indies">East Indies</a> as master of the <i>Lam</i>, one of three ships in the fleet of <a href="/wiki/Joris_van_Spilbergen" title="Joris van Spilbergen">Joris van Spilbergen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Janszoon sailed from the Netherlands for the East Indies for the third time on 18 December 1603, as captain of the <i><a href="/wiki/Duyfken" title="Duyfken">Duyfken</a></i> (or <i>Duijfken</i>, meaning "Little Dove"), one of twelve ships of the great fleet of <a href="/wiki/Steven_van_der_Hagen" title="Steven van der Hagen">Steven van der Hagen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> When the other ships left <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Java_(island)" title="Java (island)">Java</a>, Janszoon was sent to search for other outlets of trade, particularly in "the great land of New Guinea and other East and Southlands".\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Exploration_and_Discovery">Exploration and Discovery</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Janszoon_voyage_of_1605%E2%80%9306" title="Janszoon voyage of 1605&#8211;06">Janszoon voyage of 1605&#8211;06</a></div>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:StateLibQld_1_117460_Duyfhen_(ship).jpg"><img alt="" title="Duyfken">Duyfken</a></i> in the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Carpentaria" title="Gulf of Carpentaria">Gulf of Carpentaria</a></div></div></div>\n<p>On 18 November 1605, the <i><a href="/wiki/Duyfken" title="Duyfken">Duyfken</a></i> sailed from <a href="/wiki/Banten_(town)" title="Banten (town)">Bantam</a> to the coast of western <a href="/wiki/New_Guinea" title="New Guinea">New Guinea</a>. After that, Janszoon crossed the eastern end of the <a href="/wiki/Arafura_Sea" title="Arafura Sea">Arafura Sea</a> into the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Carpentaria" title="Gulf of Carpentaria">Gulf of Carpentaria</a>, without being aware of the existence of <a href="/wiki/Torres_Strait" title="Torres Strait">Torres Strait</a>. The <i>Duyfken</i> was actually in <a href="/wiki/Torres_Strait" title="Torres Strait">Torres Strait</a> in February 1606, a few months before Spanish explorer <a href="/wiki/Lu%C3%ADs_Vaz_de_Torres" title="Lu&#237;s Vaz de Torres">Lu&#237;s Vaz de Torres</a> sailed through it. On 26 February 1606, Janzoon made landfall at the <a href="/wiki/Pennefather_River" title="Pennefather River">Pennefather River</a> on the western shore of <a href="/wiki/Cape_York_Peninsula" title="Cape York Peninsula">Cape York</a> in <a href="/wiki/Queensland" title="Queensland">Queensland</a>, near what is now the town of <a href="/wiki/Weipa,_Queensland" title="Weipa, Queensland">Weipa</a>. This is the first recorded European landfall on the Australian continent. Janszoon proceeded to chart some 320&#160;km (200&#160;mi) of the coastline, which he thought was a southerly extension of New Guinea.\n</p><p>Finding the land swampy and the people inhospitable (ten of his men were killed on various shore expeditions), Janszoon decided to return at a place he named Cape Keerweer ("Turnabout"), south of Albatross Bay, and arrived back at Bantam in June 1606. He called the land he had discovered "Nieu Zeland", after the Dutch province of <a href="/wiki/Zeeland" title="Zeeland">Zeeland</a>, but the name was not adopted, and was later used by Dutch cartographers for <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>.\n</p><p>In 1607, Admiral <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cornelis_Matelieff_de_Jonge" title="Cornelis Matelieff de Jonge">Cornelis Matelieff de Jonge</a> sent Janzoon to <a href="/wiki/Ambon_Island" title="Ambon Island">Ambon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Banda_Islands" title="Banda Islands">Banda</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 1611, Janzoon returned to the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, believing that the south coast of New Guinea was joined to the land along which he had sailed, and Dutch maps reproduced that error for many years. Though there have been suggestions that earlier navigators from <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> or <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a> may have discovered parts of Australia earlier, the <i>Duyfken</i> is the first European vessel definitely known to have done so.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_voyage_to_Australia">Second voyage to Australia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Willem_Janszoon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Second voyage to Australia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Janszoon reported that on 31 July 1618, he had landed on an island at 22&#176; South with a length of 22 miles and 240 miles<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Vagueness" title="Wikipedia:Vagueness"><span title="This information is too vague. / <span class="geo">-22.717; 113.667</span></span></span></a></span>) to <a href="/wiki/North_West_Cape" title="North West Cape">North West Cape</a> (<span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Willem_Janszoon&amp;params=21_47_S_114_09_E_"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">21&#176;47&#8242;S</span> <span class="longitude">114&#176;09&#8242;E</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">&#65279; / <span class="geo">-21.783; 114.150</span></span></span></a></span>) on the <a href="/wiki/Western_Australia" title="Western Australia">Western Australian</a> coast, which Janszoon presumed was an island, without fully circumnavigating it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_life">Political life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Willem_Janszoon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Political life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Gerritsz_Nueva_Guinea_1622.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="199" data-file-width="362" decoding="async" height="121" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Gerritsz_Nueva_Guinea_1622.JPG/220px-Gerritsz_Nueva_Guinea_1622.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Gerritsz_Nueva_Guinea_1622.JPG/330px-Gerritsz_Nueva_Guinea_1622.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/4e/Gerritsz_Nueva_Guinea_1622.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Gerritsz_Nueva_Guinea_1622.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Willem Janszoon&#8217;s Vliege Bay, Dubbelde Rev., R. Visch, and Cape Keerweer on the coast of <i>Nueva Guinea</i> on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hessel_Gerritszoon" title="Hessel Gerritszoon">Hessel Gerritszoon</a>&#8217;s map of the Pacific Ocean, 1622.</div></div></div>\n<p>Around 1617/18 he was back in the Netherlands and was appointed as a member of the Council of the Indies. He served as admiral of the Dutch Defence fleet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Janszoon was awarded a gold chain worth 1,000 <a href="/wiki/Guilder" title="Guilder">guilders</a> in 1619 for his part in capturing four ships of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_East_India_Company" title="British East India Company">British East India Company</a> near <a href="/wiki/Pulau_Tikus" title="Pulau Tikus">Tiku</a> on <a href="/wiki/West_Sumatra" title="West Sumatra">West Sumatra</a>, which had aided the <a href="/wiki/Javanese_people" title="Javanese people">Javanese</a> in their defence of the town of <a href="/wiki/Jakarta" title="Jakarta">Jakarta</a> against the Dutch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In 1620 he was one of the negotiators with the English. In a combined fleet they sailed to Manila to prevent Chinese merchants dealing with the Spanish. Janszoon became vice-admiral, and the year later admiral. Near the end of his life, Janszoon served as governor of Banda (1623&#8211;1627).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He returned to <a href="/wiki/Batavia,_Dutch_East_Indies" title="Batavia, Dutch East Indies">Batavia</a> in June 1627 and soon afterwards, as admiral of a fleet of eight vessels, went on a diplomatic mission to India.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mutch_1942,_p51_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mutch_1942,_p51-12">[12]</a></sup> On 4 December 1628, he sailed for Holland and on 16 July 1629, reported on the state of the Indies at The Hague.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mutch_1942,_p51_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-Mutch_1942,_p51-12">[12]</a></sup> He was now probably about sixty years old and ready to retire from his strenuous and successful career in the service of his country. Nothing is known of his last days, but he is thought to have died in 1630.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Records">Records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Willem_Janszoon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The original journal and log made during Janszoon&#8217;s 1606 voyage have been lost. The Duyfken chart,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> which shows the location of the first landfall in Australia by the <i>Duyfken</i>, had a better fate. It was still in existence in Amsterdam when <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hessel_Gerritszoon" title="Hessel Gerritszoon">Hessel Gerritszoon</a> made his Map of the Pacific in 1622, and placed the Duyfken geography upon it, thus providing us with the first map to contain any part of Australia. The chart was still in existence around 1670, when a copy was made. This eventually went to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C3%96sterreichische_Nationalbibliothek" title="&#214;sterreichische Nationalbibliothek">Imperial Library</a> in <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a> and remained forgotten for two hundred years. The map is part of the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlas_Blaeu" title="Atlas Blaeu">Atlas Blaeu</a> Van der Hem</i>, brought to Vienna in 1730 by <a href="/wiki/Prince_Eugene_of_Savoy" title="Prince Eugene of Savoy">Prince Eugene of Savoy</a>. The information from his charts was included in the marble and copper maps of the <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/hemisphere" title="wikt:hemisphere">hemispheres</a> on the floor of The Citizens&#8217; Hall of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royal_Palace" title="Royal Palace">Royal Palace</a> <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Willem_Janszoon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em; -webkit-column-width: 30em; column-width: 30em; list-style-type: decimal;">\n<ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">The <a href="/wiki/Patronymic" title="Patronymic">patronymic</a> Janszoon means \'son of Jan,\' or \'son of Johannes\' (<i>Janszoon</i> in Dutch). In the early seventeenth century, this was in some dialects probably pronounced the same as Jansen, a name equivalent to Johnson in English. Surnames were usually not used and children were simply named for their father\'s given name. In areas where not many people lived, but also in towns and cities, he would simply be given the name Willem Jansz; thus, all that is known about him is that his father\'s name was Johannes or Jan. As in many countries, genealogical and historical research in the Netherlands can be difficult for this reason.
