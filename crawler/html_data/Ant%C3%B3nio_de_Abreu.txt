Islands</a>.</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Ant&#243;nio de Abreu</b> (c. 1480 &#8211; c. 1514) was a 16th-century <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portuguese</a> <a href="/wiki/Navigator" title="Navigator">navigator</a> and naval officer. He participated under the command of <a href="/wiki/Afonso_de_Albuquerque" title="Afonso de Albuquerque">Afonso de Albuquerque</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Capture_of_Ormuz_(1507)" title="Capture of Ormuz (1507)">conquest of Ormus</a> in 1507 and <a href="/wiki/Capture_of_Malacca_(1511)" title="Capture of Malacca (1511)">Malacca in 1511</a>, where he got injured. Departing from Malacca in November 1511 with four ships, in an exploratory voyage to the \'Spice Islands\' of <a href="/wiki/Maluku_Islands" title="Maluku Islands">Maluku</a>, he led the first European expedition to reach <a href="/wiki/Timor" title="Timor">Timor</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Banda_Islands" title="Banda Islands">Banda Islands</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, in 1512.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RICKLEFSp24_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-RICKLEFSp24-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Expeditions">Expeditions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ant%C3%B3nio_de_Abreu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Expeditions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>On 25 July 1511, during the conquest of Malacca, Ant&#243;nio de Abreu led the <a href="/wiki/Junk_(ship)" title="Junk (ship)">Chinese junk</a> that went up the <a href="/wiki/Malacca_River" title="Malacca River">Malacca River</a> at high tide, allowing the Portuguese contingent to land and conquer the city in August. Severely wounded in the face, having lost some teeth and tongue,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> he refused the proposal from Albuquerque to relieve the command. \n</p><p>In November that year, after sending ambassadors to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pegu" title="Pegu">Pegu</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Siam" title="Siam">Siam</a> one month before leaving Malacca, Albuquerque trusted Abreu with the command of a fleet of four ships sailing in search of the "<a href="/wiki/Maluku_Islands" title="Maluku Islands">Spice Islands</a>". Abreu was captain-major in the ship <i>Santa Catarina</i>, the deputy commander was <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Serr%C3%A3o" title="Francisco Serr&#227;o">Francisco Serr&#227;o</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RICKLEFSp24_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-RICKLEFSp24-1">[1]</a></sup> on the ship <i>Sabaia</i>, and a third vessel, a caravel, was under command of Sim&#227;o Afonso Bisagudo, having as pilot Francisco Rodrigues, an able cartographer who wrote about this expedition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> With a crew of 120 Portuguese and 60 slaves they were guided by <a href="/wiki/Malays_(ethnic_group)" title="Malays (ethnic group)">Malay</a> pilots, recruited to guide them through <a href="/wiki/Java" title="Java">Java</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Sunda_Islands" title="Lesser Sunda Islands">Lesser Sunda Islands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ambon_Island" title="Ambon Island">Ambon Island</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Banda_Islands" title="Banda Islands">Banda Islands</a>, where they arrived in early 1512.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>  They remained there for about a month, buying and filling their ships with <a href="/wiki/Nutmeg" title="Nutmeg">nutmeg</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cloves" title="Cloves">cloves</a>. Abreu then sailed to Amboina whilst his deputy commander Serr&#227;o stepped forward to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moluccas" title="Moluccas">Moluccas</a> but sank, ending in <a href="/wiki/Ternate" title="Ternate">Ternate</a>. Occupied with fighting elsewhere in the archipelago, such as Ambon and Ternate, he returned only in 1529.\n</p><p>Abreu returned to Malacca in December 1512, from where he departed for India with <a href="/wiki/Fern%C3%A3o_Pires_de_Andrade" title="Fern&#227;o Pires de Andrade">Fern&#227;o Pires de Andrade</a> in January 1513, then sailing for Portugal. He died in Azores, before reaching <a href="/wiki/Continental_Portugal" title="Continental Portugal">continental Portugal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ant%C3%B3nio_de_Abreu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Francisco_Serr%C3%A3o" title="Francisco Serr&#227;o">Francisco Serr&#227;o</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/History_of_Indonesia" title="History of Indonesia">History of Indonesia</a></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ant%C3%B3nio_de_Abreu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-RICKLEFSp24-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink">^ <a href="#cite_ref-RICKLEFSp24_1-0"><sup><i><b>a</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-RICKLEFSp24_1-1"><sup><i><b>b</b></i></sup></a></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation book">Ricklefs, M.C.
