class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/French" title="Help:IPA/French">[p&#613;i d&#601; dom]</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Auvergnat" title="Auvergnat">Auvergnat</a>: <i>Pu&#232;i Domat</i>, <i>Pu&#232;i de Doma</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Lava_dome" title="Lava dome">lava dome</a> and one of the youngest <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">volcanoes</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Cha%C3%AEne_des_Puys" title="Cha&#238;ne des Puys">Cha&#238;ne des Puys</a> region of <a href="/wiki/Massif_Central" title="Massif Central">Massif Central</a> in central <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>. This chain of volcanoes including numerous <a href="/wiki/Cinder_cone" title="Cinder cone">cinder cones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lava_dome" title="Lava dome">lava domes</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Maar" title="Maar">maars</a> is far from the edge of any <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tectonic_plate" title="Tectonic plate">tectonic plate</a>. Puy de D&#244;me was created by a <a href="/wiki/Pel%C3%A9an_eruption" title="Pel&#233;an eruption">Pel&#233;an eruption</a>, some 10,700 years ago. Puy de D&#244;me is approximately 10 kilometres (6&#160;mi) from <a href="/wiki/Clermont-Ferrand" title="Clermont-Ferrand">Clermont-Ferrand</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Puy-de-D%C3%B4me" title="Puy-de-D&#244;me">Puy-de-D&#244;me</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Departments_of_France" title="Departments of France">d&#233;partement</a></i> is named after the volcano.\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc"><input class="toctogglecheckbox" id="toctogglecheckbox" role="button" style="display:none" type="checkbox"/><div class="toctitle" dir="ltr" as an assembly place for spiritual ceremonies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Temples were built at the summit, including a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gallo-Roman" title="Gallo-Roman">Gallo-Roman</a> temple dedicated to the God <a href="/wiki/Mercury_(mythology)" title="Mercury (mythology)">Mercury</a>, the ruins of which were discovered in 1873.\n</p><p>In 1648, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Florin_P%C3%A9rier&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Florin P&#233;rier (page does not exist)">Florin P&#233;rier</a>, at the urging of <a href="/wiki/Blaise_Pascal" title="Blaise Pascal">Blaise Pascal</a>, proved <a href="/wiki/Evangelista_Torricelli" title="Evangelista Torricelli">Evangelista Torricelli</a>\'s theory that <a href="/wiki/Barometer" title="Barometer">barometric</a> observations were caused by the weight of air by measuring the height of a column of <a href="/wiki/Mercury_(element)" title="Mercury (element)">mercury</a> at three elevations on Puy de D&#244;me.\n</p><p>In 1875, a physics laboratory was built at the summit. Since 1956, a TDF (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/T%C3%A9l%C3%A9diffusion_de_France" title="T&#233;l&#233;diffusion de France">T&#233;l&#233;diffusion de France</a></i>) antenna is also located there. On the top of the mountain, there is a transmitter for FM and TV.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Puy_de_D%C3%B4me&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Puy_de_Dome,_Mercury_temple.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="380" data-file-width="613" decoding="async" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Puy_de_Dome%2C_Mercury_temple.jpg/220px-Puy_de_Dome%2C_Mercury_temple.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Puy_de_Dome%2C_Mercury_temple.jpg/330px-Puy_de_Dome%2C_Mercury_temple.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Puy_de_Dome%2C_Mercury_temple.jpg/440px-Puy_de_Dome%2C_Mercury_temple.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Puy_de_Dome,_Mercury_temple.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Mercury temple before its reconstruction</div></div></div>\n<p>The Puy de D&#244;me is one of the most visited sites in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Auvergne_(r%C3%A9gion)" title="Auvergne (r&#233;gion)">Auvergne</a> region, attracting nearly 500,000 visitors a year. The summit offers expansive views of the <a href="/wiki/Cha%C3%AEne_des_Puys" title="Cha&#238;ne des Puys">Cha&#238;ne des Puys</a> and <a href="/wiki/Clermont-Ferrand" title="Clermont-Ferrand">Clermont-Ferrand</a>. It is a well-known centre for <a href="/wiki/Paragliding" title="Paragliding">paragliding</a>.\n</p><p>The summit can be reached by two <a href="/wiki/Trail" title="Trail">pedestrian paths</a>: a southern one ("<i><i lang="fr" title="French language text">Le sentier des muletiers</i></i>", formerly a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_road" title="Roman road">Roman road</a>) and a northern one (<i>"Le sentier des ch&#232;vres")</i>  which runs past the <i><i lang="fr" title="French language text">Nid de la Poule</i></i> crater. The <a href="/wiki/GR_footpath" title="GR footpath">GR 4</a> long-distance trail includes both paths to cross the mountain.\n</p><p>Since May 2012, visitors can also go up the mountain by train with the <a href="/wiki/Panoramique_des_D%C3%B4mes" title="Panoramique des D&#244;mes">Panoramique des D&#244;mes</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Rack_railway" title="Rack railway">rack railway</a>.\n</p><p>A road exists along the train tracks but it is closed to the general traffic, except for the military, service vehicles or emergencies.\n</p><p>At the top of the mountain, restaurants and shops are available as well as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Visitor_centre" title="Visitor centre">visitor centre</a> giving information on the history and geology of the area.\n</p>\n<div style="clear:left;"></div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Puy_de_D%C3%B4me&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cycling">Cycling</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Puy_de_D%C3%B4me&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Cycling">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In more recent times, Puy de D&#244;me has served as an occasional stage finish in the <a href="/wiki/Tour_de_France" title="Tour de France">Tour de France</a>. It was here that in <a href="/wiki/1964_Tour_de_France" title="1964 Tour de France">1964</a> <a href="/wiki/Raymond_Poulidor" title="Raymond Poulidor">Raymond Poulidor</a> battled with <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Anquetil" title="Jacques Anquetil">Jacques Anquetil</a> in one of the race\'s most famous moments, racing side by side up almost the entire climb; and that in <a href="/wiki/1975_Tour_de_France" title="1975 Tour de France">1975</a> <a href="/wiki/Eddy_Merckx" title="Eddy Merckx">Eddy Merckx</a> was punched in the kidney by a spectator. According to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jean-Fran%C3%A7ois_Pescheux&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jean-Fran&#231;ois Pescheux (page does not exist)">Jean-Fran&#231;ois Pescheux</a>, since the construction of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rack_railway_of_Puy_de_D%C3%B4me" title="Rack railway of Puy de D&#244;me">rack railway</a>, and because of the very narrow road, the Tour will never come back to the Puy de D&#244;me, its last ascension in the race being in <a href="/wiki/1988_Tour_de_France" title="1988 Tour de France">1988</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Puy_de_dome_building.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1536" data-file-width="2048" decoding="async" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Puy_de_dome_building.jpg/220px-Puy_de_dome_building.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Puy_de_dome_building.jpg/330px-Puy_de_dome_building.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Puy_de_dome_building.jpg/440px-Puy_de_dome_building.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Puy_de_dome_building.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Tourist centre.</div></div></div>\n<p>The road is only open for cyclists during very limited periods (when other vehicles are prohibited).  In 2006 this was 7&#8211;9am on Wednesdays and Sundays between 1 May and 30 September.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-climbbybike_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-climbbybike-5">[5]</a></sup> In 2017, it will be the first checkpoint in the <a href="/wiki/Transcontinental_Race" title="Transcontinental Race">Transcontinental Race</a>, which is a non-stop, unsupported bicycle race across Europe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Motorsports">Motorsports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Puy_de_D%C3%B4me&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Motorsports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Charade" title="Circuit de Charade">Circuit de Charade</a> was a <a href="/wiki/Motorsport" title="Motorsport">motorsport</a> <a href="/wiki/Street_circuit" title="Street circuit">street circuit</a> built in 1957 using pre-existing roads around the base of the Puy de D&#244;me.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Charade_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Charade-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Volcanic_Rush_of_Clermont_Ferrand_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Volcanic_Rush_of_Clermont_Ferrand-8">[8]</a></sup> The venue hosted the <a href="/wiki/French_Grand_Prix" title="French Grand Prix">French Grand Prix</a> as well as the <a href="/wiki/French_motorcycle_Grand_Prix" title="French motorcycle Grand Prix">French motorcycle Grand Prix</a> several times in the 1960s and early 1970s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Charade_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Charade-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Volcanic_Rush_of_Clermont_Ferrand_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-The_Volcanic_Rush_of_Clermont_Ferrand-8">[8]</a></sup> In 1986, the track was shortened due to safety issues and was converted into a dedicated motorsport <a href="/wiki/Race_track" title="Race track">race track</a> hosting <a href="/wiki/Track_day" title="Track day">track days</a>, driving schools as well as <a href="/wiki/Historic_motorsport" title="Historic motorsport">historic motorsport</a> events.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Charade_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-Charade-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Volcanic_Rush_of_Clermont_Ferrand_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-The_Volcanic_Rush_of_Clermont_Ferrand-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Puy_de_D%C3%B4me&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>While the lower areas of the mountain are firmly <a href="/wiki/Oceanic_climate" title="Oceanic climate">oceanic</a> (<a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="K&#246;ppen climate classification">K&#246;ppen</a> <i>Cfb</i>), Puy de D&#244;me has a <a href="/wiki/Humid_continental_climate" title="Humid continental climate">humid continental</a> (K&#246;ppen: <i>Dfb</i>) with borderline <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Subalpine_climate" title="Subalpine climate">subalpine</a> characteristics, thanks to its high elevation. Its classification is determined from its January average being well below the -3&#186; threshold (with -5&#186; as its usual lows), and for having over 4 months of average temperatures that exceed 10 degrees (the requirement for this climate category is to have at least 3 months). \n</p>\n<table class="wikitable collapsible" style="width:100%;
