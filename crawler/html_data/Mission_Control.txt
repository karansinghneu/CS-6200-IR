control rooms in Russia and in the United States.</div></div></div>\n<p>A <b>mission control center</b> (<b>MCC</b>, sometimes called a <b>flight control center</b> or <b>operations center</b>) is a facility that manages <a href="/wiki/Spaceflight" title="Spaceflight">space flights</a>, usually from the point of launch until landing or the end of the mission. It is part of the <a href="/wiki/Ground_segment" title="Ground segment">ground segment</a> of spacecraft operations. A staff of <a href="/wiki/Flight_controller" title="Flight controller">flight controllers</a> and other support personnel monitor all aspects of the mission using <a href="/wiki/Telemetry" title="Telemetry">telemetry</a>, and send commands to the vehicle using <a href="/wiki/Ground_station" title="Ground station">ground stations</a>. Personnel supporting the mission from an MCC can include representatives of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Attitude_control_system" title="Attitude control system">attitude control system</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electric_power" title="Electric power">power</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spacecraft_propulsion" title="Spacecraft propulsion">propulsion</a>, thermal, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Attitude_dynamics_and_control" title="Attitude dynamics and control">attitude dynamics</a>, orbital operations and other subsystem disciplines. The training for these missions usually falls under the responsibility of the flight controllers, typically including extensive rehearsals in the MCC.\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc"><input
