Gs, is the <a href="/wiki/Gaussian_units" title="Gaussian units">cgs</a> unit of measurement of <a href="/wiki/Magnetic_flux" title="Magnetic flux">magnetic flux</a> density (or "<a href="/wiki/Electromagnetic_induction" title="Electromagnetic induction">magnetic induction</a>") (<b>B</b>). The <a href="/wiki/Centimetre%E2%80%93gram%E2%80%93second_system_of_units" title="Centimetre&#8211;gram&#8211;second system of units">cgs system</a> has been superseded by the <a href="/wiki/International_System_of_Units" title="International System of Units">International System of Units</a> (SI), which uses the <a href="/wiki/Tesla_(unit)" title="Tesla (unit)">tesla</a> (symbol T) as the unit of magnetic flux density.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> One gauss equals 1<span style="margin:0 .15em 0
