Active volcanoes are shown with triangles</div></div></div>\n<p>The <b>Mariana Islands</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/&#716;/: secondary stress follows">&#716;</span><span title="\'m\' in \'my\'">m</span><span title="/&#230;r/: \'arr\' in \'marry\'">&#230;r</span><span title="/i/: \'y\' in \'happy\'">i</span><span title="/&#712;/: primary stress follows">&#712;</span><span title="/&#593;&#720;/: \'a\' in \'father\'">&#593;&#720;</span><span title="\'n\' in \'nigh\'">n</span><span title="/&#601;/: \'a\' in \'about\'">&#601;</span></span>/</a></span></span>; also <b>the Marianas</b>) are a crescent-shaped <a href="/wiki/Archipelago" title="Archipelago">archipelago</a> comprising the summits of fifteen mostly dormant volcanic mountains in the western <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">North Pacific Ocean</a>, between the <a href="/wiki/12th_parallel_north" title="12th parallel north">12th</a> and <a href="/wiki/21st_parallel_north" title="21st parallel north">21st parallels north</a> and along the <a href="/wiki/145th_meridian_east" title="145th meridian east">145th meridian east</a>. They lie south-southeast of Japan, west-southwest of Hawaii, north of New Guinea and east of the Philippines, demarcating the <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Sea" title="Philippine Sea">Philippine Sea</a>\'s eastern limit.  They are found in the northern part of the western <a href="/wiki/Oceania" title="Oceania">Oceanic</a> sub-region of <a href="/wiki/Micronesia" title="Micronesia">Micronesia</a>, and are politically divided into two jurisdictions of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>: the Commonwealth of the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Mariana_Islands" title="Northern Mariana Islands">Northern Mariana Islands</a> and, at the southern end of the chain, the territory of <a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">Guam</a>. The islands were named after the influential Spanish queen <a href="/wiki/Mariana_of_Austria" title="Mariana of Austria">Mariana of Austria</a>. \n</p><p>Spaniards, who in the early 16th century were the first Europeans to arrive, eventually annexed and colonized the archipelago.  The indigenous inhabitants are the <a href="/wiki/Chamorro_people" title="Chamorro people">Chamorro people</a>.  Archaeologists in 2013 reported findings which indicated that the people who first settled the Marianas arrived there after making what was at the time the longest uninterrupted ocean voyage in human history.  They further reported findings which suggested that <a href="/wiki/Tinian" title="Tinian">Tinian</a> is likely to have been the first island in <a href="/wiki/Oceania" title="Oceania">Oceania</a> to have been settled by humans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc"><input class="toctogglecheckbox" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mariana_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:402px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Philippine_Sea_location.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="625" data-file-width="750" decoding="async" height="333" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/Philippine_Sea_location.jpg/400px-Philippine_Sea_location.jpg" href="/wiki/File:Philippine_Sea_location.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Geology of the west Pacific in the area of the Mariana Islands. The Mariana Islands are at map-right, east of the Philippine Sea and just west of the <a href="/wiki/Mariana_Trench" title="Mariana Trench">Mariana Trench</a> in the ocean floor.</div></div></div><p>The Mariana Islands are the southern part of a submerged <a href="/wiki/Mountain_range" title="Mountain range">mountain range</a> that extends 1,565 miles (2,519&#160;km) from Guam to near <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. Geographically, the Marianas are the northernmost islands of a larger island group called <a href="/wiki/Micronesia" title="Micronesia">Micronesia</a>, situated between <a href="/wiki/13th_parallel_north" title="13th parallel north">13&#176;</a> and <a href="/wiki/21st_parallel_north" title="21st parallel north">21&#176;N</a> latitude and <a href="/wiki/144th_meridian_east" title="144th meridian east">144&#176;</a> and <a href="/wiki/146th_meridian_east" title="146th meridian east">146&#176;E</a> longitude.\n</p><p>The Mariana Islands have a total land area of 1,005&#160;km<sup>2</sup> (388&#160;sq&#160;mi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>\nThey are composed of two administrative units:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Guam, a <a href="/wiki/United_States_territory" title="United States territory">US territory</a></li>\n<li>the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Mariana_Islands" title="Northern Mariana Islands">Northern Mariana Islands</a> (including the islands of <a href="/wiki/Saipan" title="Saipan">Saipan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tinian" title="Tinian">Tinian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rota_(island)" title="Rota (island)">Rota</a>), which make up a <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_(U.S._insular_area)" title="Commonwealth (U.S. insular area)">Commonwealth of the United States</a>.</li></ul>\n<p>The island chain geographically consists of two subgroups, a northern group of ten volcanic main islands, all are currently uninhabited; and a southern group of five coralline limestone islands (Rota, Guam, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aguijan" title="Aguijan">Aguijan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tinian" title="Tinian">Tinian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saipan" title="Saipan">Saipan</a>), all inhabited except Aguijan. In the northern volcanic group a maximum elevation of about 2,700 feet (820&#160;m) is reached; there are craters showing signs of activity, and earthquakes are not uncommon. Coral reefs fringe the coasts of the southern isles, which are of slight elevation.\n</p><p>The lowest point on the Earth\'s crust, the <a href="/wiki/Mariana_Trench" title="Mariana Trench">Mariana Trench</a>, is near the islands and is named after them.\n</p><p>The islands are part of a <a href="/wiki/Structural_geology" title="Structural geology">geologic structure</a> known as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Izu-Bonin-Mariana_Arc" title="Izu-Bonin-Mariana Arc">Izu-Bonin-Mariana Arc</a> system, and range in age from 5 million years old in the north to 30 million years old in the south (Guam). The island chain arises as a result of the western edge of the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Plate" title="Pacific Plate">Pacific Plate</a> moving westward and plunging downward below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mariana_plate" title="Mariana plate">Mariana plate</a>, a region which is the most volcanically active convergent plate boundary on Earth. This <a href="/wiki/Subduction" title="Subduction">subduction</a> region, just east of the island chain, forms the noted <a href="/wiki/Mariana_Trench" title="Mariana Trench">Mariana Trench</a>, the deepest part of the Earth\'s oceans and lowest part of the surface of the Earth\'s crust. In this region, according to geologic theory, water trapped in the extensive faulting of the Pacific Plate as <a href="/wiki/Serpentinite" title="Serpentinite">serpentinite</a>, is heated by the higher temperatures of depth during its subduction, the pressure from the expanding steam results in the hydrothermal activity in the area and the volcanic activity which formed the Mariana Islands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>\n</p><p>All the islands, except <a href="/wiki/Farallon_de_Medinilla" title="Farallon de Medinilla">Farallon de Medinilla</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Uracas" title="Uracas">Uracas</a> or <a href="/wiki/Farallon_de_Pajaros" title="Farallon de Pajaros">Farallon de Pajaros</a> (in the northern group), are more or less densely wooded, and the vegetation is dense, much resembling that of the <a href="/wiki/Caroline_Islands" title="Caroline Islands">Carolines</a> and also of the Philippines, from where species of plants have been introduced. Owing to the moistness of the soil <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cryptogams" title="Cryptogams">cryptogams</a> are numerous, as are also most kinds of grasses. On most of the islands there is a plentiful supply of water.\n</p><p>The fauna of the Marianas, though inferior in number and variety, is similar in character to that of the <a href="/wiki/Caroline_Islands" title="Caroline Islands">Carolines</a> and certain species are indigenous to both island groups. The climate though damp is healthy, while the heat, being tempered by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trade_wind" title="Trade wind">trade winds</a>, is milder than that of the Philippines; the variations of temperature are not great.\n</p><p>The majority of islands in the Marianas which still retain their indigenous names end in the letters -an; e.g. <a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">Guahan</a> (the indigenous name of Guam), <a href="/wiki/Agrigan" title="Agrigan">Agrigan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Agrihan" title="Agrihan">Agrihan</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aguihan" title="Aguihan">Aguihan</a>/Aguigan, <a href="/wiki/Pagan_(island)" title="Pagan (island)">Pagan</a>, Sarigan, etc.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mariana_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Prehistory">Prehistory</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mariana_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Prehistory">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Guma_Taga_ruins,_pre-1902.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="763" data-file-width="1024" decoding="async" height="224" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Guma_Taga_ruins%2C_pre-1902.jpg/300px-Guma_Taga_ruins%2C_pre-1902.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Guma_Taga_ruins%2C_pre-1902.jpg/450px-Guma_Taga_ruins%2C_pre-1902.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Guma_Taga_ruins%2C_pre-1902.jpg/600px-Guma_Taga_ruins%2C_pre-1902.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Guma_Taga_ruins,_pre-1902.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Ruins of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guma_Taga" title="Guma Taga">Guma Taga</a> on Tinian.  The pillars/columns are called latte (pronounced l&#230;\'di) stones, a common architectural element of prehistoric structures in the Mariana Islands, upon which elevated buildings were built. Earthquakes had toppled the other latte at this site by the time this photo was taken; an earthquake in 1902 toppled the one seen on the left, and today only the one on the right remains standing.</div></div></div>\n<p>The islands are part of a <a href="/wiki/Geology" title="Geology">geologic</a> structure known as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Izu-Bonin-Mariana_Arc" title="Izu-Bonin-Mariana Arc">Izu-Bonin-Mariana Arc</a> system and range in age from 5 million years old in the north to 30 million years old in the south (Guam). The islands are formed as the highly dense and very old western edge of the Pacific plate plunges downward to form the floor of the Mariana Trench and carries trapped water under the Mariana plate as it does so. This water is super-heated as the plate is carried farther downward and results in the volcanic activity which has formed the arc of Mariana Islands above this <a href="/wiki/Subduction" title="Subduction">subduction</a> region.\n</p><p>Archeological studies of human activity on the islands has revealed potteries with red-slipped, circle- and punctate-stamped designs found in the Mariana Islands dating between 1500 and 1400 BC. These artifacts show similar aesthetics to pottery found in Northern and Central Philippines, the Nagsabaran (Cagayan valley) pottery, which flourished during the period between 2000 and 1300 BC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Spanish_exploration_and_control">Spanish exploration and control</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mariana_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Spanish exploration and control">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The first <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europeans</a> to see the island group were a Spanish expedition, who on March 6, 1521, observed a string of islands and sailed between two of them during a Spanish expedition of world circumnavigation. Historically, the southern village of <a href="/wiki/Umatac,_Guam" title="Umatac, Guam">Umatac, Guam</a> has been credited as the site of the Spanish landing, however, scholarly study of the navigator\'s diary, now kept in preservation in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philippine" title="Philippine">Philippines</a>, revealed a drawing of the islands with a tiny island to the south of a much larger island above it. The described placement of the islands made it much more likely that Magellan had actually sailed between Guam and <a href="/wiki/Cocos_Island_(Guam)" title="Cocos Island (Guam)">Cocos Island</a>, and not Guam and <a href="/wiki/Rota_(island)" title="Rota (island)">Rota</a>, as originally thought. This discovery meant that Magellan could not have landed in <a href="/wiki/Umatac,_Guam" title="Umatac, Guam">Umatac</a>, but more likely in a northern location like <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dededo,_Guam" title="Dededo, Guam">Tanguisson</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tumon_Bay" title="Tumon Bay">Tumon Bay</a>.\n</p><p>Regardless of where they landed, Spanish ships arrived in Guam and was unable to get fresh food as the inhabitants, <a href="/wiki/Chamorro_people" title="Chamorro people">Chamorros</a>, "entered the ships and stole whatever they could lay their hands on", including "the small boat that was fastened to the poop of the flagship."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nowell_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nowell-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>129</span></sup> The Spanish crew, however, considered this theft and in retaliation attacked the Chamorros and dubbed the islands <b>Islas de los Ladrones</b> (Islands of the Thieves). "Those people are poor, but ingenious and very thievish, on account of which we called those three islands the islands of Ladrones."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nowell_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Nowell-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>131</span></sup> Pigafetta writes,\n</p>\n<blockquote><p>And the captain-general wished to approach the largest of these three islands to replenish his provisions. But it was not possible, for the people of those islands entered the ships and robbed us so that we could not protect ourselves from them. And when we wished to strike and take in the sails so as to land, they stole very quickly the small boat called a skiff which was fastened to the poop of the captain\'s ship. At which he, being very angry, went ashore with forty armed men. And burning some forty or fifty houses with several boats and killing seven men of the said island, they recovered their skiff.</p></blockquote>\n<p>The islands are still occasionally called the Ladrones, usually ironically by natives, or disparagingly by non-natives. Pigafetta also described the boats the inhabitants used, the sail shaped like a "lateen sail", hence the name <b>Islas de las Velas Latinas</b> (Islands of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lateen_sail" title="Lateen sail">Lateen Sails</a>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nowell_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Nowell-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>131</span></sup> the name used first as Magellan claimed them for the Spanish crown. San Lazarus archipelago, Jardines (\'gardens\') and Prazeres are among the names applied to them by later navigators.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:102px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Estampilla_espa%C3%B1ola_de_las_Islas_Marianas_5_cent_1898-99.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="442" data-file-width="378" decoding="async" height="117" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Estampilla_espa%C3%B1ola_de_las_Islas_Marianas_5_cent_1898-99.jpg/100px-Estampilla_espa%C3%B1ola_de_las_Islas_Marianas_5_cent_1898-99.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Estampilla_espa%C3%B1ola_de_las_Islas_Marianas_5_cent_1898-99.jpg/150px-Estampilla_espa%C3%B1ola_de_las_Islas_Marianas_5_cent_1898-99.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Estampilla_espa%C3%B1ola_de_las_Islas_Marianas_5_cent_1898-99.jpg/200px-Estampilla_espa%C3%B1ola_de_las_Islas_Marianas_5_cent_1898-99.jpg 2x" width="100"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Estampilla_espa%C3%B1ola_de_las_Islas_Marianas_5_cent_1898-99.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A stamp from the Marianas\' late Spanish colonial period, 1898&#8211;1899</div></div></div>\n<p>In 1667, <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spain</a> formally claimed them, established a regular <a href="/wiki/Colony" title="Colony">colony</a> there and gave the islands the official title of <i>Las Marianas</i>, in honor of Spanish Queen <a href="/wiki/Mariana_of_Austria" title="Mariana of Austria">Mariana of Austria</a>, widow of <a href="/wiki/Philip_IV_of_Spain" title="Philip IV of Spain">Philip IV of Spain</a>. They then had a population of more than 50,000 inhabitants. With the arrival of passengers and settlers aboard the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manila_Galleon" title="Manila Galleon">Manila Galleons</a> from the Americas, new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Infectious_disease" title="Infectious disease">diseases</a> were introduced in the islands, which caused many deaths in the native <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chamorros" title="Chamorros">Chamorro</a> population.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The native population, who referred to themselves as <i>Taotao Tano</i> (people of the land)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Insular_Empire_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Insular_Empire-7">[7]</a></sup> but were known to the early Spanish colonists as <i>Hachamori</i><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> has died out as a distinct people, though their descendants intermarried. At the Spanish occupation in 1668, the Chamorros were estimated at 50,000, but a century later only 1,800 natives remained, as the majority of the population was of mixed Spanish-Chamorro blood or <a href="/wiki/Mestizo" title="Mestizo">mestizo</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> They were characteristic Micronesians, with a considerable civilization. In the island of <a href="/wiki/Tinian" title="Tinian">Tinian</a> are some remarkable remains attributed to them, consisting of two rows of massive square stone columns, about 5&#160;feet 4&#160;inches (1.63&#160;m) broad and 14 feet (4.3&#160;m) high, with heavy-round <a href="/wiki/Capital_(architecture)" title="Capital (architecture)">capitals</a> called <a href="/wiki/Latte_stone" title="Latte stone">latte stones</a>. According to early Spanish accounts <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cinerary_urn" title="Cinerary urn">cinerary urns</a> were found embedded in the capitals.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Accuracy_dispute#Disputed_statement" title="Wikipedia:Accuracy dispute"><span title="The material near this tag is possibly inaccurate or nonfactual. (January 2010)">dubious</span></a> <span class="metadata"> &#8211; <a href="/wiki/Talk:Mariana_Islands#Dubious" title="Talk:Mariana Islands">discuss</a></span></i>]</sup>\n</p><p>When Spanish settlement started on 14 June 1668, they were subordinate to the Mexican colony (soon viceroyalty) of <a href="/wiki/New_Spain" title="New Spain">New Spain</a>, until 1817, when they became subordinated to the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>, like the bulk of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_East_Indies" title="Spanish East Indies">Spanish East Indies</a>.\n</p><p>Research in the archipelago was carried out by <a href="/wiki/George_Anson,_1st_Baron_Anson" title="George Anson, 1st Baron Anson">Commodore Anson</a>, who in August 1742 landed upon the island of Tinian.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The Ladrones were visited by <a href="/wiki/John_Byron" title="John Byron">Byron</a> in 1765, <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Wallis" title="Samuel Wallis">Wallis</a> in 1767 and <a href="/wiki/Crozet_Islands" title="Crozet Islands">Crozet</a> in 1772.\n</p><p>The Marianas and specifically the island of Guam were a stopover for Spanish galleons en route from <a href="/wiki/Acapulco" title="Acapulco">Acapulco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> to <a href="/wiki/Manila" title="Manila">Manila</a>, Philippines in a convoy known as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manila_Galleon" title="Manila Galleon">Galeon de Manila</a>.  Following the <a href="/wiki/1872_Cavite_mutiny" title="1872 Cavite mutiny">1872 Cavite mutiny</a>, several <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Filipino_people" title="Filipino people">Filipinos</a> were exiled to Guam, including the father of <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Paterno" title="Pedro Paterno">Pedro Paterno</a>, Maximo Paterno, Dr. Antonio M. Regidor y Jurado and Jose Maria Basa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Foreman_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Foreman-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>107&#8211;108</span></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Loss_from_Spain_and_split_in_governance">Loss from Spain and split in governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mariana_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Loss from Spain and split in governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">Guam</a> and <a href="/wiki/Northern_Mariana_Islands" title="Northern Mariana Islands">Northern Mariana Islands</a></div>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:102px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Stamp_Mariana_Islands_1901_20pf.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="622" data-file-width="524" decoding="async" height="119" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Stamp_Mariana_Islands_1901_20pf.jpg/100px-Stamp_Mariana_Islands_1901_20pf.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Stamp_Mariana_Islands_1901_20pf.jpg/150px-Stamp_Mariana_Islands_1901_20pf.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Stamp_Mariana_Islands_1901_20pf.jpg/200px-Stamp_Mariana_Islands_1901_20pf.jpg 2x" width="100"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Stamp_Mariana_Islands_1901_20pf.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A 1901 stamp from the German-era Marianas</div></div></div>\n<p>The Marianas remained a Spanish <a href="/wiki/Colony" title="Colony">colony</a> under the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/General_government_of_the_Philippines" title="General government of the Philippines">general government of the Philippines</a> until 1898, when, as a result of its loss in the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish&#8211;American War">Spanish&#8211;American War</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spain</a> ceded <a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">Guam</a> to the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. Guam has retained a different political character from the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Mariana_Islands" title="Northern Mariana Islands">Northern Marianas</a> since this time. Following the <a href="/wiki/Philippine%E2%80%93American_War" title="Philippine&#8211;American War">Philippine&#8211;American War</a>, <a href="/wiki/Apolinario_Mabini" title="Apolinario Mabini">Apolinario Mabini</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Filipinos" title="Filipinos">Filipino</a> leaders were exiled to Guam in 1901.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mabini_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mabini-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>vi</span></sup>\n</p><p>Weakened from its defeat in the Spanish&#8211;American War, <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spain</a> could no longer effectively control and protect the nearly 6,000 islands it retained throughout <a href="/wiki/Micronesia" title="Micronesia">Micronesia</a>, including the Northern Marianas, <a href="/wiki/Caroline_Islands" title="Caroline Islands">Carolines</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pelew_Islands" title="Pelew Islands">Pelew Islands</a>. Therefore, Spain entered into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German-Spanish_Treaty_(1899)" title="German-Spanish Treaty (1899)">German-Spanish Treaty of February 12, 1899</a> to sell the Northern Marianas and its other remaining islands to <a href="/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">Germany</a> for 837,500 <a href="/wiki/German_gold_mark" title="German gold mark">German gold marks</a> (about $4,100,000 at the time). The Northern Marianas and other island groups were incorporated by Germany as a small part of the larger <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_Protectorate_of_New_Guinea" title="German Protectorate of New Guinea">German Protectorate of New Guinea</a>. The total population in the Northern Marianas portion of these islands was only 2,646 inhabitants around this time, with the ten most northerly islands being actively volcanic and thus mostly uninhabited.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, allied with the <a href="/wiki/Triple_Entente" title="Triple Entente">Entente Powers</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>, seized all of Germany\'s colonial possessions in East Asia and Micronesia, including the Northern Mariana Islands, and held them through the end of the War.  Under the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Versailles" title="Treaty of Versailles">Treaty of Versailles</a> in 1919, Germany was stripped of all her colonies worldwide, including the Palau, Caroline, Northern Mariana and Marshall Islands.  By international agreement, these were all placed into trusteeship under the management of <a href="/wiki/League_of_Nations" title="League of Nations">League of Nations</a> which assigned them to Japan as the Class C <a href="/wiki/South_Pacific_Mandate" title="South Pacific Mandate">South Pacific Mandate</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nanyo_(Japanese_mandated_territory)" title="Nanyo (Japanese mandated territory)">Nanyo</a>. During this time, Japan used some of the islands for <a href="/wiki/Sugarcane" title="Sugarcane">sugarcane</a> production, modestly increasing the population of a few of the islands.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_War_II">World War II</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mariana_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: World War II">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:USMC-C-Saipan-p20a.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="408" data-file-width="640" decoding="async" height="140" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/USMC-C-Saipan-p20a.jpg/220px-USMC-C-Saipan-p20a.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/USMC-C-Saipan-p20a.jpg/330px-USMC-C-Saipan-p20a.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/USMC-C-Saipan-p20a.jpg/440px-USMC-C-Saipan-p20a.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:USMC-C-Saipan-p20a.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A U.S. Marine talks a terrified Chamorro woman and her children into abandoning their refuge. <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Saipan" title="Battle of Saipan">Battle of Saipan</a>, 1944.</div></div></div>\n<p>The island chain saw significant fighting during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. <a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">Guam</a>, a possession of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> since 1898, <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Guam_(1941)" title="Battle of Guam (1941)">was captured</a> by <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Japan" title="Empire of Japan">Japan</a> in an attack from the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Mariana_Islands" title="Northern Mariana Islands">Northern Mariana Islands</a> that began on the day of the Japanese <a href="/wiki/Attack_on_Pearl_Harbor" title="Attack on Pearl Harbor">attack on Pearl Harbor</a> (December 8, 1941, the same time as the Pearl Harbor attack across the international dateline). In 1944, the United States <a href="/wiki/Mariana_and_Palau_Islands_campaign" title="Mariana and Palau Islands campaign">captured the Mariana Islands chain from Japan</a>: the Northern Mariana Islands were desired by the U.S. as <a href="/wiki/Strategic_bombing" title="Strategic bombing">bombing</a> bases to reach the <a href="/wiki/Mainland_Japan" title="Mainland Japan">Japanese mainland</a>, with the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Saipan" title="Battle of Saipan">invasion of Saipan</a> being launched for that reason in June before the U.S. even moved to recapture Guam; a month later the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Guam_(1944)" title="Battle of Guam (1944)">recaptured Guam</a> and <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Tinian" title="Battle of Tinian">captured Tinian</a>. Once captured, the islands of <a href="/wiki/Saipan" title="Saipan">Saipan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tinian" title="Tinian">Tinian</a> were used extensively by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_military" title="United States military">United States military</a> as they finally put mainland Japan within round-trip range of American <a href="/wiki/Boeing_B-29_Superfortress" title="Boeing B-29 Superfortress">B-29 bombers</a>. In response, Japanese forces <a href="/wiki/Japanese_air_attacks_on_the_Mariana_Islands" title="Japanese air attacks on the Mariana Islands">attacked the bases</a> on Saipan and Tinian from November 1944 to January 1945. At the same time and afterwards, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Air_Forces" title="United States Army Air Forces">United States Army Air Forces</a> based out of these islands conducted <a href="/wiki/Air_raids_on_Japan" title="Air raids on Japan">an intense strategic bombing campaign</a> against the Japanese cities of military and industrial importance, including <a href="/wiki/Bombing_of_Tokyo" title="Bombing of Tokyo">Tokyo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bombing_of_Nagoya_in_World_War_II" title="Bombing of Nagoya in World War II">Nagoya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bombing_of_Osaka" title="Bombing of Osaka">Osaka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bombing_of_Kobe_in_World_War_II" title="Bombing of Kobe in World War II">Kobe</a> and others. Both the <i><a href="/wiki/Enola_Gay" title="Enola Gay">Enola Gay</a></i> and the <i><a href="/wiki/Bockscar" title="Bockscar">Bockscar</a></i> (which <a href="/wiki/Atomic_bombings_of_Hiroshima_and_Nagasaki" title="Atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki">dropped atomic bombs</a> on <a href="/wiki/Hiroshima" title="Hiroshima">Hiroshima</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nagasaki" title="Nagasaki">Nagasaki</a>, respectively) flew their missions from Tinian\'s <a href="/wiki/North_Field_(Tinian)" title="North Field (Tinian)">North Field</a>.\n</p><p>According to Werner Gruhl: "Mariana Island historians estimate that 10 percent of Guam\'s some 20,000 population were killed by violence, most by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_Imperial_Army" title="Japanese Imperial Army">Japanese Imperial Army</a> and <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Navy" title="Imperial Japanese Navy">Navy</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post_World_War_II">Post World War II</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mariana_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Post World War II">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Trust_Territory_of_the_Pacific_Islands" title="Trust Territory of the Pacific Islands">Trust Territory of the Pacific Islands</a></div>\n<p>The direct result of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> on the Mariana Islands was that, after the war, the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Mariana_Islands" title="Northern Mariana Islands">Northern Mariana Islands</a> came under control of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> in the same way they had earlier come under the control of <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Japan" title="Empire of Japan">Japan</a> after <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>.  However, this time they became part of the U.S.-administered <a href="/wiki/Trust_Territory_of_the_Pacific_Islands" title="Trust Territory of the Pacific Islands">Trust Territory of the Pacific Islands</a> (TTPI) established pursuant to <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council_Resolution_21" title="United Nations Security Council Resolution 21">Security Council Resolution 21</a>. The Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands later became a <a href="/wiki/United_States_territory" title="United States territory">U.S. territory</a> following its exit from the TTPI pursuant to <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council_Resolution_683" title="United Nations Security Council Resolution 683">Security Council Resolution 683</a>. Although now both under U.S. control, the Northern Mariana Islands have not reunited with the territory of <a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">Guam</a>, in part due to residual post-war tensions resulting from the very different histories of Guam (occupied by Japan for only 31 months, in wartime) and the Northern Mariana Islands (more peacefully occupied by Japan, for about 30 years). See the main articles above for discussion of present-day politics in these territorial areas.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="List_of_islands">List of islands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mariana_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: List of islands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<table class="wikitable" style="text-align:right;">\n<tbody><tr>\n<th align="center">Island name\n</th>\n<th align="center">Population\n</th>\n<th align="center">Municipality or territory\n</th></tr>\n<tr>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">Guam</a>\n</td>\n<td>159,358\n</td>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">Guam</a>\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Saipan" title="Saipan">Saipan</a>\n</td>\n<td>48,220\n</td>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Saipan" title="Saipan">Saipan</a>\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Tinian" title="Tinian">Tinian</a>\n</td>\n<td>3,136\n</td>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Tinian_Municipality" title="Tinian Municipality">Tinian</a>\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Rota_(island)" title="Rota (island)">Rota</a>\n</td>\n<td>2,477\n</td>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Rota_(island)" title="Rota (island)">Rota</a>\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Aguigan" title="Aguigan">Aguigan</a>\n</td>\n<td>0<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="Indigenous people still live in the minor Marianas island. There are several large tour operators in Saipan that cater to Asian tourists coming into the island. By far, the majority of tourism in the Northern Marianas is in Saipan. Several flights a day land in Saipan, mostly in the early hours between 1:00 AM and 3:30 AM. With the close of the garment industries in the Northern Marianas, tourism has grown slowly and is now a major part of the <a href="/wiki/Economy" title="Economy">economy</a> of the CNMI.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Amateur radio operators conduct <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DXpedition" title="DXpedition">DXpeditions</a> to
