(383&#160;/sq&#160;mi)</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Society Islands</b> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <i lang="fr">&#206;les de la Soci&#233;t&#233;</i> or officially <i><i lang="fr" title="French language text">Archipel de la Soci&#233;t&#233;</i></i>; <a href="/wiki/Tahitian_language" title="Tahitian language">Tahitian</a>: <i lang="ty">T&#333;taiete m&#257;</i>) are an <a href="/wiki/Archipelago" title="Archipelago">archipelago</a> located in the South <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>. Politically, they are part of <a href="/wiki/French_Polynesia" title="French Polynesia">French Polynesia</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Overseas_collectivity" title="Overseas collectivity">overseas collectivity</a> of <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>. Geographically, they form part of <a href="/wiki/Polynesia" title="Polynesia">Polynesia</a>.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Archipelago" title="Archipelago">archipelago</a> is believed to have been named by Captain <a href="/wiki/James_Cook" title="James Cook">James Cook</a> during his first voyage in 1769, supposedly in honour of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society" title="Royal Society">Royal Society</a>, the sponsor of the first British scientific survey of the islands; however, Cook stated in his journal that he called the islands <i>Society</i> "as they lay contiguous to one They have a population of 235,295 inhabitants (as of  2012<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Society_Islands&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-census_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-census-1">[1]</a></sup> They cover a land area of 1,590 square kilometres (610&#160;sq&#160;mi).\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tnone" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right:auto; width:auto; max-width:1008px;">\n<div class="thumbinner"><div class="noresize overflowbugx" style="overflow:auto;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Societyislands.jpg"><img alt="" data-file-height="2553" data-file-width="5838" decoding="async" height="437" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Societyislands.jpg/1000px-Societyislands.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Societyislands.jpg/1500px-Societyislands.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Societyislands.jpg/2000px-Societyislands.jpg 2x" width="1000"/></a></div>\n</div></div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Society_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Myth_origin">Myth origin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Society_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Myth origin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The islanders explain their origins in term of a myth. The feathered god Ta\'aroa lay in his shell. He called out but no-one answered, so he went back into his shell, where he stayed for aeons. When he came out he changed his body into the multi-layered dome of the sky. Other parts of his body he transformed into Papa-fenua, the earth. Other parts he made into Te Tuma, the ata, or shadow of his phallus. Ta\'aroa said, "Cast your eyes on my phallus. Gaze upon it and insert it in the earth." He came down to earth at "Opoa in Havai\'i" (now Ra\'iatea), one of the most sacred places in the Society Islands. Other gods were created, and these ran directly into the time of the people. The high chiefs or ari\'i rahi were descendants from the gods, reckoned to be forty generation previously. In their presence commoners showed respect by stripping to the waist. The high chiefs erected <a href="/wiki/Marae" title="Marae">marae</a> as places of worship.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" commanded by <a href="/wiki/James_Cook" title="James Cook">James Cook</a></div></div></div>\n<p>In the generations before Europeans arrived, a cult called \'Oro-maro-\'ura developed: the cult of the red-feathered girdle. This became a tangible symbol of the chief\'s power. Key followers of the \'Oro cult were the \'arioi, who lived separately from the common people. They wore scented flowers and adorned themselves with scents and scarlet-dyed cloth. The head of each \'arioi group was heavily tattooed from ankle to thigh and known as a blackleg. Both male and female blacklegs were a privileged group but they were forbidden to have children. Their babies were all killed at birth. They received and gave lavish presents. They had a wide range of artistic skills and could be priests, navigators and lore specialists. Only good-looking men or women could become \'arioi. They played a crucial role in ceremonies associated with birth, deaths and marriage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="European_contact">European contact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Society_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: European contact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The first European explorers arrived in 1722. <a href="/wiki/Jacob_Roggeveen" title="Jacob Roggeveen">Jacob Roggeveen</a>\'s Dutch West Indian Expedition was shipwrecked on Takapoto. Survivors managed to row to another small island, and islanders were killed with muskets. In 1765 <a href="/wiki/John_Byron" title="John Byron">John Byron</a>\'s two British ships, including <a href="/wiki/HMS_Dolphin_(1751)" title="HMS Dolphin (1751)">HMS <i>Dolphin</i></a>, briefly visited the area. In 1767 HMS <i>Dolphin</i> returned under Captain <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Wallis" title="Samuel Wallis">Samuel Wallis</a>. The captain and crew were quite sick with <a href="/wiki/Scurvy" title="Scurvy">scurvy</a> on arrival and were keen to obtain fresh food. The islanders, who had learned about iron 45 years earlier, were delighted at the abundance of iron on the ship and tried to board the ship. After several contacts, when natives attempted to take iron fittings, Wallis was forced to shoot cannon to regain control.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Europeans quickly found that the islanders were desperate to obtain iron. The sailors found that young women and girls were eager to exchange sex for a nail, which was used for woodworking and as fish-hooks. Traditionally young women had offered themselves to ancestor gods in the form of chiefs or other high status individuals. Some rituals involved chiefs having sex with virgins in public view, and this was offered to some European captains and officers, who declined.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>\n</p><p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Louis_de_Bougainville" title="Louis de Bougainville">Louis de Bougainville</a>, a French nobleman, sailor and soldier, led an expedition to the Society Islands in 1766.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He arrived with a crew stricken with scurvy. Despite the crew being twice as big as the <i>Dolphin</i>&#8242;s, the islanders had sufficient food to trade their surplus for axes, knives and other iron goods.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Society_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Each of the Society Islands has a small airport. <a href="/wiki/Fa%27a%27%C4%81_International_Airport" title="Fa\'a\'&#257; International Airport">Fa\'a\'&#257; International Airport</a> is located in Tahiti and is the largest airport in the Society Islands.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Society_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-census-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink">^ <a href="#cite_ref-census_1-0"><sup><i><b>a</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-census_1-1"><sup><i><b>b</b></i></sup></a></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a class="external text" href="http://www.ispf.pf/bases/Recensements/2012/Donnees_detaillees.aspx" rel="nofollow">"Population"</a>. <i>Institut de la statistique de la Polyn&#233;sie fran&#231;aise</i> (in French)<span class="reference-accessdate">.
