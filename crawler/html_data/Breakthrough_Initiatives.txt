Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>\n<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>\n<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>\n<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>\n<b>Breakthrough Initiatives</b> is a science-based program founded in 2015 and funded by Julia and <a href="/wiki/Yuri_Milner" title="Yuri Milner">Yuri Milner</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> also of <a href="/wiki/Breakthrough_Prize" title="Breakthrough Prize">Breakthrough Prize</a>, to <a href="/wiki/Search_for_extraterrestrial_intelligence" title="Search for extraterrestrial intelligence">search for extraterrestrial intelligence</a> over a span of at least 10 years. The program is divided into multiple projects. <a href="/wiki/Breakthrough_Listen" title="Breakthrough Listen">Breakthrough Listen</a> will comprise an effort to search over 1,000,000 stars for artificial radio or laser signals. A parallel project called Breakthrough Message is an effort to create a message "representative of humanity and planet Earth".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-20170628_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-20170628-3">[3]</a></sup> The project <a href="/wiki/Breakthrough_Starshot" title="Breakthrough Starshot">Breakthrough Starshot</a> aims to send a swarm of probes to the nearest star at about 20% the <a href="/wiki/Speed_of_light" title="Speed of light">speed of light</a>. The project Breakthrough Watch aims to identify and characterize Earth-sized, rocky planets around Alpha Centauri and other stars within 20 light years of Earth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">[4]</a></sup> Breakthrough plans to send a mission to <a href="/wiki/Saturn" title="Saturn">Saturn</a>\'s moon <a href="/wiki/Enceladus" title="Enceladus">Enceladus</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-:1-5">[5]</a></sup> in search for life in its warm ocean, and in 2018 singed a partnership agreement with NASA for the project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:2_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-:2-6">[6]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc"><input class="toctogglecheckbox" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Breakthrough_Initiatives&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Breakthrough Initiatives were announced to the public on July 20, 2015, at London\'s <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society" title="Royal Society">Royal Society</a> by Physicist <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Hawking" title="Stephen Hawking">Stephen Hawking</a>. Russian tycoon <a href="/wiki/Yuri_Milner" title="Yuri Milner">Yuri Milner</a> created the <i>Initiatives</i> to search for intelligent extraterrestrial life in the Universe and consider a plan for possibly transmitting messages out into <a href="/wiki/Outer_space" title="Outer space">space</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-washingtonp1_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-washingtonp1-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sa20150720_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-sa20150720-8">[8]</a></sup>\nThe announcement included an open letter co-signed by multiple scientists, including Hawking, expressing support for an intensified search for alien radio communications. During the public launch, Hawking said: "In an infinite Universe, there must be other life. There is no bigger question. It is time to commit to finding the answer."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Merali0720_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Merali0720-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-time20150720_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-time20150720-10">[10]</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$100 million</span> cash infusion is projected to mark up the pace of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SETI" title="SETI">SETI</a> research over the early 2000s rate, and will nearly double the rate NASA was spending on SETI research annually in approximately 1973&#8211;1993.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sa20150720_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-sa20150720-8">[8]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Projects">Projects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Breakthrough_Initiatives&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Projects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Breakthrough_Listen">Breakthrough Listen</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Breakthrough_Initiatives&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Breakthrough Listen">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Breakthrough_Listen" title="Breakthrough Listen">Breakthrough Listen</a></div>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:GBT.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1837" data-file-width="1632" decoding="async" height="248" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e3/GBT.png/220px-GBT.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e3/GBT.png/330px-GBT.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e3/GBT.png/440px-GBT.png 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:GBT.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/Green_Bank_Telescope" title="Green Bank Telescope">Green Bank Telescope</a> is one of the <a href="/wiki/Radio_telescope" title="Radio telescope">radio telescopes</a> used by the Breakthrough Listen project.</div></div></div>\n<p>Breakthrough Listen is a program to search for intelligent extraterrestrial communications in the Universe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-washingtonp1_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-washingtonp1-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Merali0720_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Merali0720-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> With $100 million in funding and thousands of hours of dedicated telescope time on state-of-the-art facilities, it is the most comprehensive search for alien communications to date.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Merali0720_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-Merali0720-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The project began in January 2016, and is expected to continue for 10 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>\n</p><p>The project uses radio wave observations from the <a href="/wiki/Green_Bank_Telescope" title="Green Bank Telescope">Green Bank Observatory</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Parkes_Observatory" title="Parkes Observatory">Parkes Observatory</a>, and visible light observations from the <a href="/wiki/Automated_Planet_Finder" title="Automated Planet Finder">Automated Planet Finder</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sample0720_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sample0720-14">[14]</a></sup> Targets for the project include one million nearby stars and the centers of 100 galaxies. All data generated from the project are available to the public, and SETI@Home is used for some of the data analysis. The first results were published in April 2017, with further updates expected every 6 months.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Breakthrough_Message">Breakthrough Message</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Breakthrough_Initiatives&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Breakthrough Message">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The <i>Breakthrough Message</i> program is to study the ethics of sending messages into deep space.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> It also launched an open competition with a US$1 million prize pool, to design a digital message that could be transmitted from Earth to an extraterrestrial civilization. The message should be "representative of humanity and planet Earth". The program pledges "not to transmit any message until there has been a global debate at high levels of science and politics on the risks and rewards of contacting advanced civilizations".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Breakthrough_Starshot">Breakthrough Starshot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Breakthrough_Initiatives&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Breakthrough Starshot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Breakthrough_Starshot" title="Breakthrough Starshot">Breakthrough Starshot</a></div>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Press_Conference_at_ESO_HQ2.tif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2667" data-file-width="4000" decoding="async" height="200" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Press_Conference_at_ESO_HQ2.tif/lossy-page1-300px-Press_Conference_at_ESO_HQ2.tif.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Press_Conference_at_ESO_HQ2.tif/lossy-page1-450px-Press_Conference_at_ESO_HQ2.tif.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Press_Conference_at_ESO_HQ2.tif/lossy-page1-600px-Press_Conference_at_ESO_HQ2.tif.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Press_Conference_at_ESO_HQ2.tif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>On 24 August 2016, ESO hosted a press conference to discuss the announcement of exoplanet <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Proxima_b" title="Proxima b">Proxima b</a> at its headquarters in Germany. In this picture, <a href="/wiki/Pete_Worden" title="Pete Worden">Pete Worden</a> giving a speech.</div></div></div>\n<p>Breakthrough Starshot, announced April 12, 2016, is a US $100 million program to develop a proof-of-concept <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Light_sail" title="Light sail">light sail</a> spacecraft fleet capable of making the journey to <a href="/wiki/Alpha_Centauri" title="Alpha Centauri">Alpha Centauri</a> at 20% the <a href="/wiki/Speed_of_light" title="Speed of light">speed of light</a> (60,000&#160;km/s or 215 million km/h) taking about 20 years to get there,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stone_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stone-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BR-20160412about_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-BR-20160412about-18">[18]</a></sup> and about 4 years to notify Earth of a successful arrival.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stone_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-Stone-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-20160412-db_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-20160412-db-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Starshot_home_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Starshot_home-20">[20]</a></sup>\n</p><p>The interstellar journey may include a flyby of <i><a href="/wiki/Proxima_Centauri_b" title="Proxima Centauri b">Proxima Centauri b</a></i>, an <a href="/wiki/Terrestrial_planet" title="Terrestrial planet">Earth-sized</a> <a href="/wiki/Exoplanet" title="Exoplanet">exoplanet</a> that is in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Habitable_zone" title="Habitable zone">habitable zone</a> of its host star in the Alpha Centauri system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-20160824_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-20160824-21">[21]</a></sup>  From a distance of 1 Astronomical Unit (150 million kilometers or 93 million miles), the four cameras on each of the spacecraft could potentially capture an image of high enough quality to resolve surface features.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The spacecraft fleet would have 1000 craft, and each craft, named <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/StarChip_(spacecraft)" title="StarChip (spacecraft)">StarChip</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CD-20160412_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-CD-20160412-23">[23]</a></sup> would be a very small centimeter-sized craft weighing several grams.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CD-20160412_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-CD-20160412-23">[23]</a></sup> They would be propelled by several ground-based <a href="/wiki/Laser" title="Laser">lasers</a> of up to 100 gigawatts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-concept\'_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-concept\'-24">[24]</a></sup> Each tiny spacecraft would transmit data back to Earth using a compact on-board laser communications system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-concept\'_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-concept\'-24">[24]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Pete_Worden" title="Pete Worden">Pete Worden</a> is the head of this project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> The conceptual principles to enable this interstellar travel project were described in "A Roadmap to Interstellar Flight", by Philip Lubin of <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Santa_Barbara" title="University of California, Santa Barbara">UC Santa Barbara</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UCSB-201504_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-UCSB-201504-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/METI_(Messaging_Extraterrestrial_Intelligence)" title="METI (Messaging Extraterrestrial Intelligence)">METI</a> president <a href="/wiki/Douglas_Vakoch" title="Douglas Vakoch">Douglas Vakoch</a> summarized the significance of the project, saying that "by sending hundreds or thousands of space probes the size of postage stamps,&#160;Breakthrough Starshot&#160;gets around the hazards of spaceflight that could easily end a mission relying on a single spacecraft. Only one nanocraft needs to make its way to&#160;Alpha Centauri&#160;and send back a signal for the mission to be successful. When that happens, Starshot will make history."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup>\n</p><p>In July 2017, scientists announced that precursors to <i>StarChip</i>, named <i>Sprites</i>, were successfully launched and flown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BTI-20170726_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-BTI-20170726-30">[30]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Breakthrough_Watch">Breakthrough Watch</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Breakthrough_Initiatives&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Breakthrough Watch">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Breakthrough Watch&#160;is a multimillion-dollar astronomical program to develop Earth- and space-based technologies that can find Earth-like planets in our cosmic neighborhood &#8211; and try to establish whether they host life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> The project aims to identify and characterize Earth-sized, rocky planets around Alpha Centauri and other stars within 20 light years of Earth, in search of oxygen and other "biosignatures."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">[4]</a></sup>&#160;\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Breakthrough_Enceladus">Breakthrough Enceladus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Breakthrough_Initiatives&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Breakthrough Enceladus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Breakthrough Enceladus is an <a href="/wiki/Astrobiology" title="Astrobiology">astrobiology</a> space probe mission concept to explore the possibility of life on <a href="/wiki/Saturn" title="Saturn">Saturn</a>\'s moon, <a href="/wiki/Enceladus" title="Enceladus">Enceladus</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-:1-5">[5]</a></sup> In September 2018, NASA signed a collaboration agreement with Breakthrough to jointly create the mission concept.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> This mission if launched, would be the first privately funded deep space mission.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:2_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-:2-6">[6]</a></sup> It would study the content of the plumes ejecting from <a href="/wiki/Enceladus#Subsurface_water_ocean" title="Enceladus">Enceladus\'s warm ocean</a> through its southern ice crust.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Enceladus\'s ice crust is thought to be around two to five kilometers thick,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> and a probe could use an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ice-penetrating_radar" title="Ice-penetrating radar">ice-penetrating radar</a> to constrain its structure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
