scope="row">Known&#160;for</th><td>First European to reach the Inca Empire</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Aleixo Garcia</b>, also known in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish_Language" title="Spanish Language">Spanish</a> as <b>Alejo Garc&#237;a</b> (d. 1525 <a href="/wiki/Paraguay" title="Paraguay">Paraguay</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portuguese</a> explorer and <a href="/wiki/Conquistador" title="Conquistador">conquistador</a> who explored the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rio_de_la_Plata" title="Rio de la Plata">Rio de la Plata</a> in service to <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spain</a>, and later <a href="/wiki/Paraguay" title="Paraguay">Paraguay</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">Bolivia</a>.\n</p><p>He was possibly a member of the failed expedition of <a href="/wiki/Juan_D%C3%ADaz_de_Sol%C3%ADs" title="Juan D&#237;az de Sol&#237;s">Juan D&#237;az de Sol&#237;s</a>, seeking to find a passage from the Atlantic to the Pacific Ocean. After reaching the mouths of the <a href="/wiki/Uruguay_River" title="Uruguay River">Uruguay</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paran%C3%A1_River" title="Paran&#225; River">Paran&#225;</a>, it was apparent that the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rio_de_la_Plata" title="Rio de la Plata">Rio de la Plata</a> was not such a strait. At this point, Sol&#237;s was killed - on testimony of his crew - by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cannibal" title="Cannibal">cannibal</a> Indians (variously identified as the <a href="/wiki/Charr%C3%BAa" title="Charr&#250;a">Charr&#250;a</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guarani_people" title="Guarani people">Guaran&#237;</a>) and his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lieutenants" title="Lieutenants">lieutenants</a> opted to return to Spain.\n</p><p>On their return, some of their boats were shipwrecked off <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Santa_Catarina_(island)" title="Santa Catarina (island)">Santa Catarina</a> in present-day <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. Among the 11 or 18 Spanish and Portuguese survivors was Aleixo Garcia, a <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portuguese</a> adventurer who had previously made contact with the <a href="/wiki/Guaran%C3%AD_people" title="Guaran&#237; people">Guaran&#237;</a>. With them were also the Portuguese sailors Henrique Montes (a veteran of the <a href="/wiki/Gon%C3%A7alo_Coelho" title="Gon&#231;alo Coelho">Gon&#231;alo Coelho</a> and <a href="/wiki/Amerigo_Vespucci" title="Amerigo Vespucci">Amerigo Vespucci</a> expeditions) and Francisco Pacheco, and the Spanish sailor Mechior Ramirez. Alejo de Ledesma and Francisco Chaves were also among them. Melchior Ramires, in turn, would assist and guide <a href="/wiki/Crist%C3%B3v%C3%A3o_Jacques" title="Crist&#243;v&#227;o Jacques">Crist&#243;v&#227;o Jacques</a> on his voyage of exploration to R&#237;o de la Plata and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parana_river" title="Parana river">Parana river</a> in 1521, returning again to Santa Catarina.\n</p><p>Aleixo Garcia traveled inland, living among the Guaran&#237;s who knew of the network of trails called Peabiru that covered this part of <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>. While there, he heard tales of a "White King" who lived to the west, ruling cities of incomparable riches and splendor.\n</p><p>After eight years, Garcia had gathered enough men and supplies to attempt a voyage to the land of the "White King." Marching towards the west, using the trail network Peabiru, his company discovered a great waterfall. Credit for the discovery of the <a href="/wiki/Iguazu_Falls" title="Iguazu Falls">Iguazu Falls</a> is usually given to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Governorate_of_the_Rio_de_la_Plata" title="Governorate of the Rio de la Plata">governor</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alvar_N%C3%BA%C3%B1ez_Cabeza_de_Vaca" title="Alvar N&#250;&#241;ez Cabeza de Vaca">Alvar N&#250;&#241;ez Cabeza de Vaca</a>, and historian <a href="/wiki/Efra%C3%ADm_Cardozo" title="Efra&#237;m Cardozo">Efra&#237;m Cardozo</a> asserts Garcia crossed the <a href="/wiki/Paran%C3%A1_River" title="Paran&#225; River">Paran&#225;</a> at the smaller waterfall called <a href="/wiki/Saltos_del_Monday" title="Saltos del Monday">Saltos del Monday</a>.\n</p><p>In 1524 and 1525, the small group (four or five men, or more than a dozen, depending on the sources) recruited an army of about 2,000 local Guaran&#237; soldiers from the vicinity of <a href="/wiki/Asunci%C3%B3n" title="Asunci&#243;n">Asunci&#243;n</a> as reinforcement to invade the promising new land. They then entered the <a href="/wiki/Gran_Chaco" title="Gran Chaco">Chaco</a>, a rough semi-desert region. Garcia was the first <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> to cross the Chaco and even managed to penetrate the outer defenses of the <a href="/wiki/Inca_Empire" title="Inca Empire">Inca Empire</a> on the hills of the <a href="/wiki/Andes" title="Andes">Andes</a>, in present-day <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">Bolivia</a>. He was the first European to do so, accomplishing this eight years before <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Pizarro" title="Francisco Pizarro">Francisco Pizarro</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Russell-Wood1998_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Russell-Wood1998-1">[1]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Garcia looted an impressive booty of silver. When the army of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Huayna_C%C3%A1pac" title="Huayna C&#225;pac">Huayna C&#225;pac</a> arrived to challenge him, Garcia then retreated with the spoils, only to be assassinated by his Indian allies near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Pedro,_Paraguay" title="San Pedro, Paraguay">San Pedro</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Paraguay_River" title="Paraguay River">Paraguay River</a>.\n</p><p>The Indians, however, spared the life of his son, who was the first Paraguayan <a href="/wiki/Mestizo" title="Mestizo">mestizo</a>. News of this excursion into Incan territory later distracted <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Cabot_(explorer)" title="Sebastian Cabot (explorer)">Sebastian Cabot</a> from his expedition to the <a href="/wiki/East_Indies" title="East Indies">East Indies</a> (which could have resulted in the second <a href="/wiki/Circumnavigation" title="Circumnavigation">circumnavigation</a> of the globe after <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_Magellan" title="Ferdinand Magellan">Ferdinand Magellan</a>) causing him to imprison or maroon his lieutenants and remain in the Rio de la Plata region for several years.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aleixo_Garcia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-Russell-Wood1998-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-Russell-Wood1998_1-0">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation book">A.
