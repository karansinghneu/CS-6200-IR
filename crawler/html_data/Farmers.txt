(Australia) or stockman</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>A <b>farmer</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> (also called an <b>agriculturer</b>) is a person engaged in <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a>, raising living organisms for food or raw materials. The term usually applies to people who do some combination of raising field <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crops" title="Crops">crops</a>, <a href="/wiki/Orchard" title="Orchard">orchards</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vineyard" title="Vineyard">vineyards</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poultry" title="Poultry">poultry</a>, or other <a href="/wiki/Livestock" title="Livestock">livestock</a>. A farmer might own the farmed land or might work as a laborer on land owned by others, but in advanced economies, a farmer is usually a <a href="/wiki/Farm" title="Farm">farm</a> owner, while employees of the farm are known as farm workers, or farmhands. However, in the not so distant past, a farmer was a person who promotes or improves the growth of (a plant, crop, etc.) by labor and attention, land or crops or raises animals (as livestock or fish).\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc"><input class="toctogglecheckbox" id="toctogglecheckbox" role="button" style="display:none" type="checkbox"/><div class="toctitle" dir="ltr" has been dated back as far as the <a href="/wiki/Neolithic" title="Neolithic">Neolithic</a> era. By the <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Age" title="Bronze Age">Bronze Age</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Sumer" title="Sumer">Sumerians</a> had an agriculture <a href="/wiki/Division_of_labour" title="Division of labour">specialized labor force</a> by 5000&#8211;4000 BCE, and heavily depended on irrigation to grow crops. They relied on three-person teams when harvesting in the spring.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ancient_Egyptian" title="Ancient Egyptian">Ancient Egypt</a> farmers farmed and relied and irrigated their water from the <a href="/wiki/Nile" title="Nile">Nile</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Animal_husbandry" title="Animal husbandry">Animal husbandry</a>, the practice of rearing animals specifically for farming purposes, has existed for thousands of years. <a href="/wiki/Dog" title="Dog">Dogs</a> were domesticated in East Asia about 15,000 years ago. <a href="/wiki/Goat" title="Goat">Goats</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sheep" title="Sheep">sheep</a> were domesticated around 8000 BCE in <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. <a href="/wiki/Domestic_pig" title="Domestic pig">Swine or pigs</a> were domesticated by 7000 BCE in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> and <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. The earliest evidence of <a href="/wiki/Horse" title="Horse">horse</a> domestication dates to around 4000 BCE.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BoL_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-BoL-4">[4]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Advancements_in_technology">Advancements in technology</span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:FFA_greenhouse_training_120813-A-PO167-151.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3744" data-file-width="5616" decoding="async" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/FFA_greenhouse_training_120813-A-PO167-151.jpg/220px-FFA_greenhouse_training_120813-A-PO167-151.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/FFA_greenhouse_training_120813-A-PO167-151.jpg/330px-FFA_greenhouse_training_120813-A-PO167-151.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/FFA_greenhouse_training_120813-A-PO167-151.jpg/440px-FFA_greenhouse_training_120813-A-PO167-151.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:FFA_greenhouse_training_120813-A-PO167-151.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Afghani farmers learning about <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse" title="Greenhouse">greenhouses</a>.</div></div></div>\n<p>In the U.S. of the 1930s, one farmer could only produce enough food to feed three other consumers. A modern-day farmer produces enough food to feed well over a hundred people. However, some authors consider this estimate to be flawed, as it does not take into account that farming requires energy and many other resources which have to be provided by additional workers, so that the ratio of people fed to farmers is actually smaller than 100 to 1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kirschenmann_2000_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kirschenmann_2000-5">[5]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Types">Types</span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:NRCSCT07047_-_Connecticut_(716026)(NRCS_Photo_Gallery).tif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1800" data-file-width="2700" decoding="async" height="133" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/NRCSCT07047_-_Connecticut_%28716026%29%28NRCS_Photo_Gallery%29.tif/lossy-page1-200px-NRCSCT07047_-_Connecticut_%28716026%29%28NRCS_Photo_Gallery%29.tif.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/NRCSCT07047_-_Connecticut_%28716026%29%28NRCS_Photo_Gallery%29.tif/lossy-page1-300px-NRCSCT07047_-_Connecticut_%28716026%29%28NRCS_Photo_Gallery%29.tif.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/NRCSCT07047_-_Connecticut_%28716026%29%28NRCS_Photo_Gallery%29.tif/lossy-page1-400px-NRCSCT07047_-_Connecticut_%28716026%29%28NRCS_Photo_Gallery%29.tif.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:NRCSCT07047_-_Connecticut_(716026)(NRCS_Photo_Gallery).tif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>An American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dairy_farmer" title="Dairy farmer">dairy farmer</a></div></div></div>\n<p>More distinct terms are commonly used to denote farmers who raise specific <a href="/wiki/Domestication" title="Domestication">domesticated</a> animals. For example, those who raise grazing livestock, such as <a href="/wiki/Cattle" title="Cattle">cattle</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Domestic_sheep" title="Domestic sheep">sheep</a>, <a href="/wiki/Goat" title="Goat">goats</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Horse" title="Horse">horses</a>, are known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ranching" title="Ranching"><i>ranchers</i></a> (U.S.), <i><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/grazier" title="wikt:grazier">graziers</a></i> (Australia &amp; U.K.), or simply <i>stockmen</i>. Sheep, goat, and cattle farmers might also be referred to respectively as <i><a href="/wiki/Shepherd" title="Shepherd">shepherds</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Goatherd" title="Goatherd">goatherds</a></i>, and <i><a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Cowherd_(disambiguation)#Worker" title="Cowherd (disambiguation)">cowherds</a></i>. The term <i><a href="/wiki/Dairy" title="Dairy">dairy</a> farmer</i> is applied to those engaged primarily in milk production, whether from cattle, goats, sheep, or other milk producing animals. A <i>poultry farmer</i> is one who concentrates on raising <a href="/wiki/Chicken" title="Chicken">chickens</a>, <a href="/wiki/Domestic_turkey" title="Domestic turkey">turkeys</a>, <a href="/wiki/Domestic_duck" title="Domestic duck">ducks</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Domestic_goose" title="Domestic goose">geese</a>, for either <a href="/wiki/Meat" title="Meat">meat</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Egg_(food)" title="Egg (food)">egg</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Feather" title="Feather">feather</a> production, or commonly, all three. A person who raises a variety of vegetables for market may be called a <i>truck farmer</i> or <i>market gardener</i>. <i>Dirt farmer</i> is an American colloquial term for a practical farmer, or one who farms his own land.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>\n</p><p>In developed nations, a farmer (as a profession) is usually defined as someone with an ownership interest in crops or livestock, and who provides land or management in their production. Those who provide only labor are most often called <i>farmhands</i>. Alternatively, growers who manage farmland for an absentee landowner, sharing the harvest (or its profits) are known as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sharecropper" title="Sharecropper">sharecroppers</a></i> or <i>sharefarmers</i>. In the context of <a href="/wiki/Agribusiness" title="Agribusiness">agribusiness</a>, a farmer is defined broadly, and thus many individuals not necessarily engaged in full-time farming can nonetheless legally qualify under <a href="/wiki/Agricultural_policy" title="Agricultural policy">agricultural policy</a> for various <a href="/wiki/Subsidy" title="Subsidy">subsidies</a>, incentives, and <a href="/wiki/Tax_deduction" title="Tax deduction">tax deductions</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Woman-Farmer.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A farmer in <a href="/wiki/Chad" title="Chad">Chad</a>.</div></div></div>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Techniques">Techniques</span></h3>\n<p>In the context of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Developing_nation" title="Developing nation">developing nations</a> or other pre-industrial cultures, most farmers practice a meager <a href="/wiki/Subsistence_agriculture" title="Subsistence agriculture">subsistence agriculture</a>&#8212;a simple <a href="/wiki/Organic_farming" title="Organic farming">organic farming</a> system employing <a href="/wiki/Crop_rotation" title="Crop rotation">crop rotation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seed_saving" title="Seed saving">seed saving</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slash_and_burn" title="Slash and burn">slash and burn</a>, or other techniques to maximize efficiency while meeting the needs of the household or community. Historically, one subsisting in this way may have been known as a <i><a href="/wiki/Peasant" title="Peasant">peasant</a></i>.\n</p><p>In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Developed_nation" title="Developed nation">developed nations</a>, however, a person using such techniques on small patches of land might be called a <a href="/wiki/Gardener" title="Gardener">gardener</a> and be considered a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hobbyist" title="Hobbyist">hobbyist</a>. Alternatively, one might be driven into such practices by <a href="/wiki/Poverty" title="Poverty">poverty</a> or, ironically&#8212;against the background of large-scale agribusiness&#8212;might become an organic farmer growing for discerning consumers in the <a href="/wiki/Local_food" title="Local food">local food</a> market.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Farming_organizations">Farming organizations</span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:American_bee_journal_(1914)_(17930911149).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1426" data-file-width="1950" decoding="async" height="161" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/American_bee_journal_%281914%29_%2817930911149%29.jpg/220px-American_bee_journal_%281914%29_%2817930911149%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/American_bee_journal_%281914%29_%2817930911149%29.jpg/330px-American_bee_journal_%281914%29_%2817930911149%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/American_bee_journal_%281914%29_%2817930911149%29.jpg/440px-American_bee_journal_%281914%29_%2817930911149%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:American_bee_journal_(1914)_(17930911149).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Meeting of the Eastern Illinois Beekeepers Association, 1914.</div></div></div>\n<p>Farmers are often members of local, regional, or national farmers\' unions or agricultural producers\' organizations and can exert significant political influence. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_National_Grange_of_the_Order_of_Patrons_of_Husbandry" title="The National Grange of the Order of Patrons of Husbandry">Grange</a> movement in the United States was effective in advancing farmers\' agendas, especially against railroad and agribusiness interests early in the 20th century. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Nationale_des_Syndicats_d%27Exploitants_d%27Agricoles" title="F&#233;d&#233;ration Nationale des Syndicats d\'Exploitants d\'Agricoles">FNSEA</a> is very politically active in France, especially pertaining to <a href="/wiki/Genetically_modified_food" title="Genetically modified food">genetically modified food</a>. Agricultural producers, both small and large, are represented globally by the <a href="/wiki/International_Federation_of_Agricultural_Producers" title="International Federation of Agricultural Producers">International Federation of Agricultural Producers</a> (IFAP), representing over 600 million farmers through 120 national farmers\' unions in 79 countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Income">Income</span></h2>\n<p>Farmed products might be sold either to a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Market_(place)" title="Market (place)">market</a>, in a <a href="/wiki/Farmers%27_market" title="Farmers\' market">farmers\' market</a>, or directly from a farm. In a subsistence economy, farm products might to some extent be either consumed by the farmer\'s family or pooled by the community.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Occupational_hazards">Occupational hazards</span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Claas_Tucano_430_combine_harvester.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2672" data-file-width="4000" decoding="async" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Claas_Tucano_430_combine_harvester.jpg/220px-Claas_Tucano_430_combine_harvester.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Claas_Tucano_430_combine_harvester.jpg/330px-Claas_Tucano_430_combine_harvester.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Claas_Tucano_430_combine_harvester.jpg/440px-Claas_Tucano_430_combine_harvester.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Claas_Tucano_430_combine_harvester.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A <a href="/wiki/Combine_harvester" title="Combine harvester">combine harvester</a> on an English farm</div></div></div>\n<p>There are several occupational hazards for those in agriculture; farming is a particularly dangerous industry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Farmers can encounter and be stung or bitten by dangerous insects and other arthropods, including <a href="/wiki/Scorpion" title="Scorpion">scorpions</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fire_ants" title="Fire ants">fire ants</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bees" title="Bees">bees</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wasps" title="Wasps">wasps</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hornets" title="Hornets">hornets</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Farmers also work around heavy machinery which can kill or injure them.  Farmers can also establish muscle and joints pains from repeated work.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span></h2>\n<div aria-label="Portals" class="noprint portal plainlist tright" role="navigation" style="margin:0.5em 0 0.5em 1em;border:solid #aaa 1px">\n<ul style="display:table;box-sizing:border-box;padding:0.1em;max-width:175px;background:#f9f9f9;font-size:85%;line-height:110%;font-style:italic;font-weight:bold">\n<li style="display:table-row"><span style="display:table-cell;padding:0.2em;vertical-align:middle;text-align:center"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Veranotrigo.jpg"><img alt="icon" class="noviewer" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1600" decoding="async" height="24" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Veranotrigo.jpg/32px-Veranotrigo.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Veranotrigo.jpg/48px-Veranotrigo.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Veranotrigo.jpg/64px-Veranotrigo.jpg 2x" width="32"/></a></span><span style="display:table-cell;padding:0.2em 0.2em 0.2em 0.3em;vertical-align:middle"><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Portal:Agriculture_and_Agronomy" title="Portal:Agriculture and Agronomy">Agriculture and Agronomy portal</a></span></li></ul></div>\n<div class="div-col columns column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 18em;
