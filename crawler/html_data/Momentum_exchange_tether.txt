Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>\n<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>\n<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>\n<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>A <b>momentum exchange tether</b> is a kind of <a href="/wiki/Space_tether" title="Space tether">space tether</a> that could theoretically be used as a launch system, or to change spacecraft orbits.  Momentum exchange tethers create a controlled force on the end-masses of the system due to <a href="/wiki/Centrifugal_force" title="Centrifugal force">the pseudo-force known as centrifugal force</a>.  While the tether system rotates, the objects on either end of the tether will experience continuous acceleration; the magnitude of the acceleration depends on the length of the tether and the rotation rate. Momentum exchange occurs when an end body is released during the rotation.  The transfer of momentum to the released object will cause the rotating tether to lose energy, and thus lose velocity and altitude.  However, using <a href="/wiki/Electrodynamic_tether" title="Electrodynamic tether">electrodynamic tether</a> thrusting, or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ion_propulsion" title="Ion propulsion">ion propulsion</a> the system can then re-boost itself with little or no expenditure of consumable reaction mass.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p><p>A non-rotating tether is a rotating tether that rotates exactly once per orbit so that it always has a vertical orientation relative to the parent body.  A spacecraft arriving at the lower end of this tether, or departing from the upper end, will take momentum from the tether, while a spacecraft departing from the lower end of the tether, or arriving at the upper end, will add momentum to the tether. \n</p><p>In some cases momentum exchange systems are intended to run as balanced transportation schemes where an arriving spacecraft or payload is exchanged with one leaving with the same speed and mass, and then no net change in momentum or angular momentum occurs.\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc"><input class="toctogglecheckbox" systems">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tidal_stabilization">Tidal stabilization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Momentum_exchange_tether&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Tidal stabilization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Gravity-gradient_stabilization" title="Gravity-gradient stabilization">Gravity-gradient stabilization</a></div>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Skyhooks.gif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="480" data-file-width="640" decoding="async" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Skyhooks.gif/220px-Skyhooks.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Skyhooks.gif/330px-Skyhooks.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Skyhooks.gif/440px-Skyhooks.gif 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Skyhooks.gif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A rotating tether and a tidally stabilized tether in orbit</div></div></div>\n<p>Gravity-gradient stabilization, also called "gravity stabilization" and "tidal stabilization", is a simple and reliable method for controlling the attitude of a satellite that requires no electronic control systems, rocket motors or propellant.\n</p><p>This type of <a href="/wiki/Attitude_control" title="Attitude control">attitude control</a> tether has a small mass on one end, and a <a href="/wiki/Satellite" title="Satellite">satellite</a> on the other. <a href="/wiki/Tidal_force" title="Tidal force">Tidal forces</a> stretch the tether between the two masses. There are two ways of explaining tidal forces. In one, the upper end mass of the system is moving faster than orbital velocity for its altitude, so <a href="/wiki/Centrifugal_force" title="Centrifugal force">centrifugal force</a> makes it want to move further away from the planet it is orbiting. At the same time, the lower end mass of the system is moving at less than orbital speed for its altitude, so it wants to move closer to the planet.  The end result is that the tether is under constant tension and wants to hang in a vertical orientation. Simple satellites have often been stabilized this way; either with tethers, or with how the mass is distributed within the satellite.\n</p><p>As with any freely hanging object, it can be disturbed and start to swing.  Since there is no atmospheric drag in space to slow the swing, a small bottle of fluid with baffles may be mounted in the spacecraft to damp the pendulum vibrations via the viscous friction of the fluid.\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Sky-hook">Sky-hook</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Momentum_exchange_tether&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sky-hook">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Skyhook_(structure)" title="Skyhook (structure)">Skyhook (structure)</a></div>\n<p>A sky-hook is a theoretical class of orbiting <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tether_propulsion" title="Tether propulsion">tether propulsion</a> intended to lift payloads to high altitudes and speeds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Proposals for sky-hooks include designs that employ tethers spinning at hyper-sonic speed for catching high speed payloads or high altitude aircraft and placing them in orbit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Boeing.2000_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Boeing.2000-6">[6]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Electrodynamic_tethers">Electrodynamic tethers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Momentum_exchange_tether&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Electrodynamic tethers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Electrodynamic_tether" title="Electrodynamic tether">Electrodynamic tether</a></div>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:USP6116544-Circuit.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="581" data-file-width="565" decoding="async" height="226" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/USP6116544-Circuit.png/220px-USP6116544-Circuit.png" //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/USP6116544-Circuit.png/440px-USP6116544-Circuit.png 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:USP6116544-Circuit.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Electrons flow through the conductive structure of the tether to the power system interface, where it supplies power to an associated load, not shown. (Source: U.S. patent 6,116,544, "Electrodynamic Tether And Method of Use".)</div></div></div>\n<p>In a strong planetary <a href="/wiki/Magnetic_field" title="Magnetic field">magnetic field</a> such as around the Earth, a conducting tether can be configured as an <a href="/wiki/Electrodynamic_tether" title="Electrodynamic tether">electrodynamic tether</a>. This can either be used as a <a href="/wiki/Dynamo" title="Dynamo">dynamo</a> to generate power for the satellite at the cost of slowing its orbital velocity, or it can be used to increase the orbital velocity of the satellite by putting power into the tether from the satellite\'s power system. Thus the tether can be used to either accelerate or to slow an <a href="/wiki/Orbit" title="Orbit">orbiting</a> spacecraft without using any rocket propellant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-handbook_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-handbook-7">[7]</a></sup>\n</p><p>When using this techniques with a rotating tether, the current through the tether must alternate in phase with the rotation rate of the tether in order to produce either a consistent slowing force or a consistent accelerating force.\n</p><p>Whether slowing or accelerating the satellite, the electrodynamic tether pushes against the planet\'s magnetic field, and thus the momentum gained or lost ultimately comes from the planet.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bolo">Bolo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Momentum_exchange_tether&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Bolo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>A Bolo, or rotating tether, is a tether that rotates more than once per orbit and whose endpoints have a significant tip speed (~ 1 &#8211; 3&#160;km/s).  The maximum speed of the endpoints is limited by the strength of the cable material and the safety factor it is designed for.\n</p><p>The purpose of the Bolo is to either speed up, or slow down, a spacecraft that docks with it without using any of the spacecraft\'s on-board propellant and to change the spacecraft\'s orbital flight path.  Effectively, the Bolo acts as a reusable upper stage for any spacecraft that docks with it.\n</p><p>The momentum imparted to the spacecraft by the Bolo is not free. In the same way that the Bolo changes the spacecraft\'s momentum and direction of travel, the Bolo\'s orbital <a href="/wiki/Momentum" title="Momentum">momentum</a> and <a href="/wiki/Angular_momentum" title="Angular momentum">rotational momentum</a> is also changed, and this costs <a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">energy</a> that must be replaced. The idea is that the replacement energy would come from a more efficient and lower cost source than a chemical rocket motor.  Two possible lower cost sources for this replacement energy are an ion propulsion system, or an electrodynamic tether propulsion system that would be part of the Bolo. An essentially free source of replacement energy is momentum gathered from payloads to be accelerated in the other direction, suggesting that the need for adding energy from propulsion systems will be quite minimal with balanced, two-way, space commerce.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rotovator">Rotovator</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Momentum_exchange_tether&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Rotovator">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Cycloid_f.gif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="200" data-file-width="400" decoding="async" height="110" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Cycloid_f.gif/220px-Cycloid_f.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Cycloid_f.gif/330px-Cycloid_f.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/69/Cycloid_f.gif 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Cycloid_f.gif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>If the orbital velocity and the tether rotation rate are synchronized, in the rotovator concept the tether tip moves in a cycloid, and at the lowest point is momentarily stationary with respect to the ground, where it can "hook" a payload and swing it into orbit.)</div></div></div>\n<p>Rotovators are rotating tethers with a rotational direction such that the lower endpoint of the tether is moving slower than the orbital velocity of the tether and the upper endpoint is moving faster.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The word is a <a href="/wiki/Portmanteau" title="Portmanteau">portmanteau</a> derived from the words <i><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/rotor" title="wikt:rotor">rotor</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Elevator" title="Elevator">elevator</a></i>.\n</p><p>If the tether is long enough and the rotation rate high enough, it is possible for the lower endpoint to completely cancel the orbital speed of the tether such that the lower endpoint is stationary with respect to the planetary surface that the tether is orbiting.  As described by Moravec,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nonsynch_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-nonsynch-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-moravec87_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-moravec87-10">[10]</a></sup> this is "a satellite that rotates like a wheel". The tip of the tether moves in approximately a <a href="/wiki/Cycloid" title="Cycloid">cycloid</a>, in which it is momentarily stationary with respect to the ground. In this case, a payload that is "grabbed" by a capture mechanism on the rotating tether during the moment when it is stationary would be picked up and lifted into orbit; and potentially could be released at the top of the rotation, at which point it is moving with a speed significantly greater than the escape velocity and thus could be released onto an interplanetary trajectory. (As with the bolo, discussed above, the momentum and energy given to the payload must be made up, either with a high-efficiency rocket engine, or with momentum gathered from payload moving the other direction.)\n</p><p>On bodies with an atmosphere, such as the Earth, the tether tip must stay above the dense atmosphere. On bodies with reasonably low orbital speed (such as the <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">Moon</a> and possibly <a href="/wiki/Mars" title="Mars">Mars</a>), a rotovator in low orbit can potentially touch the ground, thereby providing cheap surface transport as well as launching materials into <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cislunar_space" title="Cislunar space">cislunar space</a>. In January 2000, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Boeing_Company" title="The Boeing Company">The Boeing Company</a> completed a study of tether launch systems including <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Two-stage_tethers&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Two-stage tethers (page does not exist)">two-stage tethers</a> that had been commissioned by the <a href="/wiki/NASA_Institute_for_Advanced_Concepts" title="NASA Institute for Advanced Concepts">NASA Institute for Advanced Concepts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Boeing.2000_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Boeing.2000-6">[6]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div style="clear:both;"></div>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Earth_launch_assist_bolo">Earth launch assist bolo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Momentum_exchange_tether&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Earth launch assist bolo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Unfortunately an Earth-to-orbit rotovator cannot be built from currently available materials since the thickness and tether mass to handle the loads on the rotovator would be uneconomically large. A "watered down" rotovator with two-thirds the rotational speed, however, would halve the centripetal acceleration stresses.\n</p><p>Therefore, another trick to achieve lower stresses is that rather than picking up a cargo from the ground at zero velocity, a rotovator could pick up a moving vehicle and sling it into orbit. For example, a rotovator could pick up a <a href="/wiki/Mach_number" title="Mach number">Mach 12</a> aircraft from the upper atmosphere of the Earth and move it into orbit without using rockets, and could likewise catch such a vehicle and lower it into atmospheric flight. It is easier for a rocket to achieve the lower tip speed, so "single stage to tether" has been proposed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> One such is called the Hyper-sonic Airplane Space Tether Orbital Launch (HASTOL).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Boeing.2000_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-Boeing.2000-6">[6]</a></sup> Either air breathing or rocket to tether could save a great deal of fuel per flight, and would permit for both a simpler vehicle and more cargo.\n</p><p>The company <a href="/wiki/Tethers_Unlimited,_Inc." title="Tethers Unlimited, Inc.">Tethers Unlimited, Inc.</a> (founded by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robert_Forward" title="Robert Forward">Robert Forward</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robert_P._Hoyt" title="Robert P. Hoyt">Robert P. Hoyt</a>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> has called this approach "Tether Launch Assist".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> It has also been referred to as a <b>space bolas</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Space_elevator">Space elevator</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Momentum_exchange_tether&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Space elevator">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Space_elevator" title="Space elevator">Space elevator</a></div>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Mature_non-rotating_Skyhook.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="715" data-file-width="536" decoding="async" height="227" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Mature_non-rotating_Skyhook.png/170px-Mature_non-rotating_Skyhook.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Mature_non-rotating_Skyhook.png/255px-Mature_non-rotating_Skyhook.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Mature_non-rotating_Skyhook.png/340px-Mature_non-rotating_Skyhook.png 2x" width="170"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Mature_non-rotating_Skyhook.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Non-rotating Sky-hook first proposed by E. Sarmont in 1990</div></div></div>\n<p>A <a href="/wiki/Space_elevator" title="Space elevator">space elevator</a> is a space tether that is attached to a planetary body. For example, on <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>, a space elevator would go from the equator to well above geosynchronous orbit.\n</p><p>A space elevator does not need to be powered as a rotovator does, because it gets any required <a href="/wiki/Angular_momentum" title="Angular momentum">angular momentum</a> from the planetary body. The disadvantage is that it is much longer, and for many planets a space elevator cannot be constructed from known materials. A space elevator on Earth would require material strengths outside current technological limits (2014).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-io9.why_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-io9.why-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pop.15185070_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-pop.15185070-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Huff.3353697_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Huff.3353697-17">[17]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Planet_Mars" title="Planet Mars">Martian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lunar_space_elevator" title="Lunar space elevator">lunar space elevators</a> could be built with modern-day materials however.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> A space elevator on Phobos has also been proposed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Space elevators also have larger amounts of potential energy than a rotovator, and if heavy parts (like a "dropped wrench") should fall they would reenter at a steep angle and impact the surface at near orbital speeds. On most anticipated designs, if the cable component itself fell, it would burn up before hitting the ground.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cislunar_transportation_system">Cislunar transportation system</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Momentum_exchange_tether&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Cislunar transportation system">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Cislunar_potential.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="566" data-file-width="1028" decoding="async" height="121" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/47/Cislunar_potential.png/220px-Cislunar_potential.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/47/Cislunar_potential.png/330px-Cislunar_potential.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/47/Cislunar_potential.png/440px-Cislunar_potential.png 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Cislunar_potential.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Potential energy in the Earth&#8211;Moon system. Because the Moon has higher potential energy, tethers can work together to pick an object off the Moon (the tiny dimple on the right), and place it closer to the Earth in LEO, taking essentially no propellant and even generating energy while doing so.</div></div></div>\n<p>Although it might be thought that this requires constant energy input, it can in fact be shown to be energetically favorable to lift cargo off the surface of the <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">Moon</a> and drop it into a lower Earth orbit, and thus it can be achieved without any significant use of propellant, since the Moon\'s surface is in a comparatively higher potential energy state. Also, this system could be built with a total mass of less than 28 times the mass of the payloads.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lunarforward_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-lunarforward-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Rotovators can thus be charged by <a href="/wiki/Momentum" title="Momentum">momentum</a> exchange. Momentum charging uses the rotovator to move mass from a place that is "higher" in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gravity_field" title="Gravity field">gravity field</a> to a place that is "lower". The technique to do this uses the <a href="/wiki/Oberth_effect" title="Oberth effect">Oberth effect</a>, where releasing the payload when the tether is moving with higher linear speed, lower in a gravitational potential gives more <a href="/wiki/Specific_energy" title="Specific energy">specific energy</a>, and ultimately more speed than the energy lost picking up the payload at a higher gravitational potential, even if the rotation rate is the same. For example, it is possible to use a system of two or three rotovators to implement trade between the <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">Moon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>. The rotovators are charged by lunar mass (dirt, if exports are not available) dumped on or near the Earth, and can use the momentum so gained to boost Earth goods to the Moon. The momentum and energy exchange can be balanced with equal flows in either direction, or can increase over time.\n</p><p>Similar systems of rotovators could theoretically open up inexpensive transportation throughout the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_system" title="Solar system">solar system</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tether_cable_catapult_system">Tether cable catapult system</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Momentum_exchange_tether&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Tether cable catapult system">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>A tether cable catapult system is a system where two or more long conducting tethers are held rigidly in a straight line, attached to a heavy mass. Power is applied to the tethers and is picked up by a vehicle that has linear magnet motors on it, which it uses to push itself along the length of the cable. Near the end of the cable the vehicle releases a payload and slows and stops itself and the payload carries on at very high velocity. The calculated maximum speed for this system is extremely high, more than 30 times the speed of sound in the cable; and velocities of more than 30&#160;km/s seem to be possible.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div style="clear:both;"></div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Momentum_exchange_tether&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em; -webkit-column-width: 30em; column-width: 30em; list-style-type: decimal;">\n<ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">H.
