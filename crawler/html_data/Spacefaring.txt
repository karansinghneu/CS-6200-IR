It involves a knowledge of a variety of topics and development of specialised skills including: <a href="/wiki/Aeronautics" title="Aeronautics">aeronautics</a>; <a href="/wiki/Astronautics" title="Astronautics">astronautics</a>; programs to train <a href="/wiki/Astronaut" title="Astronaut">astronauts</a>; <a href="/wiki/Space_weather" title="Space weather">space weather</a> and forecasting; ship-handling and small craft handling; operation of various equipment; <a href="/wiki/Spacecraft" title="Spacecraft">spacecraft</a> design and construction; atmospheric takeoff and reentry; <a href="/wiki/Orbital_mechanics" title="Orbital mechanics">orbital mechanics</a> (a.k.a. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Astrodynamics" title="Astrodynamics">astrodynamics</a>); communications; engines and rockets; execution of evolutions such as towing, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Micro-gravity" title="Micro-gravity">micro-gravity</a> construction, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space_docking" title="Space docking">space docking</a>; cargo handling equipment, dangerous cargoes and cargo storage; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spacewalk" title="Spacewalk">spacewalking</a>; dealing with emergencies; <a href="/wiki/Space_and_survival" title="Space and survival">survival at space</a> and first aid; fire fighting; <a href="/wiki/Life_support" title="Life support">life support</a>. The degree of knowledge needed within these areas is dependent upon the nature of the work and the type of vessel employed. "Spacefaring" is analogous to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seafaring" title="Seafaring">seafaring</a>.\n</p><p>Until now, there has never been a crewed mission outside the <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>&#8211;<a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">Moon</a> system. However, the United States, Russia, China, <a href="/wiki/European_Space_Agency" title="European Space Agency">European Space Agency</a> countries, and a few corporations and enterprises have plans in various stages to travel to <a href="/wiki/Mars" title="Mars">Mars</a> (see <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manned_mission_to_Mars" title="Manned mission to Mars">Manned mission to Mars</a>).\n</p><p>Spacefaring entities can be both <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_state" title="Sovereign state">sovereign states</a> and private <a href="/wiki/Corporation" title="Corporation">corporations</a>. Spacefaring nations are those capable of independently building and launching craft into space.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> A growing number of private entities have become or are becoming space faring.\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc"><input class="toctogglecheckbox" id="toctogglecheckbox" role="button" style="display:none" type="checkbox"/><div class="toctitle" dir="ltr" href="/wiki/Suborbital" title="Suborbital">suborbital spacecraft</a></i>, and could also, in a loose sense, be considered spacefaring.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>  If one considers merely launching an object beyond the <a href="/wiki/K%C3%A1rm%C3%A1n_line" title="K&#225;rm&#225;n line">K&#225;rm&#225;n line</a> to be the minimum requirement of spacefaring, then <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, with the <a href="/wiki/V-2_rocket" title="V-2 rocket">V-2 rocket</a>, became the first spacefaring nation in 1942.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Also several countries, such as Canada, Italy and Australia, had semi-independent spacefaring capability, launching locally-built satellites on foreign launchers.   Canada had designed and built satellites (Alouette 1 &amp; 2) in 1962 &amp; 1965 which were orbited using US launch vehicles.   Italy has designed and built several satellites, as well as pressurized (crewed) modules for the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a>. \nEarly Italian satellites were launched using vehicles provided by NASA, first from <a href="/wiki/Wallops_Flight_Facility" title="Wallops Flight Facility">Wallops Flight Facility</a> in 1964 and then from a spaceport in Kenya (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Marco_Platform" title="San Marco Platform">San Marco Platform</a>) between 1967 and 1988;<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> \nItaly has led the development of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vega_(launcher)" title="Vega (launcher)">Vega</a> rocket programme within the European Space Agency since 1998.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ESA_Vega_Programme_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-ESA_Vega_Programme-10">[10]</a></sup>\nThe <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> abandoned its independent space launch programme in 1972 in favour of co-operating with the European Launcher Development Organisation (ELDO) on launch technologies until 1974. Australia abandoned its launcher programme shortly after the successful launch of <a href="/wiki/WRESAT" title="WRESAT">WRESAT</a>, and became the only non-European member of ELDO.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Non-governmental_suborbital_flights">Non-governmental suborbital flights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spacefaring&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Non-governmental suborbital flights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>A number of private organizations are developing crewed spacefaring missions, though still largely suborbital. <a href="/wiki/Mike_Melvill" title="Mike Melvill">Mike Melvill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brian_Binnie" title="Brian Binnie">Brian Binnie</a> of <a href="/wiki/Mojave_Aerospace_Ventures" title="Mojave Aerospace Ventures">Mojave Aerospace Ventures</a> are the first such private individuals to achieve suborbital space travel status. A range of organizations including <a href="/wiki/UP_Aerospace" title="UP Aerospace">UP Aerospace</a> are also planning private launches, while others such as <a href="/wiki/SpaceX" title="SpaceX">SpaceX</a> plan eventually to launch crewed orbital missions.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="General_space_civilization">General space civilization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spacefaring&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: General space civilization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Generally, a planet-based civilization might have varying levels of difficulty in reaching space. Both the planet\'s <a href="/wiki/Gravity" title="Gravity">gravity</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Celestial_body_atmosphere" title="Celestial body atmosphere">atmosphere</a> influence the ease of <a href="/wiki/Space_exploration" title="Space exploration">space exploration</a>. Gravity on a more massive planet would generally make it more difficult to launch a <a href="/wiki/Spacecraft" title="Spacecraft">spacecraft</a> into <a href="/wiki/Orbital_spaceflight" title="Orbital spaceflight">orbit</a>. The effects of atmospheric <a href="/wiki/Friction" title="Friction">friction</a> on the craft must also be taken into consideration, as well as the amount of resources, including exotic materials possibly needed for construction of a spacecraft. Generally, only a species or civilization capable of interplanetary and/or interstellar flight is considered<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. (August 2013)">by whom?</span></a></i>]</sup> "truly" spacefaring. In fiction, such civilizations often possess colonies, either in their home system or possibly other <a href="/wiki/Planetary_system" title="Planetary system">planetary systems</a>.  As of present-day technology, humanity would not be considered<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. (August 2013)">by whom?</span></a></i>]</sup> a space faring civilization because interplanetary travel, even within the <a href="/wiki/Solar_System" title="Solar System">Solar System</a>, has not yet been achieved by an actual living human.  Many probes and non-human relays have been dispatched throughout the system and in outlying space however.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Spacefaring_in_popular_culture">Spacefaring in popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spacefaring&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Spacefaring in popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">Science fiction</a> often deals with more advanced spacefaring cultures, and the opportunities and challenges that this might bring to such societies.\n</p><p>One example is <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek" title="Star Trek">Star Trek</a></i>, where humanity, though already spacefaring, is still struggling to adapt to interstellar life, and in many cases, politics.\n</p><p>An additional example would follow the premise of science fiction author <a href="/wiki/Larry_Niven" title="Larry Niven">Larry Niven</a>. In his short story, <i>The Fourth Profession</i> Mr. Niven postulates the arrival of a spacefaring culture of traders that depend in part on the technical sophistication of the civilizations being visited to build the requisite <a href="/wiki/Laser_propulsion" title="Laser propulsion">launch lasers</a> to accelerate their mothership to the next star system.  Cultures lacking resources to achieve crewed spaceflight are ignored as animals.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spacefaring&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Kardashev_scale" title="Kardashev scale">Kardashev scale</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Space_advocacy" title="Space advocacy">Space advocacy</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Space_colonization" title="Space colonization">Space colonization</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Space_and_survival" title="Space and survival">Space and survival</a></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spacefaring&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><a class="external text" href="http://dictionary.reference.com/search?q=spacefaring" rel="nofollow">spacefaring - Definitions from Dictionary.com</a></span>\n</li>\n<li id="cite_note-2"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-2">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><a class="external text" href="http://www.bartleby.com/61/70/S0597000.html" rel="nofollow">spacefaring.
