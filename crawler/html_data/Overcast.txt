href="/wiki/File:Overcast_Mehamn.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A completely overcast sky in <a href="/wiki/Mehamn" title="Mehamn">Mehamn</a>, Norway</div></div></div>\n<p><b>Overcast</b> or <b>overcast weather</b>, as defined by the <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">World Meteorological Organization</a>, is the <a href="/wiki/Meteorology" title="Meteorology">meteorological</a> condition of <a href="/wiki/Cloud" title="Cloud">clouds</a> obscuring at least 95% of the sky.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wmo_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-wmo-1">[1]</a></sup> However, the total <a href="/wiki/Cloud_cover" title="Cloud cover">cloud cover</a> must not be entirely due to obscuring phenomena near the surface, such as <a href="/wiki/Fog" title="Fog">fog</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMS_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-AMS-2">[2]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Overcast, written as "OVC" in the <a href="/wiki/METAR" title="METAR">METAR</a> observation, is reported when the cloud cover is observed to equal eight <a href="/wiki/Okta" title="Okta">oktas</a> (eighths).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wmo_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-wmo-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wmo2_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-wmo2-3">[3]</a></sup> An overcast sky may be explicitly identified as thin (mostly <a href="/wiki/Transparency_and_translucency" title="Transparency and translucency">transparent</a>), but otherwise is considered <a href="/wiki/Opacity_(optics)" title="Opacity (optics)">opaque</a>, which always constitutes a <a href="/wiki/Ceiling_(cloud)" title="Ceiling (cloud)">ceiling</a> in aviation meteorology.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMS_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-AMS-2">[2]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Sometimes clouds can be different colors such as black or white, but overcast usually refers to darker skies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In some cases, it can be impossible to see distinct borders of clouds or the sky may be covered by a single type of cloud, such as <a href="/wiki/Stratus_cloud" title="Stratus cloud">stratus</a> and the whole sky will be a dull white.\n</p><p>Periods of overcast weather can range from a few hours to several days. Overcast weather can also affect people suffering from <a href="/wiki/Seasonal_affective_disorder" title="Seasonal affective disorder">seasonal affective disorder</a>. \n</p><p>The same weather, when observed from above, might be referred to as (an) <b>undercast</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMS2_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-AMS2-5">[5]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline"
