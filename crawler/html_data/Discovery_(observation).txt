With reference to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sciences" title="Sciences">sciences</a> and <a href="/wiki/Discipline_(academia)" title="Discipline (academia)">academic disciplines</a>, discovery is the <a href="/wiki/Observation" title="Observation">observation</a> of new phenomena, new actions, or new events and providing new reasoning to explain the knowledge gathered through such observations with previously acquired knowledge from abstract thought and everyday experiences.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbcHiggs121211_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbcHiggs121211-1">[1]</a></sup> A discovery may sometimes be based on earlier discoveries, collaborations, or ideas. Some discoveries represent a radical breakthrough in knowledge or technology.\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc"><input class="toctogglecheckbox" id="toctogglecheckbox" role="button" class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Discovery_(observation)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>New discoveries are acquired through various <a href="/wiki/Sense" title="Sense">senses</a> and are usually assimilated, merging with pre-existing <a href="/wiki/Knowledge" title="Knowledge">knowledge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Action_(philosophy)" title="Action (philosophy)">actions</a>. Questioning is a major form of human thought and interpersonal communication, and plays a key role in discovery. Discoveries are often made due to <a href="/wiki/Question" title="Question">questions</a>. Some discoveries lead to the <a href="/wiki/Invention" title="Invention">invention</a> of objects, processes, or techniques. A discovery may sometimes be based on earlier discoveries, collaborations or ideas, and the process of discovery requires at least the awareness that an existing concept or method can be modified or transformed. However, some discoveries also represent a radical breakthrough in knowledge.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Within_science">Within science</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Discovery_(observation)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Within science">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Within scientific disciplines, discovery is the observation of new <a href="/wiki/Phenomenon" title="Phenomenon">phenomena</a>, actions, or events which helps explain knowledge gathered through previously acquired <a href="/wiki/Scientific_evidence" title="Scientific evidence">scientific evidence</a>. In science, exploration is one of three purposes of research, the other two being description and explanation. Discovery is made by providing observational evidence and attempts to develop an initial, rough understanding of some phenomenon.\n</p><p>Discovery within the field of <a href="/wiki/Particle_physics" title="Particle physics">particle physics</a> has an accepted definition for what constitutes a discovery:  a <a href="/wiki/Standard_deviation#Table" title="Standard deviation">five-sigma</a> level of certainty.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbcHiggs121211_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-bbcHiggs121211-1">[1]</a></sup> Such a level defines <a href="/wiki/Statistic" title="Statistic">statistically</a> how unlikely it is that an <a href="/wiki/Experiment" title="Experiment">experimental</a> result is due to <a href="/wiki/Randomness" title="Randomness">chance</a>. The combination of a five-sigma level of certainty, and independent confirmation by other experiments, turns findings into accepted discoveries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbcHiggs121211_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-bbcHiggs121211-1">[1]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Within_education">Within education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Discovery_(observation)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Within education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<table class="box-Original_research plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Original_research" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><img alt="" data-file-height="40" data-file-width="40" decoding="async" height="40" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/b4/Ambox_important.svg/40px-Ambox_important.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/b4/Ambox_important.svg/60px-Ambox_important.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/b4/Ambox_important.svg/80px-Ambox_important.svg.png 2x" width="40"/></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section  <b>possibly contains <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research" title="Wikipedia:No original research">original research</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Discovery_(observation)&amp;action=edit">improve it</a> by <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">verifying</a> the claims made and adding <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources#Inline_citations" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">inline citations</a>. Statements consisting only of original research should be removed.</span> <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">December 2018</span>)</i></small><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>Within the field of education, discovery occurs through observations. These observations are common and come in various forms. Observations can occur as observations of students done by the teacher or observations of teachers done by other professionals. Student observations help teachers to identify where the students are developmentally and cognitively in the realm of their studies. Teacher observations are used by administrators to hold teachers accountable as they stay on target with their learning goals and treat the students with respect. From these different types of observations we discover the best possible education practices.\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Observations_of_students_completed_by_teachers">Observations of students completed by teachers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Discovery_(observation)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Observations of students completed by teachers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>Teachers observe students throughout the day in the classroom. These observations can be informal or formal. Teachers often use checklist, anecdotal notes, videos, interviews, written work or assessment, etc. In completing these observations, teachers can determine at which level the student is understanding the lessons. Using the information from observations, then allows teachers to make the necessary adaptations for the students in the classroom. These observations can also provide the foundation for strong relationships between teachers and students. When students have these relationships they feel safer and more comfortable in the classroom and are more willing and eager to learn.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Through observations teachers discover the most developmentally appropriate practices to implement in their classrooms. These discoveries encourage and promote healthier learning styles and positive classroom atmospheres.\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Observations_of_teachers_completed_by_other_professionals">Observations of teachers completed by other professionals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Discovery_(observation)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Observations of teachers completed by other professionals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>Within the education system, there are a set of standards put in place by government officials. Teachers are responsible for following these academic standards as a guideline for developmentally appropriate instruction. Within following those academic goals, teachers are also observed by administrators to ensure positive classroom environments. One of the tools that teachers could be graded with is called the  nationally recognized CLASS tool. After using this tool "over 150 research studies prove that students in classrooms with high CLASS scores have better academic and social outcomes<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>" (Touchstone Training, LLC., 2018). The tool itself is known for encouraging positive classroom environments, regard for the students perspectives, behavior management skills, quality of feedback, and language modeling. The administrators rate each of the ten categories with the number scale of one to seven. One being the lowest score and seven being the highest score that the teacher may receive. It is through tools such as this one that administrators are able to hold their teachers to high standards and ensure the best educational practices for the students.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Exploration">Exploration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Discovery_(observation)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Exploration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Discovery can also be used to describe the first incursions of peoples from one culture into the geographical and cultural environment of others. <a href="/wiki/Western_culture" title="Western culture">Western culture</a> has used the term "discovery" in their histories to subtly emphasize the importance of "<a href="/wiki/Exploration" title="Exploration">exploration</a>" in the history of the world, such as in the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Age_of_Exploration" title="Age of Exploration">Age of Exploration</a>".\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span
