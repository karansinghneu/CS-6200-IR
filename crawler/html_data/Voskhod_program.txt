Two one-day manned missions were flown using the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Voskhod_spacecraft" title="Voskhod spacecraft">Voskhod spacecraft</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Voskhod_rocket" title="Voskhod rocket">rocket</a>, one in 1964 and one in 1965, and two dogs flew on a 22-day mission in 1966.\n</p><p>Voskhod development was both a follow-on to the <a href="/wiki/Vostok_programme" title="Vostok programme">Vostok programme</a> and a recycling of components left over from that programme\'s cancellation following its first six flights. The Voskhod programme was superseded by the <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_programme" title="Soyuz programme">Soyuz programme</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc"><input class="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Voskhod_programme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Voskhod spacecraft was basically a Vostok spacecraft that had a backup, solid-fueled retrorocket added to the top of the descent module. As it was much heavier, the launch vehicle would be the 11A57, a Molniya 8K78M with the Blok L stage removed and later the basis of the Soyuz booster. The ejection seat was removed and two or three crew couches were added to the interior at a 90-degree angle to that of the Vostok crew position. However, the position of the in-flight controls was not changed, so the crew had to crane their heads 90 degrees to see the instruments.\n</p><p>In the case of Voskhod 2, an inflatable exterior airlock was also added to the descent module opposite the entry hatch.  The airlock was jettisoned after use.  This apparatus was needed because the vehicle avionics and environmental systems were air-cooled, and depressurization in orbit would cause overheating. A solid-fueled <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Braking_rocket" title="Braking rocket">braking rocket</a> was also added to the parachute lines to provide for a softer landing at touchdown. This was necessary because, unlike the Vostok, the Voskhod descent module landed with the crewmen still inside.\n</p><p>Unlike Vostok and the later Soyuz, Voskhod had no launch abort system, meaning that the crew lacked any means of escape from a malfunctioning launch vehicle.\n</p><p>Voskhod had a solid-fueled backup retrorocket on top of the capsule in case the main one failed (as it did on <a href="/wiki/Voskhod_2" title="Voskhod 2">Voskhod 2</a>). While Vostok lacked this feature, it was not considered a problem since the spacecraft would decay from orbit within 10 days. Relatively lightweight Voskhod was well below the 11A57 booster\'s lift capacity, meaning that it launched into a much higher orbit and would not decay as quickly.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Flights">Flights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Voskhod_programme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Flights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Voskhod flights, with launch dates:\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Unmanned">Unmanned</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Voskhod_programme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Unmanned">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Kosmos_47" title="Kosmos 47">Kosmos 47</a></b> &#8211; 15-day two-man mission with military and other experiments and multiple spacewalks to test new EVA jet belt <a class="external autonumber" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20100317222320/http://www.astronautix.com/flights/voskhod6.htm" rel="nofollow">[4]</a></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Results">Results</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Voskhod_programme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Results">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>While the Vostok programme was dedicated more toward understanding the effects of space travel and microgravity on the human body, Voskhod\'s two flights were more aimed towards spectacular firsts. Although achieving the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extra-vehicular_activity" title="Extra-vehicular activity">EVA</a> ("spacewalk") became the main success of the programme, beating the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">U.S.</a> <a href="/wiki/Project_Gemini" title="Project Gemini">Project Gemini</a> to put the first multiman crew in orbit was the objective that initially motivated the programme. After those goals were realized, the programme planned to focus on other advances the spacecraft could accomplish, such as longer duration and a second female flight. However, there were delays preparing for Voskhod 3, and during that time the Gemini programme accomplished most of what had been planned for future Voskhods.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In the end, the Voskhod programme was abandoned, aided by a change in Soviet leadership which was less concerned about stunt and prestige flights, and this allowed the Soviet designers to concentrate on the <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_programme" title="Soyuz programme">Soyuz programme</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Voskhod_programme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20100207151749/http://www.astronautix.com/flights/voskhod3.htm" rel="nofollow">"Archived copy"</a>.
