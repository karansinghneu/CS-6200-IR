For <a href="/wiki/Ludwig_von_Mises" title="Ludwig von Mises">Ludwig von Mises</a>\' work on this subject, see <a href="/wiki/Human_Action" title="Human Action">Human Action</a>.</div>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/Philosophy" title="Philosophy">philosophy</a>, an <b>action</b> is that which is done by an agent. In common speech, the term is often used interchangeably with the term "behaviour". However, in the <a href="/wiki/Action_theory_(philosophy)" title="Action theory (philosophy)">philosophy of action</a>, <a href="/wiki/Behavioural_sciences" title="Behavioural sciences">behavioural sciences</a>, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Social_sciences" title="Social sciences">social sciences</a>, a distinction is made: behavior is automatic and reflexive activity, while action is an intentional, purposive, conscious and subjectively meaningful activity<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. Thus, things like running or throwing a ball is an instance of action; it involves intention, a goal, and a bodily movement guided by the agent. On the other hand, catching a cold is not considered an action because it is something which happens <i>to</i> a person, not something done <i>by</i> one.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. That is, we carry out actions as an essential part of perceiving the world. Alva No&#235; states:\n</p>\n<blockquote><p>We move our eyes, head and body in taking in what is around us... [we] crane our necks, peer, squint, reach for our glasses or draw near to get a better look...\'&#8230;\'Perception is a mode of  activity on the part of the whole animal...It cannot be represented in terms of merely passive, and internal, processes...\' <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p></blockquote>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Movement">Movement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Action_(philosophy)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Movement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Some would prefer to define actions as requiring <a href="/wiki/Human_body" title="Human body">bodily</a> movement (see <a href="/wiki/Behaviorism" title="Behaviorism">behaviorism</a>).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unintended_consequence" title="Unintended consequence">side effects</a> of actions are considered by some to be part of the action; in an example from <a href="/wiki/G._E._M._Anscombe" title="G. E. M. Anscombe">Anscombe</a>\'s manuscript <i>Intention</i>, pumping water can also be an instance of poisoning the inhabitants.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> This introduces a <a href="/wiki/Moral" title="Moral">moral</a> dimension to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Discussion" title="Discussion">discussion</a> (see also <a href="/wiki/Moral_agency" title="Moral agency">Moral agency</a>).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> If the poisoned water resulted in a death, that death might be considered part of the action of the agent that pumped the water.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Whether a side effect is considered part of an action is especially unclear in cases in which the agent isn\'t aware of the possible side effects.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>  For example, an agent that accidentally cures a person by administering a poison he was intending to kill him with.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reasons_for_action">Reasons for action</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Action_(philosophy)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reasons for action">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>A primary concern of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philosophy_of_action" title="Philosophy of action">philosophy of action</a> is to analyze the nature of actions and distinguish them from similar phenomena. Other concerns include individuating actions, explaining the relationship between actions and their effects, explaining how an action is related to the beliefs and desires which cause and/or justify it (see <a href="/wiki/Practical_reason" title="Practical reason">practical reason</a>), as well as examining the nature of agency.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> A primary concern is the nature of free will and whether actions are determined by the mental states that precede them (see <a href="/wiki/Determinism" title="Determinism">determinism</a>). Some philosophers (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Donald_Davidson_(philosopher)" title="Donald Davidson (philosopher)">Donald Davidson</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>) have argued that the mental states the agent invokes as justifying his action are physical states that cause the action.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Problems have been raised for this view because the mental states seem to be reduced to mere physical causes.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Their mental properties don\'t seem to be doing any work.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> If the reasons an agent cites as justifying his action, however, are not the cause of the action, they must explain the action in some other way or be causally impotent.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
