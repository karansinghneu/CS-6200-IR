showing the NASA heliosphysics missions from near Earth orbit out to the orbit of the Moon.</div></div></div>\n<p>The term <b>heliophysics</b>  means "physics of the Sun"  (the prefix "<a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/helio-" title="wikt:helio-">helio</a>", from <a href="/wiki/Attic_Greek" title="Attic Greek">Attic Greek</a> <i>h&#7703;lios</i>, means Sun), and appears to have been used only in that sense until quite recently. In the early times, heliophysics was concerned principally with the superficial layers of the star, and was synonymous with what is now more commonly called "solar physics". Usage was extended explicitly in 1981 to its literal meaning, denoting the physics of the entire <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">Sun</a>: from center to <a href="/wiki/Corona" title="Corona">corona</a>, and has been used in that sense since.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gough_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gough-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gough2012_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gough2012-2">[2]</a></sup> As such it was a direct translation from the French <i>h&#233;liophysique</i>, which had been introduced to provide a distinction from <i>physique solaire</i> (<a href="/wiki/Solar_physics" title="Solar physics">solar physics</a>).  It thus became a subdiscipline of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heliology" title="Heliology">heliology</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Christensen-Dalsgarrd_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Christensen-Dalsgarrd-3">[3]</a></sup> Early in the 21st century the meaning of the term was extended by Dr George Siscoe of Boston University to include the physics of the <a href="/wiki/Heliosphere" title="Heliosphere">heliosphere</a> (the space around the Sun beyond the corona, in principle out to the shock where the solar wind encounters the interstellar medium, but excluding the planets and other condensed bodies), although Siscoe\'s view of the discipline appears not to contain most of the true realm of endeavour.  The term was adopted in Siscoe\'s restricted sense by the <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a> Science Mission Directorate to denote the study of the heliosphere and the objects that interact with it&#8212;most notably planetary <a href="/wiki/Atmosphere" title="Atmosphere">atmospheres</a> and <a href="/wiki/Magnetosphere" title="Magnetosphere">magnetospheres</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_corona" title="Solar corona">solar corona</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Interstellar_medium" title="Interstellar medium">interstellar medium</a>.  Heliophysics combines several other disciplines, including  <a href="/wiki/Solar_physics" title="Solar physics">solar physics</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stellar_physics" title="Stellar physics">stellar physics</a> in general, and also several branches of <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_physics" title="Nuclear physics">nuclear physics</a>,  <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Plasma_physics" title="Plasma physics">plasma physics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Space_physics" title="Space physics">space physics</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Magnetospheric_physics" title="Magnetospheric physics">magnetospheric physics</a>. Solar wind interaction with magnetized planets, Solar wind propagation, Solar activity effects on planetary magnetospheres. Solar magnetic field configuration from the Sun to the Heliopause. The recent extension of heliophysics is closely tied to the study of <a href="/wiki/Space_weather" title="Space weather">space weather</a> and the phenomena that affect it, and consequently to <a href="/wiki/Space_climate" title="Space climate">space climate</a> and to terrestrial <a href="/wiki/Climatology" title="Climatology">climatology</a>.  To quote Siscoe from a recent conference presentation:\n</p>\n<blockquote><p>Heliophysics [encompasses] environmental science, a unique hybrid between meteorology and astrophysics,  comprising a body of data and a set of paradigms (general laws&#8212;perhaps mostly still undiscovered) specific to magnetized plasmas and neutrals in the heliosphere interacting with themselves and with gravitating bodies and their atmospheres.</p></blockquote>\n<p>"Heliophysics" is now the name of one of four divisions within NASA\'s Science Mission Directorate (Earth Science, Planetary Science, Heliophysics, and Astrophysics).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NASA_SMD_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-NASA_SMD-4">[4]</a></sup>  The title was used to simplify the name of the "Sun--Solar-System Connections" Division (and before that, the "Sun-Earth Connections" Division).   \n</p><p>NASA\'s restricted use of the term heliophysics has also been adopted in naming the <a href="/wiki/International_Heliophysical_Year" title="International Heliophysical Year">International Heliophysical Year</a> in 2007-2008.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tnone" style="margin-left: auto;
