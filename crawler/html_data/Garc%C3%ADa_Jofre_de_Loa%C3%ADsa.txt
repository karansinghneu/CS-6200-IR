Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">&#160;&#160;(Redirected from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/w/index.php?title=Garc%C3%ADa_Jofre_de_Loa%C3%ADsa&amp;redirect=no" title="Garc&#237;a Jofre de Loa&#237;sa">Garc&#237;a Jofre de Loa&#237;sa</a>)</span></div>\n<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>\n<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>\n<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>Loa&#237;sa expedition</b> was a 16th-century voyage of discovery to the Pacific Ocean, commanded by Garcia Jofre de Loa&#237;sa (1490 &#8211; 20 July 1526) and ordered by King <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charles_I_of_Spain" title="Charles I of Spain">Charles I of Spain</a> to colonize the <a href="/wiki/Maluku_Islands" title="Maluku Islands">Spice Islands</a> in the <a href="/wiki/East_Indies" title="East Indies">East Indies</a>. The seven-ship fleet sailed from La Coru&#241;a (Spain) in July 1525 and became the second naval expedition to cross the Pacific Ocean in history, after <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Timeline_of_Magellan%27s_circumnavigation" title="Timeline of Magellan\'s circumnavigation">Magellan-Elcano\'s voyage</a>. The expedition resulted in the discovery of the <a href="/wiki/Drake_Passage" title="Drake Passage">Sea of Hoces</a>, south of Cape Horn and, the <a href="/wiki/Marshall_Islands" title="Marshall Islands">Marshall Islands</a> in the Pacific. One ship ultimately arrived in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spice_Islands" title="Spice Islands">Spice Islands</a> on New Year\'s Day of 1527.\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc"><input reading</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loa%C3%ADsa_expedition&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>De Loa&#237;sa\'s expedition was conceived both as a rescue mission and a voyage of discovery. The <i>Victoria</i>, a vessel from Magellan\'s expedition to the Pacific, had returned to Spain with word that her sister ship the <i>Trinidad</i> had last been seen attempting to return home by sailing east from the Spice Islands to South America. De Loa&#237;sa was ordered to seek <i>Trinidad</i>, or news of her fate, by voyaging along her expected return route to Spain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kelsey151_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kelsey151-1">[1]</a></sup> Failing this he was to locate and colonise Magellan\'s Spice Islands, and also  to bring back news of the semi-mythical land of <a href="/wiki/Ophir" title="Ophir">Ophir</a> which Spanish scholars believed may be somewhere near China. (Most possibly the Philippines)-<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kelsey151_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Kelsey151-1">[1]</a></sup> For this purpose, de Loa&#237;sa was assigned seven vessels and a total of 450 men including tradesmen and administrators for the Spice Islands settlement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Atlantic_voyage">Atlantic voyage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loa%C3%ADsa_expedition&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Atlantic voyage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Loaisas_journey.PNG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="150" data-file-width="300" decoding="async" height="150" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/f6/Loaisas_journey.PNG" width="300"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Loaisas_journey.PNG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Loa&#237;sa&#8217;s expedition</div></div></div>\n<p>The expedition set sail from <a href="/wiki/A_Coru%C3%B1a" title="A Coru&#241;a">Corunna</a> on July 24, 1525. It consisted of seven ships, <i>Santa Mar&#237;a de la Victoria</i>, <i>Sancti Spiritus</i>, <i>Anunciada</i>, <i>San Gabriel</i>, <i>Santa Mar&#237;a del Parral</i> and <i>San Lesmes</i> and a <a href="/wiki/Patache" title="Patache">patache</a>, <i>Santiago</i>. De Loa&#237;sa was named captain along with <a href="/wiki/Juan_Sebasti%C3%A1n_Elcano" title="Juan Sebasti&#225;n Elcano">Juan Sebasti&#225;n Elcano</a>, who had reached the Spice Islands in 1521 during the <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_Magellan" title="Ferdinand Magellan">Magellan</a> expedition.\n</p><p>The fleet headed southwest to the <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a> and then south along the <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">African</a> coastline.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nowell329_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nowell329-3">[3]</a></sup> In November 1525 de Loa&#237;sa moved west across the Atlantic to <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, reaching the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Patagonian" title="Patagonian">Patagonian</a> shore in January 1526.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nowell329_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Nowell329-3">[3]</a></sup> There was no sign of <i>Trinidad</i>, and de Loa&#237;sa decided to abandon the search for her and continue instead to the Spice Islands. However the weather was poor, and over the next several weeks, in high winds while trying to enter the <a href="/wiki/Strait_of_Magellan" title="Strait of Magellan">Strait of Magellan</a>, the ships alternately gathered and dispersed. Two ships, <i>Sancti Spiritus</i> and <i>Anunciada</i> were wrecked, and one, <i>San Gabriel</i> tacked into the Atlantic and deserted the expedition.\n</p><p>The <i>San Lesmes</i> under the captaincy of <a href="/wiki/Francisco_de_Hoces" title="Francisco de Hoces">Francisco de Hoces</a> was driven south along the coast, possibly to a latitude of 57&#176;, where the crew noted "an end of land" which may have been the first European sighting of <a href="/wiki/Cape_Horn" title="Cape Horn">Cape Horn</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Berguno_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Berguno-4">[4]</a></sup> After some difficulty Hoces was able to steer his galleon northward once more, rejoining the other three vessels that remained with the expedition. On 26 May 1526, this diminished fleet of four ships (three galleons and the patache), passed through the Strait and entered the Pacific.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Berguno_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Berguno-4">[4]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pacific_voyage">Pacific voyage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loa%C3%ADsa_expedition&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Pacific voyage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The bad weather which had originally scattered de Loa&#237;sa \'s fleet continued in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific" title="Pacific">Pacific</a>. The four remaining vessels quickly lost sight of each other in the heavy rain and were unable to regroup when the storm finally passed on 1 June.\n</p><p>The <i>Santiago</i> sailed for north, and in a 10,000-kilometre voyage, reached the Pacific coast of <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> in July 1526, achieving the first navigation from Europe to the western coast of <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>.\n</p><p><i>San Lesmes</i> disappeared entirely. Twentieth century speculation suggests she may have run aground in the <a href="/wiki/Marquesas_Islands" title="Marquesas Islands">Marquesas Islands</a>, either on the island of <a href="/wiki/Anaa" title="Anaa">Anaa</a> where a 1774 expedition found a cross erected on the beach, or off the <a href="/wiki/Amanu" title="Amanu">Amanu</a> atoll where an old Spanish cannon was later found.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Berguno_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Berguno-4">[4]</a></sup>\n</p><p>The third ship, <i>Santa Mar&#237;a del Parral</i>, sailed the Pacific to <a href="/wiki/Sangihe_Islands" title="Sangihe Islands">Sangir</a> off the northern coast of <a href="/wiki/Sulawesi" title="Sulawesi">Sulawesi</a>, where the ship was beached and its crew were variously killed or enslaved by the natives. Four survivors were rescued in 1528 by another Spanish expedition coming from Mexico.\n</p><p>The last galleon, <i>Santa Maria de la Victoria</i>, was the only ship to reach the Spice Islands, landing in September 1526.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Berguno_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-Berguno-4">[4]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Survivors">Survivors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loa%C3%ADsa_expedition&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Survivors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Loa&#237;sa himself died of <a href="/wiki/Scurvy" title="Scurvy">scurvy</a> on July 30, 1526, Elcano a few days later, and <a href="/wiki/Alonso_de_Salazar" title="Alonso de Salazar">Alonso de Salazar</a> three weeks after that. Y&#241;igez reached the islands of <a href="/wiki/Visayas" title="Visayas">Visayas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mindanao" title="Mindanao">Mindanao</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maluku_islands" title="Maluku islands">Moluccas</a>, but died of food poisoning. Only <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9s_de_Urdaneta" title="Andr&#233;s de Urdaneta">Andr&#233;s de Urdaneta</a> and 24 other men survived to land in the Spice Islands. They returned to Spain in 1536 in the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_India_Armadas" title="Portuguese India Armadas">Portuguese India Armada</a> and under Portuguese guard to complete second world <a href="/wiki/Circumnavigation" title="Circumnavigation">circumnavigation</a> in History.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loa%C3%ADsa_expedition&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The fate of the <i>San Lesmes</i> is explored in Greg Scowen\'s conspiracy thriller <i><a href="/wiki/The_Spanish_Helmet" title="The Spanish Helmet">The Spanish Helmet</a></i> which is based on <a href="/wiki/Robert_Adrian_Langdon" title="Robert Adrian Langdon">Robert Langdon\'s</a> theory that the lost vessel made its way to, and discovered, <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
