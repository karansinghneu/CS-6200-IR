id="bodyContent">\n<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>\n<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump navigation-not-searchable" role="note">For the exploration problem in recreational mathematics, see <a href="/wiki/Jeep_problem" title="Jeep problem">jeep problem</a>.</div>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/Robotics" title="Robotics">robotics</a>, the <b>exploration problem</b> deals with the use of a <a href="/wiki/Robot" title="Robot">robot</a> to maximize the <a href="/wiki/Knowledge" title="Knowledge">knowledge</a> over a particular area. The exploration problem arises in mapping and search &amp; rescue situations, where an environment might be dangerous or inaccessible to humans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Trun2005_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Trun2005-1">[1]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc"><input class="toctogglecheckbox" id="toctogglecheckbox" role="button" style="display:none" type="checkbox"/><div class="toctitle" dir="ltr" lang="en"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Overview"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Overview</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Information_gain"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Information gain</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#See_also"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">See also</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Exploration_problem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The exploration problem naturally arises in situations in which a robot is utilized to survey an area that is dangerous or inaccessible for humans. The field of robotic explorations draws from various fields of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Information_gathering" title="Information gathering">information gathering</a> and <a href="/wiki/Decision_theory" title="Decision theory">decision theory</a>, and have been studied as far back as the 1950s. \n</p><p>The earliest work in robotic exploration was done in the context of simple finite state automata known as bandits, where algorithms were designed to distinguish and map different states in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Finite_state_automaton" title="Finite state automaton">finite state automaton</a>. Since then, the primary emphasis has been shifted to the robotics system development domain, where exploration-algorithms guided robot have been used to survey volcanos,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bares1999_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bares1999-2">[2]</a></sup> search and rescue, and abandoned mines mapping.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Thrun2003_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Thrun2003-3">[3]</a></sup> Current state of the art system include advanced techniques on active localization, <a href="/wiki/Simultaneous_localization_and_mapping" title="Simultaneous localization and mapping">simultaneous localization and mapping</a> (SLAM) based exploration, and multi-agent cooperative exploration.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Information_gain">Information gain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Exploration_problem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Information gain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The key concept in the exploration problem is the notion of information gain, that is, the amount of knowledge acquired while pushing the frontiers.
