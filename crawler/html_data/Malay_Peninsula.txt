Station</a>.</div></div></div>\n<p>The <b>Malay Peninsula</b> (<a href="/wiki/Malay_language" title="Malay language">Malay</a>: <i>Semenanjung Tanah Melayu</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Peninsula" title="Peninsula">peninsula</a> in <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a>. The land mass runs approximately north-south and, at its terminus, is the southernmost point of the Asian mainland. The area contains <a href="/wiki/Peninsular_Malaysia" title="Peninsular Malaysia">Peninsular Malaysia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Southern_Thailand" title="Southern Thailand">Southern Thailand</a>, and the southernmost tip of <a href="/wiki/Myanmar" title="Myanmar">Myanmar</a> (<a href="/wiki/Kawthaung_District" title="Kawthaung District">Kawthaung</a>) as well as the city state <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, indigenous to or historically inhabited by the <a href="/wiki/Malays_(ethnic_group)" title="Malays (ethnic group)">Malays</a>, an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Austronesian_people" title="Austronesian people">Austronesian people</a>.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Titiwangsa_Mountains" title="Titiwangsa Mountains">Titiwangsa Mountains</a> are part of the <a href="/wiki/Tenasserim_Hills" title="Tenasserim Hills">Tenasserim Hills</a> system, and form the backbone of the Peninsula. They form the southernmost section of the central <a href="/wiki/Cordillera" title="Cordillera">cordillera</a> which runs from <a href="/wiki/Tibet" title="Tibet">Tibet</a> through the <a href="/wiki/Kra_Isthmus" title="Kra Isthmus">Kra Isthmus</a> (the Peninsula\'s narrowest point) into the Malay Peninsula.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Strait_of_Malacca" title="Strait of Malacca">Strait of Malacca</a> separates the Malay Peninsula from the Indonesian island of <a href="/wiki/Sumatra" title="Sumatra">Sumatra</a> while the south coast is separated from the island of <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Straits_of_Johor" title="Straits of Johor">Straits of Johor</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc"><input class="toctogglecheckbox" id="toctogglecheckbox" role="button" style="display:none" type="checkbox"/><div class="toctitle" dir="ltr" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malay_Peninsula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Malay term <i>Tanah Melayu</i> is derived from the word <i>Tanah</i> (land) and <i>Melayu</i> (<a href="/wiki/Malays_(ethnic_group)" title="Malays (ethnic group)">Malays</a>), thus it means "the Malay land". The term can be found in various Malay texts, of which the oldest dating back to the early 17th century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It is frequently mentioned in the <i><a href="/wiki/Hikayat_Hang_Tuah" title="Hikayat Hang Tuah">Hikayat Hang Tuah</a></i>, a well known classical work that began as oral tales associated with the legendary heroes of <a href="/wiki/Malacca_Sultanate" title="Malacca Sultanate">Malacca Sultanate</a>. <i>Tanah Melayu</i> in the text is consistently employed to refer to the area under Malaccan dominance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>\n</p><p>In the early 16th century, <a href="/wiki/Tom%C3%A9_Pires" title="Tom&#233; Pires">Tom&#233; Pires</a>, a Portuguese <a href="/wiki/Apothecary" title="Apothecary">apothecary</a> who stayed in <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Malacca" title="Portuguese Malacca">Malaca</a> from 1512 to 1515, writes an almost identical term, <i>Terra de Tana Malaio</i> which he referred to the southeastern part of Sumatra, where the deposed Sultan of Malacca, <a href="/wiki/Mahmud_Shah_of_Malacca" title="Mahmud Shah of Malacca">Mahmud Shah</a> established his exiled government. The 17th century\'s account of Portuguese historian, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emanuel_Godinho_de_Er%C3%A9dia" title="Emanuel Godinho de Er&#233;dia">Emanuel Godinho de Er&#233;dia</a>, noted on the region of <i>Malaios</i> surrounded by the <a href="/wiki/Andaman_Sea" title="Andaman Sea">Andaman Sea</a> in the north, the entire <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Malacca_Strait" title="Malacca Strait">Malacca Strait</a> in the centre, a part of <a href="/wiki/Sunda_Strait" title="Sunda Strait">Sunda Strait</a> in the south, and the western part of <a href="/wiki/South_China_Sea" title="South China Sea">South China Sea</a> in the east.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Prior to the foundation of Malacca, ancient and medieval references to a Malay peninsula exist in various foreign sources. According to several Indian scholars, the word <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Malayadvipa" title="Malayadvipa">Malayadvipa</a></i> ("mountain-insular continent"), mentioned in the ancient Indian text, <i><a href="/wiki/Vayu_Purana" title="Vayu Purana">Vayu Purana</a></i>, may possibly refer to the Malay peninsula.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Another Indian source, an inscription on the south wall of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brihadeeswarar_Temple" title="Brihadeeswarar Temple">Brihadeeswarar Temple</a>, recorded the word <i>Malaiur</i>, referring to a kingdom in Malay peninsula that had "a strong mountain for its rampart".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The Greek source, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geographia_(Ptolemy)" title="Geographia (Ptolemy)">Geographia</a></i>, written by <a href="/wiki/Ptolemy" title="Ptolemy">Ptolemy</a>, labelled a geographical part of <i><a href="/wiki/Golden_Chersonese" title="Golden Chersonese">Golden Chersonese</a></i> as <i>Maleu-kolon</i>, a term thought to derive from Sanskrit <i>malayakolam</i> or <i>malaikurram</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> While the Chinese chronicle of <a href="/wiki/Yuan_dynasty" title="Yuan dynasty">Yuan dynasty</a> mentioned the word <i>Ma-li-yu-er</i>, referring to a nation of Malay peninsula that threatened by the southward expansion of <a href="/wiki/Sukhothai_Kingdom" title="Sukhothai Kingdom">Sukhothai Kingdom</a> under King <a href="/wiki/Ram_Khamhaeng" title="Ram Khamhaeng">Ram Khamhaeng</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> During the same era, <a href="/wiki/Marco_Polo" title="Marco Polo">Marco Polo</a> made a reference to <i>Malauir</i> in his <a href="/wiki/The_Travels_of_Marco_Polo" title="The Travels of Marco Polo">travelogue</a>, as a kingdom located in the Malay peninsula, possibly similar to the one mentioned in Yuan chronicle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>\n</p><p>In the early 20th century, the term <i>Tanah Melayu</i> was generally used by the Malays of the peninsula during the rise of <a href="/wiki/Early_Malay_nationalism" title="Early Malay nationalism">Malay nationalism</a> to describe uniting all <a href="/wiki/Peninsular_Malaysia" title="Peninsular Malaysia">Malay states</a> on the peninsula under one Malay nation, although this ambition was largely realised with the formation of <i>Persekutuan Tanah Melayu</i> (<a href="/wiki/Malay_language" title="Malay language">Malay</a> for "<a href="/wiki/Federation_of_Malaya" title="Federation of Malaya">Federation of Malaya</a>") in 1948.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
