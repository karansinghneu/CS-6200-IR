Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>\n<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>\n<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>\n<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Y&#241;igo Ortiz de Retez</b> (<a href="/wiki/Floruit" title="Floruit"><i>fl.</i></a> 1545) was a 16th-century <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spanish</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_maritime_explorers" title="List of maritime explorers">maritime explorer</a> of <a href="/wiki/Basques" title="Basques">Basque</a> origin, who navigated the northern coastline of the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific</a>&#8211;<a href="/wiki/Melanesia" title="Melanesia">Melanesian</a> island of <a href="/wiki/New_Guinea" title="New Guinea">New Guinea</a>, and is credited with bestowing the island\'s name (<i>"Nueva Guinea"</i>).\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Y%C3%B1igo_Ortiz_de_Retez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Y&#241;igo Ortiz de Retez was born in Retes de Llanteno (<a href="/wiki/%C3%81lava" title="&#193;lava">Alava</a>, Spain) in a non-titled nobility family (<a href="/wiki/Hidalgo_(nobility)" title="Hidalgo (nobility)">hidalgos</a>) in the first decade of the 16th century. The first accounts we have of him are as part of <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_Alvarado" title="Pedro de Alvarado">Alvarado\'s</a> expedition of 1538 to take the governorship of <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a> and <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduras</a>. He participated in the expedition to relieve the siege of <a href="/wiki/Nochistl%C3%A1n" title="Nochistl&#225;n">Nochistl&#225;n</a> in 1541 during the <a href="/wiki/Mixt%C3%B3n_War" title="Mixt&#243;n War">Mixt&#243;n war</a>, led by Alvarado. In 1542 he was appointed to the expedition of <a href="/wiki/Ruy_L%C3%B3pez_de_Villalobos" title="Ruy L&#243;pez de Villalobos">Lopez de Villalobos</a> to the <i>Islas de Poniente</i> (<a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>). Upon his arrival in <a href="/wiki/Mindanao" title="Mindanao">Mindanao</a> on February 1543, Ortiz de Retes was promoted to <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maestre_de_Campo" title="Maestre de Campo">Maestre de Campo</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ochoa_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ochoa-1">[1]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Discovery_of_New_Guinea">Discovery of New Guinea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Y%C3%B1igo_Ortiz_de_Retez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Discovery of New Guinea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Retezs_journey.PNG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="150" data-file-width="300" decoding="async" height="150" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/a/a6/Retezs_journey.PNG" width="300"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Retezs_journey.PNG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Retez\'s expedition</div></div></div>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:282px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:NovaGuinea1600map.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="448" data-file-width="700" decoding="async" height="179" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/81/NovaGuinea1600map.jpg/280px-NovaGuinea1600map.jpg" href="/wiki/File:NovaGuinea1600map.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Map of New Guinea, 1600</div></div></div>\n<p>After the unsuccessful attempt of <a href="/wiki/Bernardo_de_la_Torre" title="Bernardo de la Torre">Bernardo de la Torre</a> in 1543 to make the round trip and return to Mexico, L&#243;pez de Villalobos commissioned Ortiz de Retez for the same mission but this time taking a more southerly route. On 16&#160;May 1545 Ortiz de Retez, in command of the <i>San Juan de Letran</i>, left port in <a href="/wiki/Tidore" title="Tidore">Tidore</a>, an island which was Spain\'s stronghold in the <a href="/wiki/Maluku_Islands" title="Maluku Islands">Maluku Islands</a> and going by way of the <a href="/wiki/Talaud_Islands" title="Talaud Islands">Talaud Islands</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Schouten_Islands" title="Schouten Islands">Schoutens</a>, reached the northern coast of New Guinea, which was coasted till the end of August when, having got to the 5&#176;S latitude, contrary winds and currents forced a return to Tidore where he arrived on 5&#160;October 1545. Many islands were encountered and first charted, along the northern coast of New Guinea, and in the <a href="/wiki/Cenderawasih_Bay" title="Cenderawasih Bay">Padaidos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Schouten_Islands_(Papua_New_Guinea)" title="Schouten Islands (Papua New Guinea)">Le Maires</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ninigo_Islands" title="Ninigo Islands">Ninigos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kaniet_Islands" title="Kaniet Islands">Kaniets</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hermit_Islands" title="Hermit Islands">Hermits</a>, to some of which Spanish names were given.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brand_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Brand-4">[4]</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 20 June 1545 at the mouth of the <a href="/wiki/Mamberamo_River" title="Mamberamo River">Mamberamo river</a> (that was charted as <i>San Agustin</i>) he took possession of the land for the Spanish Crown, in the process giving the island the name by which it is known today. He called it <i>Nueva Guinea</i> owing to the resemblance of the local inhabitants to the peoples of the <a href="/wiki/Guinea_(region)" title="Guinea (region)">Guinea</a> coast in <a href="/wiki/West_Africa" title="West Africa">West Africa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>\n</p><p>The main source for Retez\'s voyage is that of Garcia de Escalante Alvarado, who was part of the Villalobos expedition, and who, after his return to Spain, wrote a chronicle that he sent to <a href="/wiki/Antonio_de_Mendoza" title="Antonio de Mendoza">Antonio de Mendoza</a>, viceroy of <a href="/wiki/New_Spain" title="New Spain">New Spain</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shaw_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Shaw-7">[7]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Ortiz de Retez was later imprisoned by the Portuguese in the Malukus along with the remaining members of Villalobos\' expedition; according to Escalante, he was one of the 117 who were later repatriated by the Portuguese in 1548.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Y%C3%B1igo_Ortiz_de_Retez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-Ochoa-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-Ochoa_1-0">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation journal">Gonz&#225;lez Ochoa, Jos&#233; Mar&#237;a (2006).
