Nations Committee on the Peaceful Uses of Outer Space</b> (COPUOS) was established in 1959 (shortly after the launch of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sputnik" title="Sputnik">Sputnik</a>) as an <i>ad hoc</i> committee. In 1959, it was formally established by <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_resolution" title="United Nations resolution">United Nations resolution</a> 1472 (XIV).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>\n</p><p>The mission of COPUOS is "to review the scope of international cooperation in peaceful uses of <a href="/wiki/Outer_space" title="Outer space">outer space</a>, to devise programmes in this field to be undertaken under <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> auspices, to encourage continued research and the dissemination of information on outer space matters, and to study legal problems arising from the <a href="/wiki/Space_exploration" title="Space exploration">exploration of outer space</a>."\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Office_for_Outer_Space_Affairs" title="United Nations Office for Outer Space Affairs">United Nations Office for Outer Space Affairs</a> (UNOOSA) is Secretariat to the Committee. All documents related to the Committee and its subcommittees, the Scientific and Technical Subcommittee<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and the Legal Subcommittee<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>, can be found at the UNOOSA website.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> involvement in space related activities can be traced back to the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Space_Race" title="Space Race">Space Race</a>. After the first man-made object orbited the Earth in 1957, the UN has focused on ensuring outer space is used for peaceful purposes. The Launch of <a href="/wiki/Sputnik_1" title="Sputnik 1">Sputnik</a> marked the beginning of the Space Race as well as the beginning of satellite use for the advancement of science.\n</p><p>As the <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a> began, fear of Outer Space being used for military purposes spread through the international community. This led to the creation of multiple organizations with the intent of governing how outer space can be used in order to assure it does not become the next frontier for conflict.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In 1958, the United Nations established the Committee on the Peaceful Uses of Outer Space which originally consisted of 18 members: Argentina, Australia, Belgium, Brazil, Canada, Czechoslovakia, France, India, Iran, Italy, Japan, Mexico, Poland, Sweden, the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics, the United Arab Republic, the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland, and the United States of America.\n</p><p>In 1959, the United Nations permanently established the Committee on the Peaceful Uses of Outer Space<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and grew to involve 24 countries (Albania, Austria, Bulgaria, Hungary, Lebanon, and Romania.) The main focuses of COPUOS is to promote cooperation in the peaceful use of outer space, and share information regarding outer space and its exploration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">[5]</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1962, the two COPUOS subcommittees: the Scientific and Technical Subcommittee and the Legal Subcommittee met for the first time and continue to do so annually.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The text near this tag is dated. ratification and enforcement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Both the former USSR and the USA engaged in an "arms race" including weapons systems functional in low-orbit altitudes; US President <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Reagan" title="Ronald Reagan">Ronald Reagan</a> termed it "star wars"; this raised serious concerns. treaty.\nIn 2017, with the reports of "Sonic attacks" on US staff of diplomatic missions in Cuba, the ban on space-based weapons systems is again on the radar.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Near-Earth_object_deflection_and_disaster_response">Near-Earth object deflection and disaster response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Nations_Committee_on_the_Peaceful_Uses_of_Outer_Space&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Near-Earth object deflection and disaster response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main articles: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Impact_events" title="Impact events">Impact events</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asteroid_impact_avoidance" title="Asteroid impact avoidance">Asteroid impact avoidance</a>, and <a href="/wiki/B612_Foundation" title="B612 Foundation">B612 Foundation</a></div>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Association_of_Space_Explorers" title="Association of Space Explorers">Association of Space Explorers</a> (ASE),  working in conjunction with <a href="/wiki/B612_Foundation" title="B612 Foundation">B612 Foundation</a> members, helped obtain UN oversight of <a href="/wiki/Near-Earth_object" title="Near-Earth object">near-Earth object</a> (NEO) tracking and deflection missions through COPUOS along with its Action Team 14 (AT-14) expert group. Several members of B612 and ASE have worked with COPUOS since 2001 to establish international involvement for both impact disaster responses, and on deflection missions to prevent impact events.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Earth_&amp;_Sky-2013.10.28_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Earth_&amp;_Sky-2013.10.28-8">[8]</a></sup> As explained by B612 Foundation <i>Chair Emeritus</i> <a href="/wiki/Rusty_Schweickart" title="Rusty Schweickart">Rusty Schweickart</a> in 2013, "No government in the world today has explicitly assigned the responsibility for planetary protection to any of its agencies".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Discovery.com-2013.10.28_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Discovery.com-2013.10.28-9">[9]</a></sup>\n</p><p>In October 2013, the UN committee approved several measures to deal with terrestrial asteroid impacts, including the creation of an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Asteroid_Warning_Network" title="International Asteroid Warning Network">International Asteroid Warning Network</a> (IAWN) to act as a clearing house for shared information on dangerous asteroids and for any future terrestrial impact events that are identified. A UN <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space_Missions_Planning_Advisory_Group" title="Space Missions Planning Advisory Group">Space Missions Planning Advisory Group</a> (SMPAG) will also coordinate joint studies of the technologies for deflection missions, and as well provide oversight of actual missions. This is due to deflection missions typically involving a progressive movement of an asteroid\'s predicted impact point across the surface of the Earth (and also across the territories of uninvolved countries) until the NEO has been deflected either ahead of, or behind the planet at the point their orbits intersect.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-New_Scientist-2013.10.28_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-New_Scientist-2013.10.28-10">[10]</a></sup> Schweickart states that an initial framework of international cooperation at the UN is needed to guide the policy makers of its member nations on several important NEO-related aspects.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LA_Times-2013.10.28_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-LA_Times-2013.10.28-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-New_York_Times-2013.11.06_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-New_York_Times-2013.11.06-12">[12]</a></sup>\n</p><p>At about the same time (Oct 2013) of the UN\'s policy adoption in New York City, Schweickart and four other ASE member, including B612 head <a href="/wiki/Ed_Lu" title="Ed Lu">Ed Lu</a> and strategic advisers <a href="/wiki/Dumitru_Prunariu" title="Dumitru Prunariu">Dumitru Prunariu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thomas_David_Jones" title="Thomas David Jones">Tom Jones</a>, participated at a public forum moderated by <a href="/wiki/Neil_deGrasse_Tyson" title="Neil deGrasse Tyson">Neil deGrasse Tyson</a> not far from the UN\'s headquarters, urging the global community to adopt further important steps towards planetary defense against the threat of NEO impacts. Their recommendations included:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Earth_&amp;_Sky-2013.10.28_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Earth_&amp;_Sky-2013.10.28-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LA_Times-2013.10.28_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-LA_Times-2013.10.28-11">[11]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li>UN delegates briefing their home countries\' policymakers on the UN\'s newest roles,</li>\n<li>having each country\'s government create defined asteroid disaster response plans, assigning fiscal resources to deal with asteroid impacts, and delegating a lead agency to handle its disaster response in order to create clear lines of communication from the IAWN to the affected countries,</li>\n<li>having their governments support the ASE\'s and B612\'s efforts to identify "city-killer" NEOs capable of impacting Earth, estimated at about a million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Discovery.com-2013.10.28_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Discovery.com-2013.10.28-9">[9]</a></sup> by deploying a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space_observatory" title="Space observatory">space-based asteroid telescope</a>, and</li>\n<li>committing member states to launching an international test deflection mission within 10 years.</li></ul>\n<p>The first meetings of IAWN and SMPAG were held in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Member_States">Member States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
