Only one ship, commanded by <a href="/wiki/Juan_Sebasti%C3%A1n_Elcano" title="Juan Sebasti&#225;n Elcano">Juan Sebasti&#225;n Elcano</a>, returns to Spain in 1522 with 18 men remaining.</li>\n<li>1524: Third trip of <a href="/wiki/Vasco_da_Gama" title="Vasco da Gama">Vasco da Gama</a> to India.</li>\n<li>1542: <a href="/wiki/Ant%C3%B3nio_Mota" title="Ant&#243;nio Mota">Ant&#243;nio da Mota</a> is thrown by a storm to the island of <a href="/wiki/Tanegashima" title="Tanegashima">Tanegashima</a>, establishing the first European contact with Japan.</li>\n<li>1549: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Francis_Xavier" title="Saint Francis Xavier">Saint Francis Xavier</a> arrives in Japan accompanied by Father <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cosme_de_Torr%C3%A8s" title="Cosme de Torr&#232;s">Cosme de Torr&#232;s</a>, Brother <a href="/wiki/Juan_Fern%C3%A1ndez_(missionary)" title="Juan Fern&#225;ndez (missionary)">Juan Fern&#225;ndez</a>, the Japanese <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anjiro" title="Anjiro">Anjiro</a>, two baptized Japanese named Antonio and Joane, a Chinese named Manuel, and an Indian named Amador.
