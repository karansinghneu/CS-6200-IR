1619), also known as <b>Johann Lippershey</b> or <b>Lipperhey</b>, was a <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a>-<a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a> <a href="/wiki/Glasses" title="Glasses">spectacle-maker</a>. He is commonly associated with the invention of the <a href="/wiki/Telescope" title="Telescope">telescope</a>, because he was the first one who tried to obtain a patent for it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It is, however, unclear if he was the first one to build a telescope.\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc"><input class="toctogglecheckbox" links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hans_Lippershey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Lippershey was born in <a href="/wiki/Wesel" title="Wesel">Wesel</a>, now in western <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, in 1570. He settled in <a href="/wiki/Middelburg" title="Middelburg">Middelburg</a>, the capital of the province of <a href="/wiki/Zeeland" title="Zeeland">Zeeland</a>, now in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, in 1594, married the same year and became a citizen of Zeeland in 1602. During that time he became a master lens grinder and <a href="/wiki/Glasses" title="Glasses">spectacle</a> maker and established a shop. He remained in Middelburg until his death, in September 1619.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Invention_of_the_telescope">Invention of the telescope</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hans_Lippershey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Invention of the telescope">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_telescope" title="History of the telescope">History of the telescope</a></div>\n<p>Hans Lippershey is known for the earliest written record of a <a href="/wiki/Refracting_telescope" title="Refracting telescope">refracting telescope</a>, a patent he filed in 1608.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> His work with optical devices grew out of his work as a spectacle maker,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> an industry that had started in Venice and Florence in the thirteenth century,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and later expanded to the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> and Germany.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Lippershey applied to the <a href="/wiki/States_General_of_the_Netherlands" title="States General of the Netherlands">States General of the Netherlands</a> on 2 October 1608 for a patent for his instrument "<i>for seeing things far away as if they were nearby</i>",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> a few weeks before another Dutch instrument-maker\'s patent, that of <a href="/wiki/Jacob_Metius" title="Jacob Metius">Jacob Metius</a>.  Lippershey failed to receive a patent since the same claim for invention had also been made by other spectacle-makers<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> but he was handsomely rewarded by the Dutch <a href="/wiki/Government" title="Government">government</a> for copies of his <a href="/wiki/Design" title="Design">design</a>.\n</p><p>Lippershey\'s application for a patent was mentioned at the end of a diplomatic report on an embassy to Holland from the <a href="/wiki/Ayutthaya_Kingdom" title="Ayutthaya Kingdom">Kingdom of Siam</a> sent by the Siamese king <a href="/wiki/Ekathotsarot" title="Ekathotsarot">Ekathotsarot</a>: <i>Ambassades du Roy de Siam envoy&#233; &#224; l\'Excellence du Prince Maurice, arriv&#233; &#224; La Haye le 10 Septemb. 1608</i> (<i>Embassy of the King of Siam sent to his Excellency Prince Maurice, arrived at The Hague on 10 September 1608</i>). This report was issued in October 1608 and distributed across Europe, leading to experiments by other scientists, such as the Italian <a href="/wiki/Paolo_Sarpi" title="Paolo Sarpi">Paolo Sarpi</a>, who received the report in November, the Englishman <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Harriot" title="Thomas Harriot">Thomas Harriot</a>, who was using a six-powered telescope by the summer of 1609, and <a href="/wiki/Galileo_Galilei" title="Galileo Galilei">Galileo Galilei</a>, who improved the device.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>\n</p><p>There are many stories as to how Lippershey came by his invention. One version has Lippershey observing two children playing with <a href="/wiki/Lens_(optics)" title="Lens (optics)">lenses</a> in his shop and commenting how they could make a far away <a href="/wiki/Weather_vane" title="Weather vane">weather-vane</a> seem closer when looking at it through two lenses. Other stories have Lippershey\'s apprentice coming up with the idea or have Lippershey copying someone else\'s discovery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Lippershey\'s original instrument consisted of either two <a href="/wiki/Lens_(optics)#Types_of_simple_lenses" title="Lens (optics)">convex</a> lenses with an inverted image or a convex <a href="/wiki/Objective_(optics)" title="Objective (optics)">objective</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Lens_(optics)#Types_of_simple_lenses" title="Lens (optics)">concave</a> <a href="/wiki/Eyepiece" title="Eyepiece">eyepiece</a> lens so it would have an upright image.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> This "Dutch perspective glass" (the name "<i>telescope</i>" would not be coined until three years later by <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_Demisiani" title="Giovanni Demisiani">Giovanni Demisiani</a>) had a three-times (or 3X) <a href="/wiki/Magnification" title="Magnification">magnification</a>.\n</p><p>The lunar crater <a href="/wiki/Lippershey_(crater)" title="Lippershey (crater)">Lippershey</a>, the minor planet <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/31338_Lipperhey" title="31338 Lipperhey">31338 Lipperhey</a>, and the exoplanet <a href="/wiki/55_Cancri_d" title="55 Cancri d">Lipperhey</a> (55 Cancri d) are named after him.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pronunciation">Pronunciation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hans_Lippershey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Pronunciation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><a title="/&#618;/: \'i\' in \'kit\'">&#618;</span><span title="\'p\' in \'pie\'">p</span><span title="/&#601;r/: \'er\' in \'letter\'">&#601;r</span><span title="/&#716;/: secondary stress follows">&#716;</span><span title="/&#643;/: \'sh\' in \'shy\'">&#643;</span><span title="/e&#618;/: \'a\' in \'face\'">e&#618;</span></span>/</a></span></span>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_pronunciation" title="German pronunciation">German pronunciation</a> is <small></small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[&#712;l&#618;p&#592;s&#716;ha&#618;]</a></span>, whereas the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_phonology" title="Dutch phonology">Dutch pronunciation</a> is closer to <small></small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Dutch" title="Help:IPA/Dutch">[&#712;l&#618;p&#601;rs&#716;&#614;&#603;i]</a></span>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hans_Lippershey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="mw-references-wrap mw-references-columns"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><a class="external text" href="https://books.google.com/books?id=KAWwzHlDVksC&amp;pg=PA30&amp;dq=Hans+Lippershey+inventor+of+the+telescope.&amp;hl=en&amp;ei=uJKiTJvkIsP6lweQ4dHbAw&amp;sa=X&amp;oi=book_result&amp;ct=result&amp;resnum=5&amp;ved=0CD8Q6AEwBDgK#v=onepage&amp;q=Hans%20Lippershey%20inventor%20of%20the%20telescope.&amp;f=false" rel="nofollow">The History of the Telescope, by Henry C.
