January 9, 1942) was an American <a href="/wiki/Astronomer" title="Astronomer">astronomer</a>. He participated in 11 expeditions for the study of <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse" title="Solar eclipse">solar eclipses</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-obit_2-0"><a links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heber_Doust_Curtis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>He was born on June 27, 1872, the elder son of Orson Blair Curtis and Sarah Eliza Doust.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>\n</p><p>He studied at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Michigan" title="University of Michigan">University of Michigan</a> and at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Virginia" title="University of Virginia">University of Virginia</a>, earning a degree in astronomy from the latter.\n</p><p>From 1902 to 1920 Curtis worked at <a href="/wiki/Lick_Observatory" title="Lick Observatory">Lick Observatory</a>, continuing the survey of <a href="/wiki/Nebula" title="Nebula">nebulae</a> initiated by <a href="/wiki/James_Edward_Keeler" title="James Edward Keeler">Keeler</a>. In 1912 he was elected president of the <a href="/wiki/Astronomical_Society_of_the_Pacific" title="Astronomical Society of the Pacific">Astronomical Society of the Pacific</a>.\n</p><p>In 1918 he observed <a href="/wiki/Messier_87" title="Messier 87">Messier 87</a> and was the first to notice the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polar_jet" title="Polar jet">polar jet</a> which he described as a "curious straight ray ... apparently connected with the nucleus by a thin line of matter."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-plo13_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-plo13-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1920 he was appointed director of the <a href="/wiki/Allegheny_Observatory" title="Allegheny Observatory">Allegheny Observatory</a>. In the same year he participated in the <i><a href="/wiki/Great_Debate_(astronomy)" title="Great Debate (astronomy)">Great Debate</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Harlow_Shapley" title="Harlow Shapley">Harlow Shapley</a> on the nature of nebulae and <a href="/wiki/Galaxy" title="Galaxy">galaxies</a>, and the size of the universe. Curtis advocated the now-accepted view that other galaxies apart from the <a href="/wiki/Milky_Way" title="Milky Way">Milky Way</a> existed.\n</p><p>Curtis also invented a type of film plate comparator in about 1925, allowing 2&#160;plates, each 8&#215;10&#160;<a href="/wiki/Inch" title="Inch">in</a>, to be compared using a set of prisms and placing the plates on stacked and aligned stages rather than next to one another as was the norm, this allowed the body of the device to measure just 60&#215;51&#160;<a href="/wiki/Centimetre" title="Centimetre">cm</a>. \nThis device is packed in crates and resided at UCO <a href="/wiki/Lick_Observatory" title="Lick Observatory">Lick Observatory</a> as of Aug&#160;2011.\nHis article describing the device appears in the <i>Publications of the Allegheny Observatory</i>, vol.&#160;VIII, no.&#160;2.\n</p><p>In 1930 Curtis was appointed director of the University of Michigan observatories, but the shortage of funds following the <a href="/wiki/Great_Depression" title="Great Depression">Great Depression</a> prevented the construction of a large reflector he had designed for the university at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ann_Arbor" title="Ann Arbor">Ann Arbor</a>. He contributed to develop the McMath&#8211;Hulbert private observatory at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lake_Angelus" title="Lake Angelus">Lake Angelus</a>.\n</p><p>He died on January 9, 1942.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-obit_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-obit-2">[2]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heber_Doust_Curtis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Heber Doust Curtis Memorial Telescope at the <a href="/wiki/Portage_Lake_Observatory" title="Portage Lake Observatory">Portage Lake Observatory</a> was dedicated in 1950 in Curtis\' memory. It no longer operates, but remains as a memorial to Curtis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heber_Doust_Curtis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-MPC-Discoverers-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-MPC-Discoverers_1-0">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a class="external text" href="http://www.minorplanetcenter.net/iau/lists/MPDiscsNum.html" rel="nofollow">"Minor Planet Discoverers (by number)"</a>. <i>Minor Planet Center</i>. 20 June 2016<span class="reference-accessdate">.
