1603 &#8211; 10 October 1659) was a <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Republic" title="Dutch Republic">Dutch</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sea_explorer" title="Sea explorer">seafarer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Exploration" title="Exploration">explorer</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Merchant" title="Merchant">merchant</a>, best known for his voyages of 1642 and 1644 in the service of the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_India_Company" title="Dutch East India Company">Dutch East India Company</a> (VOC). title="Tasmania">Tasmania</a>) and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, and to sight the <a href="/wiki/Fiji" title="Fiji">Fiji</a> islands.\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="Relocation_to_the_Dutch_East_Indies">Relocation to the Dutch East Indies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abel_Tasman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Relocation to the Dutch East Indies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Employed by the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_India_Company" title="Dutch East India Company">Dutch East India Company</a> (VOC), Tasman sailed from <a href="/wiki/Texel" title="Texel">Texel</a> to <a href="/wiki/Batavia,_Dutch_East_Indies" title="Batavia, Dutch East Indies">Batavia</a> in 1633, taking the southern <a href="/wiki/Brouwer_Route" title="Brouwer Route">Brouwer Route</a>. During this period, Tasman took part in a voyage to <a href="/wiki/Seram_Island" title="Seram Island">Seram Island</a>; the locals had sold spices to other European nationalities than the Dutch. He had a narrow escape from death, when in an incautious landing several of his companions were killed by people of Seram.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>\n</p><p>In August 1637, Tasman was back in Amsterdam, and the following year he signed on for another ten years and took his wife with him to Batavia. On 25 March 1638 he tried to sell his property in the Jordaan, but the purchase was cancelled.\n</p><p>He was second-in-command of a 1639 exploration expedition in the north Pacific under <a href="/wiki/Matthijs_Quast" title="Matthijs Quast">Matthijs Quast</a>. The fleet included the ships <i>Engel</i> and <i>Gracht</i> and reached <a href="/wiki/Fort_Zeelandia_(Taiwan)" title="Fort Zeelandia (Taiwan)">Fort Zeelandia</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dutch_Formosa" title="Dutch Formosa">Dutch Formosa</a>) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deshima" title="Deshima">Deshima</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="First_major_voyage">First major voyage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abel_Tasman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: First major voyage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In August 1642, the Council of the Indies, consisting of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antonie_van_Diemen" title="Antonie van Diemen">Antonie van Diemen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cornelis_van_der_Lijn" title="Cornelis van der Lijn">Cornelis van der Lijn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joan_Maetsuycker" title="Joan Maetsuycker">Joan Maetsuycker</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Justus_Schouten&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Justus Schouten (page does not exist)">Justus Schouten</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salomon_Sweers" title="Salomon Sweers">Salomon Sweers</a>, Cornelis Witsen, and Pieter Boreel in Batavia despatched Tasman and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Franchoijs_Jacobszoon_Visscher&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Franchoijs Jacobszoon Visscher (page does not exist)">Franchoijs Jacobszoon Visscher</a> on a voyage of exploration to little-charted areas east of the <a href="/wiki/Cape_of_Good_Hope" title="Cape of Good Hope">Cape of Good Hope</a>, west of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Staten_Island,_Argentina" title="Staten Island, Argentina">Staten Land</a> (near Cape Horn, South America) and south of the <a href="/wiki/Solomon_Islands" title="Solomon Islands">Solomon Islands</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>\n</p><p>One of the objectives was to obtain knowledge of "all the totally unknown" <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Beach" title="Provinces of Beach">Provinces of Beach</a>:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-:1-7">[7]</a></sup> a purported, yet <a href="/wiki/Phantom_island" title="Phantom island">non-existent landmass</a> with plentiful gold (which had appeared on European maps since the 15th century, as a result of an error in some editions of Marco Polo\'s works &#8211; see the box, right for more information).\n</p><p>This expedition was to use two small ships, <i>Heemskerck</i> and <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Zeehaen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Zeehaen (page does not exist)">Zeehaen</a></i>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mauritius">Mauritius</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abel_Tasman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Mauritius">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In accordance with Visscher\'s directions, Tasman sailed from Batavia on 14 August 1642<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-robinsonlibrary_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-robinsonlibrary-8">[8]</a></sup> and arrived at Mauritius on 5 September 1642, according to the captain\'s journal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-abeltasman.org_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-abeltasman.org-9">[9]</a></sup> The reason for this was the crew could be fed well on the island; there was plenty of fresh water and timber to repair the ships. Tasman got the assistance of the governor <a href="/wiki/Adriaan_van_der_Stel" title="Adriaan van der Stel">Adriaan van der Stel</a>.\n</p><p>Because of the prevailing winds Mauritius was chosen as a turning point. After a four-week stay on the island both ships left on 8 October using the <a href="/wiki/Roaring_Forties" title="Roaring Forties">Roaring Forties</a> to sail east as fast as possible. (No one had gone as far as <a href="/wiki/Pieter_Nuyts" title="Pieter Nuyts">Pieter Nuyts</a> in 1626/27.) On 7 November snow and hail influenced the ship\'s council to alter course to a more north-eastern direction,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gutenberg_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-gutenberg-10">[10]</a></sup> expecting to arrive one day at the <a href="/wiki/Solomon_Islands" title="Solomon Islands">Solomon Islands</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tasmania">Tasmania</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abel_Tasman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Tasmania">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Coastal-cliffs_Tasman-peninsula.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1544" data-file-width="2080" decoding="async" height="163" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Coastal-cliffs_Tasman-peninsula.jpg/220px-Coastal-cliffs_Tasman-peninsula.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Coastal-cliffs_Tasman-peninsula.jpg/330px-Coastal-cliffs_Tasman-peninsula.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Coastal-cliffs_Tasman-peninsula.jpg/440px-Coastal-cliffs_Tasman-peninsula.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Coastal-cliffs_Tasman-peninsula.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Coastal cliffs of <a href="/wiki/Tasman_Peninsula" title="Tasman Peninsula">Tasman Peninsula</a></div></div></div>\n<p>On 24 November 1642 Abel Tasman reached and sighted the west coast of Tasmania, north of <a href="/wiki/Macquarie_Harbour" title="Macquarie Harbour">Macquarie Harbour</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nla_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-nla-11">[11]</a></sup> He named his discovery Van Diemen\'s Land after <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antonio_van_Diemen" title="Antonio van Diemen">Antonio van Diemen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Governor-General_of_the_Dutch_East_Indies" title="Governor-General of the Dutch East Indies">Governor-General of the Dutch East Indies</a>.\n</p><p>Proceeding south Tasman skirted the southern end of Tasmania and turned north-east. He then tried to work his two ships into <a href="/wiki/Adventure_Bay,_Tasmania" title="Adventure Bay, Tasmania">Adventure Bay</a> on the east coast of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Bruny_Island" title="South Bruny Island">South Bruny Island</a> where he was blown out to sea by a storm. This area he named <a href="/wiki/Storm_Bay" title="Storm Bay">Storm Bay</a>. Two days later Tasman anchored to the north of Cape Frederick Hendrick just north of the <a href="/wiki/Forestier_Peninsula" title="Forestier Peninsula">Forestier Peninsula</a>. Tasman then landed in Blackman Bay &#8211; in the larger <a href="/wiki/Marion_Bay,_Tasmania" title="Marion Bay, Tasmania">Marion Bay</a>. The next day, an attempt was made to land in North Bay. However, because the sea was too rough the carpenter swam through the surf and planted the Dutch flag. Tasman then claimed formal possession of the land on 3 December 1642.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEChisholm1911_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEChisholm1911-12">[12]</a></sup>\n</p><p>For two more days, he continued to follow the east coast northward to see how far it went. When the land veered to the north-west at <a href="/wiki/Eddystone_Point" title="Eddystone Point">Eddystone Point</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> he tried to keep in with it but his ships were suddenly hit by  the <a href="/wiki/Roaring_Forties" title="Roaring Forties">Roaring Forties</a> howling through Banks Strait.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The impenetrable wind wall indicated that here was a strait, not a bay. Tasman was on a mission to find the Southern Continent, not more islands, so he abruptly turned away to the east and continued his continent-hunting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_Zealand">New Zealand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abel_Tasman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: New Zealand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Gilsemans_1642.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1948" data-file-width="3000" decoding="async" height="143" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Gilsemans_1642.jpg/220px-Gilsemans_1642.jpg" //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Gilsemans_1642.jpg/440px-Gilsemans_1642.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Gilsemans_1642.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Murderers\' Bay, drawing by Isaack Gilsemans<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-govt_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-govt-16">[16]</a></sup></div></div></div>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:DanceMaori.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="379" data-file-width="483" decoding="async" height="173" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/DanceMaori.jpg/220px-DanceMaori.jpg" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:DanceMaori.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>M&#257;ori <a href="/wiki/Haka" title="Haka">haka</a></div></div></div>\n<p>After some exploration, Tasman had intended to proceed in a northerly direction but as the wind was unfavourable he steered east. The expedition endured an extremely rough voyage and in one of his diary entries Tasman credited his compass, claiming it was the only thing that had kept him alive.\n</p><p>On 13 December 1642 they sighted land on the north-west coast of the <a href="/wiki/South_Island" title="South Island">South Island</a>, New Zealand, becoming the first Europeans to do so.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Tasman named it <i>Staten Landt</i> "in honour of the <a href="/wiki/States_General_of_the_Netherlands" title="States General of the Netherlands">States General</a>" (Dutch parliament).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> He wrote, "it is possible that this land joins to the Staten Land but it is uncertain",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> referring to <a href="/wiki/Isla_de_los_Estados" title="Isla de los Estados">a landmass of the same name</a> at the southern tip of South America, discovered by <a href="/wiki/Jacob_Le_Maire" title="Jacob Le Maire">Jacob Le Maire</a> in 1616.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> He continued: \'We believe that this is the mainland coast of the unknown Southland\'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Tasman thought he had found the western side of the long-imagined <a href="/wiki/Terra_Australis" title="Terra Australis">Terra Australis</a> that stretched across the Pacific to near the southern tip of South America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup>\n</p><p>\nAfter sailing north, then east for five days, the expedition anchored about 7&#160;km from the coast off what is now believed to have been <a href="/wiki/Golden_Bay" title="Golden Bay">Golden Bay</a>. Tasman sent ship\'s boats to gather water, but one of his boats was attacked by <a href="/wiki/M%C4%81ori_people" title="M&#257;ori people">M&#257;ori</a> in a double hulled <a href="/wiki/Waka_(canoe)" title="Waka (canoe)">waka</a> (canoe) and four of his men were killed with <a href="/wiki/Mere_(weapon)" title="Mere (weapon)">mere</a> (clubs). <style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r856303468">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style></p><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>In the evening about one hour after sunset we saw many lights on land and four vessels near the shore, two of which betook themselves towards us. When our two boats returned to the ships reporting that they had found not less than thirteen fathoms of water, and with the sinking of the sun (which sank behind the high land) they had been still about half a mile from the shore. After our people had been on board about one glass, people in the two canoes began to call out to us in gruff, hollow voices. We could not in the least understand any of it; however, when they called out again several times we called back to them as a token answer. But they did not come nearer than a stone\'s shot. They also blew many times on an instrument, which produced a sound like the moors\' trumpets. We had one of our sailors (who could play somewhat on the trumpet) play some tunes to them in answer."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-abeltasman.org_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-abeltasman.org-9">[9]</a></sup>\n</p></blockquote><p> As Tasman sailed out of the bay he observed 22 waka near the shore, of which "eleven swarming with people came off towards us." The waka approached the <i>Zeehaen</i> which fired and hit a man in the largest waka holding a small white flag. <a href="/wiki/Canister_shot" title="Canister shot">Canister shot</a> also hit the side of a waka.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-abeltasman.org_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-abeltasman.org-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Archeological research has shown the Dutch had tried to land at a major agricultural area, which the M&#257;ori may have been trying to protect.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Tasman named the area "Murderers\' Bay".\n</p><p>The expedition then sailed north, sighting <a href="/wiki/Cook_Strait" title="Cook Strait">Cook Strait</a>, which it mistook for a <a href="/wiki/Bight_(geography)" title="Bight (geography)">bight</a> and named "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zeehaen%27s_Bight" title="Zeehaen\'s Bight">Zeehaen\'s Bight</a>". Two names that the expedition gave to landmarks in the far north of New Zealand still endure: <a href="/wiki/Cape_Maria_van_Diemen" title="Cape Maria van Diemen">Cape Maria van Diemen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Three_Kings_Islands" title="Three Kings Islands">Three Kings Islands</a>.  (<i>Kaap Pieter Boreels</i> was renamed <a href="/wiki/Cape_Egmont" title="Cape Egmont">Cape Egmont</a> by Captain <a href="/wiki/James_Cook" title="James Cook">James Cook</a> 125 years later.)\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_voyage">Return voyage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abel_Tasman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Return voyage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tasman-dagboek-b.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="644" data-file-width="1022" decoding="async" height="139" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Tasman-dagboek-b.jpg/220px-Tasman-dagboek-b.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Tasman-dagboek-b.jpg/330px-Tasman-dagboek-b.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Tasman-dagboek-b.jpg/440px-Tasman-dagboek-b.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tasman-dagboek-b.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Tongatapu" title="Tongatapu">Tongatapu</a>, the main island of <a href="/wiki/Tonga" title="Tonga">Tonga</a>; drawing by Isaack Gilsemans</div></div></div>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tasman-dagboek-a.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="631" data-file-width="988" decoding="async" height="141" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/Tasman-dagboek-a.jpg/220px-Tasman-dagboek-a.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/Tasman-dagboek-a.jpg/330px-Tasman-dagboek-a.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/Tasman-dagboek-a.jpg/440px-Tasman-dagboek-a.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tasman-dagboek-a.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The bay of Tongatapu with the two ships; drawing by Isaack Gilsemans</div></div></div>\n<p>En route back to Batavia, Tasman came across the <a href="/wiki/Tonga" title="Tonga">Tongan</a> archipelago on 20 January 1643. While passing the <a href="/wiki/Fiji" title="Fiji">Fiji Islands</a> Tasman\'s ships came close to being wrecked on the dangerous reefs of the north-eastern part of the Fiji group. He charted the eastern tip of <a href="/wiki/Vanua_Levu" title="Vanua Levu">Vanua Levu</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cikobia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cikobia (page does not exist)">Cikobia</a> before making his way back into the open sea.\n</p><p>The expedition turned north-west towards <a href="/wiki/New_Guinea" title="New Guinea">New Guinea</a> and arrived at Batavia on 15 June 1643.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEChisholm1911_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEChisholm1911-12">[12]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_major_voyage">Second major voyage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abel_Tasman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Second major voyage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Tasman left <a href="/wiki/Batavia,_Dutch_East_Indies" title="Batavia, Dutch East Indies">Batavia</a> on 30 January 1644 on his second voyage with three ships (<i>Limmen</i>, <i>Zeemeeuw</i> and the tender <i>Braek</i>). He followed the south coast of New Guinea eastwards in an attempt to find a passage to the eastern side of <a href="/wiki/New_Holland_(Australia)" title="New Holland (Australia)">New Holland</a>. However, he missed the <a href="/wiki/Torres_Strait" title="Torres Strait">Torres Strait</a> between New Guinea and Australia, probably due to the numerous reefs and islands obscuring potential routes, and continued his voyage by following the shore of the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Carpentaria" title="Gulf of Carpentaria">Gulf of Carpentaria</a> westwards along the north Australian coast. He mapped the north coast of Australia making observations on <a href="/wiki/New_Holland_(Australia)" title="New Holland (Australia)">New Holland</a>, and its people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Quanchi_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-Quanchi-25">[25]</a></sup> He arrived back in <a href="/wiki/Batavia,_Dutch_East_Indies" title="Batavia, Dutch East Indies">Batavia</a> in August 1644.\n</p><p>From the point of view of the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_India_Company" title="Dutch East India Company">Dutch East India Company</a>, Tasman\'s explorations were a disappointment: he had neither found a promising area for trade nor a useful new shipping route. Although received modestly, the company was upset to a degree that Tasman did not fully explore the lands he found, and decided that a more "persistent explorer" should be chosen for any future expeditions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> For over a century, until the era of James Cook, Tasmania and New Zealand were not visited by Europeans &#8211; mainland Australia was visited, but usually only by accident.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life">Later life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abel_Tasman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Later life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>On 2 November 1644 Abel Tasman was appointed a member of the Council of Justice at Batavia. He went to Sumatra in 1646, and in August 1647 to Siam (now <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>) with letters from the company to the King. In May 1648 he was in charge of an expedition sent to <a href="/wiki/Manila" title="Manila">Manila</a> to try to intercept and loot the Spanish silver ships coming from America, but he had no success and returned to Batavia in January 1649. In November 1649 he was charged and found guilty of having in the previous year hanged one of his men without trial, was suspended from his office of commander, fined, and made to pay compensation to the relatives of the sailor. On 5 January 1651 he was formally reinstated in his rank and spent his remaining years at Batavia. He was in good circumstances, being one of the larger landowners in the town. He died at Batavia on 10 October 1659 and was survived by his second wife and a daughter by his first wife. His property was divided between his wife and his daughter by his first marriage. In his testimony (dating from 1657<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup>) he left 25 guilders to the poor of his village Lutjegast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history-nz_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-history-nz-28">[28]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Although Tasman\'s pilot, Frans Visscher, published <i>Memoir concerning the discovery of the South land</i> in 1642, Tasman\'s detailed journal was not published until 1898; however, some of his charts and maps were in general circulation and used by subsequent explorers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Quanchi_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-Quanchi-25">[25]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abel_Tasman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Tasman\'s ten-month voyage in 1642-43 had significant consequences. By circumnavigating Australia (albeit at a distance) Tasman proved that the small fifth continent was not joined to any larger sixth continent, such as the long-imagined Southern Continent. Further, Tasman\'s suggestion that New Zealand was the western side of that Southern Continent was seized upon by many European cartographers who, for the next century, depicted New Zealand as the west coast of a Terra Australis rising gradually from the waters around Tierra del Fuego. \'Bonaparte\' map</div></div></div>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:State_Library_of_New_South_Wales000.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2112" data-file-width="2816" decoding="async" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/State_Library_of_New_South_Wales000.jpg/220px-State_Library_of_New_South_Wales000.jpg" class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:State_Library_of_New_South_Wales000.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>State Library of New South Wales vestibule showing the Tasman map</div></div></div>\n<p>Held within the collection of the <a href="/wiki/State_Library_of_New_South_Wales" title="State Library of New South Wales">State Library of New South Wales</a> is the <a class="external text" href="http://library.sl.nsw.gov.au/record=b2058092~S2" rel="nofollow">Tasman Map</a>, thought to have been drawn by Isaac Gilsemans, or completed under the supervision of Franz Jacobszoon Visscher.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-THE_TASMAN_MAP_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-THE_TASMAN_MAP-31">[31]</a></sup> The map is also known as the Bonaparte map, as it was once owned by <a href="/wiki/Roland_Bonaparte" title="Roland Bonaparte">Prince Roland Bonaparte</a>, the great-nephew of <a href="/wiki/Napoleon" title="Napoleon">Napoleon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:2_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-:2-32">[32]</a></sup> The map was completed sometime after 1644 and is based on the original charts drawn during Tasman\'s first and second voyages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-33">[33]</a></sup> As none of the journals or logs composed during Tasman\'s second voyage have survived, the Bonaparte map remains as an important contemporary artefact of Tasman\'s voyage to the northern coast of the Australian continent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_33-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-33">[33]</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Tasman map largely reveals the extent of understanding the Dutch had of the Australian continent at the time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:3_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-:3-34">[34]</a></sup> The map includes the western and southern coasts of Australia, accidentally encountered by Dutch voyagers as they journeyed by way of the Cape of Good Hope to the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_India_Company" title="Dutch East India Company">VOC</a>  headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Batavia,_Dutch_East_Indies" title="Batavia, Dutch East Indies">Batavia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:2_32-1"><a href="#cite_note-:2-32">[32]</a></sup> In addition, the map shows the tracks of Tasman\'s two voyages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:2_32-2"><a href="#cite_note-:2-32">[32]</a></sup> Of his second voyage, the map shows the area of the <a href="/wiki/Banda_Islands" title="Banda Islands">Banda Islands</a>, the southern coast of <a href="/wiki/New_Guinea" title="New Guinea">New Guinea</a> and much of the northern coast of Australia. However, the area of the Torres Strait is shown unexamined; this is despite having been given orders by VOC Council at Batavia to explore the possibility of a channel between New Guinea and the Australian continent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_33-2"><a href="#cite_note-:0-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:3_34-1"><a href="#cite_note-:3-34">[34]</a></sup>\n</p><p>There is debate as to the origin of the map.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:4_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-:4-35">[35]</a></sup> It is widely believed that the map was produced in Batavia; however, it has also been argued that the map was produced in Amsterdam.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:2_32-3"><a href="#cite_note-:2-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:4_35-1"><a href="#cite_note-:4-35">[35]</a></sup> The authorship of the map has also been debated: while the map is commonly attributed to Tasman, it is now thought to have been the result of a collaboration, probably involving Franchoijs Visscher and Isaack Gilseman, who took part in both of Tasman\'s voyages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-:1-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:4_35-2"><a href="#cite_note-:4-35">[35]</a></sup> Whether the map was produced in 1644 is also subject to debate, as a VOC company report in December 1644 suggests that at that time no maps showing Tasman\'s voyages were yet complete.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:4_35-3"><a href="#cite_note-:4-35">[35]</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1943, a mosaic version of the map, composed of coloured marble and brass, was inlaid into the vestibule floor of the <a href="/wiki/State_Library_of_New_South_Wales" title="State Library of New South Wales">Mitchell Library</a> in Sydney.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The work was commissioned by the Principal&#160;Librarian <a href="/wiki/William_Herbert_Ifould" title="William Herbert Ifould">William Ifould</a>, and completed by the <a class="external text" href="http://adb.anu.edu.au/biography/melocco-galliano-13274" rel="nofollow">Melocco Brothers</a> of Annandale, who also worked on <a href="/wiki/ANZAC_War_Memorial" title="ANZAC War Memorial">ANZAC War Memorial</a> in Hyde Park and the crypt at <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s_Cathedral,_Sydney" title="St Mary\'s Cathedral, Sydney">St Mary\'s Cathedral, Sydney</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-THE_TASMAN_MAP_31-1"><a href="#cite_note-THE_TASMAN_MAP-31">[31]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span
