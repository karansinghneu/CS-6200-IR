title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/&#712;/: primary stress follows">&#712;</span><span title="\'d\' in \'dye\'">d</span><span title="/i&#720;/: \'ee\' in \'fleece\'">i&#720;</span><span title="/&#601;/: \'a\' in \'about\'">&#601;</span><span title="\'s\' in \'sigh\'">s</span></span>/</a></span></span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a Dias is the first <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Age_of_Discovery" title="Age of Discovery">Age of Discovery</a> to anchor at what is present-day <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc"><input links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_setting_and_purposes_of_the_Dias_expedition">Historical setting and purposes of the Dias expedition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bartolomeu_Dias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Historical setting and purposes of the Dias expedition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Bartolomeu Dias was a squire of the royal court, superintendent of the royal warehouses, and sailing-master of the <a href="/wiki/Man-of-war" title="Man-of-war">man-of-war</a> <i>S&#227;o Crist&#243;v&#227;o</i> (<a href="/wiki/Saint_Christopher" title="Saint Christopher">Saint Christopher</a>). Very little is known of his early life. King <a href="/wiki/John_II_of_Portugal" title="John II of Portugal">John II of Portugal</a> appointed him, on 10 October 1486, to head an expedition to sail around the southern tip of Africa in the hope of finding a trade route to India. Dias was also charged with searching for the lands ruled by  <a href="/wiki/Prester_John" title="Prester John">Prester John</a>, a fabled Christian priest and ruler of a territory somewhere beyond Europe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He left 10 months later in August 1487. In the previous decades Portuguese mariners, most famously <a href="/wiki/Prince_Henry_the_Navigator" title="Prince Henry the Navigator">Prince Henry the Navigator</a> (whose contribution was more as a patron and sponsor of voyages of discovery than as a sailor), had explored the areas of the Atlantic Ocean off Southern Europe and Western Africa as far as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_Verde_Islands" title="Cape Verde Islands">Cape Verde Islands</a> and modern day <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Leone" title="Sierra Leone">Sierra Leone</a>, and had gained sufficient knowledge of oceanic shipping and wind patterns to enable subsequent voyages of greater distance. In the early 1480s <a href="/wiki/Diogo_C%C3%A3o" title="Diogo C&#227;o">Diogo C&#227;o</a> in two voyages (he died towards the end of the second) had explored the mouth of the <a href="/wiki/Congo_River" title="Congo River">Congo River</a> and sailed south of the <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">Equator</a> to present-day <a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a> and <a href="/wiki/Namibia" title="Namibia">Namibia</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_journey">The journey</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bartolomeu_Dias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The journey">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><i>S&#227;o Crist&#243;v&#227;o</i> was piloted by <a href="/wiki/P%C3%AAro_de_Alenquer" title="P&#234;ro de Alenquer">P&#234;ro de Alenquer</a>. A second caravel, the <i>S&#227;o Pantale&#227;o</i>, was commanded by <a href="/wiki/Jo%C3%A3o_Infante" title="Jo&#227;o Infante">Jo&#227;o Infante</a> and piloted by <a href="/wiki/%C3%81lvaro_Martins" title="&#193;lvaro Martins">&#193;lvaro Martins</a>. Dias\' brother <a href="/wiki/P%C3%AAro_Dias" title="P&#234;ro Dias">P&#234;ro Dias</a> was the captain of the square-rigged support ship with Jo&#227;o de Santiago as pilot.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Diaz_on_his_voyage_to_the_cape.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1263" data-file-width="1562" decoding="async" height="178" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Diaz_on_his_voyage_to_the_cape.jpg/220px-Diaz_on_his_voyage_to_the_cape.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Diaz_on_his_voyage_to_the_cape.jpg/330px-Diaz_on_his_voyage_to_the_cape.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Diaz_on_his_voyage_to_the_cape.jpg/440px-Diaz_on_his_voyage_to_the_cape.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Diaz_on_his_voyage_to_the_cape.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>An illustration of the <i>S&#227;o Crist&#243;v&#227;o</i> and <i>S&#227;o Pantale&#227;o</i></div></div></div>\n<p>The expedition sailed around the west coast of Africa. And more provisions were picked up on the way at the Portuguese fortress of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Jorge_de_Mina" title="S&#227;o Jorge de Mina">S&#227;o Jorge de Mina</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Gold_Coast" title="Portuguese Gold Coast">Gold Coast</a>. After having sailed south of modern day <a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a>, Dias reached the Golfo da Conceic&#227;o (<a href="/wiki/Walvis_Bay" title="Walvis Bay">Walvis Bay</a>, in modern <a href="/wiki/Namibia" title="Namibia">Namibia</a>) by December. Continuing south, he discovered first Angra dos Ilheus, being hit, then, by a violent storm. Thirteen days later, from the open ocean, he searched the coast again to the east, discovering and using the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volta_do_Mar" title="Volta do Mar">westerlies winds</a> - the <a href="/wiki/Ocean_gyre" title="Ocean gyre">ocean gyre</a>, but finding just ocean. Having rounded the <a href="/wiki/Cape_of_Good_Hope" title="Cape of Good Hope">Cape of Good Hope</a> at a considerable distance to the west and southwest, he turned towards the east, and taking advantage of the winds of Antarctica that blow strongly in the South Atlantic, he sailed northeast. After 30 days without seeing land, he entered what he named Aguada de S&#227;o Br&#225;s (Bay of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Blaise" title="Saint Blaise">Saint Blaise</a>)&#8212;later renamed <a href="/wiki/Mossel_Bay" title="Mossel Bay">Mossel Bay</a>&#8212;on 4 February 1488. Dias\'s expedition reached its furthest point on 12 March 1488 when they anchored at <a href="/wiki/Kwaaihoek" title="Kwaaihoek">Kwaaihoek</a>, near the mouth of the <a href="/wiki/Boesmans_River_(Eastern_Cape)" title="Boesmans River (Eastern Cape)">Boesmans River</a>, where a <a href="/wiki/Padr%C3%A3o" title="Padr&#227;o">padr&#227;o</a>&#8212;the Padr&#227;o de S&#227;o Greg&#243;rio&#8212;was erected before turning back.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eera_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-eera-6">[6]</a></sup> Dias wanted to continue sailing to India, but he was forced to turn back when his crew refused to go further and the rest of the officers unanimously favoured returning to Portugal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> It was only on the return voyage that he actually discovered the <a href="/wiki/Cape_of_Good_Hope" title="Cape of Good Hope">Cape of Good Hope</a>, in May 1488. Dias returned to <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a> in December of that year, after an absence of sixteen months and seventeen days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>\n</p><p>The discovery of the passage around southern Africa was significant because, for the first time, Europeans could trade directly with India and the <a href="/wiki/Far_East" title="Far East">Far East</a>, bypassing the overland Euro-Asian route with its expensive European, Middle Eastern and Central Asian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wholesaler" title="Wholesaler">middlemen</a>.The official report of the expedition has been lost.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p><p>Bartolomeu Dias originally named the Cape of Good Hope the Cape of Storms (<i>Cabo das Tormentas</i>). It was later renamed (by King <a href="/wiki/John_II_of_Portugal" title="John II of Portugal">John II of Portugal</a>) the Cape of Good Hope (<i>Cabo da Boa Esperan&#231;a</i>) because it represented the opening of a route to the east.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Follow-up_voyages">Follow-up voyages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bartolomeu_Dias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Follow-up voyages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>After these early attempts, the Portuguese took a decade-long break from Indian Ocean exploration. During that hiatus, it is likely that they received valuable information from a secret agent, <a href="/wiki/P%C3%AAro_da_Covilh%C3%A3" title="P&#234;ro da Covilh&#227;">P&#234;ro da Covilh&#227;</a>, who had been sent overland to India and returned with reports useful to their navigators.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Using his experience with explorative travel, Dias helped in the construction of the <i><a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Gabriel_(ship)" title="S&#227;o Gabriel (ship)">S&#227;o Gabriel</a></i> and its sister ship the <i>S&#227;o Rafael</i> that were used in 1498 by <a href="/wiki/Vasco_da_Gama" title="Vasco da Gama">Vasco da Gama</a> to sail past the <a href="/wiki/Cape_of_Good_Hope" title="Cape of Good Hope">Cape of Good Hope</a> and continue to India. Dias only participated in the first leg of Da Gama\'s voyage, until the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_Verde_Islands" title="Cape Verde Islands">Cape Verde Islands</a>. Two years later he was one of the captains of the <a href="/wiki/2nd_Portuguese_India_Armada_(Cabral,_1500)" title="2nd Portuguese India Armada (Cabral, 1500)">second Indian expedition</a>, headed by <a href="/wiki/Pedro_%C3%81lvares_Cabral" title="Pedro &#193;lvares Cabral">Pedro &#193;lvares Cabral</a>. This flotilla first reached the coast of <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, landing there in 1500, and then continued eastwards to India. Dias perished near the Cape of Good Hope that he presciently had named <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_of_Storms" title="Cape of Storms">Cape of Storms</a>. Four ships encountered a huge storm off the cape and were lost, including Dias\', on 29 May 1500. A shipwreck found in 2008 by the Namdeb Diamond Corporation off <a href="/wiki/Namibia" title="Namibia">Namibia</a> was at first thought to be Dias\' ship;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> however, recovered coins come from a later time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bartolomeu_Dias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Bartolomeu Dias was married and had two children:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Sim&#227;o Dias de Novais, who died unmarried and without issue.</li>\n<li>Ant&#243;nio Dias de Novais, a <a href="/wiki/Knight" title="Knight">Knight</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Christ_(Portugal)" title="Order of Christ (Portugal)">Order of Christ</a>, married to (apparently his relative, since the surname <i>Novais</i> was transmitted through  her brother\'s offspring) Joana Fernandes, daughter of Fern&#227;o Pires and wife Guiomar Mont&#234;s (and sister of Brites Fernandes and Fern&#227;o Pires, married to In&#234;s Nogueira, daughter of Jorge Nogueira and wife, and had issue). Dias\' grandson <a href="/wiki/Paulo_Dias_de_Novais" title="Paulo Dias de Novais">Paulo Dias de Novais</a> was a Portuguese coloniser of Africa in the 16th century. Dias\' granddaughter, Guiomar de Novais married twice, as his second wife to Dom Rodrigo de Castro, son of Dom Nuno de Castro and wife Joana da Silveira, by whom she had Dona Paula de Novais and Dona Violante de Castro, both died unmarried and without issue, and to Pedro Correia da Silva, natural son of Crist&#243;v&#227;o Correia da Silva, without issue.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bartolomeu_Dias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Dias_Cross_Memorial" title="Dias Cross Memorial">Dias Cross Memorial</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Diogo_C%C3%A3o" title="Diogo C&#227;o">Diogo C&#227;o</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Diogo_Dias" title="Diogo Dias">Diogo Dias</a></li>\n<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Oceana_Rojas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Oceana Rojas (page does not exist)">Oceana Rojas</a></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bartolomeu_Dias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap mw-references-columns"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a class="external text" href="http://www.biography.com/people/bartolomeu-dias-9273850" rel="nofollow">"Bartolomeu Dias Biography"</a>. <i>Biography.com</i>.
