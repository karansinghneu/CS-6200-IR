class="mw-headline" id="Early_exploration">Early exploration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ocean_exploration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early exploration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<ul><li>130,000 BC Researchers working on the island of Crete discover stone tools indicating ocean exploration capabilities of early humans dating to at least 130,000 years ago<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>\n<li>4500 BC Around this time, cultures like those in <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a> and <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> began diving into the sea as a source of food gathering, commerce, and possibly even warfare.</li>\n<li>4000 BC <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egyptians</a> developed <a href="/wiki/Sail" title="Sail">sailing</a> vessels, which were probably used only in the eastern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean">Mediterranean</a> near the mouth of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nile_River" title="Nile River">Nile River</a>.</li>\n<li>4000 BC - 1000 AD <a href="/wiki/Polynesia" title="Polynesia">Polynesian</a> colonization of <a href="/wiki/Oceania" title="Oceania">South Pacific</a> Islands.</li>\n<li>1800 BC Basic measuring of the depths is done in Egypt.</li>\n<li>1500 BC Middle Eastern peoples explored the Indian Ocean</li>\n<li>600 BC <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phoenicians" title="Phoenicians">Phoenicians</a> developed sea routes around the entire Mediterranean and into the <a href="/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">Red Sea</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a>. Going around <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> they reached <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> by sailing along the western European coast. Although they understood <a href="/wiki/Celestial_navigation" title="Celestial navigation">celestial navigation</a>, they probably stayed within sight of land whenever possible.</li>\n<li>500-200 BC Greeks developed trade routes in the Mediterranean using the length of the day (corrected for the time of the year) to estimate <a href="/wiki/Latitude" title="Latitude">latitude</a>.</li>\n<li>450 BC <a href="/wiki/Herodotus" title="Herodotus">Herodotus</a> publishes a map of the Mediterranean region.</li>\n<li>325 BC <a href="/wiki/Pytheas" title="Pytheas">Pytheas</a>, a Greek <a href="/wiki/Astronomer" title="Astronomer">astronomer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Geographer" title="Geographer">geographer</a>, sailed north out of the Mediterranean, reaching England and possibly even Iceland and Norway. He also developed the use of sightings on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Star" title="North Star">North Star</a> to determine latitude.</li>\n<li>c.240 BC <a href="/wiki/Eratosthenes" title="Eratosthenes">Eratosthenes</a> of <a href="/wiki/Alexandria" title="Alexandria">Alexandria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> determines fairly accurately the <a href="/wiki/Circumference" title="Circumference">circumference</a> of the Earth using angles of shadows in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Syene" title="Syene">Syene</a> and Alexandria.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sverdrup_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sverdrup-2">[2]</a></sup></li>\n<li>150 AD <a href="/wiki/Ptolemy" title="Ptolemy">Ptolemy</a> produces a map of the Roman world, including lines of latitude and <a href="/wiki/Longitude" title="Longitude">longitude</a>, the continents of <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, Europe, and Africa and the surrounding oceans.</li>\n<li>900-1430 <a href="/wiki/Vikings" title="Vikings">Vikings</a> explore and colonize <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_(island)" title="Newfoundland (island)">Newfoundland</a>.</li>\n<li>1002  Leif Erikson reaches North America 500 years before Columbus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sverdrup_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Sverdrup-2">[2]</a></sup></li>\n<li>1405-1433 Chinese send <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Treasure_voyages" title="Treasure voyages">seven voyages</a> to extend Chinese influence and impress their neighbor states. These expensive voyages are ended after a short time. See <a href="/wiki/Zheng_He" title="Zheng He">Zheng He</a> (1371&#8211;1433).</li></ul>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="From_Age_of_Exploration_to_present">From Age of Exploration to present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ocean_exploration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: From Age of Exploration to present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/European_and_American_voyages_of_scientific_exploration" title="European and American voyages of scientific exploration">European and American voyages of scientific exploration</a></div>\n<ul><li>1492-1504 <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a> reaches <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">America</a> looking for a searoute to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indies" title="Indies">Indies</a>; discovering  various lands and islands and establishing a colony on <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">Hispaniola</a></li>\n<li>1498 <a href="/wiki/Vasco_da_Gama" title="Vasco da Gama">Vasco da Gama</a> sails around Africa from <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a> reaching <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> and establishing trade routes</li>\n<li>1499-1504 Amerigo Vespucci explores New World</li>\n<li>1519-1522 <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_Magellan" title="Ferdinand Magellan">Ferdinand Magellan</a>\'s ships <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Circumnavigate" title="Circumnavigate">circumnavigate</a> the world.</li>\n<li>1620 Dutch inventor <a href="/wiki/Cornelis_Drebbel" title="Cornelis Drebbel">Cornelis Drebbel</a> builds the world\'s first <a href="/wiki/Submarine" title="Submarine">submarine</a> and makes several trips in the <a href="/wiki/River_Thames" title="River Thames">River Thames</a> near <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> at a depth of about 12 or 15 feet.</li>\n<li>1698-1700 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edmund_Halley" title="Edmund Halley">Edmund Halley</a> made probably the first primarily scientific voyage to study the variation of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Magnetic_compass" title="Magnetic compass">magnetic compass</a>, sailing as far as 52 deg S. in the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>. On a previous expedition to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Helena" title="St. Helena">St. Helena</a>, he made an important contribution to knowledge of the trade winds.</li>\n<li>1768-1780 <a href="/wiki/James_Cook" title="James Cook">James Cook</a> explores the southern parts of the oceans looking for the southern continent. He was the first to use a <a href="/wiki/Marine_chronometer" title="Marine chronometer">marine chronometer</a> to determine longitude.</li>\n<li>1785 <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Franklin" title="Benjamin Franklin">Benjamin Franklin</a> writes <a class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20051218185445/http://oceanexplorer.noaa.gov/library/readings/gulf/gulf.html" rel="nofollow">Sundry Marine Observations</a> on improvements to ships and the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Stream" title="Gulf Stream">Gulf Stream</a>.</li>\n<li>1831-1836 <a href="/wiki/Charles_Darwin" title="Charles Darwin">Charles Darwin</a> sails on the Beagle, exploring the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Galapagos" title="Galapagos">Galapagos</a> and many other areas.  It is this work which led him to develop the concepts of <a href="/wiki/Natural_selection" title="Natural selection">natural selection</a> and <a href="/wiki/Evolution" title="Evolution">evolution</a>.</li>\n<li>1860 First chart of the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Stream" title="Gulf Stream">Gulf Stream</a> published by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Coast_Survey" title="U.S. Coast Survey">U.S. Coast Survey</a>.</li>\n<li>1872-1876 <a href="/wiki/Challenger_expedition" title="Challenger expedition">H.M.S. Challenger</a> travels around the world on a scientific mission taking sediment samples, water samples, soundings, and collecting many biological specimens.</li>\n<li>1960 <a href="/wiki/Bathyscaphe_Trieste" title="Bathyscaphe Trieste">Bathyscaphe Trieste</a> dives to what was believed to be the deepest point in the <a href="/wiki/Mariana_Trench" title="Mariana Trench">Mariana Trench</a>. A depth of 10,915 meters was observed.</li>\n<li>1969 The <a href="/wiki/Ben_Franklin_(PX-15)" title="Ben Franklin (PX-15)">Ben Franklin (PX-15)</a> drifts submerged for 30 days in the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Stream" title="Gulf Stream">Gulf Stream</a>.</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ocean_exploration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Deep-sea_exploration" title="Deep-sea exploration">Deep-sea exploration</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Ocean" title="Ocean">Ocean</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Oceanography" title="Oceanography">Oceanography</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Exploration" title="Exploration">Exploration</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Office_of_Ocean_Exploration" title="Office of Ocean Exploration">Office of Ocean Exploration</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/List_of_explorers" title="List of explorers">List of explorers</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/List_of_maritime_explorers" title="List of maritime explorers">List of maritime explorers</a></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ocean_exploration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation journal">Strasser, Thomas F.; Eleni Pangopoulou; Curtis N.
