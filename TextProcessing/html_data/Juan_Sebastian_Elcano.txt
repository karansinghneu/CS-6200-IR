<span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Sebasti%C3%A1n_Elcano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Elcano was born in around 1486 to Domingo Sebasti&#225;n Elcano and Catalina del Puerto. He had three brothers: Domingo Elcano, a Catholic <a href="/wiki/Priest#Roman_Catholic_and_Orthodox" title="Priest">priest</a>, Mart&#237;n P&#233;rez Elcano, and Ant&#243;n Mart&#237;n Elcano.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Military_life">Military life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Sebasti%C3%A1n_Elcano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Military life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Elcano fought in the <a href="/wiki/Italian_Wars" title="Italian Wars">Italian Wars</a> under the command of <a href="/wiki/Gonzalo_Fern%C3%A1ndez_de_C%C3%B3rdoba" title="Gonzalo Fern&#225;ndez de C&#243;rdoba">Gonzalo Fern&#225;ndez de C&#243;rdoba</a> in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, and in 1509 he joined the Spanish expedition organized by Cardinal <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Jim%C3%A9nez_de_Cisneros" title="Francisco Jim&#233;nez de Cisneros">Francisco Jim&#233;nez de Cisneros</a> against <a href="/wiki/Algiers" title="Algiers">Algiers</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Merchant_shipping">Merchant shipping</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Sebasti%C3%A1n_Elcano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Merchant shipping">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Elcano settled in <a href="/wiki/Seville" title="Seville">Seville</a> and became a merchant ship captain. After breaking Spanish laws by surrendering a ship to <a href="/wiki/Genoa" title="Genoa">Genoan</a> bankers in repayment of a debt, he sought a pardon from the Spanish king <a href="/wiki/Charles_V,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor">Charles V</a>, by signing on as a subordinate officer for the Magellan expedition to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indies" title="Indies">East Indies</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Voyage_of_circumnavigation">Voyage of circumnavigation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Sebasti%C3%A1n_Elcano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Voyage of circumnavigation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Magellan%27s_voyage_EN.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1378" data-file-width="2488" decoding="async" height="122" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Magellan%27s_voyage_EN.svg/220px-Magellan%27s_voyage_EN.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Magellan%27s_voyage_EN.svg/330px-Magellan%27s_voyage_EN.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Magellan%27s_voyage_EN.svg/440px-Magellan%27s_voyage_EN.svg.png 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Magellan%27s_voyage_EN.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Magellan expedition.</div></div></div>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:NaoVictoria.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="4000" data-file-width="3000" decoding="async" height="267" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/NaoVictoria.JPG/200px-NaoVictoria.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/NaoVictoria.JPG/300px-NaoVictoria.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/NaoVictoria.JPG/400px-NaoVictoria.JPG 2x" width="200"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:NaoVictoria.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Victoria_(ship)" title="Victoria (ship)">Nao <i>Victoria</i></a>, Elcano ship Replica in <a href="/wiki/Punta_Arenas" title="Punta Arenas">Punta Arenas</a></div></div></div><p>\nElcano served as a naval commander of Charles V of Spain and took part in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Timeline_of_Magellan%27s_circumnavigation" title="Timeline of Magellan\'s circumnavigation">expedition</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>. In 1519, this 241-man expedition set sail with five ships, <i><a href="/wiki/Trinidad_(ship)" title="Trinidad (ship)">Trinidad</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Concepci%C3%B3n_(carrack)" title="Concepci&#243;n (carrack)">Concepci&#243;n</a></i>, <i>San Antonio</i>, <i>Santiago</i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Victoria_(ship)" title="Victoria (ship)">Victoria</a></i>. Elcano participated in a fierce mutiny against Magellan before the convoy discovered the passage through South America, the <a href="/wiki/Strait_of_Magellan" title="Strait of Magellan">Strait of Magellan</a>. He was spared by Magellan and after five months of hard labour in chains was made captain of the <a href="/wiki/Galleon" title="Galleon">galleon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> <i>Santiago</i> was later destroyed in a storm. The fleet sailed across the Atlantic Ocean to the eastern coast of <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> and into <a href="/wiki/Puerto_San_Juli%C3%A1n" title="Puerto San Juli&#225;n">Puerto San Juli&#225;n</a> in <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>. Several days later they discovered a passage now known as the <a href="/wiki/Strait_of_Magellan" title="Strait of Magellan">Strait of Magellan</a> located in the southern tip of <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a> and sailed through the strait. The crew of <i>San Antonio</i> mutinied and returned to Spain. On 28 November 1520, three ships set sail for the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a> and about 19 men died before they reached <a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">Guam</a> on 6 March 1521. Conflicts with the nearby island of Rota prevented Magellan and Elcano from resupplying their ships with food and water. They eventually gathered enough supplies and continued their journey to the Philippines and remained there for several weeks. Close relationships developed between the Spaniards and the islanders. They took part in converting the Cebuano tribes to Christianity and became involved in tribal warfare between rival Filipino groups in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mactan_Island" title="Mactan Island">Mactan Island</a>. </p><div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Elkano_Spice_Islands.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="745" data-file-width="711" decoding="async" height="231" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/92/Elkano_Spice_Islands.jpg/220px-Elkano_Spice_Islands.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/92/Elkano_Spice_Islands.jpg/330px-Elkano_Spice_Islands.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/92/Elkano_Spice_Islands.jpg/440px-Elkano_Spice_Islands.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Elkano_Spice_Islands.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Route of the Spanish expedition through the Spice Islands. The red cross shows the location of Mactan Island in the Philippines where Magellan was killed in 1521.</div></div></div>\n<p>On 27 April 1521, Magellan was killed and the Spaniards defeated by natives in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Mactan" title="Battle of Mactan">Battle of Mactan</a> in the Philippines. The surviving members of the expedition could not decide who should succeed Magellan. The men finally voted on a joint command with the leadership divided between <a href="/wiki/Duarte_Barbosa" title="Duarte Barbosa">Duarte Barbosa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jo%C3%A3o_Serr%C3%A3o" title="Jo&#227;o Serr&#227;o">Jo&#227;o Serr&#227;o</a>. Within four days these two were also dead. They were killed after being betrayed at a feast at the hands of Rajah Humabon. The mission was now teetering on disaster and Jo&#227;o Lopes de Carvalho took command of the fleet and led it on a meandering journey through the Philippine archipelago.\n</p><p>During the six-month listless journey after Magellan died, and before reaching the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moluccas" title="Moluccas">Moluccas</a>, Elcano\'s stature grew as the men became disillusioned with the weak leadership of Carvalho. The two ships, <i>Victoria</i> and <i>Trinidad</i> finally reached their destination, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moluccas" title="Moluccas">Moluccas</a>, on 6 November. They rested and re-supplied in this haven, and filled their holds with the precious cargo of <a href="/wiki/Clove" title="Clove">cloves</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nutmeg" title="Nutmeg">nutmeg</a>. On 18 December, the ships were ready to leave. <i>Trinidad</i> sprang a leak, and was unable to be repaired. Carvalho stayed with the ship along with 52 others hoping to return later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>\n</p><p><i>Victoria</i>, commanded by Elcano along with 17 other European survivors of the 240 man expedition and 4 (survivors out of 13) Timorese Asians continued its westward voyage to Spain crossing the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Oceans</a>. They eventually reached <a href="/wiki/Sanl%C3%BAcar_de_Barrameda" title="Sanl&#250;car de Barrameda">Sanl&#250;car de Barrameda</a> on 6 September 1522.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Antonio_Pigafetta" title="Antonio Pigafetta">Antonio Pigafetta</a>, an Italian scholar, was a crew member of the Magellan and Elcano expedition. He wrote several documents about the events of the expedition. According to Pigafetta the voyage covered 14,460 leagues&#8212;about 81,449 kilometres (50,610&#160;mi).\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Honours">Honours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Sebasti%C3%A1n_Elcano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Honours">edit</a><span class="thumbimage" data-file-height="488" data-file-width="401" decoding="async" height="183" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/CoAElcano.png/150px-CoAElcano.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/CoAElcano.png/225px-CoAElcano.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/CoAElcano.png/300px-CoAElcano.png 2x" width="150"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:CoAElcano.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Elcano\'s coat of arms</div></div></div>\n<p>Elcano was awarded a <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">coat of arms</a> by Charles I of Spain, featuring a globe with the motto: <i>Primus circumdedisti me</i> (in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_language" title="Latin language">Latin</a>, "You went around me first"),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and an annual pension.\n</p>\n<h2><span id="Loa.C3.ADsa_expedition"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Loa&#237;sa_expedition">Loa&#237;sa expedition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Sebasti%C3%A1n_Elcano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Loa&#237;sa expedition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In 1525, Elcano returned to sea, and became a member of the <a href="/wiki/Loa%C3%ADsa_expedition" title="Loa&#237;sa expedition">Loa&#237;sa expedition</a>. He was appointed leader along with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Garc%C3%ADa_Jofre_de_Loa%C3%ADsa" title="Garc&#237;a Jofre de Loa&#237;sa">Garc&#237;a Jofre de Loa&#237;sa</a> as captains, who commanded seven ships and sent to claim the East Indies for King Charles I of Spain. Both Elcano and Loa&#237;sa and many other sailors died of <a href="/wiki/Malnutrition" title="Malnutrition">malnutrition</a> in the Pacific Ocean, but the survivors reached their destination and a few of them managed to return to Spain.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family_life">Family life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Sebasti%C3%A1n_Elcano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Family life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Elcano never married but he had a son by Mar&#237;a Hern&#225;ndez Dernialde named Domingo Elcano, whom he legitimized in his last will and testament.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.
