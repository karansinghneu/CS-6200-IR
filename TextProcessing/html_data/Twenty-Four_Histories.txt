href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh">&#20108;&#21313;&#22235;&#21490;</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">&#200;rsh&#237;s&#236; Sh&#464;</span></i>; <a href="/wiki/Wade%E2%80%93Giles" title="Wade&#8211;Giles">Wade&#8211;Giles</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-wadegile">Erh-shih-szu shih</span></i>), also known as the <i><b>Orthodox Histories</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh">&#27491;&#21490;</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Zh&#232;ngsh&#464;</span></i>) are the <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">Chinese</a> <a href="/wiki/Official_history" title="Official history">official historical books</a> covering a period from 3000 BC to the <a href="/wiki/Ming_dynasty" title="Ming dynasty">Ming dynasty</a> in the 17th century.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Han_dynasty" title="Han dynasty">Han dynasty</a> official <a href="/wiki/Sima_Qian" title="Sima Qian">Sima Qian</a> established many of the conventions of the genre, but the form was not fixed until much later. Starting with the <a href="/wiki/Tang_dynasty" title="Tang dynasty">Tang dynasty</a>, each dynasty established an official office to write the history of its predecessor using official court records. As fixed and edited in the <a href="/wiki/Qing_dynasty" title="Qing dynasty">Qing dynasty</a>, the whole set contains 3213 volumes and about 40 million words. It is considered one of the most important sources on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chinese_history" title="Chinese history">Chinese history</a> and culture.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>\n</p><p>The title "Twenty-Four Histories" dates from 1775 which was the 40th year in the reign of the <a href="/wiki/Qianlong_Emperor" title="Qianlong Emperor">Qianlong Emperor</a>. This was when the last volume, the <i><a href="/wiki/History_of_Ming" title="History of Ming">History of Ming</a></i> was reworked and a complete set of the histories produced.\n</p>\n<div class="toc" class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Twenty-Four_Histories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Modern attempts at creating the official Qing history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In 1961, to commemorate the 50th anniversary of the declaration of the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_China_(1912%E2%80%931949)" title="Republic of China (1912&#8211;1949)">Republic of China</a> (ROC), the ROC government in <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a> published the <i>History of Qing</i>, adding 21 supplementary chapters to the <i>Draft History of Qing</i> and revising many existing chapters to denounce the <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">People\'s Republic of China</a> (PRC) as an illegitimate, impostor regime. It also removed passages that were derogatory towards the <a href="/wiki/Xinhai_Revolution" title="Xinhai Revolution">Xinhai Revolution</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> This edition has not been widely accepted as the official Qing history because it is recognized that it was a rushed job motivated by political objectives. It does not correct most of the errors known to exist in the <i>Draft History of Qing</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-making_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-making-5">[5]</a></sup>\n</p><p>An additional project, attempting to write a <i>New History of Qing</i> incorporating new materials and improvements in historiography, lasted from 1988 to 2000. Only 33 chapters out of the projected 500 were published.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-making_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-making-5">[5]</a></sup> This project was later abandoned following the rise of the <a href="/wiki/Pan-Green_Coalition" title="Pan-Green Coalition">Pan-Green Coalition</a>, which sees Taiwan as a distinct entity from <a href="/wiki/Mainland_China" title="Mainland China">Mainland China</a>, both culturally and politically. As such, it is argued that it is not the duty of the Taiwanese regime to compile the Qing history.\n</p><p>In 1961, the PRC also attempted to complete the Qing history, but historians were prevented from doing so against the backdrop of the <a href="/wiki/Cultural_Revolution" title="Cultural Revolution">Cultural Revolution</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-endymion_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-endymion-6">[6]</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2002, the PRC once again announced that it would complete the <i>History of Qing</i>. The project is under the leadership of <a href="/wiki/Dai_Yi" title="Dai Yi">Dai Yi</a>. As of December 2013, the project has been delayed twice and will not be completed until 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Modern_editions">Modern editions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Twenty-Four_Histories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Modern editions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In China, the <a href="/wiki/Zhonghua_Book_Company" title="Zhonghua Book Company">Zhonghua Book Company</a> (<i>Zhonghua Shuju</i>) have edited a number of these histories. They have been collated, edited, and punctuated by Chinese specialists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Xu_Elina-Qian,_p.19_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Xu_Elina-Qian,_p.19-8">[8]</a></sup>\n</p><p>From 1991 to 2003, it translated <a href="/wiki/Classical_Chinese" title="Classical Chinese">Classical Chinese</a> into modern <a href="/wiki/Written_vernacular_Chinese" title="Written vernacular Chinese">Written vernacular Chinese</a>, edited by <a href="/wiki/Xu_Jialu" title="Xu Jialu">Xu Jialu</a> and other scholars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Translations">Translations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Twenty-Four_Histories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Translations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>One of the <i>Twenty-Four Histories</i> is in the process of being fully translated into English: <i><a href="/wiki/Records_of_the_Grand_Historian" title="Records of the Grand Historian">Records of the Grand Historian</a></i> by William Nienhauser, in nine volumes,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>\n</p><p>In Korean and Vietnamese, only the <i>Records</i> has been translated. Most of the histories have been translated into Japanese.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
