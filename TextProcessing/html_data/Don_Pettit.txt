colspan="2">\n</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Donald Roy Pettit</b> (born April 20, 1955) is an American <a href="/wiki/Chemical_engineer" title="Chemical engineer">chemical engineer</a> and a <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a> <a href="/wiki/Astronaut" title="Astronaut">astronaut</a>. He is a veteran of two long-duration stays aboard the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a>, one space shuttle mission and a six-week expedition to find <a href="/wiki/Meteorite" title="Meteorite">meteorites</a> in <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctica</a>. As of 2018, at age 63, he is NASA\'s oldest active astronaut.\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc"><input class="toctogglecheckbox" id="toctogglecheckbox" role="button" style="display:none" href="/wiki/Eagle_Scout_(Boy_Scouts_of_America)" title="Eagle Scout (Boy Scouts of America)">Eagle Scout</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Donald_Pettit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>He earned a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor\'s degree">bachelor\'s degree</a> in <a href="/wiki/Chemical_engineering" title="Chemical engineering">chemical engineering</a> from <a href="/wiki/Oregon_State_University" title="Oregon State University">Oregon State University</a> in 1978 and a doctoral degree from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Arizona" title="University of Arizona">University of Arizona</a> in 1983.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="NASA_career">NASA career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Donald_Pettit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: NASA career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>He worked as a scientist at the <a href="/wiki/Los_Alamos_National_Laboratory" title="Los Alamos National Laboratory">Los Alamos National Laboratory</a> until 1996, when he was selected as an astronaut candidate by <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a>. He is married and has twin sons.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Spaceflight_experience">Spaceflight experience</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Donald_Pettit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Spaceflight experience">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Expedition_6">Expedition 6</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Donald_Pettit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Expedition 6">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Iss006-348-005.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1986" data-file-width="2961" decoding="async" height="148" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f9/Iss006-348-005.jpg/220px-Iss006-348-005.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f9/Iss006-348-005.jpg/330px-Iss006-348-005.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f9/Iss006-348-005.jpg/440px-Iss006-348-005.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Iss006-348-005.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Pettit pictured during an EVA</div></div></div>\n<p>Pettit\'s first space mission was as a mission specialist on ISS <a href="/wiki/Expedition_6" title="Expedition 6">Expedition 6</a> in 2002 and 2003. During his six-month stay aboard the space station, he performed two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extra-vehicular_activity" title="Extra-vehicular activity">EVAs</a> to help install external scientific equipment.  During free time on his stay aboard the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a>, he conducted demonstrations showing how fluids react in an extremely low gravity environment in a series he called "Saturday Morning Science".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Expedition 6 mission was extended by about two months, following the loss of the <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Columbia" title="Space Shuttle Columbia">Space Shuttle Columbia</a> in February 2003. Instead of returning on a shuttle the crew returned in a Russian Soyuz capsule, the first time American astronauts had flown in a foreign spacecraft.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Too_far_-_Jones_\'07_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Too_far_-_Jones_\'07-3">[3]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="STS-126">STS-126</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Donald_Pettit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: STS-126">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Pettit was Mission Specialist 1 on the <a href="/wiki/STS-126" title="STS-126">STS-126</a> mission to deliver equipment and supplies to the ISS. He spent 15 days 20 hours 29 minutes and 37 seconds on board Endeavour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Pettit also performed experiments on board ISS related to clumping of solid particles in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Microgravity" title="Microgravity">microgravity</a>.  The experiments showed that particles of various materials which varied in size between 1 micrometer and 6&#160;mm naturally clumped together in microgravity when confined to a volume of 4 liters that included a few grams of the materials. The cause was theorized to be electrostatic. This presents a plausible mechanism for the initial stages of planetary formation, since particles of this size do not have sufficient gravity to cause this phenomenon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span id="Expedition_30.2F31"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Expedition_30/31">Expedition 30/31</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Donald_Pettit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Expedition 30/31">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Pettit again launched to the International Space Station on December 21, 2011 as part of the <a href="/wiki/Expedition_30" title="Expedition 30">Expedition 30</a>/<a href="/wiki/Expedition_31" title="Expedition 31">31</a> crew.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He and fellow crewmembers <a href="/wiki/Oleg_Kononenko" title="Oleg Kononenko">Oleg Kononenko</a> and <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Kuipers" title="Andr&#233; Kuipers">Andr&#233; Kuipers</a> arrived at the ISS on December 23.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>\nAmong his off-duty video demonstrations on the space station has been on water as <a href="/wiki/Thin_film" title="Thin film">thin film</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marangoni_convection" title="Marangoni convection">Marangoni convection</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>\n</p><p>On May 25, 2012, Pettit and Kuipers operated the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadarm2" title="Canadarm2">Canadarm2</a> to grapple the <a href="/wiki/SpaceX_Dragon" title="SpaceX Dragon">SpaceX Dragon</a> and berth it to the <a href="/wiki/Harmony_(ISS_module)" title="Harmony (ISS module)">Harmony module</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>  Pettit was the first to enter the unmanned supply ship on May 26, making him the first astronaut in the history of space exploration to successfully enter a commercially-built and operated spacecraft berthed to the ISS in orbit.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Angry_Birds_Space_Demos">Angry Birds Space Demos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
