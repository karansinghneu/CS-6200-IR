Sebasti&#225;n Elcano">Juan Sebasti&#225;n Elcano</a>.\n</p><p>Born into a Portuguese noble family in around 1480, Magellan became a skilled sailor and naval officer and was eventually selected by King <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charles_I_of_Spain" title="Charles I of Spain">Charles I of Spain</a> to search for a westward route to the <a href="/wiki/Maluku_Islands" title="Maluku Islands">Maluku Islands</a> (the "Spice Islands"). Commanding a fleet of five vessels, he headed south through the Atlantic Ocean to Patagonia, passing through the <a href="/wiki/Strait_of_Magellan" title="Strait of Magellan">Strait of Magellan</a> into a body of water he named the "peaceful sea" (the modern <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>). Despite a series of storms and mutinies, the expedition reached the Spice Islands in 1521 and returned home via the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a> to complete the first circuit of the globe. Magellan did not complete the entire voyage, as he was killed during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Mactan" title="Battle of Mactan">Battle of Mactan</a> in the Philippines in 1521. His gift, the <a href="/wiki/Santo_Ni%C3%B1o_de_Ceb%C3%BA" title="Santo Ni&#241;o de Ceb&#250;">Santo Ni&#241;o de Ceb&#250;</a> image, remains one of his legacies during his arrival.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (January 2019)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p><p>Magellan had already reached the <a href="/wiki/Malay_Archipelago" title="Malay Archipelago">Malay Archipelago</a> in Southeast Asia on previous voyages traveling east (from 1505 to 1511&#8211;1512). By visiting this area again but now travelling west, Magellan achieved a nearly complete personal circumnavigation of the globe for the first time in history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Magellanic_penguin" title="Magellanic penguin">Magellanic penguin</a> is named after him, as he was the first European to note it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Magellan\'s navigational skills have also been class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dwarf_galaxies" title="Dwarf galaxies">dwarf galaxies</a>; the twin lunar craters of <a href="/wiki/Magelhaens_(lunar_crater)" title="Magelhaens (lunar crater)">Magelhaens</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Magelhaens_A" title="Magelhaens A">Magelhaens A</a>; and the <a href="/wiki/Magelhaens_(Martian_crater)" title="Magelhaens (Martian Portugal</div></div></div>\n<p>Magellan was born in northern Portugal in around 1480, either at <a href="/wiki/Vila_Nova_de_Gaia" title="Vila Nova de Gaia">Vila Nova de Gaia</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Porto" title="Porto">Porto</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Douro_Litoral_Province" title="Douro Litoral Province">Douro Litoral Province</a>, or at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sabrosa_Municipality" title="Sabrosa Municipality">Sabrosa</a>, near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vila_Real,_Portugal" title="Vila Real, Portugal">Vila Real</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Tr%C3%A1s-os-Montes_e_Alto_Douro_Province" title="Tr&#225;s-os-Montes e Alto Douro Province">Tr&#225;s-os-Montes e Alto Douro Province</a>. He was the son of Rodrigo de Magalh&#227;es, <i>Alcaide-Mor</i> of Aveiro (1433&#8211;1500, son of Pedro Afonso de Magalh&#227;es and wife Quinta de Sousa) and wife Alda de Mesquita and brother of Leonor or Genebra de Magalh&#227;es, wife with issue of Jo&#227;o Fernandes Barbosa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>\n</p><p>In March 1505 at the age of 25, Magellan enlisted in the fleet of 22 ships sent to host D. <a href="/wiki/Francisco_de_Almeida" title="Francisco de Almeida">Francisco de Almeida</a> as the first viceroy of Portuguese India. Although his name does not appear in the chronicles, it is known that he remained there eight years, in <a href="/wiki/Goa" title="Goa">Goa</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cochin" title="Cochin">Cochin</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quilon" title="Quilon">Quilon</a>. He participated in several battles, including the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Cannanore" title="Battle of Cannanore">battle of Cannanore</a> in 1506, where he was wounded. In 1509 he fought in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Diu_(1509)" title="Battle of Diu (1509)">battle of Diu</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He later sailed under <a href="/wiki/Diogo_Lopes_de_Sequeira" title="Diogo Lopes de Sequeira">Diogo Lopes de Sequeira</a> in the first Portuguese embassy to <a href="/wiki/Malacca" title="Malacca">Malacca</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Serr%C3%A3o" title="Francisco Serr&#227;o">Francisco Serr&#227;o</a>, his friend and possibly cousin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In September, after arriving at Malacca, the expedition fell victim to a conspiracy ending in retreat. Magellan had a crucial role, warning Sequeira and saving Francisco Serr&#227;o, who had landed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1511, under the new governor <a href="/wiki/Afonso_de_Albuquerque" title="Afonso de Albuquerque">Afonso de Albuquerque</a>, Magellan and Serr&#227;o participated in the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Malacca" title="Portuguese Malacca">conquest of Malacca</a>. After the conquest their ways parted: Magellan was promoted, with a rich plunder and, in the company of a Malay he had <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indentured_servant" title="Indentured servant">indentured</a> and baptized, <a href="/wiki/Enrique_of_Malacca" title="Enrique of Malacca">Enrique of Malacca</a>, he returned to Portugal in 1512. Serr&#227;o departed in the first expedition sent to find the "<a href="/wiki/Maluku_Islands" title="Maluku Islands">Spice Islands</a>" in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moluccas" title="Moluccas">Moluccas</a>, where he remained. He married a woman from <a href="/wiki/Ambon_Island" title="Ambon Island">Amboina</a> and became a military advisor to the Sultan of <a href="/wiki/Ternate" title="Ternate">Ternate</a>, Bayan Sirrullah. His letters to Magellan would prove decisive, giving information about the spice-producing territories.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>\n</p><p>After taking a leave without permission, Magellan fell out of favour. Serving in <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a>, he was wounded, resulting in a permanent limp. He was  accused of trading illegally with the <a href="/wiki/Moors" title="Moors">Moors</a>. The accusations were proved false, but he received no further offers of employment after 15 May 1514. Later on in 1515, he got an employment offer as a crew member on a Portuguese ship, but rejected this. In 1517 after a quarrel with <a href="/wiki/Manuel_I_of_Portugal" title="Manuel I of Portugal">King Manuel I</a>, who denied his persistent demands to lead an expedition to reach the spice islands from the east (i.e., while sailing westwards, seeking to avoid the need to sail around the tip of Africa<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>), he left for Spain. In <a href="/wiki/Seville" title="Seville">Seville</a> he befriended his countryman <a href="/wiki/Duarte_Barbosa" title="Duarte Barbosa">Diogo Barbosa</a> and soon married the daughter of Diogo\'s second wife, Mar&#237;a Caldera Beatriz Barbosa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> They had two children: Rodrigo de Magalh&#227;es<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and Carlos de Magalh&#227;es, both of whom died at a young age. His wife died in <a href="/wiki/Seville" title="Seville">Seville</a> around 1521.\n</p><p>Meanwhile, Magellan devoted himself to studying the most recent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Portolan_charts" title="Portolan charts">charts</a>, investigating, in partnership with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cosmographer" title="Cosmographer">cosmographer</a> <a href="/wiki/Rui_Faleiro" title="Rui Faleiro">Rui Faleiro</a>, a gateway from the Atlantic to the South Pacific and the possibility of the Moluccas being Spanish according to the demarcation of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Tordesillas" title="Treaty of Tordesillas">Treaty of Tordesillas</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Voyage_of_circumnavigation">Voyage of circumnavigation</span></h2>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Timeline_of_the_Magellan%E2%80%93Elcano_circumnavigation" title="Timeline of the Magellan&#8211;Elcano circumnavigation">Timeline of the Magellan&#8211;Elcano circumnavigation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Armada_de_Molucca" title="Armada de Molucca">Armada de Molucca</a></div>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background:_Spanish_search_for_a_westward_route_to_Asia">Background: Spanish search for a westward route to Asia</span></h3>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a>\'s voyages to the West (1492&#8211;1503) had the goal of reaching the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indies" title="Indies">Indies</a> and to establish direct commercial relations between Spain and the Asian kingdoms. The Spanish soon realized that the lands of the Americas were not a part of Asia, but a new continent. The 1494 <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Tordesillas" title="Treaty of Tordesillas">Treaty of Tordesillas</a> reserved for Portugal the eastern routes that went around Africa, and <a href="/wiki/Vasco_da_Gama" title="Vasco da Gama">Vasco da Gama</a> and the Portuguese arrived in <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> in 1498.\n</p><p>Castile (<i>Spain</i>) urgently needed to find a new commercial route to Asia. After the Junta de Toro conference of 1505, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish_Crown" title="Spanish Crown">Spanish Crown</a> commissioned expeditions to discover a route to the west. Spanish explorer <a href="/wiki/Vasco_N%C3%BA%C3%B1ez_de_Balboa" title="Vasco N&#250;&#241;ez de Balboa">Vasco N&#250;&#241;ez de Balboa</a> reached the Pacific Ocean in 1513 after crossing the <a href="/wiki/Isthmus_of_Panama" title="Isthmus of Panama">Isthmus of Panama</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Juan_D%C3%ADaz_de_Sol%C3%ADs" title="Juan D&#237;az de Sol&#237;s">Juan D&#237;az de Sol&#237;s</a> died in <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_de_la_Plata" title="R&#237;o de la Plata">R&#237;o de la Plata</a> in 1516 while exploring South America in the service of Spain.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Funding_and_preparation">Funding and preparation</span></h3>\n<p>In October 1517 in <a href="/wiki/Seville" title="Seville">Seville</a>, Magellan contacted Juan de Aranda, <a href="/wiki/Factor_(agent)" title="Factor (agent)">Factor</a> of the <i><a href="/wiki/Casa_de_Contrataci%C3%B3n" title="Casa de Contrataci&#243;n">Casa de Contrataci&#243;n</a></i>. Following the arrival of his partner Rui Faleiro, and with the support of Aranda, they presented their project to the Spanish king, Charles I, future Holy Roman Emperor <a href="/wiki/Charles_V,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor">Charles V</a>. Magellan\'s project, if successful, would  realize Columbus\' plan of a <a href="/wiki/Spice_trade" title="Spice trade">spice route</a> by sailing west without damaging relations with the Portuguese. The idea was in tune with the times and had already been discussed after Balboa\'s discovery of the Pacific. On 22 March 1518 the king named Magellan and Faleiro captains so that they could travel in search of the Spice Islands in July. He raised them to the rank of Commander of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Santiago" title="Order of Santiago">Order of Santiago</a>. The king granted them:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li>Monopoly of the discovered route for a period of ten years.</li>\n<li>Their appointment as governors of the lands and islands found, with 5% of the resulting net gains.</li>\n<li>A fifth of the gains of the travel.</li>\n<li>The right to levy one thousand ducats on upcoming trips, paying only 5% on the remainder.</li>\n<li>Granting of an island for each one, apart from the six richest, from which they would receive a fifteenth.</li></ul>\n<p>The expedition was funded largely by the Spanish Crown, which provided ships carrying supplies for two years of travel. Expert <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cartographer" title="Cartographer">cartographer</a> <a href="/wiki/Jorge_Reinel" title="Jorge Reinel">Jorge Reinel</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diego_Ribero" title="Diego Ribero">Diogo Ribeiro</a>, a Portuguese who had started working for Charles V in 1518<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> as a cartographer at the <i>Casa de Contrataci&#243;n</i>, took part in the development of the maps to be used in the travel. Several problems arose during the preparation of the trip, including lack of money, the king of Portugal trying to stop them, Magellan and other Portuguese incurring suspicion from the Spanish, and the difficult nature of Faleiro.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Finally, thanks to the tenacity of Magellan, the expedition was ready. Through the bishop <a href="/wiki/Juan_Rodr%C3%ADguez_de_Fonseca" title="Juan Rodr&#237;guez de Fonseca">Juan Rodr&#237;guez de Fonseca</a> they obtained the participation of merchant <a href="/wiki/Christopher_de_Haro" title="Christopher de Haro">Christopher de Haro</a>, who provided a quarter of the funds and goods to barter.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fleet">Fleet</span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Detail_from_a_map_of_Ortelius_-_Magellan%27s_ship_Victoria.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1538" data-file-width="2522" decoding="async" height="134" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Detail_from_a_map_of_Ortelius_-_Magellan%27s_ship_Victoria.png/220px-Detail_from_a_map_of_Ortelius_-_Magellan%27s_ship_Victoria.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Detail_from_a_map_of_Ortelius_-_Magellan%27s_ship_Victoria.png/330px-Detail_from_a_map_of_Ortelius_-_Magellan%27s_ship_Victoria.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Detail_from_a_map_of_Ortelius_-_Magellan%27s_ship_Victoria.png/440px-Detail_from_a_map_of_Ortelius_-_Magellan%27s_ship_Victoria.png 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Detail_from_a_map_of_Ortelius_-_Magellan%27s_ship_Victoria.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i><a href="/wiki/Victoria_(ship)" title="Victoria (ship)">Victoria</a></i>, the sole ship of Magellan\'s fleet to complete the circumnavigation. Detail from a map by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ortelius" title="Ortelius">Ortelius</a>, 1590.</div></div></div>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:ReplicaNaoVictoriaMagallanes.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="397" data-file-width="595" decoding="async" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/ReplicaNaoVictoriaMagallanes.jpg/220px-ReplicaNaoVictoriaMagallanes.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/ReplicaNaoVictoriaMagallanes.jpg/330px-ReplicaNaoVictoriaMagallanes.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/ReplicaNaoVictoriaMagallanes.jpg/440px-ReplicaNaoVictoriaMagallanes.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:ReplicaNaoVictoriaMagallanes.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Nao Victoria Replica in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nao_Victoria_Museum" title="Nao Victoria Museum">Nao Victoria Museum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Punta_Arenas" title="Punta Arenas">Punta Arenas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a></div></div></div>\n<p>The fleet provided by King Charles V included five ships:\n</p>\n<ul><li>The <a href="/wiki/Flagship" title="Flagship">flagship</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Trinidad_(ship)" title="Trinidad (ship)">Trinidad</a></i> (110 tons, crew 55), under Magellan\'s command</li>\n<li><i>San Antonio</i> (120 tons; crew 60) commanded by Juan de Cartagena</li>\n<li><i>Concepci&#243;n</i> (90 tons, crew 45) commanded by Gaspar de Quesada</li>\n<li><i>Santiago</i> (75 tons, crew 32) commanded by <a href="/wiki/Jo%C3%A3o_Serr%C3%A3o" title="Jo&#227;o Serr&#227;o">Jo&#227;o Serr&#227;o</a></li>\n<li><i><a href="/wiki/Victoria_(ship)" title="Victoria (ship)">Victoria</a></i> (85 tons, crew 43), named after the church of Santa Maria de la Victoria de Triana, where Magellan took an oath of allegiance to Charles V; commanded by Luis Mendoza.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></li></ul>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Crew">Crew</span></h3>\n<p>The crew of about 270 included men from several nations, including Spain, Portugal, Italy, Germany, Belgium, Greece, England and France.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Levinson2001_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Levinson2001-20">[20]</a></sup> Spanish authorities were wary of Magellan, so that they almost prevented him from sailing, switching his mostly Portuguese crew to mostly men of Spain. It included about 40 Portuguese, among them Magellan\'s brother-in-law <a href="/wiki/Duarte_Barbosa" title="Duarte Barbosa">Duarte Barbosa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jo%C3%A3o_Serr%C3%A3o" title="Jo&#227;o Serr&#227;o">Jo&#227;o Serr&#227;o</a>, a relative of Francisco Serr&#227;o, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Esteban_G%C3%B3mez" title="Esteban G&#243;mez">Est&#234;v&#227;o Gomes</a> and Magellan\'s indentured servant <a href="/wiki/Enrique_of_Malacca" title="Enrique of Malacca">Enrique of Malacca</a>. Faleiro, who had planned to accompany the voyage, withdrew prior to boarding.\n<a href="/wiki/Juan_Sebasti%C3%A1n_Elcano" title="Juan Sebasti&#225;n Elcano">Juan Sebasti&#225;n Elcano</a>, a Spanish merchant ship captain settled at Seville, embarked seeking the king\'s pardon for previous misdeeds.  <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Pigafetta" title="Antonio Pigafetta">Antonio Pigafetta</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Venice" title="Republic of Venice">Venetian</a> scholar and traveller, asked to be on the voyage, accepting the title of "<a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/supernumerary" title="wikt:supernumerary">supernumerary</a>" and a modest salary. He became a strict assistant of Magellan and kept an accurate journal. The only other sailor to report the voyage would be Francisco Albo, who kept a formal <a href="/wiki/Logbook" title="Logbook">logbook</a>.\n<a href="/wiki/Juan_de_Cartagena" title="Juan de Cartagena">Juan de Cartagena</a> was named Inspector General of the expedition, responsible for its financial and trading operations.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Departure_and_crossing_of_the_Atlantic">Departure and crossing of the Atlantic</span></h3>\n<p>On 10 August 1519, the five ships under Magellan\'s command left <a href="/wiki/Seville" title="Seville">Seville</a> and descended the <a href="/wiki/Guadalquivir" title="Guadalquivir">Guadalquivir River</a> to reach the Atlantic Ocean at <a href="/wiki/Sanl%C3%BAcar_de_Barrameda" title="Sanl&#250;car de Barrameda">Sanl&#250;car de Barrameda</a>, at the mouth of the river. There they remained more than five weeks. Finally they set sail on 20 September 1519 and left Spain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>\n</p><p>King <a href="/wiki/Manuel_I_of_Portugal" title="Manuel I of Portugal">Manuel I</a> ordered a Portuguese naval detachment to pursue Magellan, but the explorer evaded them. After stopping at the <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a>, Magellan arrived at <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde" title="Cape Verde">Cape Verde</a>, where he set course for Cape St. Augustine in <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. On 27 November the expedition crossed the <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">equator</a>; on 6 December the crew sighted South America.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p><p>On 13 December anchored near present-day <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>. Although in 1500, Pedro Alvares Cabral claimed the eastern most shores of Brazil for Portugal, Portugal did not maintain a permanent settlement there to protect its brazilwood monopoly (the French were able to help themselves to the timber without interference.) Magellan\'s armada arrived without Portuguese notice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> There the crew was resupplied, but bad conditions caused them to delay. Afterwards, they continued to sail south along South America\'s east coast, looking for the strait that Magellan believed would lead to the Spice Islands. The fleet reached <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_de_la_Plata" title="R&#237;o de la Plata">R&#237;o de la Plata</a> in early February, 1520.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup>\n</p><p>For overwintering, Magellan established a temporary settlement called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Puerto_San_Julian" title="Puerto San Julian">Puerto San Julian</a> on March 30, 1520. On Easter (April 1 and 2), a mutiny broke out involving three of the five ship captains. Magellan took quick and decisive action. Luis de Mendoza, the captain of <i>Victoria</i>, was killed by a party sent by Magellan, and the ship was recovered. After <i>Concepci&#243;n\'s</i> anchor cable had been secretly cut by his forces, the ship drifted towards the well-armed <i>Trinidad</i>, and <i>Concepcion\'s</i> captain de Quesada and his inner circle surrendered. Juan de Cartagena, the head of the mutineers on the <i>San Antonio,</i> subsequently gave up. <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Pigafetta" title="Antonio Pigafetta">Antonio Pigafetta</a> reported that Gaspar Quesada, the captain of <i>Concepci&#243;n</i>, and other mutineers were executed, while Juan de Cartagena, the captain of <i>San Antonio</i>, and a priest named Padre Sanchez de la Reina were <a href="/wiki/Marooning" title="Marooning">marooned</a> on the coast. Most of the men, including <a href="/wiki/Juan_Sebasti%C3%A1n_Elcano" title="Juan Sebasti&#225;n Elcano">Juan Sebasti&#225;n Elcano</a>, were needed and forgiven.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Reportedly those killed were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drawn_and_quartered" title="Drawn and quartered">drawn and quartered</a> and <a href="/wiki/Impalement" title="Impalement">impaled</a> on the coast; years later, their bones were found by Sir <a href="/wiki/Francis_Drake" title="Francis Drake">Francis Drake</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Passage_into_the_Pacific">Passage into the Pacific</span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Strait_of_Magellan.jpeg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="805" data-file-width="1009" decoding="async" height="176" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Strait_of_Magellan.jpeg/220px-Strait_of_Magellan.jpeg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Strait_of_Magellan.jpeg/330px-Strait_of_Magellan.jpeg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Strait_of_Magellan.jpeg/440px-Strait_of_Magellan.jpeg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Strait_of_Magellan.jpeg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/Strait_of_Magellan" title="Strait of Magellan">Strait of Magellan</a> cuts through the southern tip of South America connecting the Atlantic Ocean and Pacific Ocean.</div></div></div>\n<p>The journey resumed. The help of Duarte Barbosa was crucial in facing the riot in Puerto San Julian; Magellan appointed him as captain of the <i><a href="/wiki/Victoria_(ship)" title="Victoria (ship)">Victoria</a></i>. The <i>Santiago</i> was sent down the coast on a scouting expedition and was wrecked in a sudden storm. All of its crew survived and made it safely to shore. Two of them returned overland to inform Magellan of what had happened, and to bring rescue to their comrades. After this experience, Magellan decided to wait for a few weeks more before resuming the voyage with the four remaining ships.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p><p>At 52&#176;S latitude on 21 October 1520, the fleet reached <a href="/wiki/Cape_Virgenes" title="Cape Virgenes">Cape Virgenes</a> and concluded they had found the passage, because the waters were <a href="/wiki/Brine" title="Brine">brine</a> and deep inland. Four ships began an arduous trip through the 373-mile (600&#160;km) long passage that Magellan called the <i>Estrecho (Canal) de Todos los Santos</i>, ("All Saints\' Channel"), because the fleet travelled through it on 1 November or <a href="/wiki/All_Saints%27_Day" title="All Saints\' Day">All Saints\' Day</a>. The strait is now named the <a href="/wiki/Strait_of_Magellan" title="Strait of Magellan">Strait of Magellan</a>. He first assigned <i>Concepcion</i> and <i>San Antonio</i> to explore the strait, but the latter, commanded by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Esteban_G%C3%B3mez" title="Esteban G&#243;mez">G&#243;mez</a>, deserted and headed back to Spain on 20 November. On 28 November, the three remaining ships entered the South Pacific. Magellan named the waters the <i>Mar Pacifico</i> (Pacific Ocean) because of its apparent stillness.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NewAdvent09526b_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-NewAdvent09526b-27">[27]</a></sup> Magellan and his crew were the first Europeans to reach <a href="/wiki/Tierra_del_Fuego" title="Tierra del Fuego">Tierra del Fuego</a> just east of the Pacific side of the strait.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Death_in_the_Philippines">Death in the Philippines</span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:MactanShrineTower2.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2048" data-file-width="1536" decoding="async" height="293" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/MactanShrineTower2.jpg/220px-MactanShrineTower2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/MactanShrineTower2.jpg/330px-MactanShrineTower2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/MactanShrineTower2.jpg/440px-MactanShrineTower2.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:MactanShrineTower2.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Monument in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lapu-Lapu_City" title="Lapu-Lapu City">Lapu-Lapu City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cebu" title="Cebu">Cebu</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>.</div></div></div>\n<p>Heading northwest, the crew reached the equator on 13 February 1521. On 6 March they reached the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marianas" title="Marianas">Marianas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">Guam</a>. Pigafetta described the "lateen sail" used by the inhabitants of Guam, hence the name "Island of Sails", but he also writes the inhabitants "entered the ships and stole whatever they could lay their hands on", including "the small boat that was fastened to the poop of the flagship."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nowell_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nowell-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>129</span></sup> "Those people are poor, but ingenious and very thievish, on account of which we called those three islands the islands of Ladroni."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nowell_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-Nowell-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>131</span></sup>\n</p><p>On 16 March Magellan reached the island of <a href="/wiki/Homonhon" title="Homonhon">Homonhon</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>, with 150 crew left. Members of his expedition became the first Europeans to reach the Philippine archipelago.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Magellan relied on <a href="/wiki/Enrique_of_Malacca" title="Enrique of Malacca">Enrique</a>, his <a href="/wiki/Malays_(ethnic_group)" title="Malays (ethnic group)">Malay</a> servant and interpreter, to communicate with the native tribes. He had been <a href="/wiki/Indentured_servitude" title="Indentured servitude">indentured</a> by Magellan in 1511 after the colonization of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Melaka#European_colonization" title="Melaka">Malacca</a>, and had accompanied him through later adventures. They traded gifts with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rajah" title="Rajah">Rajah</a> Siaiu of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mazaua&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mazaua (page does not exist)">Mazaua</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> who guided them to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cebu_Island" title="Cebu Island">Cebu</a> on 7 April.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Rajah_Humabon" title="Rajah Humabon">Rajah Humabon</a> of Cebu was friendly towards Magellan and the Spaniards; both he and his queen Hara Amihan were baptized as Christians and were given the image of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Holy_Child" title="Holy Child">Holy Child</a> (later known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Santo_Ni%C3%B1o_de_Cebu" title="Santo Ni&#241;o de Cebu">Santo Ni&#241;o de Cebu</a>) which along with a cross (<a href="/wiki/Magellan%27s_Cross" title="Magellan\'s Cross">Magellan\'s Cross</a>) symbolizes the Christianization of the Philippines. Afterward, Rajah Humabon and his ally Datu Zula convinced Magellan to kill their enemy, Datu <a href="/wiki/Lapu-Lapu" title="Lapu-Lapu">Lapu-Lapu</a>, on Mactan. Magellan wanted to convert Lapu-Lapu to Christianity, as he had Humabon, but Lapu-Lapu rejected that. On the morning of 27 April 1521, Magellan sailed to Mactan with a small force. During the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Mactan" title="Battle of Mactan">resulting battle</a> against Lapu-Lapu\'s troops, Magellan was struck by a bamboo spear, and later surrounded and finished off with other weapons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eyewitnesstohistory1_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-eyewitnesstohistory1-31">[31]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Pigafetta and Gin&#233;s de Mafra provided written documents of the events culminating in Magellan\'s death:\n</p>\n<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r856303468">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>When morning came, forty-nine of us leaped into the water up to our thighs, and walked through water for more than two cross-bow flights before we could reach the shore. The boats could not approach nearer because of certain rocks in the water. The other eleven men remained behind to guard the boats. When we reached land, [the natives] had formed in three divisions to the number of more than one thousand five hundred people. When they saw us, they charged down upon us with exceeding loud cries... The musketeers and crossbow-men shot from a distance for about a half-hour, but uselessly... Recognizing the captain, so many turned upon him that they knocked his helmet off his head twice... A native hurled a bamboo spear into the captain\'s face, but the latter immediately killed him with his lance, which he left in the native\'s body. Then, trying to lay hand on sword, he could draw it out but halfway, because he had been wounded in the arm with a bamboo spear. When the natives saw that, they all hurled themselves upon him. One of them wounded him on the left leg with a large cutlass, which resembles a scimitar, only being larger. That caused the captain to fall face downward, when immediately they rushed upon him with iron and bamboo spears and with their cutlasses, until they killed our mirror, our light, our comfort, and our true guide. When they wounded him, he turned back many times to see whether we were all in the boats. Thereupon, beholding him dead, we, wounded, retreated, as best we could, to the boats, which were already pulling off.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eyewitnesstohistory1_31-1"><a href="#cite_note-eyewitnesstohistory1-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint noexcerpt Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTRS" title="Wikipedia:NOTRS"><span title="Eyewitness to History series is not high quality (March 2015)">better&#160;source&#160;needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p></blockquote>\n<p>Magellan provided in his will that Enrique, his interpreter, was to be freed upon his death. But after the battle, the remaining ships\' masters refused to free the Malay. Enrique escaped his indenture on 1 May with the aid of Rajah Humabon, amid the deaths of almost 30 crewmen.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p><p>Pigafetta had been jotting down words in both <a href="/wiki/Butuanon_language" title="Butuanon language">Butuanon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cebuano_language" title="Cebuano language">Cebuano languages</a> &#8211; which he started at Mazaua on 29 March and his list grew to a total of 145 words. He continued communications with indigenous peoples during the rest of the voyage.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p>\n<blockquote><p>"Nothing of Magellan\'s body survived, that afternoon the grieving rajah-king, hoping to recover his remains, offered Mactan\'s victorious chief a handsome ransom of copper and iron for them but Datu Lapulapu refused. He intended to keep the body as a war trophy. Since his wife and child died in Seville before any member of the expedition could return to Spain, it seemed that every evidence of Ferdinand Magellan\'s existence had vanished from the earth."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p></blockquote>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return">Return</span></h3>\n<div class="center"><div class="thumb tnone"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:802px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Magellan_Elcano_Circumnavigation-en.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1139" data-file-width="2246" decoding="async" height="406" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/Magellan_Elcano_Circumnavigation-en.svg/800px-Magellan_Elcano_Circumnavigation-en.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/Magellan_Elcano_Circumnavigation-en.svg/1200px-Magellan_Elcano_Circumnavigation-en.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/Magellan_Elcano_Circumnavigation-en.svg/1600px-Magellan_Elcano_Circumnavigation-en.svg.png 2x" width="800"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Magellan_Elcano_Circumnavigation-en.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><center>The Magellan&#8211;Elcano voyage. <i><a href="/wiki/Victoria_(ship)" title="Victoria (ship)">Victoria</a></i>, one of the original five ships, circumnavigated the globe, finishing 16 months after Magellan\'s death.</center></div></div></div></div>\n<p>The casualties suffered in the Philippines left the expedition with too few men to sail all three of the remaining ships. Consequently, on 2 May they abandoned and burned <i>Concepci&#243;n.</i> Reduced to <i>Trinidad</i> and <i>Victoria</i>, the expedition fled westward to <a href="/wiki/Palawan" title="Palawan">Palawan</a>. They left that island on 21 June and were guided to <a href="/wiki/Brunei" title="Brunei">Brunei</a>, <a href="/wiki/Borneo" title="Borneo">Borneo</a>, by <a href="/wiki/Moro_people" title="Moro people">Moro</a> pilots, who could navigate the shallow seas. They anchored off the Brunei breakwater for 35 days, where Pigafetta, an Italian from <a href="/wiki/Vicenza" title="Vicenza">Vicenza</a>, recorded the splendour of Rajah Siripada\'s court (gold, two <a href="/wiki/Pearl" title="Pearl">pearls</a> the size of hens\' eggs, <a href="/wiki/Porcelain" title="Porcelain">porcelain</a> from China, <a href="/wiki/Glasses" title="Glasses">eyeglasses</a> from Europe etc.). In addition, Brunei boasted tame elephants and an armament of 62 cannons, more than five times the armament of Magellan\'s ships. Brunei people were not interested in the Spanish cargo of <a href="/wiki/Clove" title="Clove">cloves</a>, but these proved more valuable than gold upon the return to Spain.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p><p>When reaching the <a href="/wiki/Maluku_Islands" title="Maluku Islands">Maluku Islands</a> (the Spice Islands) on 6 November, the total crew numbered 115. They traded with the <a href="/wiki/Sultanate_of_Tidore" title="Sultanate of Tidore">Sultan of Tidore</a>, a rival of the <a href="/wiki/Sultanate_of_Ternate" title="Sultanate of Ternate">Sultan of Ternate</a>, who was the ally of the Portuguese.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p><p>The two remaining ships, laden with valuable spices, tried to return to Spain by sailing westwards. However, as they left the Spice Islands, the <i>Trinidad</i> began to take on water. The crew tried to discover and repair the leak, but failed. They concluded that <i>Trinidad</i> would need to spend considerable time being overhauled, but the small <i>Victoria</i> was not large enough to accommodate all the surviving crew. As a result, <i>Victoria</i> with some of the crew sailed west for Spain. Several weeks later, <i>Trinidad</i> departed and tried to return to Spain via the Pacific route. This attempt failed. <i>Trinidad</i> was captured by the Portuguese and was eventually wrecked in a storm while at anchor under Portuguese control.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p><p><i>Victoria</i> set sail via the Indian Ocean route home on 21 December, commanded by <a href="/wiki/Juan_Sebasti%C3%A1n_Elcano" title="Juan Sebasti&#225;n Elcano">Juan Sebasti&#225;n Elcano</a>. By 6 May 1522 the <i>Victoria</i> rounded the <a href="/wiki/Cape_of_Good_Hope" title="Cape of Good Hope">Cape of Good Hope</a>, with only rice for rations. Twenty crewmen died of starvation before Elcano put into <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde" title="Cape Verde">Cape Verde</a>, a Portuguese holding, where he abandoned 13 more crew on 9 July in fear of losing his cargo of 26 <a href="/wiki/Ton" title="Ton">tons</a> of spices (cloves and <a href="/wiki/Cinnamon" title="Cinnamon">cinnamon</a>).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\nOn 6 September 1522, Elcano and the remaining crew of Magellan\'s voyage arrived in Spain aboard the <i>Victoria</i>, almost exactly three years after the fleet of five ships had departed. Magellan had not intended to circumnavigate the world, but rather had intended only to find a secure route through which the Spanish ships could navigate to the Spice Islands. After Magellan\'s death, Elcano decided to push westward, thereby completing the first known voyage around the entire Earth.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Maximilianus_Transylvanus" title="Maximilianus Transylvanus">Maximilianus Transylvanus</a> interviewed some of the surviving members of the expedition when they presented themselves to the Spanish court at <a href="/wiki/Valladolid" title="Valladolid">Valladolid</a> in the autumn of 1522. He wrote the first account of the voyage, which was published in 1523. Pigafetta\'s account was not published until 1525, and was not published in its entirety until 1800. This was the Italian transcription by <a href="/wiki/Carlo_Amoretti" title="Carlo Amoretti">Carlo Amoretti</a> of what is now called the "Ambrosiana codex." The expedition eked out a small profit, but the crew was not paid full wages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Four crewmen of the original 55 on <i>Trinidad</i> finally returned to Spain in 1522; 51 had died in war or from disease. In total, approximately 232 sailors of assorted nationalities died on the expedition around the world with Magellan.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Survivors">Survivors</span></h3>\n<p>When <i>Victoria</i>, the one surviving ship and the smallest carrack in the fleet, returned to the harbor of departure after completing the first circumnavigation of the Earth, only 18 men out of the original 237 men were on board. Among the survivors were two Italians, <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Pigafetta" title="Antonio Pigafetta">Antonio Pigafetta</a> and Martino de Judicibus. <b>Martino de Judicibus</b> (Spanish: <i lang="es"><b>Mart&#237;n de Judicibus</b></i>) was a <a href="/wiki/Genoa" title="Genoa">Genoese</a> or Savonese<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chief_Steward" title="Chief Steward">Chief Steward</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> His history is preserved in the nominative registers at the <i>Archivo General de Indias</i> in <a href="/wiki/Seville" title="Seville">Seville</a>, Spain. The family name is referred to with the exact Latin patronymic, "de Judicibus". Martino de Judicibus, initially assigned to the caravel <i>Concepci&#243;n</i>, one of five ships of the Spanish fleet of Magellan, had embarked on the expedition with the rank of captain.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. The statue looks towards the Strait of Magellan.</div></div></div>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Antonio_Pigafetta" title="Antonio Pigafetta">Antonio Pigafetta\'s</a> journal is the main source for much of what is known about Magellan and Elcano\'s voyage. The other direct report of the voyage was that of Francisco Albo, the last <i>Victoria\'</i>s pilot, who kept a formal <a href="/wiki/Logbook" title="Logbook">logbook</a>. Europeans first learned of the circumnavigation through an account written by <a href="/wiki/Maximilianus_Transylvanus" title="Maximilianus Transylvanus">Maximilianus Transylvanus</a>, a relative of sponsor <a href="/wiki/Christopher_de_Haro" title="Christopher de Haro">Christopher de Haro</a>, who interviewed survivors in 1522 and published his account in 1523.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p><p>Since there was not a set limit to the east, in 1524 both kingdoms had tried to find the exact location of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antimeridian" title="Antimeridian">antimeridian</a> of Tordesillas, which would divide the world into two equal <a href="/wiki/Sphere" title="Sphere">hemispheres</a> and to resolve the "Moluccas issue". A board met several times without reaching an agreement: the knowledge at that time was insufficient for an accurate calculation of <a href="/wiki/Longitude" title="Longitude">longitude</a>, and each gave the islands to their sovereign. An agreement was reached only with the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Zaragoza" title="Treaty of Zaragoza">Treaty of Zaragoza</a>, signed on 1529 between Spain and <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>. It assigned the Moluccas to Portugal and the Philippines to Spain. The course that Magellan charted was followed by other navigators, such as <a href="/wiki/Francis_Drake" title="Francis Drake">Sir Francis Drake</a>. In 1565, <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9s_de_Urdaneta" title="Andr&#233;s de Urdaneta">Andr&#233;s de Urdaneta</a> discovered the <a href="/wiki/Manila_galleon" title="Manila galleon">Manila-Acapulco route</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p><p>In 1525, soon after the return of Magellan\'s expedition, Charles V sent an expedition led by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Garc%C3%ADa_Jofre_de_Loa%C3%ADsa" title="Garc&#237;a Jofre de Loa&#237;sa">Garc&#237;a Jofre de Loa&#237;sa</a> to occupy the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moluccas" title="Moluccas">Moluccas</a>, claiming that they were in his zone of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Tordesillas" title="Treaty of Tordesillas">Treaty of Tordesillas</a>. This expedition included the most notable Spanish navigators: Juan Sebasti&#225;n Elcano, who, along with many other sailors, died of <a href="/wiki/Malnutrition" title="Malnutrition">malnutrition</a> during the voyage, and the young <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9s_de_Urdaneta" title="Andr&#233;s de Urdaneta">Andr&#233;s de Urdaneta</a>. They had difficulty reaching the Moluccas, docking at <a href="/wiki/Tidore" title="Tidore">Tidore</a>. The Portuguese were already established in nearby <a href="/wiki/Ternate" title="Ternate">Ternate</a> and the two nations had nearly a decade of skirmishing over the "possession." (occupied by indigenous peoples.)<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p><p>Magellan\'s expedition was the first to circumnavigate the globe and the first to navigate the strait in South America connecting the Atlantic and the Pacific oceans. Magellan\'s name for the Pacific was adopted by other Europeans.\n</p><p>Magellan\'s crew observed several animals that were entirely new to European science, including a "<a href="/wiki/Camel" title="Camel">camel</a> without humps", which was probably a <a href="/wiki/Guanaco" title="Guanaco">guanaco</a>, whose range extends to Tierra del Fuego. The <a href="/wiki/Llama" title="Llama">llama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vicu%C3%B1a" title="Vicu&#241;a">vicu&#241;a</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alpaca" title="Alpaca">alpaca</a> natural ranges were in the Andes mountains. A black "<a href="/wiki/Goose" title="Goose">goose</a>" that had to be skinned instead of plucked was a penguin.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p><p>The full extent of the globe was realized, since their voyage was 14,460 Spanish leagues (60,440&#160;km or 37,560&#160;mi). The global expedition showed the need for an <a href="/wiki/International_Date_Line" title="International Date Line">International Date Line</a> to be established. Upon returning the expedition found its date was a day behind, although they had faithfully maintained the ship\'s log. They lost one day because they traveled west during their circumnavigation of the globe, opposite to Earth\'s daily rotation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> This caused great excitement at the time, and a special delegation was sent to the <a href="/wiki/Pope" title="Pope">Pope</a> to explain the oddity to him.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Magellan" title="Order of Magellan">Order of Magellan</a> was established in 1902 to honour those who complete a circumnavigation and make other contributions to humanity.\n</p><p>Two of the closest <a href="/wiki/Galaxy" title="Galaxy">galaxies</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Magellanic_Clouds" title="Magellanic Clouds">Magellanic Clouds</a> in the southern celestial hemisphere, were named for Magellan sometime after 1800. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Magellan_probe" title="Magellan probe">Magellan probe</a>, which mapped the planet <a href="/wiki/Venus" title="Venus">Venus</a> from 1990 to 1994, was named after Magellan. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ferdinand_Magellan_Railcar" title="Ferdinand Magellan Railcar">Ferdinand Magellan train rail car</a> (also known as U.S. Car. No. 1) is a former Pullman Company observation car that was re-built by the U.S. Government for presidential use from 1943 until 1958.\n</p><p>A replica of the <i>Victoria</i>, the only ship of Magellan\'s to survive the entire voyage, can be visited in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Puerto_San_Julian" title="Puerto San Julian">Puerto San Julian</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p><p>Three craters, two located on the <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">Moon</a> and one on <a href="/wiki/Mars" title="Mars">Mars</a>, have been named after Magellan using the spelling "Magelhaens". The names were adopted by the <a href="/wiki/International_Astronomical_Union" title="International Astronomical Union">International Astronomical Union</a> in 1935 (<a href="/wiki/Magelhaens_(lunar_crater)" title="Magelhaens (lunar crater)">Magelhaens</a> on the Moon), 1976 (<a href="/wiki/Magelhaens_(Martian_crater)" title="Magelhaens (Martian crater)">Magelhaens</a> on Mars), and 2006 (Magelhaens A on the Moon).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IAU_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-IAU-6">[6]</a></sup> The asteroid <a href="/wiki/4055_Magellan" title="4055 Magellan">4055 Magellan</a>, discovered in 1985, and the <a href="/wiki/Magellan_(spacecraft)" title="Magellan (spacecraft)">Magellan probe</a> to Venus (1989&#8211;1994) were also named after him.\n</p><p>The five hundredth anniversary of Magellan\'s expedition and circumnavigation will be commemorated in a series of events organised by the municipal council of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sanlucar_de_Barrameda_2019-2022" title="Sanlucar de Barrameda 2019-2022">Sanlucar de Barrameda</a> in Spain, and supported by philanthropic organisations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media_portrayals">Media portrayals</span></h2>\n<ul><li>Portrayed by Oscar Keesee in the 1955 Filipino
