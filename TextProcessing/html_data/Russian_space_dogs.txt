href="/wiki/Dog" title="Dog">dogs</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sub-orbital" title="Sub-orbital">sub-orbital</a> and <a href="/wiki/Orbital_spaceflight" title="Orbital spaceflight">orbital space flights</a> to determine whether human spaceflight were feasible. In this period, the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> launched missions with passenger slots for at least 57 dogs. The number of dogs in space is smaller, as some dogs flew more than once. Most survived; the few that died were lost mostly through technical failures, according to the parameters of the test. A notable exception is <a href="/wiki/Laika" title="Laika">Laika</a>, the first dog to be sent into orbit, whose death was expected from the outset.<sup class="reference" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soviet_space_dogs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Training">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Dogs were the preferred animal for the experiments because scientists felt dogs were well suited to endure long periods of inactivity. As part of their training, they were confined in small boxes for 15&#8211;20 days at a time. Stray dogs, rather than animals accustomed to living in a house, were chosen because the scientists felt they would be able to tolerate the rigorous and extreme stresses of space flight better than other dogs. Female dogs were used because of their temperament and because the suit the dogs wore in order to collect urine and feces was equipped with a special device, designed to work only with females.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spacedogs_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-spacedogs-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spaced_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Spaced-3">[3]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Their training included standing still for long periods of time, wearing <a href="/wiki/Space_suit" title="Space suit">space suits</a>, being placed in simulators that acted like a rocket during launch, riding in <a href="/wiki/Centrifuge" title="Centrifuge">centrifuges</a> that simulated the high acceleration of a rocket launch and being kept in progressively smaller cages to prepare them for the confines of the space module. Dogs that flew in orbit were fed a nutritious jelly-like protein. This was high in fiber and assisted the dogs to eliminate during long periods of time while in their small space module. More than 60% of dogs to enter space were reportedly suffering from <a href="/wiki/Constipation" title="Constipation">constipation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gallstone" title="Gallstone">gallstones</a> on arrival back to base.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sub-orbital_flights">Sub-orbital flights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soviet_space_dogs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sub-orbital flights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<table class="box-More_citations_needed plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Refimprove" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg"><img alt="" data-file-height="399" data-file-width="512" decoding="async" height="39" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" width="50"/></a></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>needs additional citations for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">verification</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Soviet_space_dogs&amp;action=edit">improve this article</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Introduction_to_referencing_with_Wiki_Markup/1" title="Help:Introduction to referencing with Wiki Markup/1">adding citations to reliable sources</a>. The dogs wore pressure suits with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Acrylic_glass" title="Acrylic glass">acrylic glass</a> bubble helmets. From 1957 to 1960, 11 flights with dogs were made on the <a href="/wiki/R-2_(missile)" title="R-2 (missile)">R-2A</a> series, which flew to about 200&#160;km.  Three flights were made to an altitude of about 450&#160;km on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/R-5_(rocket)" title="R-5 (rocket)">R-5A</a> rockets in 1958.  In the R-2 and R-5 rockets, the dogs were contained in a pressured cabin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span id="Dezik.2C_Tsygan.2C_and_Lisa"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Dezik,_Tsygan,_and_Lisa">Dezik, Tsygan, and Lisa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soviet_space_dogs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Dezik, Tsygan, and Lisa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><b>Dezik</b> (&#1044;&#1077;&#1079;&#1080;&#1082;) and <b>Tsygan</b> (&#1062;&#1099;&#1075;&#1072;&#1085;, "Gypsy") were the first dogs to make a sub-orbital flight on 22 July 1951. Both dogs were recovered unharmed after travelling to a maximum altitude of 110&#160;km.  Dezik made another sub-orbital flight in July 29, 1951 with a dog named <b>Lisa</b> (&#1051;&#1080;&#1089;&#1072;, "Fox"), although neither survived because the parachute failed to deploy.  After the death of Dezik, Tsygan was adopted as a pet by Soviet physicist <a href="/wiki/Anatoli_Blagonravov" title="Anatoli Blagonravov">Anatoli Blagonravov</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lisa_and_Ryzhik">Lisa and Ryzhik</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soviet_space_dogs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Lisa and Ryzhik">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><b>Lisa</b> (&#1051;&#1080;&#1089;&#1072;, "Fox" or "Vixen") and <b>Ryzhik</b> (&#1056;&#1099;&#1078;&#1080;&#1082;, "Ginger" (red-haired)) flew to an altitude of 100&#160;km on 2 June 1954.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Smelaya_and_Malyshka">Smelaya and Malyshka</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soviet_space_dogs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Smelaya and Malyshka">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><b>Smelaya</b> (&#1057;&#1084;&#1077;&#1083;&#1072;&#1103;, "Brave" or "Courageous") was due to make a flight in September but ran away the day before the launch. She was found the next day and went on to make a successful flight with a dog named <b>Malyshka</b> (&#1052;&#1072;&#1083;&#1099;&#1096;&#1082;&#1072;, "Baby").\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bobik_and_ZIB">Bobik and ZIB</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soviet_space_dogs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Bobik and ZIB">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><b>Bobik</b> (&#1041;&#1086;&#1073;&#1080;&#1082;) ran away just days before his flight in September 1951. A replacement named <b>ZIB</b> (a Russian acronym for <i>"Substitute for Missing Bobik"</i>, "&#1047;&#1072;&#1084;&#1077;&#1085;&#1072; &#1048;&#1089;&#1095;&#1077;&#1079;&#1085;&#1091;&#1074;&#1096;&#1077;&#1084;&#1091; &#1041;&#1086;&#1073;&#1080;&#1082;&#1091;" <i>Zamena Ischeznuvshemu Bobiku</i>), who was an untrained street dog found running around the barracks, was quickly located and made a successful flight.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Otvazhnaya_and_Snezhinka">Otvazhnaya and Snezhinka</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soviet_space_dogs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Otvazhnaya and Snezhinka">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><b>Otvazhnaya</b> (&#1054;&#1090;&#1074;&#1072;&#1078;&#1085;&#1072;&#1103;, "Brave One" (Female)) made a flight on 2 July 1959 along with a rabbit named <b>Marfusha</b> (&#1052;&#1072;&#1088;&#1092;&#1091;&#1096;&#1072;, "Little Martha") and another dog named <b>Snezhinka</b> (&#1057;&#1085;&#1077;&#1078;&#1080;&#1085;&#1082;&#1072;, "Snowflake"). She went on to make 5 other flights between 1959 and 1960.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Beischer1962_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Beischer1962-7">[7]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Albina_and_Tsyganka">Albina and Tsyganka</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soviet_space_dogs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Albina and Tsyganka">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><b>Albina</b> (&#1040;&#1083;&#1100;&#1073;&#1080;&#1085;&#1072;, a real female name) and <b>Tsyganka</b> (&#1062;&#1099;&#1075;&#1072;&#1085;&#1082;&#1072;, "Gypsy girl") were both ejected out of their capsule at an altitude of 85&#160;km and landed safely. Albina was one of the dogs shortlisted for <a href="/wiki/Sputnik_2" title="Sputnik 2">Sputnik 2</a>, but never flew in orbit.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Damka_and_Krasavka">Damka and Krasavka</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soviet_space_dogs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Damka and Krasavka">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><b>Damka</b> (&#1044;&#1072;&#1084;&#1082;&#1072;, "Queen of checkers") and <b>Krasavka</b> (&#1050;&#1088;&#1072;&#1089;&#1072;&#1074;&#1082;&#1072;, "Little Beauty") were to make an orbital flight on 22 December 1960 as a part of the <a href="/wiki/Vostok_programme" title="Vostok programme">Vostok programme</a> which also included mice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-siberiantimes1_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-siberiantimes1-8">[8]</a></sup> However their mission was marked by a string of equipment failures.\n</p><p>The upper-stage rocket failed and the craft re-entered the atmosphere after reaching a sub-orbital apogee of 214&#160;km.  In the event of unscheduled return to the surface, the craft was to eject the dogs and self-destruct, but the ejection seat failed and the primary destruct mechanism shorted out.  The animals were thus still in the intact capsule when it returned to the surface.  The backup self-destruct mechanism was set to a 60-hour timer, so a team was quickly sent out to locate and recover the capsule.\n</p><p>Although the capsule was reached in deep snow on the first day, there was insufficient remaining daylight to disarm the self-destruct mechanism and open the capsule.  The team could only report that the window was frosted over in the &#8722;45 degree temperatures and no signs of life were detected.  On the second day, however, the dogs were heard barking as the capsule was opened.  The dogs were wrapped in sheepskin coats and flown to Moscow alive, though all the mice aboard the capsule were found dead because of the cold.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Damka was also known as <b>Shutka</b> (&#1064;&#1091;&#1090;&#1082;&#1072;, "Joke") or <b>Zhemchuzhnaya</b> (&#1046;&#1077;&#1084;&#1095;&#1091;&#1078;&#1085;&#1072;&#1103;, "Pearly") and Krasavka was also known as <b>Kometka</b> (&#1050;&#1086;&#1084;&#1077;&#1090;&#1082;&#1072;, "Little Comet") or <b>Zhulka</b> (&#1046;&#1091;&#1083;&#1100;&#1082;&#1072;, "Cheater"). After this incident Krasavka was adopted by <a href="/wiki/Oleg_Gazenko" title="Oleg Gazenko">Oleg Gazenko</a>, a leading soviet scientist working with animals used in space flights. She went on to have puppies and continued living with Gazenko and his family until her death 14 years later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-siberiantimes1_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-siberiantimes1-8">[8]</a></sup>\nAfter the incident <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sergey_Korolyov" title="Sergey Korolyov">Sergey Korolyov</a>, who was the designer of the rocket, wanted to make the story public, but was prevented from doing so by state censorship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bars_and_Lisichka">Bars and Lisichka</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soviet_space_dogs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Bars and Lisichka">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><b>Bars</b> (&#1041;&#1072;&#1088;&#1089; (pron. "Barss" not "Barz"); "<a href="/wiki/Snow_leopard" title="Snow leopard">Snow leopard</a>") and <b>Lisichka</b> (&#1051;&#1080;&#1089;&#1080;&#1095;&#1082;&#1072;, "Little <a href="/wiki/Fox" title="Fox">Fox</a>") were also on a mission to orbit as a part of the <a href="/wiki/Vostok_programme" title="Vostok programme">Vostok programme</a>, but died after their rocket exploded 28.5 seconds into the launch on 28 July 1960. Bars was also known as <b>Chayka</b> (&#1063;&#1072;&#1081;&#1082;&#1072;, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seagull" title="Seagull">Seagull</a>").\n</p><p>Other dogs that flew on sub-orbital flights include <b>Dymka</b> (&#1044;&#1099;&#1084;&#1082;&#1072;, "Smoky"), <b>Modnitsa</b> (&#1052;&#1086;&#1076;&#1085;&#1080;&#1094;&#1072;, "Fashionable") and <b>Kozyavka</b> (&#1050;&#1086;&#1079;&#1103;&#1074;&#1082;&#1072;, "Little Gnat").\n</p><p>At least four other dogs flew in September 1951, and two or more were lost.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" (&#1051;&#1072;&#1081;&#1082;&#1072;, "Barker") became the first living Earth-born creature (other than microbes) in orbit, aboard <a href="/wiki/Sputnik_2" title="Sputnik 2">Sputnik 2</a> on November 3, 1957. Some call her the first living passenger to go into space, but many sub-orbital flights with animal passengers passed the edge of space first. She was also known as Zhuchka (&#1046;&#1091;&#1095;&#1082;&#1072;, "Little Bug") and Limonchik (&#1051;&#1080;&#1084;&#1086;&#1085;&#1095;&#1080;&#1082;, "Little Lemon"). The <a href="/wiki/Media_of_the_United_States" title="Media of the United States">American media</a> dubbed her "Muttnik", making a <a href="/wiki/Pun" title="Pun">play-on-words</a> for the canine follow-on to the first orbital mission, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sputnik" title="Sputnik">Sputnik</a>. She died between five and seven hours into the flight from stress and overheating.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Her true cause of death was not made public until October 2002; officials previously gave reports that she died when the oxygen supply ran out.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Beischer1962_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Beischer1962-7">[7]</a></sup> At a Moscow press conference in 1998 <a href="/wiki/Oleg_Gazenko" title="Oleg Gazenko">Oleg Gazenko</a>, a senior Soviet scientist involved in the project, stated "The more time passes, the more I\'m sorry about it. width="200"/></a></div></div><div class="tsingle" style="float:left;margin:1px;width:202px;max-width:202px"><div class="thumbimage"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Strelka_Dog.jpg"><img alt="" width="200"/></a></div></div><div style="clear:left"></div></div></div>\n<p><b>Belka</b> (&#1041;&#1077;&#1083;&#1082;&#1072;, literally, "Squirrel" or alternatively "Whitey") and <b>Strelka</b> (&#1057;&#1090;&#1088;&#1077;&#1083;&#1082;&#1072;, "Little Arrow") spent a day in space aboard <a href="/wiki/Korabl-Sputnik_2" title="Korabl-Sputnik 2">Korabl-Sputnik 2</a> (Sputnik 5) on 19 August 1960 before safely returning to Earth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Beischer1962_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-Beischer1962-7">[7]</a></sup>\n</p><p>\nThey were accompanied by a grey rabbit, 42 mice, two rats, flies and several plants and fungi. All passengers survived. They were the first Earth-born creatures to go into orbit and return alive.</p><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:%D0%91%D0%B5%D0%BB%D0%BA%D0%B0%D0%A1%D1%82%D1%80%D0%B5%D0%BB%D0%BA%D0%B0_VizuIMG_3703.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2286" data-file-width="2939" decoding="async" height="171" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/%D0%91%D0%B5%D0%BB%D0%BA%D0%B0%D0%A1%D1%82%D1%80%D0%B5%D0%BB%D0%BA%D0%B0_VizuIMG_3703.JPG/220px-%D0%91%D0%B5%D0%BB%D0%BA%D0%B0%D0%A1%D1%82%D1%80%D0%B5%D0%BB%D0%BA%D0%B0_VizuIMG_3703.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/%D0%91%D0%B5%D0%BB%D0%BA%D0%B0%D0%A1%D1%82%D1%80%D0%B5%D0%BB%D0%BA%D0%B0_VizuIMG_3703.JPG/330px-%D0%91%D0%B5%D0%BB%D0%BA%D0%B0%D0%A1%D1%82%D1%80%D0%B5%D0%BB%D0%BA%D0%B0_VizuIMG_3703.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/%D0%91%D0%B5%D0%BB%D0%BA%D0%B0%D0%A1%D1%82%D1%80%D0%B5%D0%BB%D0%BA%D0%B0_VizuIMG_3703.JPG/440px-%D0%91%D0%B5%D0%BB%D0%BA%D0%B0%D0%A1%D1%82%D1%80%D0%B5%D0%BB%D0%BA%D0%B0_VizuIMG_3703.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:%D0%91%D0%B5%D0%BB%D0%BA%D0%B0%D0%A1%D1%82%D1%80%D0%B5%D0%BB%D0%BA%D0%B0_VizuIMG_3703.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Belka and Strelka in <a href="/wiki/Graffiti" title="Graffiti">graffiti</a>. 2008</div></div></div><p>Strelka went on to have six puppies with a male dog named <b>Pushok</b> who participated in many ground-based space experiments, but never made it into space.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-13">[13]</a></sup> One of the pups was named <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pushinka" title="Pushinka">Pushinka</a></i> (&#1055;&#1091;&#1096;&#1080;&#1085;&#1082;&#1072;, "Fluffy") and was presented to President <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a> by <a href="/wiki/Nikita_Khrushchev" title="Nikita Khrushchev">Nikita Khrushchev</a> in 1961. A Cold War romance bloomed between Pushinka and a Kennedy dog named Charlie, resulting in the birth of four pups that JFK referred to jokingly as <i>pupniks</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Two of their pups, <b>Butterfly</b> and <b>Streaker</b> were given away to children in the Midwest. The other two puppies, <b>White Tips</b> and <b>Blackie</b>, stayed at the Kennedy home on Squaw Island but were eventually given away to family friends.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-13">[13]</a></sup> Pushinka\'s descendants are still living today.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (January 2019)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> A photo of descendants of some of the Space Dogs is on display at the Zvezda Museum in <a href="/wiki/Tomilino" title="Tomilino">Tomilino</a> outside Moscow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>\n</p><p>A Russian animated feature film called <i><a href="/wiki/Space_Dogs" title="Space Dogs">Belka and Strelka: Star Dogs</a></i> (English title: <i>Space Dogs</i>) was released in 2010.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pchyolka_and_Mushka">Pchyolka and Mushka</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soviet_space_dogs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Pchyolka and Mushka">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">"Mushka" redirects here. For the racehorse, see <a href="/wiki/Mushka_(horse)" title="Mushka (horse)">Mushka (horse)</a>.</div>\n<p><b>Pchyolka</b> (&#1055;&#1095;&#1105;&#1083;&#1082;&#1072;, "Little Bee") and <b>Mushka</b> (&#1052;&#1091;&#1096;&#1082;&#1072;, "Little Fly") spent a day in orbit on 1 December 1960 on board <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sputnik_6" title="Sputnik 6">Korabl-Sputnik-3</a> (Sputnik 6) with "other animals", plants and insects.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Beischer1962_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-Beischer1962-7">[7]</a></sup> Due to a <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_entry" title="Atmospheric entry">reentry</a> error when the <a href="/wiki/Retrorocket" title="Retrorocket">retrorockets</a> failed to shut off when planned, their spacecraft was intentionally destroyed by remote self-destruct to prevent foreign powers from inspecting the capsule on 2 December and all died.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Mushka was one of the three dogs trained for <a href="/wiki/Sputnik_2" title="Sputnik 2">Sputnik 2</a> and was used during ground tests. She did not fly on Sputnik 2 because she refused to eat properly.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chernushka">Chernushka</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soviet_space_dogs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Chernushka">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Rymdhundarna_Veterok_och_Ugoljok_(16493301588).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1300" data-file-width="1032" decoding="async" height="214" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Rymdhundarna_Veterok_och_Ugoljok_%2816493301588%29.jpg/170px-Rymdhundarna_Veterok_och_Ugoljok_%2816493301588%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Rymdhundarna_Veterok_och_Ugoljok_%2816493301588%29.jpg/255px-Rymdhundarna_Veterok_och_Ugoljok_%2816493301588%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Rymdhundarna_Veterok_och_Ugoljok_%2816493301588%29.jpg/340px-Rymdhundarna_Veterok_och_Ugoljok_%2816493301588%29.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Rymdhundarna_Veterok_och_Ugoljok_(16493301588).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Space dogs Veterok and Ugoljok</div></div></div>\n<p><b>Chernushka</b> (&#1063;&#1077;&#1088;&#1085;&#1091;&#1096;&#1082;&#1072;, "Blackie") made one orbit on board <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sputnik_9" title="Sputnik 9">Korabl-Sputnik-4</a> (Sputnik 9) on 9 March 1961 with a cosmonaut dummy (whom Soviet officials nicknamed <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Ivanovich_(Vostok_programme)" title="Ivan Ivanovich (Vostok programme)">Ivan Ivanovich</a>), <a href="/wiki/Mouse" title="Mouse">mice</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Guinea_pig" title="Guinea pig">guinea pig</a>. The dummy was ejected out of the capsule during re-entry and made a soft landing using a parachute. Chernushka was recovered unharmed inside the capsule.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Zvyozdochka">Zvyozdochka</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soviet_space_dogs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Zvyozdochka">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><b>Zvyozdochka</b> (<b>Zvezdochka</b>, &#1047;&#1074;&#1105;&#1079;&#1076;&#1086;&#1095;&#1082;&#1072;, "Starlet"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-p267_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-p267-16">[16]</a></sup>), who was named by <a href="/wiki/Yuri_Gagarin" title="Yuri Gagarin">Yuri Gagarin</a>,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> made one orbit on board <a href="/wiki/Korabl-Sputnik_5" title="Korabl-Sputnik 5">Korabl-Sputnik 5</a> on 25 March 1961 with a wooden cosmonaut dummy in the final practice flight before Gagarin\'s historic flight on 12 April.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-p267_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-p267-16">[16]</a></sup> Again, the dummy was ejected out of the capsule while Zvezdochka remained inside. Both were recovered successfully.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Veterok_and_Ugolyok">Veterok and Ugolyok</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soviet_space_dogs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Veterok and Ugolyok">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><b>Veterok</b> (&#1042;&#1077;&#1090;&#1077;&#1088;&#1086;&#1082;, "Light Breeze") and <b>Ugolyok</b> (&#1059;&#1075;&#1086;&#1083;&#1105;&#1082;, "Coal") were launched on 22 February 1966 on board <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cosmos_110" title="Cosmos 110">Cosmos 110</a>, and spent 22 days in orbit before landing on 16 March.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spaced_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Spaced-3">[3]</a></sup> This spaceflight of record-breaking duration was not surpassed by humans until <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_11" title="Soyuz 11">Soyuz 11</a> in June 1971 and still stands as the longest space flight by dogs.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
