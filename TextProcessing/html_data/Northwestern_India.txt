template">e</abbr></a></li></ul></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>North-West Frontier Province</b> (<b>NWFP</b>) was a province of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_India" title="British India">British India</a> and subsequently of <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>. It was established in 1901 and was known by this name until 2010. The area became <a href="/wiki/Khyber_Pakhtunkhwa" title="Khyber Pakhtunkhwa">Khyber Pakhtunkhwa</a> province on 19 April 2010 when the <a href="/wiki/Eighteenth_Amendment_to_the_Constitution_of_Pakistan" title="Eighteenth Amendment to the Constitution of Pakistan">Eighteenth Amendment</a> was signed by President <a href="/wiki/Asif_Ali_Zardari" title="Asif Ali Zardari">Asif Ali Zardari</a>.\n</p><p>The province covered an area of 70,709&#160;km&#178;, including much of the current Khyber Pakhtunkhwa but excluding the <a href="/wiki/Princely_state" title="Princely state">princely states</a> of <a href="/wiki/Amb_(princely_state)" title="Amb (princely state)">Amb</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chitral_(princely_state)" title="Chitral (princely state)">Chitral</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dir_(princely_state)" title="Dir (princely state)">Dir</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phulra_(princely_state)" title="Phulra (princely state)">Phulra</a> and <a href="/wiki/Swat_(princely_state)" title="Swat (princely state)">Swat</a>. The capital was the city of <a href="/wiki/Peshawar" title="Peshawar">Peshawar</a>, and the province was composed of three divisions (<a href="/wiki/Peshawar_Division" title="Peshawar Division">Peshawar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dera_Ismail_Khan_Division" title="Dera Ismail Khan Division">Dera Ismail Khan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Malakand_Division" title="Malakand Division">Malakand</a>). Until 1947, the province was bordered by five princely states to the north, the minor states of the <a href="/wiki/Gilgit_Agency" title="Gilgit Agency">Gilgit Agency</a> to the northeast, the province of <a href="/wiki/West_Punjab" title="West Punjab">West Punjab</a> to the east and the province of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baluchistan_(Chief_Commissioners_Province)" title="Baluchistan (Chief Commissioners Province)">Balochistan</a> to the south. <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Afghanistan" title="Kingdom of Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a> lay to the northwest, with the tribal agencies forming a buffer zone.\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc"><input class="toctogglecheckbox" id="toctogglecheckbox" role="button" style="display:none" type="checkbox"/><div class="toctitle" dir="ltr" class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North-West_Frontier_Province_(1901%E2%80%932010)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Most of the territory of this province was part of the <a href="/wiki/Durrani_Empire" title="Durrani Empire">Durrani Empire</a> from the 18th century to around the 1820s, when <i>Maharaja</i> <a href="/wiki/Ranjit_Singh" title="Ranjit Singh">Ranjit Singh</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Sikh_Empire" title="Sikh Empire">Sikh Empire</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Lahore" title="Lahore">Lahore</a>, taking advantage of the internal chaos of the Afghan ruling family, annexed it to his empire.\n</p><p>Later on, after the <a href="/wiki/Second_Anglo-Sikh_War" title="Second Anglo-Sikh War">Second Anglo-Sikh War</a> of 1848&#8211;1849, when the Punjab came under the control of British East India Company,  this region along with the \'Frontier Tribal Areas\' acted as a \'buffer\' zone with Afghanistan. The Province was formally created in 1901 by the British administration, out of the North-Westerly areas of the originally <a href="/wiki/Pashtunistan" title="Pashtunistan">Pashtun</a> lands which were merged with old Punjab, initially under a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chief_Commissioner" title="Chief Commissioner">Chief Commissioner</a>, and then a full-fledged Governor beginning circa 1938.\n</p><p>At the <a href="/wiki/Partition_of_India" title="Partition of India">Partition of India</a>, a referendum was held to decide the future of NWFP. Following a boycott of the vote by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Khan_Abdul_Ghaffar_Khan" title="Khan Abdul Ghaffar Khan">Bacha Khan</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Khudai_Khidmatgars" title="Khudai Khidmatgars">Khudai Khidmatgars</a>, the NWFP became part of the new nation of <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>. The province lasted until 1955 when it was merged into the new province of <a href="/wiki/West_Pakistan" title="West Pakistan">West Pakistan</a>, under the <a href="/wiki/One_Unit" title="One Unit">One Unit</a> policy  announced by <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Pakistan" title="Prime Minister of Pakistan">Prime Minister</a> <a href="/wiki/Chaudhry_Mohammad_Ali" title="Chaudhry Mohammad Ali">Chaudhry Mohammad Ali</a>. <a href="/wiki/Mianwali" title="Mianwali">Mianwali</a> and <a href="/wiki/Attock" title="Attock">Attock</a> were removed from it and merged with <a href="/wiki/Punjab" title="Punjab">Punjab</a>. It was recreated after the dissolution of the One Unit system and lasted under its old nomenclature until April 2010, when it was renamed as the \'<a href="/wiki/Khyber_Pakhtunkhwa" title="Khyber Pakhtunkhwa">Khyber Pakhtunkhwa</a>\' province.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North-West_Frontier_Province_(1901%E2%80%932010)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>At independence there was a clear Muslim Pashtun majority in then North-West Frontier Province, although there were some small minorities of Hindus and Sikhs. The languages of the North-West Frontier Province included Pashto, Hindko, Kohistani and others, although most of the population spoke <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pashto_language" title="Pashto language">Pashto</a>. Prior to the arrival of the British, the <i><a href="/wiki/Official_language" title="Official language">official language</a></i>, for governmental uses and such, was Persian.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government">Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North-West_Frontier_Province_(1901%E2%80%932010)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The offices of <b>Governor</b> and <b>Chief Minister</b> of the North-West Frontier Province lasted until 14 October 1955.\n</p>\n<table class="wikitable">\n<tbody><tr>\n<th>Tenure</th>\n<th>Governors of the North-West Frontier Province <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-worldstatesmen_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-worldstatesmen-1">[1]</a></sup>\n</th></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>14 August 1947 &#8211; 8 April 1948</td>\n<td><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/George_Cunningham_(governor)" title="George Cunningham (governor)">Sir George Cunningham</a>\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>8 April 1948 &#8211; 16 July 1949</td>\n<td><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ambrose_Dundas" title="Ambrose Dundas">Sir Ambrose Dundas Flux Dundas</a>\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>16 July 1949 &#8211; 14 January 1950</td>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Sahibzada_Mohammad_Kursheed" title="Sahibzada Mohammad Kursheed">Sahibzada Mohammad Kursheed</a>\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>14 January 1950 &#8211; 21 February 1950</td>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Mohammad_Ibrahim_Khan_Jhagra" title="Mohammad Ibrahim Khan Jhagra">Mohammad Ibrahim Khan Jhagra</a> (acting)\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>21 February 1950 &#8211; 23 November 1951</td>\n<td><a href="/wiki/I._I._Chundrigar" title="I.
