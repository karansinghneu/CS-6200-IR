Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>\n<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>\n<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>\n<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Spaceflight osteopenia</b> refers to the characteristic <a href="/wiki/Osteoporosis" title="Osteoporosis">bone loss</a> that occurs during <a href="/wiki/Spaceflight" title="Spaceflight">spaceflight</a>.  <a href="/wiki/Astronaut" title="Astronaut">Astronauts</a> lose an average of more than 1% bone mass per month spent in <a href="/wiki/Outer_space" title="Outer space">space</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> There is concern that during long duration flights, excessive bone loss and the associated increase in serum <a href="/wiki/Calcium" title="Calcium">calcium</a> ion levels will interfere with execution of mission tasks and result in irreversible <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Skeletal" title="Skeletal">skeletal</a> damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NASA_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-NASA-2">[2]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc"><input class="toctogglecheckbox" id="toctogglecheckbox" role="button" style="display:none" type="checkbox"/><div class="toctitle" dir="ltr" lang="en"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#History"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">History</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Cause"><span class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spaceflight_osteopenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Bone loss has been observed during spaceflight since at least as early as <a href="/wiki/Project_Gemini" title="Project Gemini">Gemini</a> in the 1960s.  Although most early measurements of the amount of bone loss were not reliable, they did show bone loss in Gemini, <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_9" title="Soyuz 9">Soyuz 9</a>, <a href="/wiki/Apollo_program" title="Apollo program">Apollo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Skylab" title="Skylab">Skylab</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salyut_7" title="Salyut 7">Salyut 7</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mir" title="Mir">Mir</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/William_E._Thornton" title="William E. Thornton">William E. Thornton</a>, an astronaut and physician, was one of the biggest proponents of exercise as a way of preventing bone loss.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cavanagh_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-cavanagh-4">[4]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cause">Cause</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spaceflight_osteopenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cause">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Bone remodels in response to stress in order to maintain constant strain energy per bone mass throughout.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marzban_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Marzban-5">[5]</a></sup>  To do this, it grows more dense in areas experiencing high stress, while resorbing density in areas experiencing low stress.  On Mars, where gravity is about one-third that of earth, the gravitational forces acting on astronauts\' bodies would be much lower, causing bones to decrease in mass and density.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ACSM_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-ACSM-6">[6]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Average bone loss of 1-2% was recorded in astronauts on Mir each month.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NASA_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-NASA-2">[2]</a></sup>  This is in comparison to 1&#8211;1.5% bone loss in the elderly <i>per year</i>, and 2&#8211;3% in postmenopausal women.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Countermeasures">Countermeasures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spaceflight_osteopenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Countermeasures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:TVIS_treadmill.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3030" data-file-width="2002" decoding="async" height="333" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/TVIS_treadmill.jpg/220px-TVIS_treadmill.jpg" href="/wiki/File:TVIS_treadmill.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Astronaut <a href="/wiki/Sunita_Williams" title="Sunita Williams">Sunita "Suni" Williams</a> bungeed to the <a href="/wiki/Treadmill_with_Vibration_Isolation_Stabilization" title="Treadmill with Vibration Isolation Stabilization">TVIS treadmill</a> aboard the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a>.</div></div></div>\n<p>Since Gemini, exercise has been tried as a way of preventing bone loss, but it has not been shown to be successful. This may be in part due to lack of adequately designed studies (no controlled study had been done as of 2005, either in space or using bedrest as an attempt to simulate conditions which lead to bone loss). It is not known whether a different exercise regimen (perhaps including larger loads than past ones) would be effective.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cavanagh_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-cavanagh-4">[4]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Increasing dietary <a href="/wiki/Calcium" title="Calcium">calcium</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vitamin_D" title="Vitamin D">vitamin D</a> is a standard countermeasure for <a href="/wiki/Osteoporosis" title="Osteoporosis">osteoporosis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cavanagh_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-cavanagh-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Clay" title="Clay">Clay</a> is reportedly used by NASA for retaining calcium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>\n</p><p>A variety of drug remedies currently used or proposed for osteoporosis may work for spaceflight, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hormone_replacement_therapy_(menopause)" title="Hormone replacement therapy (menopause)">hormone therapy</a> (estrogen and/or progestin), <a href="/wiki/Selective_estrogen_receptor_modulator" title="Selective estrogen receptor modulator">selective estrogen receptor modulators</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bisphosphonate" title="Bisphosphonate">bisphosphonates</a>, <a href="/wiki/Teriparatide" title="Teriparatide">teriparatide</a>, and others. Whether they can provide the same benefits for spaceflight as they do for osteoporosis is not yet known.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cavanagh_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-cavanagh-4">[4]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
