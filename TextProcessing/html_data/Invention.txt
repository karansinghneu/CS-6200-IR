For other uses, see <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Invention_(disambiguation)" title="Invention (disambiguation)">Invention (disambiguation)</a>.</div>\n<p class="mw-empty-elt">\n</p>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Further information on inventions throughout history: <a href="/wiki/Timeline_of_historic_inventions" title="Timeline of historic inventions">Timeline of historic inventions</a></div>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Science_and_Invention_Nov_1928_Cover_2.jpg"><img alt="Cover of Science and Invention Magazine" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1163" data-file-width="850" decoding="async" height="301" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/Science_and_Invention_Nov_1928_Cover_2.jpg/220px-Science_and_Invention_Nov_1928_Cover_2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/Science_and_Invention_Nov_1928_Cover_2.jpg/330px-Science_and_Invention_Nov_1928_Cover_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/Science_and_Invention_Nov_1928_Cover_2.jpg/440px-Science_and_Invention_Nov_1928_Cover_2.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Science_and_Invention_Nov_1928_Cover_2.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\'BUILD YOUR OWN TELEVISION RECEIVER.\' <i><a href="/wiki/Electrical_Experimenter" title="Electrical Experimenter">Science and Invention</a></i> magazine cover, November 1928</div></div></div>\n<p>An <b>invention</b> is a unique or <a href="/wiki/Novelty_(patent)" title="Novelty (patent)">novel</a> <a href="/wiki/Machine" title="Machine">device</a>, method, composition or process. The invention process is a process within an overall <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">engineering</a> and product development process. It may be an improvement upon a machine or product or a new process for creating an object or a result.  An invention that achieves a completely unique function or result may be a radical breakthrough.  Such works are novel and <a href="/wiki/Inventive_step_and_non-obviousness" title="Inventive step and non-obviousness">not obvious</a> to <a href="/wiki/Person_having_ordinary_skill_in_the_art" title="Person having ordinary skill in the art">others skilled in the same field</a>. An inventor may be taking a big step in success or failure.\n</p><p>Some inventions can be patented.  A <a href="/wiki/Patent" title="Patent">patent</a> legally protects the intellectual property rights of the inventor and legally recognizes that a claimed invention is actually an invention.  The rules and requirements for patenting an invention vary from country to country and the process of obtaining a patent is often expensive.\n</p><p>Another meaning of invention is <b><a href="/wiki/Cultural_invention" title="Cultural invention">cultural invention</a></b>, which is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Innovative" title="Innovative">innovative</a> set of useful <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Social_behaviour" title="Social behaviour">social behaviours</a> adopted by people and passed on to others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Institute_for_Social_Inventions" title="Institute for Social Inventions">Institute for Social Inventions</a> collected many such ideas in magazines and books.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>\nInvention is also an important component of artistic and design <a href="/wiki/Creativity" title="Creativity">creativity</a>. Inventions often extend the boundaries of human knowledge, experience or capability.\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc"><input class="toctogglecheckbox" id="toctogglecheckbox" role="button" invention</span></h2>\n<p>Inventions are of three kinds: scientific-technological\n(including medicine), sociopolitical (including economics and law), and humanistic, or cultural.\n</p><p>Scientific-technological inventions include railroads,\n<a href="/wiki/Aviation" title="Aviation">aviation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vaccination" title="Vaccination">vaccination</a>, hybridization, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antibiotics" title="Antibiotics">antibiotics</a>, astronautics, <a href="/wiki/Holography" title="Holography">holography</a>, the atomic bomb, computing, the <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a>, and the smartphone.\n</p><p>Sociopolitical inventions comprise new laws, institutions, and procedures that change modes of social behavior and establish new forms of human interaction and organization. Examples include the British <a href="/wiki/Parliament" title="Parliament">Parliament</a>, the US <a href="/wiki/Constitution" title="Constitution">Constitution</a>, the Manchester (UK) General Union of Trades, the Boy Scouts, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Cross" title="Red Cross">Red Cross</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Games" title="Olympic Games">Olympic Games</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a>, the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Universal_Declaration_of_Human_Rights" title="Universal Declaration of Human Rights">Universal Declaration of Human Rights</a>, as well as movements such as <a href="/wiki/Socialism" title="Socialism">socialism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zionism" title="Zionism">Zionism</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suffragism" title="Suffragism">suffragism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Feminism" title="Feminism">feminism</a>, and animal-rights veganism.\n</p><p>Humanistic inventions encompass culture in its entirety and are as transformative and important as any in the sciences, although people tend to take them for granted. In the domain of linguistics, for example, many alphabets have been inventions, as are all <a href="/wiki/Neologism" title="Neologism">neologisms</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shakespeare" title="Shakespeare">Shakespeare</a> invented about 1,700 words). Literary inventions include the epic, <a href="/wiki/Tragedy" title="Tragedy">tragedy</a>, comedy, the <a href="/wiki/Novel" title="Novel">novel</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Sonnet" title="Sonnet">sonnet</a>, the\n<a href="/wiki/Renaissance" title="Renaissance">Renaissance</a>, neoclassicism, <a href="/wiki/Romanticism" title="Romanticism">Romanticism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Symbolism_(arts)" title="Symbolism (arts)">Symbolism</a>, Aestheticism, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Socialist_Realism" title="Socialist Realism">Socialist Realism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Surrealism" title="Surrealism">Surrealism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Postmodernism" title="Postmodernism">postmodernism</a>, and (according to Freud) <a href="/wiki/Psychoanalysis" title="Psychoanalysis">psychoanalysis</a>. Among the inventions of artists and musicians are oil painting, printmaking, <a href="/wiki/Photography" title="Photography">photography</a>, <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">cinema</a>, musical tonality, atonality, <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a>, rock, <a href="/wiki/Opera" title="Opera">opera</a>, and the symphony orchestra. Philosophers have invented logic (several times), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dialectics" title="Dialectics">dialectics</a>, idealism, materialism, <a href="/wiki/Utopia" title="Utopia">utopia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anarchism" title="Anarchism">anarchism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Semiotics" title="Semiotics">semiotics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phenomenology_(philosophy)" title="Phenomenology (philosophy)">phenomenology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Behaviorism" title="Behaviorism">behaviorism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Positivism" title="Positivism">positivism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pragmatism" title="Pragmatism">pragmatism</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Deconstruction" title="Deconstruction">deconstruction</a>. Religious thinkers are responsible for such inventions as <a href="/wiki/Monotheism" title="Monotheism">monotheism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pantheism" title="Pantheism">pantheism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Methodism" title="Methodism">Methodism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mormonism" title="Mormonism">Mormonism</a>, iconoclasm, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Puritanism" title="Puritanism">puritanism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Deism" title="Deism">deism</a>, secularism, ecumenism, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baha%E2%80%99i" title="Baha&#8217;i">Baha&#8217;i</a>. Some of these disciplines, genres, and trends may seem to have existed eternally or to have emerged spontaneously of their own accord, but most of them have had inventors.\n<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Process_of_invention">Process of invention</span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practical_means_of_invention">Practical means of invention</span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Alessandro_Volta.jpeg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="480" data-file-width="390" decoding="async" height="246" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Alessandro_Volta.jpeg/200px-Alessandro_Volta.jpeg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Alessandro_Volta.jpeg/300px-Alessandro_Volta.jpeg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/52/Alessandro_Volta.jpeg 2x" width="200"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Alessandro_Volta.jpeg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Volta" title="Alessandro Volta">Alessandro Volta</a> with the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electrical_battery" title="Electrical battery">electrical battery</a>. Volta is recognized as one of the most influential inventors of all time.</div></div></div>\n<p>Idea for an Invention may be developed on paper or on a computer, by writing or drawing, by <a href="/wiki/Trial_and_error" title="Trial and error">trial and error</a>, by making models, by <a href="/wiki/Experiment" title="Experiment">experimenting</a>, by testing and/or by making the invention in its whole form. <a href="/wiki/Brainstorming" title="Brainstorming">Brainstorming</a> also can spark new ideas for an invention.  Collaborative creative processes are frequently used by engineers, designers, architects and scientists. Co-inventors are frequently named on patents.\n</p><p>In addition, many inventors keep <a href="/wiki/Inventor%27s_notebook" title="Inventor\'s notebook">records</a> of their working process - <a href="/wiki/Inventor%27s_notebook" title="Inventor\'s notebook">notebooks</a>, photos, etc., including <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_da_Vinci" title="Leonardo da Vinci">Leonardo da Vinci</a>, <a href="/wiki/Galileo_Galilei" title="Galileo Galilei">Galileo Galilei</a>, <a href="/wiki/Evangelista_Torricelli" title="Evangelista Torricelli">Evangelista Torricelli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Jefferson" title="Thomas Jefferson">Thomas Jefferson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Albert_Einstein" title="Albert Einstein">Albert Einstein</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>\n</p><p>In the process of developing an invention, the initial idea may change. The invention may become simpler, more practical, it may expand, or it may even <i>morph</i> into something totally different. Working on one invention can lead to others too.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>\n</p><p>History shows that turning the concept of an invention into a working device is not always swift or direct.  Inventions may also become more useful after time passes and other changes occur.  For example, the <a href="/wiki/Parachute" title="Parachute">parachute</a> became more useful once powered <a href="/wiki/Flight" title="Flight">flight</a> was a reality.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Conceptual_means">Conceptual means</span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:192px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Edison_and_phonograph_edit2.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3327" data-file-width="2640" decoding="async" height="239" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Edison_and_phonograph_edit2.jpg/190px-Edison_and_phonograph_edit2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Edison_and_phonograph_edit2.jpg/285px-Edison_and_phonograph_edit2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Edison_and_phonograph_edit2.jpg/380px-Edison_and_phonograph_edit2.jpg 2x" width="190"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Edison_and_phonograph_edit2.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Thomas_Edison" title="Thomas Edison">Thomas Edison</a> with <a href="/wiki/Phonograph" title="Phonograph">phonograph</a>. Edison was one of the most prolific inventors in history, holding <a href="/wiki/List_of_Edison_patents" title="List of Edison patents">1,093 U.S. patents in his name</a>.</div></div></div>\n<p>Invention is often a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Creative_process" title="Creative process">creative process</a>. An open and curious mind allows an inventor to see beyond what is known. Seeing a new possibility, connection or relationship can spark an invention. Inventive thinking frequently involves combining concepts or elements from different realms that would not normally be put together. Sometimes inventors disregard the boundaries between distinctly separate territories or fields.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>  Several concepts may be considered when thinking about invention.\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Play">Play</span></h4>\n<p>Play may lead to invention. Childhood curiosity, experimentation, and imagination can develop one\'s play instinct. Inventors feel the need to play with things that interest them, and to explore, and this internal drive brings about novel creations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-inventionatplay1_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-inventionatplay1-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Sometimes inventions and ideas may seem to arise spontaneously while <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daydreaming" title="Daydreaming">daydreaming</a>, especially when the mind is free from its usual concerns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> For example, both J. K. Rowling (the creator of <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and Frank Hornby (the inventor of <a href="/wiki/Meccano" title="Meccano">Meccano</a>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> first had their ideas while on <a href="/wiki/Train" title="Train">train</a> journeys.\n</p><p>In contrast, the successful aerospace engineer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Max_Munk" title="Max Munk">Max Munk</a> advocated "aimful thinking".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Re-envision">Re-envision</span></h4>\n<p>To invent is to see anew.  Inventors often envision a new idea, seeing it in their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mind%27s_eye" title="Mind\'s eye">mind\'s eye</a>. New ideas can arise when the conscious mind turns away from the subject or problem when the inventor\'s focus is on something else, or while relaxing or sleeping. A novel idea may come in a flash&#8212;a <a href="/wiki/Eureka_effect" title="Eureka effect">Eureka</a>! moment. For example, after years of working to figure out the general theory of relativity, the solution came to Einstein suddenly in a dream "like a giant die making an indelible impress, a huge map of the universe outlined itself in one clear vision".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Inventions can also be accidental, such as in the case of <a href="/wiki/Polytetrafluoroethylene" title="Polytetrafluoroethylene">polytetrafluoroethylene</a> (Teflon).\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Insight">Insight</span></h4>\n<p>Insight can also be a vital element of invention. Such inventive insight may begin with questions, doubt or a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intuition_(knowledge)" title="Intuition (knowledge)">hunch</a>. It may begin by recognizing that something unusual or accidental may be useful or that it could open a new avenue for exploration. For example, the odd metallic color of plastic made by accidentally adding a thousand times too much catalyst led scientists to explore its metal-like properties, inventing electrically conductive plastic and light emitting plastic-&#8212;an invention that won the Nobel Prize in 2000 and has led to innovative lighting, display screens, wallpaper and much more (see <a href="/wiki/Conductive_polymer" title="Conductive polymer">conductive polymer</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Organic_light-emitting_diode" title="Organic light-emitting diode">organic light-emitting diode</a> or <a href="/wiki/OLED" title="OLED">OLED</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" There are failures as well as successes. Inspiration can start the process, but no matter how complete the initial idea, inventions typically must be developed.\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Improvement">Improvement</span></h4>\n<p>Inventors may, for example, try to improve something by making it more effective, healthier, faster, more efficient, easier to use, serve more purposes, longer lasting, cheaper, more <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ecological" title="Ecological">ecologically</a> friendly, or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aesthetic" title="Aesthetic">aesthetically</a> different, lighter weight, more <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ergonomic" title="Ergonomic">ergonomic</a>, structurally different, with new light or color properties, etc.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Implementing_Inventions">Implementing Inventions</span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:402px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Arabic_Numerals.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="107" data-file-width="800" decoding="async" height="54" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/Arabic_Numerals.svg/400px-Arabic_Numerals.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/Arabic_Numerals.svg/600px-Arabic_Numerals.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/Arabic_Numerals.svg/800px-Arabic_Numerals.svg.png 2x" width="400"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Arabic_Numerals.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_Arabic_numerals" title="Western Arabic numerals">Western Arabic numerals</a> - an example of non-material inventions.</div></div></div>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:ZST_Bratislava_Petrzalka_3.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1704" data-file-width="2272" decoding="async" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/ZST_Bratislava_Petrzalka_3.jpg/220px-ZST_Bratislava_Petrzalka_3.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/ZST_Bratislava_Petrzalka_3.jpg/330px-ZST_Bratislava_Petrzalka_3.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/ZST_Bratislava_Petrzalka_3.jpg/440px-ZST_Bratislava_Petrzalka_3.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:ZST_Bratislava_Petrzalka_3.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Railways&#160;&#8212; probably the most important invention in <a href="/wiki/Land_transport" title="Land transport">land transport</a>. (Railway station in <a href="/wiki/Bratislava" title="Bratislava">Bratislava</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovakia</a>)</div></div></div>\n<p>In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Economic_theory" title="Economic theory">economic theory</a>, inventions are one of the chief examples of "<a href="/wiki/Externality" title="Externality">positive externalities</a>", a beneficial side-effect that falls on those outside a transaction or activity.  One of the central concepts of economics is that externalities should be internalized&#8212;unless some of the benefits of this positive externality can be captured by the parties, the parties are under-rewarded for their inventions, and systematic under-rewarding leads to under-investment in activities that lead to inventions.  The <a href="/wiki/Patent" title="Patent">patent</a> system captures those <a href="/wiki/Externality" title="Externality">positive externalities</a> for the inventor or other patent owner so that the economy as a whole invests an optimum amount of resources in the invention process.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Invention_vs._innovation">Invention vs. innovation</span></h2>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Innovation" title="Innovation">Innovation</a></div>\n<p>In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Social_sciences" title="Social sciences">social sciences</a>, an innovation is something that is new and better and has been adopted and proven to create positive value. This is a key distinction from an invention which may not create positive value but furthers progress in a given area of development. The theory for adoption of an innovation, called <i><a href="/wiki/Diffusion_of_innovations" title="Diffusion of innovations">diffusion of innovations</a></i>, considers the likelihood that an innovation is adopted and the taxonomy of persons likely to adopt it or spur its adoption. This theory was first put forth by <a href="/wiki/Everett_Rogers" title="Everett Rogers">Everett Rogers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Gabriel_Tarde" title="Gabriel Tarde">Gabriel Tarde</a> also dealt with the adoption of innovations in his <i>Laws of Imitation</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Purposes_of_invention">Purposes of invention</span></h2>\n<p>An invention can serve many purposes, and does not necessarily create positive value. These purposes might differ significantly and may change over time.  An invention or its development may serve purposes never envisioned by its inventors.  Plastic is a good example.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-inventionatplay1_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-inventionatplay1-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Invention_as_defined_by_patent_law">Invention as defined by patent law</span></h2>\n<table class="box-Unreferenced_section As is often the case for legal concepts, its legal meaning is slightly different from common usage of the word.  Additionally, the <i>legal</i> concept of invention is quite different in American and European patent law.\n</p><p>In Europe, the first test a patent application must pass is, "Is this an invention?" If it is, subsequent questions are whether it is new and sufficiently inventive. The implication&#8212;counter-intuitively&#8212;is that a legal invention is not inherently novel. Whether a patent application relates to an invention is governed by Article 52 of the European Patent Convention, that excludes, e.g., discoveries <i>as such</i> and software <i>as such</i>. The EPO Boards of Appeal decided that the technical character of an application is decisive for it to represent an invention, following an age-old Italians and German tradition. British courts don\'t agree with this interpretation. Following a 1959 Australian decision ("NRDC"), they believe that it is not possible to grasp the invention concept in a single rule. A British court once stated that the technical character test implies a "...restatement of the problem in more imprecise terminology."\n</p><p>In the United States, all patent applications are considered inventions. The statute explicitly says that the American invention concept includes discoveries (35 USC &#167; 100(a)), contrary to the European invention concept. The European invention concept corresponds to the American "patentable subject matter" concept: the first test a patent application is submitted to. While the statute (35 USC &#167; 101)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USPTO_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-USPTO-32">[32]</a></sup> virtually poses no limits to patenting whatsoever, courts have decided in binding precedents that abstract ideas, natural phenomena and laws of nature are not patentable. Various attempts were made to substantiate the "abstract idea" test, which suffers from abstractness itself, but eventually, none of them was successful. The last attempt so far was the "machine or transformation" test, but the U.S. Supreme Court decided in 2010 that it is merely an indication at best.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Invention_in_the_arts">Invention in the arts</span></h2>\n<p>Invention has a long and important history in <a href="/wiki/The_arts" title="The arts">the arts</a>.  Inventive thinking has always played a vital role in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Creative_process" title="Creative process">creative process</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup>  While some inventions in the arts are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Patentable" title="Patentable">patentable</a>, others are not because they cannot fulfill the strict requirements governments have established for granting them.  (see <a href="/wiki/Patent" title="Patent">patent</a>).\n</p><p>Some inventions in art include the:\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Collage" title="Collage">Collage</a> and construction invented by Picasso</li>\n<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Readymade" title="Readymade">Readymade</a> invented by <a href="/wiki/Marcel_Duchamp" title="Marcel Duchamp">Marcel Duchamp</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Mobile_(sculpture)" title="Mobile (sculpture)">mobile</a> invented by <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Calder" title="Alexander Calder">Alexander Calder</a></li>\n<li>Combine invented by <a href="/wiki/Robert_Rauschenberg" title="Robert Rauschenberg">Robert Rauschenberg</a></li>\n<li>Shaped painting invented by <a href="/wiki/Frank_Stella" title="Frank Stella">Frank Stella</a></li>\n<li>Motion picture, the invention of which is attributed to <a href="/wiki/Eadweard_Muybridge" title="Eadweard Muybridge">Eadweard Muybridge</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></li></ul>\n<p>Likewise, <a href="/wiki/Jackson_Pollock" title="Jackson Pollock">Jackson Pollock</a> invented an entirely new form of painting and a new kind of abstraction by dripping, pouring, splashing and splattering paint onto un-stretched canvas lying on the floor.\n</p><p>Inventive tools of the artist\'s trade also produced advances in creativity. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Impressionist" title="Impressionist">Impressionist</a> painting became possible because of newly invented collapsible, resealable metal paint tubes that facilitated spontaneous painting outdoors.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>  Inventions originally created in the form of artwork can also develop other uses, i.e., Alexander Calder\'s mobile, which is now commonly used over babies\' cribs. Funds generated from patents on inventions in art, design and architecture can support the realization of the invention or other creative work. <a href="/wiki/Fr%C3%A9d%C3%A9ric_Auguste_Bartholdi" title="Fr&#233;d&#233;ric Auguste Bartholdi">Fr&#233;d&#233;ric Auguste Bartholdi</a>\'s 1879 <a href="/wiki/Design_patent" title="Design patent">design patent</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Statue_of_Liberty" title="Statue of Liberty">Statue of Liberty</a> helped fund the famous statue because it covered small replicas, including those sold as souvenirs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Timeline_for_invention_in_the_arts" title="Timeline for invention in the arts">Timeline for invention in the arts</a>
