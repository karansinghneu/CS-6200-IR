class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>\n<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Wang Dayuan</b> (simplified Chinese: <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E6%B1%AA" title="wikt:&#27754;">&#27754;</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%A4%A7" title="wikt:&#22823;">&#22823;</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E6%B8%8A" title="wikt:&#28170;">&#28170;</a>, fl. 1311&#8211;1350)  was a traveller from <a href="/wiki/Quanzhou" title="Quanzhou">Quanzhou</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mongol_Yuan_Dynasty" title="Mongol Yuan Dynasty">Mongol Yuan Dynasty</a> in the 14th century. He made two major trips on ships. During 1328&#8211;1333, he sailed along the <a href="/wiki/South_China_Sea" title="South China Sea">South China Sea</a> and visited many places in <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a>. He reached as far as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Asian" title="South Asian">South Asian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Australia_(continent)" title="Australia (continent)">Australian</a> sub continent, landing in modern day <a href="/wiki/Bengal" title="Bengal">Bengal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> and areas close to modern day <a href="/wiki/Darwin,_Northern_Territory" title="Darwin, Northern Territory">Darwin, Australia</a>. In 1334&#8211;1339 he visited <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">north Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/East_Africa" title="East Africa">East Africa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Around 1330, Wang visited the island of <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, where he wrote about a small settlement called Danmaxi (&#28129;&#39532;&#38177;, from Malay <a href="/wiki/Temasek" title="Temasek">Temasek</a>) with Malay and Chinese residents. His account of his travel, <i>Daoyi Zhil&#252;e Guangzheng Xia</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> (<i><a href="/wiki/Daoyi_Zhil%C3%BCe" title="Daoyi Zhil&#252;e">Daoyi Zhil&#252;e</a></i>), dated 1349, is one of the few records documenting the <a href="/wiki/Early_history_of_Singapore" title="Early history of Singapore">early history of Singapore</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wang_Dayuan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">W.W.
