id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=East&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The word <i>east</i> comes from <a href="/wiki/Middle_English" title="Middle English">Middle English</a> <i>est</i>, from <a href="/wiki/Old_English" title="Old English">Old English</a> <i>&#275;ast</i>, which itself comes from the <a href="/wiki/Proto-Germanic_language" title="Proto-Germanic language">Proto-Germanic</a> *<i>aus-to-</i> or *<i>austra-</i> "east, toward the sunrise", from <a href="/wiki/Proto-Indo-European_language" title="Proto-Indo-European language">Proto-Indo-European</a> *aus- "to shine," or "dawn".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> This is similar to <a href="/wiki/Old_High_German" title="Old High German">Old High German</a> <i>*&#333;star</i> "to the east", <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>aurora</i> "dawn", and <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a> <i>&#275;&#333;s</i> <span lang="grc" title="Ancient Greek language text">&#7968;&#974;&#962;</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/%C4%92ostre" title="&#274;ostre">&#274;ostre</a></i>, a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Germanic_deities" title="List of Germanic deities">Germanic goddess</a> of dawn, might have been a personification of both dawn and the cardinal points.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Navigation">Navigation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=East&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Navigation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>By <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Norm_(social)" title="Norm (social)">convention</a>, the right hand side of a <a href="/wiki/Map" title="Map">map</a> is east. This convention has developed from the use of a compass, which places north at the top. However, on maps of planets such as Venus which rotate <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Retrograde_motion" title="Retrograde motion">retrograde</a>, the left hand side is east.\n</p><p>To go east using a compass for <a href="/wiki/Navigation" title="Navigation">navigation</a>, one sets a <a href="/wiki/Bearing_(navigation)" title="Bearing (navigation)">bearing</a> or <a href="/wiki/Azimuth" title="Azimuth">azimuth</a> of 90&#176;.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural">Cultural</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=East&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cultural">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>East is the direction toward which the <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a> rotates about its <a href="/wiki/Rotation_around_a_fixed_axis" title="Rotation around a fixed axis">axis</a>, and therefore the general direction from which the <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">Sun</a> appears to rise. The practice of praying towards the East is older than Christianity, but has been adopted by this religion as the Orient was thought of as containing mankind\'s original home. Hence, some Christian churches have been traditionally oriented towards the east.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <i>Orient</i> is the <i>East</i>, traditionally comprising anything that belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_world" title="Eastern world">Eastern world</a>, in relation to <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>. In <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>, it is largely a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metonym" title="Metonym">metonym</a> for, and coterminous with, the <a href="/wiki/Continent" title="Continent">continent</a> of <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, divided into the <a href="/wiki/Far_East" title="Far East">Far East</a>, <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Near_East" title="Near East">Near East</a>. Despite this Eurocentric origin, these regions are still located to the east of the <a href="/wiki/Geographical_centre_of_Earth" title="Geographical centre of Earth">Geographical centre of Earth</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=East&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Intermediate_Region" title="Intermediate Region">Intermediate Region</a></li>\n<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Easting" title="Easting">Easting</a></li>\n<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oriental" title="Oriental">Oriental</a></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=East&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a class="external text" href="http://www.etymonline.com/index.php?term=east&amp;allowed_in_frame=0" rel="nofollow">"east"</a>.
