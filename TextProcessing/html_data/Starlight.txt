title="Light">light</a> emitted by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stars" title="Stars">stars</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-krob_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-krob-1">[1]</a></sup> It typically refers to <a href="/wiki/Visible_spectrum" title="Visible spectrum">visible</a> <a href="/wiki/Electromagnetic_radiation" title="Electromagnetic radiation">electromagnetic radiation</a> from stars other than the <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">Sun</a> observable from Earth during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Night_time" title="Night time">night time</a> although a component of starlight is observable from the Earth during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daytime_(astronomy)" title="Daytime (astronomy)">daytime</a>.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Sunlight" title="Sunlight">Sunlight</a> is the term used for the Sun\'s starlight observed during daytime.  During nighttime, <a href="/wiki/Albedo" title="Albedo">albedo</a> describes solar reflections from other Solar System objects including <a href="/wiki/Moonlight" id="Observation">Observation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Starlight&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Observation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Observation and measurement of starlight through telescopes is the basis for many fields of <a href="/wiki/Astronomy" title="Astronomy">astronomy</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mac_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-mac-2">[2]</a></sup> including photometry and stellar spectroscopy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hearn_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-hearn-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Hipparchus" title="Hipparchus">Hipparchus</a> did not have a telescope or any instrument that could measure apparent brightness accurately, so he simply made estimates with his eyes. He sorted the stars into six brightness categories, which he called magnitudes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">[4]</a></sup> He referred to the brightest stars in his catalog as first-magnitudes stars, which were the brightest stars and those so faint he could barely see them were sixth-magnitude stars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">[4]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Starlight is also a notable part of <a href="/wiki/Personal_experience" title="Personal experience">personal experience</a> and human <a href="/wiki/Culture" title="Culture">culture</a>, impacting a diverse range of pursuits including  poetry,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-skinner_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-skinner-5">[5]</a></sup> astronomy,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mac_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-mac-2">[2]</a></sup> and military strategy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mort_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-mort-6">[6]</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">United States Army</a> spent millions of dollars in the 1950s and onward to develop a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Starlight_scope" title="Starlight scope">starlight scope</a>, that could amplify starlight, moonlight filtered by clouds, and the <a href="/wiki/Fluorescence" title="Fluorescence">fluorescence</a> of rotting <a href="/wiki/Vegetation" title="Vegetation">vegetation</a> about 50,000 times to allow a person to see in the night.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mort_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-mort-6">[6]</a></sup> In contrast to previously developed active infrared system such as <i>sniperscope</i>, it was a passive device and did not require additional light emission to see.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mort_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-mort-6">[6]</a></sup>\n</p><p>The average color of starlight in the <a href="/wiki/Observable_universe" title="Observable universe">observable universe</a> is a shade of yellowish-white that has been given the name  <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cosmic_Latte" title="Cosmic Latte">Cosmic Latte</a>. \n</p><p>Starlight spectroscopy, examination of the stellar spectra, was pioneered by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joseph_Fraunhofer" title="Joseph Fraunhofer">Joseph Fraunhofer</a> in 1814.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hearn_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-hearn-3">[3]</a></sup> Starlight can be understood to be composed of three main spectra types, <i>continuous spectrum</i>, <i>emission spectrum</i>, and <i>absorption spectrum</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-krob_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-krob-1">[1]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Oldest_starlight">Oldest starlight</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Starlight&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Oldest starlight">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>One of the oldest stars yet identified (oldest not most distant in this case) was identified in 2014, the star SMSS J031300.362670839.3 was determined to be 6000 light years away but date to 13.8 billion years ago.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-space.com_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-space.com-7">[7]</a></sup> The starlight shining on Earth would include this star.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-space.com_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-space.com-7">[7]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Photography">Photography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Starlight&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Photography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Night_photography" title="Night photography">Night photography</a> includes photographing subjects that are lit primarily by starlight.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>  Directly taking images of night sky is also a part of <a href="/wiki/Astrophotography" title="Astrophotography">astrophotography</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-books.google.com_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-books.google.com-9">[9]</a></sup>  Like other photography, it can be used for the pursuit of science and/or leisure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>  Subjects include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nocturnal_animals" title="Nocturnal animals">nocturnal animals</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-books.google.com_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-books.google.com-9">[9]</a></sup>  In many cases starlight photography may also overlap with a need to understand the impact of <a href="/wiki/Moonlight" title="Moonlight">moonlight</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-books.google.com_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-books.google.com-9">[9]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See
