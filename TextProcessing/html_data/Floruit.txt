(June 2018)\'>clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology_and_use">Etymology and use</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Floruit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology and use">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_language" title="Latin language">Latin</a>: <i lang="la">fl&#333;ruit</i> is the third-person singular perfect active indicative of the Latin verb <i><i lang="la" title="Latin language text">fl&#333;re&#333;</i></i>, <i><i lang="la" title="Latin language text">fl&#333;r&#275;re</i></i> "to bloom, flower, or flourish", from the noun <i><i lang="la" title="Latin language text">fl&#333;s</i></i>, <i><i lang="la" title="Latin language text">fl&#333;ris</i></i>, "flower".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AH_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-AH-2">[2]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Broadly, the term is employed in reference to the peak of activity for a person, movement, or such. More specifically, it often is used in <a href="/wiki/Genealogy" title="Genealogy">genealogy</a> and historical writing when a person\'s birth or death dates are unknown, but some other evidence exists that indicates when he or she was alive.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adeleye_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Adeleye-4">[4]</a></sup> For example, if there are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Will_(law)" title="Will (law)">wills</a> <a href="/wiki/Attestation_clause" title="Attestation clause">attested</a> by John Jones in 1204 and 1229, and a record of his marriage in 1197, a record concerning him might be written as "John Jones (fl.&#160;1197&#8211;1229)".\n</p><p>The term is often used in <a href="/wiki/Art_history" title="Art history">art history</a> when dating the career of an artist. In this context, it specifically denotes the period of the individual\'s artistic activity, not just the known existence of the artist, which might differ significantly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>\n</p><p>In some cases, it can be replaced by the words "active between <var style="padding-right: 1px;">[date]</var> and <var style="padding-right: 1px;">[date]</var>", depending on context and if space or style allows.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
