href="/wiki/File:Biopan_Space_Expo_001.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Experiment BIOPAN-5 part of the Foton-M2 mission</div></div></div>\n<p><b>BIOPAN</b> is a multi-user research program by the <a href="/wiki/European_Space_Agency" title="European Space Agency">European Space Agency</a> (ESA) designed to investigate the effect of the space environment on biological material.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kayser_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kayser-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Experimental_methods_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Experimental_methods-2">[2]</a></sup> The experiments in BIOPAN are exposed to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Insolation" title="Insolation">solar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cosmic_ray" title="Cosmic ray">cosmic radiation</a>, the space vacuum and weightlessness, or a selection thereof. Optionally, the experiment temperature can be stabilized. BIOPAN hosts <a href="/wiki/Astrobiology" title="Astrobiology">astrobiology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Radiobiology" title="Radiobiology">radiobiology</a> and materials science experiments.\n</p><p>The BIOPAN facility is installed on the external surface of Russian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Foton_(space_programs)" title="Foton (space programs)">Foton descent capsules</a> protruding from the thermal blanket that envelops the satellite.\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc"><input class="toctogglecheckbox" id="toctogglecheckbox" role="button" style="display:none" type="checkbox"/><div class="toctitle" dir="ltr" lang="en"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Design_and_features"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Design and features</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Missions"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Missions</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#See_also"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">See also</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design_and_features">Design and features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BIOPAN&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Design and features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The BIOPAN program started in the early nineties with an ESA contract for the a joint development by <a href="/wiki/Kayser-Threde" title="Kayser-Threde">Kayser-Threde</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kayser_Italia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kayser Italia (page does not exist)">Kayser Italia</a>. It was based on the heritage of a low-tech Russian exposure container called KNA (<i>Kontejner Nauchnoj Apparatury</i>). The BIOPAN facilities are installed on the external surface of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Foton_(space_programs)" title="Foton (space programs)">Foton descent capsules</a>. It has a motor-driven hinged lid, which opens 180&#176; in Earth orbit to expose the experiment samples to the harsh space environment. For <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_entry" title="Atmospheric entry">re-entry</a>, the closed facility is protected with an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ablative_heat_shield#Ablative" title="Ablative heat shield">Ablative heat shield</a>.\n</p><p>The BIOPAN facilities are equipped with thermometers, <a href="/wiki/Ultraviolet" title="Ultraviolet">UV</a> sensors, a <a href="/wiki/Radiometer" title="Radiometer">radiometer</a>, a pressure sensor and an active radiation <a href="/wiki/Dosimeter" title="Dosimeter">dosimeter</a>. Data acquired by the sensors is stored by BIOPAN throughout each mission and can be accessed after flight.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kayser_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Kayser-1">[1]</a></sup>  The possibility of overheating during atmospheric re-entry was acknowledged early during the development, therefore, a quite massive heat shield was designed for it.  While the total weight of BIOPAN is close to 27&#160;kg, including the experiments, the heat shield is responsible for 12&#160;kg of that figure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Horneck_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Horneck-3">[3]</a></sup>\n</p><p>The BIOPAN electronics consists of the following units: signal acquisition board, microcontroller board with its flight software, memory board and <a href="/wiki/Telespazio_VEGA_Deutschland" title="Telespazio VEGA Deutschland">EGSE</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kayser_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Kayser-1">[1]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Missions">Missions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BIOPAN&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Missions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The missions flown so far are:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Horneck_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Horneck-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<table class="wikitable">\n<tbody><tr>\n<th>Mission</th>\n<th>Spacecraft</th>\n<th>Launch date</th>\n<th>Duration<br/>(days)</th>\n<th>Experiments\n</th></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>BIOPAN-0</td>\n<td>Foton-8</td>\n<td>1992-10-08</td>\n<td>15.6 d</td>\n<td>Test flight with non-ESA sponsored experiments\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>BIOPAN-1</td>\n<td>Foton-9</td>\n<td>1994-06-14</td>\n<td>17.6 d</td>\n<td>6 experiments: Base, Shrimp.
