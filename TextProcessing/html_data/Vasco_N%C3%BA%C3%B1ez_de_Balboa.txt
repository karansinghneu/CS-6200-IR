International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">[&#712;basko &#712;nu&#626;e&#952; &#240;e &#946;al&#712;&#946;o.a]</a></span>; c. 1475&#160;&#8211; around January 12&#8211;21, 1519)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish_people" title="Spanish people">Spanish</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Explorer" title="Explorer">explorer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Governor" title="Governor">governor</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Conquistador" title="Conquistador">conquistador</a>. He is best known for having crossed the <a href="/wiki/Isthmus_of_Panama" title="Isthmus of Panama">Isthmus of Panama</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a> in 1513, becoming the first European to lead an expedition to have seen or reached the Pacific from the <a href="/wiki/New_World" title="New World">New World</a>.\n</p><p>He traveled to the <a href="/wiki/New_World" title="New World">New World</a> in 1500 and, after some exploration, settled on the island of <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">Hispaniola</a>. He founded the settlement of <a href="/wiki/Santa_Mar%C3%ADa_la_Antigua_del_Dari%C3%A9n" title="Santa Mar&#237;a la Antigua del Dari&#233;n">Santa Mar&#237;a la Antigua del Dari&#233;n</a> in present-day <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">Panama</a> in 1510, which was the first permanent <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> settlement on the mainland of the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a> (a settlement by <a href="/wiki/Alonso_de_Ojeda" title="Alonso de Ojeda">Alonso de Ojeda</a> the previous year at <a href="/wiki/San_Sebasti%C3%A1n_de_Urab%C3%A1" title="San Sebasti&#225;n de Urab&#225;">San Sebasti&#225;n de Urab&#225;</a> had already been abandoned).\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc"><input life</span></h2>\n<p>Balboa was born in <a href="/wiki/Jerez_de_los_Caballeros" title="Jerez de los Caballeros">Jerez de los Caballeros</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. He was a descendant of the Lord mason of the castle of <a href="/wiki/Balboa,_Le%C3%B3n" title="Balboa, Le&#243;n">Balboa</a>, on the borders of Le&#243;n and Galicia. His mother was the Lady de Badajoz, and his father was the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hidalgo_(Spanish_nobility)" title="Hidalgo (Spanish nobility)">hidalgo</a> (nobleman), Nu&#241;o Arias de Balboa. Little is known of Vasco\'s early childhood except that he was the third of four boys in his family. During his adolescence, he served as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Page_(occupation)" title="Page (occupation)">page</a> and <a href="/wiki/Squire" title="Squire">squire</a> to <a title="Spanish missions in the Americas">Spanish missions in the Americas</a></li></ul></td>\n</tr><tr><td style="text-align:right;font-size:115%"><div class="plainlinks hlist navbar mini"><ul><li class="nv-view"><a href="/wiki/Template:Spanish_colonization_of_the_Americas" title="Template:Spanish colonization of the Americas"><abbr title="View this template">v</abbr></a></li><li class="nv-talk"><a href="/wiki/Template_talk:Spanish_colonization_of_the_Americas" title="Template talk:Spanish colonization of the Americas"><abbr title="Discuss this template">t</abbr></a></li><li class="nv-edit"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Template:Spanish_colonization_of_the_Americas&amp;action=edit"><abbr title="Edit this template">e</abbr></a></li></ul></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>In 1500, motivated by his master after the news of <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a>\'s voyages to the <a href="/wiki/New_World" title="New World">New World</a> became known, he decided to embark on his first voyage to the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Juan_de_la_Cosa" title="Juan de la Cosa">Juan de la Cosa</a>, on <a href="/wiki/Rodrigo_de_Bastidas" title="Rodrigo de Bastidas">Rodrigo de Bastidas</a>\' expedition. Bastidas had a license to bring back treasure for the king and queen, while keeping four-fifths for himself, under a policy known as the <a href="/wiki/Quinto_real" title="Quinto real">quinto real</a>, or "royal fifth".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In 1501, he crossed the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> coasts from the east of <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">Panama</a>, along the <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombian</a> coast, through the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Urab%C3%A1" title="Gulf of Urab&#225;">Gulf of Urab&#225;</a> toward <a href="/wiki/Cabo_de_la_Vela" title="Cabo de la Vela">Cabo de la Vela</a>. The expedition continued to explore the north east of South America, until they realized they did not have enough men and sailed to <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">Hispaniola</a>.\n</p><p>With his share of the earnings from this campaign, Balboa settled in Hispaniola in 1505, where he resided for several years as a planter and pig farmer. He was not successful in this enterprise, however, and ended up in debt. Finally, he was forced to abandon life on the island.\n</p><p>In 1508, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_of_Spain" title="King of Spain">king of Spain</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fernando_el_Cat%C3%B3lico" title="Fernando el Cat&#243;lico">Ferdinand II "The Catholic"</a>, launched the <a href="/wiki/Conquest_(military)" title="Conquest (military)">conquest</a> of <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Tierra_Firme" title="Province of Tierra Firme">Tierra Firme</a> (the area roughly corresponding to the <a href="/wiki/Isthmus_of_Panama" title="Isthmus of Panama">Isthmus of Panama</a>). He created two new territories in the region between El <a href="/wiki/Cabo_de_la_Vela" title="Cabo de la Vela">Cabo de la Vela</a> (near the eastern border of Colombia) and El <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cabo_de_Gracias_a_Dios" title="Cabo de Gracias a Dios">Cabo de Gracias a Dios</a> (the border between <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduras</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">Nicaragua</a>). The Gulf of Urab&#225; became the border between the two territories: <a href="/wiki/New_Andalusia_Province" title="New Andalusia Province">Nueva Andaluc&#237;a</a> to the east, governed by <a href="/wiki/Alonso_de_Ojeda" title="Alonso de Ojeda">Alonso de Ojeda</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Veragua" title="Veragua">Veragua</a> to the west, governed by <a href="/wiki/Diego_de_Nicuesa" title="Diego de Nicuesa">Diego de Nicuesa</a>.\n</p><p>In 1509, wishing to escape his creditors in <a href="/wiki/Santo_Domingo" title="Santo Domingo">Santo Domingo</a>, Balboa set sail as a <a href="/wiki/Stowaway" title="Stowaway">stowaway</a>, hiding inside a barrel together with his dog <i>Leoncico</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-smithsonian_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-smithsonian-2">[2]</a></sup> in the expedition commanded by the <i>Alcalde Mayor</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> of Nueva Andaluc&#237;a, <a href="/wiki/Mart%C3%ADn_Fern%C3%A1ndez_de_Enciso" title="Mart&#237;n Fern&#225;ndez de Enciso">Mart&#237;n Fern&#225;ndez de Enciso</a>, whose mission it was to aid Alonso de Ojeda, his superior.\n</p><p>Ojeda, together with seventy men, had founded the settlement of San Sebasti&#225;n de Urab&#225; in Nueva Andaluc&#237;a, on the location where the city of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cartagena_de_Indias" title="Cartagena de Indias">Cartagena de Indias</a> would later be built. However, the settlers encountered resistance from  natives living in the area, who used poisoned weapons, and Ojeda was injured in the leg. A short time later, Ojeda sailed for Hispaniola, leaving the colony under the supervision of <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Pizarro" title="Francisco Pizarro">Francisco Pizarro</a>, who, at that time, was only a soldier waiting for Enciso\'s expedition to arrive. Ojeda asked Pizarro to leave some men in the settlement for fifty days and, if no help arrived at the end of that time, to use all possible means to get back to Hispaniola.\n</p><p>Before the expedition arrived at San Sebasti&#225;n de Urab&#225;, Fern&#225;ndez de Enciso discovered Balboa aboard the ship, and threatened to leave him at the first uninhabited island they encountered; he later thought better of this and decided that Balboa\'s knowledge of that region, which he had explored eight years before, would be of great utility. This, in addition to the crew\'s pleas for his life, left Fern&#225;ndez de Enciso with no choice but to spare Balboa and keep him aboard. Moreover, both agreed on removing Nicuesa as governor of Veragua.\n</p><p>After the fifty days had passed, Pizarro started preparations for the return to Hispaniola, when Enciso\'s ship arrived. Balboa had gained popularity among the crew because of his charisma and his knowledge of the region. By contrast Fern&#225;ndez de Enciso was not well liked by the men: many disapproved of his order to return to San Sebasti&#225;n, especially after discovering, once they had arrived, that the settlement had been completely destroyed and that the natives were already waiting for them, leading to a series of relentless attacks.\n</p>\n<h2><span id="The_founding_of_Santa_Mar.C3.ADa"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="The_founding_of_Santa_Mar&#237;a">The founding of Santa Mar&#237;a</span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Indias_1513.PNG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="430" data-file-width="427" decoding="async" height="222" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Indias_1513.PNG/220px-Indias_1513.PNG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Indias_1513.PNG/330px-Indias_1513.PNG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/86/Indias_1513.PNG 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Indias_1513.PNG" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Province_of_Tierra_Firme" title="Province of Tierra Firme">Tierra Firme</a> 1513 - <a href="/wiki/Castilla_de_Oro" title="Castilla de Oro">Castilla de Oro</a></div></div></div>\n<p>Balboa suggested that the settlement of San Sebasti&#225;n be moved to the region of <a href="/wiki/Dari%C3%A9n_Province" title="Dari&#233;n Province">Dari&#233;n</a>, to the west of the Gulf of Urab&#225;, where the soil was more fertile and the natives presented less resistance. Fern&#225;ndez de Enciso gave serious consideration to this suggestion, and the regiment later went to Dari&#233;n, where the native <i><a href="/wiki/Cacique" title="Cacique">cacique</a></i> (chieftain) C&#233;maco had 500 warriors waiting, ready for battle. The Spanish, fearful of the large number of enemy combatants, made a vow to the <i>Virgen de la Antigua</i>, venerated in <a href="/wiki/Seville" title="Seville">Seville</a>, that they would name a settlement in the region after her should they prevail. It was a difficult battle for both sides, but, by a stroke of luck, the Spanish came out victorious.\n</p><p>C&#233;maco, together with his warriors, abandoned the town and headed for the jungle. The Spanish plundered the houses and gathered a treasure-trove of golden ornaments. Balboa kept his vow, and, in September 1510, founded the first permanent settlement on mainland American soil, and called it <a href="/wiki/Santa_Mar%C3%ADa_la_Antigua_del_Dari%C3%A9n" title="Santa Mar&#237;a la Antigua del Dari&#233;n">Santa Mar&#237;a la Antigua del Dari&#233;n</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span id="Mayor_of_Santa_Mar.C3.ADa"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Mayor_of_Santa_Mar&#237;a">Mayor of Santa Mar&#237;a</span></h2>\n<p>The victory of the Spanish over the natives and the founding of Santa Mar&#237;a la Antigua del Dari&#233;n, now located in a relatively calm region, earned Balboa authority and respect among his companions. They were increasingly hostile toward Alcalde Mayor Fern&#225;ndez de Enciso, whom they considered a greedy despot because of the restrictions he imposed on their appropriation of the natives\' gold.\n</p><p>Balboa took advantage of the situation, acting as the spokesman for the disgruntled settlers. He removed Fern&#225;ndez de Enciso from the position of <i>alcalde mayor</i>, using the following legal manoeuvre: Fern&#225;ndez de Enciso was now controlling an area in Veragua, to the west of the Gulf of Urab&#225;; since he was substituting for Alonso de Ojeda, his mandate was illegitimate, because the governor of Veragua was Diego de Nicuesa, not Ojeda; therefore, Fern&#225;ndez de Enciso should be deposed and arrested. After Fern&#225;ndez de Enciso\'s <a href="/wiki/Coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="Coup d\'&#233;tat">ouster</a>, a more open government was established and a municipal council was elected (the first in the Americas). Two <i>alcaldes</i> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> were appointed: Mart&#237;n Samudio and Vasco N&#250;&#241;ez de Balboa.\n</p><p>Shortly after this, a <a href="/wiki/Flotilla" title="Flotilla">flotilla</a> led by Rodrigo Enrique de Colmenares arrived in Santa Mar&#237;a. His objective was to find Nicuesa, who was also facing some difficulties in the north of Panam&#225;. When de Colmenares learned about the recent events, he convinced the town\'s settlers that they should submit to the authority of Nicuesa, since their land was under his jurisdiction. Enrique de Colmenares invited two representatives, to be named by the local government, to travel with his flotilla and offer Nicuesa authority over the city. The two representatives were Diego de Albites and Diego del Corral.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governor_of_Veragua">Governor of Veragua</span></h2>\n<p>Enrique de Colmenares found Nicuesa near the town of <a href="/wiki/Nombre_de_Dios,_Col%C3%B3n" title="Nombre de Dios, Col&#243;n">Nombre de Dios</a>, badly wounded and with few men remaining, on account of a skirmish with local natives. After his rescue, Governor Nicuesa heard about Balboa\'s exploits, the chieftain C&#233;maco\'s bounty, and Santa Mar&#237;a\'s prosperity. He vowed that he would punish Balboa as soon as he gained control of the city, since he regarded his actions as a challenge to his authority in Veragua.\n</p><p>A certain <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lope_de_Olano&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lope de Olano (page does not exist)">Lope de Olano</a>, who was jailed together with other malcontents, persuaded Santa Mar&#237;a\'s representatives that they would make a serious error in handing control over to Nicuesa, whom he described as cruel, greedy, and able to singlehandedly destroy the city\'s prosperity. With this evidence, Albites and del Corral fled to Dari&#233;n ahead of Nicuesa and informed Balboa and the municipal authorities of the governor\'s intentions.\n</p><p>When Nicuesa arrived at the city\'s port, a mob appeared, and the ensuing disturbance prevented the governor from disembarking into the city. Nicuesa insisted on being received, no longer as governor, but as a simple soldier, but still the colonists did not allow him to disembark. He and 17 others were forced to board an unseaworthy boat with few supplies, and were put out to sea on March 1, 1511. The ship disappeared, leaving no trace of Nicuesa and his men. In this way, Balboa became governor (<i>gobernador</i>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> of Veragua.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conquistador">Conquistador</span></h2>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Spanish_conquest_of_the_Chibchan_Nations" title="Spanish conquest of the Chibchan Nations">Spanish conquest of the Chibchan Nations</a></div>\n<p>With the title of governor came absolute authority in Santa Mar&#237;a and all of Veragua. One of Balboa\'s first acts as governor was the trial of Fern&#225;ndez de Enciso, accused of usurping the governor\'s authority. Fern&#225;ndez de Enciso was sentenced to prison and his possessions were confiscated. However, he was to remain imprisoned only for a short time: Balboa set him free under the condition that he return immediately to Hispaniola and from there to Spain. With him on the same ship were two representatives from Balboa, who were to inform the colonial authorities of the situation, and request more men and supplies to continue the conquest of Veragua.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Balboamurder.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="314" data-file-width="390" decoding="async" height="177" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Balboamurder.jpg/220px-Balboamurder.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Balboamurder.jpg/330px-Balboamurder.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/17/Balboamurder.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Balboamurder.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Balboa setting his dogs upon <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_of_the_Americas" title="Indigenous peoples of the Americas">Indian</a> practitioners of <a href="/wiki/Homosexuality#History" title="Homosexuality">homosexuality</a> (1594) engraving from the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Public_Library" title="New York Public Library">New York Public Library</a>. The rendering was by the Flemish Protestant artist <a href="/wiki/Theodor_de_Bry" title="Theodor de Bry">Theodor de Bry</a>.</div></div></div>\n<p>Balboa continued defeating various tribes and befriending others, exploring rivers, mountains, and sickly swamps, while always searching for gold and <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slaves</a> and enlarging his territory. He was also able to quell revolts among those of his men who challenged this authority, and, through force, diplomacy, and negotiation, he earned a certain respect and fear among the natives. In a letter addressed to the King of Spain, he expressed, somewhat ironically, that he had to act as a conciliatory force during the course of his expeditions.\n</p><p>He succeeded in planting <a href="/wiki/Maize" title="Maize">corn</a>, received fresh supplies from Hispaniola and Spain, and got his men used to life as explorers in the new territories. Balboa managed to collect a great deal of gold, much of it from the ornaments worn by the native women, and the rest obtained by violence.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Peter_Martyr_d%27Anghiera" title="Peter Martyr d\'Anghiera">Peter Martyr d\'Anghiera</a> in his <i>De orbe novo decades</i>, wrote how Balboa had fed forty local homosexual men to his dogs. Balboa, upset with "<i>a brother of the king and other young men, obliging men, [who] dressed effeminately with women\'s clothing [... of those which the brother of the king] went too far with unnatural"</i> temerity, threw forty of them as food to the dogs.  D\'Anghiera continues his story saying that the indigenous people\'s "<i>natural hate for unnatural sin</i>" drove them so that, "<i>spontaneously and violently, they searched for all the rest that they would know who were infected</i>".  After all, D\'Anghiera mentions that "<i>only the nobles and the gentlemen practiced that kind of desire.  [... The] indigenous people knew that sodomy gravely offended God.  [... And that these acts provoked] the tempests that with thunder and lightning so frequently afflicted them, or the floods that drowned their fruits that had caused hunger and sickness.</i>"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>\n</p><p>At the end of 1512 and the first months of 1513, he arrived in a region dominated by the <i>cacique</i> Careta, whom he easily defeated and then befriended. Careta was <a href="/wiki/Baptism" title="Baptism">baptized</a> and became one of Balboa\'s chief allies; he ensured the survival of the settlers by promising to supply the Spaniards with food. Balboa then proceeded on his journey, arriving in the lands of Careta\'s neighbour and rival, <i>cacique</i> Ponca, who fled to the mountains with his people, leaving his village open to the plundering of the Spaniards and Careta\'s men. Days later, the expedition arrived in the lands of <i>cacique</i> Comagre, fertile but reportedly dangerous terrain. However, Balboa was received peacefully and even invited to a feast in his honor; Comagre, like Careta, was then baptized.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Balboa_Voyage_1513.PNG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="316" data-file-width="581" decoding="async" height="120" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Balboa_Voyage_1513.PNG/220px-Balboa_Voyage_1513.PNG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Balboa_Voyage_1513.PNG/330px-Balboa_Voyage_1513.PNG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Balboa_Voyage_1513.PNG/440px-Balboa_Voyage_1513.PNG 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Balboa_Voyage_1513.PNG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Balboa\'s travel route to the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">South Sea</a>, 1513</div></div></div>\n<p>In 1513, Balboa wrote a lengthy letter to the King of Spain, requesting more men (who were already acclimatized) from Hispaniola, weapons, supplies, carpenters versed in shipbuilding, and all the necessary materials for the building of a <a href="/wiki/Shipyard" title="Shipyard">shipyard</a>. In a subsequent letter, from 1515, he would refer to his humanitarian policies regarding the natives, while at the same time recommending extreme severity in dealing with <a href="/wiki/Cannibalism" title="Cannibalism">cannibals</a> and violent tribes.\n</p><p>It was in Comagre\'s lands that Balboa first heard of "the other sea." It started with a squabble among the Spaniards, unsatisfied by the meager amounts of gold they were being allotted. Comagre\'s eldest son, Panquiaco, angered by the Spaniards\' avarice, knocked over the scales used to measure gold and exclaimed: "If you are so hungry for gold that you leave your lands to cause strife in those of others, I shall show you a province where you can quell this hunger". Panquiaco told them of a kingdom to the south, where people were so rich that they ate and drank from plates and goblets made of gold, but that the conquerors would need at least a thousand men to defeat the tribes living inland and those on the coast of "the other sea".\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="European_discovery_of_the_Pacific_Ocean">European discovery of the Pacific Ocean</span></h2>\n<p>Balboa received the unexpected news of a new kingdom &#8211; rich in gold &#8211; with great interest. He returned to Santa Mar&#237;a at the beginning of 1513 to recruit more men from Hispaniola. There he learned that Fern&#225;ndez de Enciso had told the colonial authorities what had happened at Santa Mar&#237;a. After seeing that there would be no assistance from Hispaniola, Balboa sent Enrique de Colmenares directly to Spain to look for help.\n</p><p>While the expedition to the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">South Sea</a> (the name at the time of the Pacific Ocean<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>) was being organized in Santa Mar&#237;a, some explorers travelled ten leagues (around 50&#160;km or 30 miles) up the <a href="/wiki/Atrato_River" title="Atrato River">Atrato River</a> toward the interior, but came back empty-handed. Balboa\'s request for men and supplies had been denied: Enciso\'s case was by then widely known in the <a href="/wiki/Cortes_Generales" title="Cortes Generales">Spanish court</a>. Therefore, Balboa had no choice but to carry out his expedition with the few resources that he had on hand in Santa Mar&#237;a.\n</p><p>Using information given by various friendly <i>caciques</i>, Balboa started his journey across the <a href="/wiki/Isthmus_of_Panama" title="Isthmus of Panama">Isthmus of Panama</a> on September 1, 1513, together with 190 Spaniards, a few native guides, and a pack of dogs. Using a small <a href="/wiki/Brigantine" title="Brigantine">brigantine</a> and ten native <a href="/wiki/Canoe" title="Canoe">canoes</a>, they sailed along the coast and made landfall in <i>cacique</i> Careta\'s territory. On September 6, the expedition continued, now reinforced with 1,000 of Careta\'s men, and entered <i>cacique</i> Ponca\'s land. Ponca had reorganized and attacked, but he was defeated and forced to ally himself with Balboa. After a few days, and with several of Ponca\'s men, the expedition entered the dense jungle on September 20, and, with some difficulty, arrived four days later in the lands of <i>cacique</i> Torecha, who ruled in the village of Cuarecu&#225;. In this village, a fierce battle took place, during which Balboa\'s forces defeated Torecha, who was killed by one of Balboa\'s dogs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Torecha\'s followers decided to join the expedition. However, the group was by then exhausted and several men were badly wounded, so many decided to stay in Cuarecu&#225; to regain their strength.\n</p><p>The few men who continued the journey with Balboa entered the mountain range along the <a href="/wiki/Chucunaque_River" title="Chucunaque River">Chucunaque River</a> the next day. According to information from the natives, one could see the South Sea from the summit of this range. Balboa went ahead and, before noon that day, September 25, he reached the summit and saw, far away in the horizon, the waters of the undiscovered sea. The emotions were such that the others eagerly joined in to show their joy at Balboa\'s discovery. Andr&#233;s de Vera, the expedition\'s chaplain, intoned the <i><a href="/wiki/Te_Deum" title="Te Deum">Te Deum</a></i>, while the men erected stone pyramids, and engraved crosses on the barks of trees with their swords, to mark the place where the discovery of the South Sea was made.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Possession_and_conquest_of_the_South_Sea">Possession and conquest of the South Sea</span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Balboa_s%C3%BCdsee.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="653" data-file-width="440" decoding="async" height="327" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/66/Balboa_s%C3%BCdsee.jpg/220px-Balboa_s%C3%BCdsee.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/66/Balboa_s%C3%BCdsee.jpg/330px-Balboa_s%C3%BCdsee.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/66/Balboa_s%C3%BCdsee.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Balboa_s%C3%BCdsee.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Balboa claiming possession of the South Sea (<small>19th century engraving by unknown artist</small>)</div></div></div>\n<p>After the moment of discovery, the expedition descended from the mountain range towards the sea, arriving in the lands of <i>cacique</i> Chiapes, who was defeated after a brief battle and invited to join the expedition. From Chiapes\' land, three groups departed in the search for routes to the coast. The group headed by Alonso Mart&#237;n reached the shoreline two days later. They took a canoe for a short reconnaissance trip, thus becoming the first Europeans to navigate the Pacific Ocean off the coast of New World. Back in Chiapes\' domain, Mart&#237;n informed Balboa, who, with 26 men, marched towards the coast. Once there, Balboa raised his hands, his sword in one and a standard with the image of the Virgin Mary in the other, walked knee-deep into the ocean, and claimed possession of the new sea and all adjoining lands in the name of the Spanish sovereigns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-smithsonian_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-smithsonian-2">[2]</a></sup>\n</p><p>After traveling more than 110&#160;km (68&#160;mi), Balboa named the bay where they ended up <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_San_Miguel" title="Bay of San Miguel"><i>San Miguel</i></a>, because they arrived on September 29, the feast day of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Michael" title="Saint Michael">archangel Michael</a>. He named the new sea <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean"><i>Mar del Sur</i></a>, since they had traveled south to reach it.\n</p><p>Balboa\'s main purpose in the expedition was the search for the gold-rich kingdoms promised by Panquiaco. To this end, he crossed through the lands of <i>caciques</i> Coquera and Tumaco, defeating them easily and taking their riches of gold and <a href="/wiki/Pearl" title="Pearl">pearls</a>. He then learned that pearls were abundant in the islands ruled by Terarequ&#237;, a powerful and feared <i>cacique</i>. Balboa set out in several canoes towards these islands, even though it was the beginning of October and the weather conditions were not favorable. He was barely able to make out the islands, and named the largest one <i>Isla Rica</i> (Rich Island, today known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Isla_del_Rey_(Panama)" title="Isla del Rey (Panama)">Isla del Rey</a>). He named the entire group <i><a href="/wiki/Pearl_Islands" title="Pearl Islands">Archipi&#233;lago de las Perlas</a></i>, which they are still called today.\n</p><p>In November, Balboa decided to return to Santa Mar&#237;a but by a different route in order to further expand his territory and procure more gold. He passed through the regions of Teoca, Pacra, Bugue Bugue, Bononaima, and Chorizo, defeating some by force and befriending others through diplomacy. A particularly bloody battle took place against the <i>cacique</i> Tubanam&#225;, whom Balboa eventually defeated. In December, the expedition arrived back in the Caribbean coast, by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kuna_Yala" title="Kuna Yala">Gulf of San Blas</a>, a strip of land ruled by <i>cacique</i> Pocorosa. From there, he headed to the lands of Comagre, to find that his elderly ally had died. His son, Panquiaco, was now the new chieftain.\n</p><p>From there, he crossed the lands of Ponca and Careta, to finally arrive in Santa Mar&#237;a on January 19, 1514, with a treasure in cotton goods, more than 100,000 <i>castellanos</i>  worth of gold, to say nothing of the pearls. All this, however, did not compare to the magnitude of the discovery of the South Sea on behalf of Spain. Balboa commanded Pedro de Arbolancha to set sail for Spain with news of this discovery. He also sent one fifth of the treasure to the king, as the law required.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Disputes_with_Pedro_Arias">Disputes with Pedro Arias</span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Vasco_N%C3%BA%C3%B1ez_de_Balboa_(E._P%C3%A9rez)_01.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2802" data-file-width="1816" decoding="async" height="339" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Vasco_N%C3%BA%C3%B1ez_de_Balboa_%28E._P%C3%A9rez%29_01.jpg/220px-Vasco_N%C3%BA%C3%B1ez_de_Balboa_%28E._P%C3%A9rez%29_01.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Vasco_N%C3%BA%C3%B1ez_de_Balboa_%28E._P%C3%A9rez%29_01.jpg/330px-Vasco_N%C3%BA%C3%B1ez_de_Balboa_%28E._P%C3%A9rez%29_01.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Vasco_N%C3%BA%C3%B1ez_de_Balboa_%28E._P%C3%A9rez%29_01.jpg/440px-Vasco_N%C3%BA%C3%B1ez_de_Balboa_%28E._P%C3%A9rez%29_01.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Vasco_N%C3%BA%C3%B1ez_de_Balboa_(E._P%C3%A9rez)_01.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Statue of Balboa in <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a> (<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Enrique_P%C3%A9rez_Comendador&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Enrique P&#233;rez Comendador (page does not exist)">Enrique P&#233;rez Comendador</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;[<a class="extiw" href="https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enrique_P%C3%A9rez_Comendador" title="es:Enrique P&#233;rez Comendador">es</a>]</span>, 1954).</div></div></div>\n<p>The accusations of Fern&#225;ndez de Enciso, whom Balboa had deposed, and the removal and disappearance of Governor Ojeda, forced the king to name <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pedro_Arias_de_Avila" title="Pedro Arias de Avila">Pedro Arias de &#193;vila</a> as governor of the newly created province of <a href="/wiki/Castilla_de_Oro" title="Castilla de Oro">Castilla de Oro</a>. Arias, better known as <i>Pedrarias D&#225;vila</i> and who would later become notorious for his cruelty, took control of Veragua and managed to calm the situation. Pedrarias arrived from <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Arbolancha&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Arbolancha (page does not exist)">Arbolancha</a>, Spain with an expedition of 1,500 men and 17 ships, thereby ensuring that Balboa\'s requests to the crown for more men and supplies were met. This would be to that date the largest and most complete expedition to leave Spain for the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">New World</a>.\n</p><p>Pedrarias was accompanied on this expedition by <a href="/wiki/Gaspar_de_Espinosa" title="Gaspar de Espinosa">Gaspar de Espinosa</a>, who held the office of <i>alcalde mayor</i>; the very same Mart&#237;n Fern&#225;ndez de Enciso whom Balboa had forced into exile, now as Chief Constable (<i>Alguacil Mayor</i>);<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> the royal officer and chronicler <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gonzalo_Fernandez_de_Oviedo" title="Gonzalo Fernandez de Oviedo">Gonzalo Fern&#225;ndez de Oviedo</a>; as well as several captains, among them <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_de_Ayora&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Juan de Ayora (page does not exist)">Juan de Ayora</a>, Pedrarias\' lieutenant. There were also several clerics, most notably the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Franciscan" title="Franciscan">Franciscan</a> <a href="/wiki/Friar" title="Friar">friar</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quevedo,_Juan_de" title="Quevedo, Juan de">Juan de Quevedo</a>, appointed <a href="/wiki/Bishop" title="Bishop">bishop</a> of Santa Mar&#237;a. There were also women among the travellers, among them Isabel de Bobadilla, Pedrarias\' wife. More than 500 men died from starvation or due to the inclemencies of the weather soon after reaching Dari&#233;n. Fern&#225;ndez de Oviedo was to note that <a href="/wiki/Knight" title="Knight">knights</a> covered in silk and brocade, who distinguished themselves valiantly in the <a href="/wiki/Italian_Wars" title="Italian Wars">Italian Wars</a>, would die, consumed by hunger and fever, due to the nature of the tropical jungle.\n</p><p>Balboa received Pedrarias and his representatives in July 1514 and accepted resignedly his replacement as governor and mayor. The settlers, however, did not like the change and some were planning to take up arms against Pedrarias, even as Balboa showed respect to the new colonial authorities.\n</p><p>As soon as Pedrarias took charge, Gaspar de Espinosa had Balboa arrested and tried "in absentia", sentencing him to pay reparations to Fern&#225;ndez de Enciso and others. He was, however, found innocent of the charge of murdering Nicuesa, so he was freed shortly afterwards.\n</p><p>Due to overpopulation in Santa Mar&#237;a, Pedrarias called on several expeditionaries to search for new locations fit for settlement. Balboa requested of Pedrarias that he be allowed to explore the Dabaibe region, along the <a href="/wiki/Atrato" title="Atrato">Atrato</a> river, for there was a rumour of the existence of a temple filled with vast riches there. However, this expedition turned out to be a failure, leaving Balboa wounded due to constant attacks by the region\'s natives.\n</p><p>This setback, however, did not deter Balboa\'s ambitions of returning to explore the South Sea. Secretly, he arranged to recruit a contingent of men from <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>. The ship carrying them berthed just outside Santa Mar&#237;a, and its caretaker informed Balboa of their arrival, receiving in return 70 gold castellanos. Pedrarias, however, soon found out about the ship; furious, he had Balboa arrested, took away all his men and was planning to lock him up in a wooden cage. He was held back from doing this by Bishop de Quevedo, who appealed to him not to abuse his power on Balboa.\n</p><p>Luckily for Balboa, around that time the Spanish Crown would finally recognize his valuable services. The king bestowed on him the titles of "<i><a href="/wiki/Adelantado" title="Adelantado">Adelantado</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> of the South Seas" and "<i>Gobernador</i> of <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">Panama</a> and <a href="/wiki/Coiba" title="Coiba">Coiba</a>". On top of this, the King instructed Pedrarias to show Balboa the greatest respect and to consult him on all matters pertaining to the conquest and government of Castilla de Oro. Because of all this, Pedrarias was to release and exonerate Balboa, lifting all charges brought up against him in the matter of the clandestine recruitment of an expeditionary party.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Downfall_and_death">Downfall and death</span></h2>\n<p>At that point, the rivalry between Balboa and Pedrarias ceased abruptly, due in large part to the intercession of Bishop de Quevedo and <a href="/wiki/Isabel_de_Bobadilla" title="Isabel de Bobadilla">Isabel de Bobadilla</a>, who arranged for Balboa\'s marriage to Mar&#237;a de Pe&#241;alosa, one of Pedrarias\' daughters,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Diaz_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Diaz-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>15</span></sup>  who was in Spain. Shortly thereafter, the bishop left for Spain and the marriage took place by proxy (they would never meet because she was in Spain and Balboa would never return to his homeland). The friendship between Pedrarias and Balboa lasted barely two years, but in that time Balboa came to show great filial affection toward his father-in-law.\n</p><p>Balboa wished to continue exploring the South Sea, but Pedrarias did everything possible to delay his departure. However, in light of the new relationship between them, Pedrarias could not stop him indefinitely, and he finally consented to let Balboa go on his new expedition, giving him license to explore for a year and a half.\n</p><p>In 1519, Balboa moved to <a href="/wiki/Acla" title="Acla">Acla</a> with 300 men and, using the manpower of the natives and African slaves, managed to gather the materials necessary to fashion new ships. He traveled up to the <a href="/wiki/Balsas_River" title="Balsas River">Balsas River</a> (R&#237;o Balsas), where he had four ships built. He travelled 74&#160;km (46&#160;mi) through the Pacific, surrounding the Pearl Islands and the coasts of Dari&#233;n, up to <i>Puerto Pi&#241;as</i>, so named because of the large amounts of pineapples <i>(pi&#241;as)</i> he found there. He then returned to Acla, to continue the construction of sturdier ships.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Execution_of_Balboa.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1468" data-file-width="2433" decoding="async" height="133" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Execution_of_Balboa.jpg/220px-Execution_of_Balboa.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Execution_of_Balboa.jpg/330px-Execution_of_Balboa.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Execution_of_Balboa.jpg/440px-Execution_of_Balboa.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Execution_of_Balboa.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Image of the execution of Balboa in <i>Vasco Nu&#241;ez de Balboa</i> by <a href="/wiki/Frederick_A._Ober" title="Frederick A. Ober">Frederick A. Ober</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></div></div></div>\n<p>On his return, however, Pedrarias wrote warm letters urging Balboa to meet him as soon as possible. Balboa quickly obeyed. Halfway to Santa Mar&#237;a, he encountered a group of soldiers commanded by <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Pizarro" title="Francisco Pizarro">Francisco Pizarro</a>, who arrested him in the name of the governor and accused him of trying to usurp Pedrarias\' power and create a separate government in the South Sea. Outraged, Balboa denied all charges and demanded that he be taken to Spain to stand trial. Pedrarias, however, together with Martin Enciso, ordered that the trial take place without delay.\n</p><p>Balboa\'s trial began in January, 1519, and on the fifteenth of that month,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Espinosa sentenced him to death by decapitation. Four of Balboa\'s friends, Fernando de Arg&#252;ello, Luis Botello, Hern&#225;n Mu&#241;oz, and Andr&#233;s Valderr&#225;bano, accused as accomplices, were sentenced to the same fate. The sentence was to be carried out in Acla, to show that the conspiracy had its roots in that colony.\n</p><p>As Balboa and his friends were being led to the block, the town crier announced: "This is the justice that the King and his lieutenant Pedro Arias de &#193;vila impose upon these men, traitors and usurpers of the Crown\'s territories." Balboa could not restrain his indignation and replied: "Lies, lies! Never have such crimes held a place in my heart, I have always loyally served the King, with no thought in my mind but to increase his dominions." Pedrarias observed the execution, hidden behind a platform. The executioner beheaded Balboa and his four friends with an axe. Balboa\'s head did not come off clean on the first try; it took three.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Their heads remained in public display for several days, as a sign of Pedrarias\' might. The final location of Balboa\'s remains is unknown, partly because there is no record of what happened in Acla after the execution.\n</p><p>Gaspar de Espinosa, Pedrarias\' underling, sailed the South Sea aboard the very ships that Balboa had commissioned. In 1520, <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_Magellan" title="Ferdinand Magellan">Ferdinand Magellan</a> renamed the sea the <i><a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a></i> because of its calm waters.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:1-cent_Panama-Pacific_Expo_1913_U.S._stamp.1.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Balboa 1-cent, 1913 issue</div></div></div>\n<p>Although Balboa suffered a premature death, his actions and deeds are remembered by history as the first European to cross <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">America</a>. Several parks and avenues throughout <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">Panama</a> bear the name "Vasco N&#250;&#241;ez de Balboa," and a number of monuments honour his discovery of the South Sea. The Panamanian currency is called the <a href="/wiki/Panamanian_balboa" title="Panamanian balboa">Balboa</a>, and his likeness appears on the obverse of most Panamanian coins. His name is also attached to <a href="/wiki/Panama_City" title="Panama City">Panama City</a>\'s main port, <a href="/wiki/Balboa,_Panama" title="Balboa, Panama">Balboa</a> (the Pacific entrance to the <a href="/wiki/Panama_Canal" title="Panama Canal">Panama Canal</a>) and the Balboa District within <a href="/wiki/Panam%C3%A1_Province" title="Panam&#225; Province">Panam&#225; Province</a> to which the <a href="/wiki/Pearl_Islands" title="Pearl Islands">Pearl Islands</a> that he discovered belong. In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Francisco,_California" title="San Francisco, California">San Francisco, California</a>, USA, Balboa\'s name appears among a row of avenues which are named after Spanish conquistadors and in a San Francisco neighborhood <a href="/wiki/Balboa_Park,_San_Francisco" title="Balboa Park, San Francisco">Balboa Park</a>. There is also a large park (<a href="/wiki/Balboa_Park_(San_Diego)" title="Balboa Park (San Diego)">Balboa Park</a>) adjacent to downtown <a href="/wiki/San_Diego" title="San Diego">San Diego</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> which was named after Balboa in 1910.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Balboa\'s name is also honoured in <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a> with a street and a <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BA%C3%B1ez_de_Balboa_(Madrid_Metro)" title="N&#250;&#241;ez de Balboa (Madrid Metro)">metro station</a>.\n</p><p>One of the highest <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Order_(decoration)" title="Order (decoration)">orders</a> granted by the Panamanian government to distinguished and outstanding figures, foreign and domestic, is the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orden_Vasco_N%C3%BA%C3%B1ez_de_Balboa" title="Orden Vasco N&#250;&#241;ez de Balboa">Orden Vasco N&#250;&#241;ez de Balboa</a></i>, in various degrees, as established by Law 27 from 28 January 1933.\n</p><p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lunar_crater" title="Lunar crater">lunar crater</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Balboa_(crater)" title="Balboa (crater)">Balboa</a> was named after him.\n</p><p>Balboa appears in the lyrics to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=The_Great_Nations_of_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="The Great Nations of Europe (page does not exist)">The Great Nations of Europe</a> by composer/singer <a href="/wiki/Randy_Newman" title="Randy Newman">Randy Newman</a>.\n</p><p>Vasco Nunez de Balboa was featured on the 1-cent denomination of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Panama-Pacific_Exposition" title="Panama-Pacific Exposition">Panama-Pacific Exposition</a> of 1913. The 1-cent Balboa paid the one-cent card rate, and it was used in combination with other denominations to meet large weight and foreign destinations. The Bureau of Engraving and Printing issued over 330 million of these to the public.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>\n</p><p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SENAN" title="SENAN">SENAN</a>\'s <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Base_Naval_Vasco_Nu%C3%B1ez_de_Balboa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Base Naval Vasco Nu&#241;ez de Balboa (page does not exist)">Base Naval Vasco Nu&#241;ez de Balboa</a> in <a href="/wiki/Panama_City" title="Panama City">Panama City</a> is named for Balboa.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span></h2>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Age_of_Discovery" title="Age of Discovery">Age of Discovery</a></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span></h2>\n<ul><li><cite class="citation book">Asenjo Garc&#237;a, Frutos (November 1991). <i>Vasco N&#250;&#241;ez de Balboa: El descubrimiento del Mar del Sur</i>.
