title="Portuguese people">Portuguese</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dom_(title)" title="Dom (title)">Dom</a> Pedro Mascarenhas</b> (1470 &#8211; 16 June 1555) was a <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portuguese</a> explorer and colonial administrator. He was the first European to discover the island of <a href="/wiki/Diego_Garcia" title="Diego Garcia">Diego Garcia</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a> in 1512. He also encountered the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a> island of <a href="/wiki/Mauritius" title="Mauritius">Mauritius</a> in 1512, although he may not have been the first Portuguese explorer to do so; earlier expeditions by <a href="/wiki/Diogo_Dias" title="Diogo Dias">Diogo Dias</a> and <a href="/wiki/Afonso_de_Albuquerque" title="Afonso de Albuquerque">Afonso de Albuquerque</a> along with <a href="/wiki/Diogo_Fernandes_Pereira" title="Diogo Fernandes Pereira">Diogo Fernandes Pereira</a> may have encountered the islands. In 1528 explorer <a href="/wiki/Diogo_Rodrigues" title="Diogo Rodrigues">Diogo Rodrigues</a> (after whom the island of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rodrigues_(island)" title="Rodrigues (island)">Rodrigues</a> is named) named the islands of <a href="/wiki/R%C3%A9union" title="R&#233;union">R&#233;union</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mauritius" title="Mauritius">Mauritius</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rodrigues_(island)" title="Rodrigues (island)">Rodrigues</a> the <a href="/wiki/Mascarene_Islands" title="Mascarene Islands">Mascarene Islands</a>, after Mascarenhas.\n</p><p>Mascarenhas served as Captain-Major of the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Malacca" title="Portuguese Malacca">Portuguese Malacca</a> from 1525 to 1526.\n</p><p>He was ambassador from Portugal to the <a href="/wiki/Holy_See" title="Holy See">Holy See</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uc.pt_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-uc.pt-1">[1]</a></sup> where he appealed to <a href="/wiki/Pope_Paul_III" title="Pope Paul III">Pope Paul III</a> for the coming of the first <a href="/wiki/Society_of_Jesus" title="Society of Jesus">jesuits</a> for the Portuguese missions in India, at request of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_John_III_of_Portugal" title="King John III of Portugal">King John III</a> and <a href="/wiki/Diogo_de_Gouveia" title="Diogo de Gouveia">Diogo de Gouveia</a>. His mission ended on March 15, 1540, when he travelled back to Portugal together with <a href="/wiki/Francis_Xavier" title="Francis Xavier">Francis Xavier</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uc.pt_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-uc.pt-1">[1]</a></sup>\n</p><p>He was <a href="/wiki/Viceroy" title="Viceroy">viceroy</a> at <a href="/wiki/Goa" title="Goa">Goa</a>, capital of the Portuguese possessions in <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, from 1554 until his death in 1555, in Goa. He was succeeded as viceroy by <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Barreto" title="Francisco Barreto">Francisco Barreto</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kerr_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-kerr-2">[2]</a></sup> While viceroy of <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_India" title="Portuguese India">Portuguese India</a>, at the direction of the King of Portugal he sent Fr. James Dias and Fr. Gon&#231;alo Rodrigues to <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a> in order to determine whether Emperor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gelawdewos_of_Ethiopia" title="Gelawdewos of Ethiopia">Galawdewos</a> would be receptive to receiving a Patriarch anointed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism_in_Ethiopia" title="Roman Catholicism in Ethiopia">Roman Catholic church</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pedro_Mascarenhas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-uc.pt-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink">^ <a href="#cite_ref-uc.pt_1-0"><sup><i><b>a</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-uc.pt_1-1"><sup><i><b>b</b></i></sup></a></span> <span class="reference-text"><a class="external free" href="http://www.uc.pt/fluc/eclassicos/publicacoes/ficheiros/humanitas52/12_Pinho.pdf" rel="nofollow">http://www.uc.pt/fluc/eclassicos/publicacoes/ficheiros/humanitas52/12_Pinho.pdf</a></span>\n</li>\n<li id="cite_note-kerr-2"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-kerr_2-0">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation book"><a href="/wiki/Robert_Kerr_(writer)" title="Robert Kerr (writer)">Robert Kerr</a>, ed.
