id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>\nKnowledge of the <b>location of Earth</b> has been shaped by 400 years of telescopic observations, and has expanded radically in the last century. Initially, Earth was believed to be the <a href="/wiki/Geocentric_model" title="Geocentric model">center of the Universe</a>,  \nwhich consisted only of those planets visible with the <a href="/wiki/Classical_planet" title="Classical planet">naked eye</a> and an outlying sphere of <a href="/wiki/Fixed_stars" title="Fixed stars">fixed stars</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> After the acceptance of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heliocentric_model" title="Heliocentric model">heliocentric model</a> in the 17th century, observations by <a href="/wiki/William_Herschel" title="William Herschel">William Herschel</a> and others showed that the Sun lay within a vast, disc-shaped <a href="/wiki/Galaxy" title="Galaxy">galaxy</a> of stars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> By the 20th century, observations of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spiral_nebula" title="Spiral nebula">spiral nebulae</a> revealed that our galaxy was one of billions in an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Expanding_universe" title="Expanding universe">expanding universe</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> grouped into clusters and <a href="/wiki/Supercluster" title="Supercluster">superclusters</a>. By the end of the 20th century, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Large-scale_structure_of_the_cosmos" title="Large-scale structure of the cosmos">overall structure</a> of the visible universe was becoming clearer, with superclusters forming into a vast web of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Galactic_filament" title="Galactic filament">filaments</a> and <a href="/wiki/Void_(astronomy)" title="Void (astronomy)">voids</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Superclusters, filaments and voids are the largest coherent structures in the Universe that we can observe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> At still larger scales (over 1000 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Megaparsec" title="Megaparsec">megaparsecs</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-parsec_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-parsec-7">[a]</a></sup>) the Universe becomes homogeneous meaning that all its parts have on average the same density, composition and structure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kirshner_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kirshner-8">[7]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Since there is believed to be no "center" or "edge" of the Universe, there is no particular reference point with which to plot the overall location of the Earth in the universe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Klaus2002_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Klaus2002-9">[8]</a></sup> Because the <a href="/wiki/Observable_universe" title="Observable universe">observable universe</a> is defined as that region of the Universe visible to terrestrial observers, Earth is, by definition, the center of Earth\'s observable universe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[9]</a></sup> Reference can be made to the Earth\'s position with respect to specific structures, which exist at various scales. It is still undetermined whether the Universe is <a href="/wiki/Infinity" title="Infinity">infinite</a>. There have been numerous hypotheses that our universe may be only one such example within a higher <a href="/wiki/Multiverse" title="Multiverse">multiverse</a>; however, no direct evidence of any sort of multiverse has ever been observed, and some have argued that the hypothesis is not <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Falsifiable" title="Falsifiable">falsifiable</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[11]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner"
