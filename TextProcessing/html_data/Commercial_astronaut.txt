Smith, Associate Administrator for Commercial Space Transportation at the FAA, presents SpaceShipOne pilot <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Michael_Melvill" title="Michael Melvill">Michael Melvill</a> the department\'s first commercial astronaut wings</div></div></div><p>A <b>commercial astronaut</b> is a person trained to command, pilot, or serve as a crew member of a privately funded <a href="/wiki/Spacecraft" title="Spacecraft">spacecraft</a>. This is distinct from an otherwise non-government astronaut, for example <a href="/wiki/Charles_D._Walker" title="Charles D. Walker">Charlie Walker</a>, who flies while representing a non-government corporation but with funding and/or training coming from government sources.\n</p><div class="toc" id="toc"><input class="toctogglecheckbox" id="toctogglecheckbox" role="button" style="display:none" type="checkbox"/><div class="toctitle" dir="ltr" lang="en"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Criteria"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Criteria</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#FAA_Commercial_Astronaut_rating"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">FAA Commercial Astronaut rating</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#List_of_Commercial_Astronauts"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">List of Commercial Astronauts</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#See_also"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">See also</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criteria">Criteria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Commercial_astronaut&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Criteria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The definition of &#8220;<a href="/wiki/Astronaut" title="Astronaut">astronaut</a>&#8221; and the criteria for determining who has achieved <a href="/wiki/Human_spaceflight" title="Human spaceflight">human spaceflight</a> vary. The <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_A%C3%A9ronautique_Internationale" title="F&#233;d&#233;ration A&#233;ronautique Internationale">FAI</a> defines spaceflight as any flight over 100 kilometers (62&#160;mi) of altitude. In the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, professional, military, and commercial astronauts who travel above an altitude of 80 kilometers (50&#160;mi) are eligible to be awarded <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Astronaut_Badge" title="Astronaut Badge">astronaut wings</a>. Until 2003, <a href="/wiki/Astronaut" title="Astronaut">professional space travelers</a> were sponsored and trained exclusively by governments, whether by the military or by civilian space agencies. However, with the first sub-orbital flight of the privately funded <a href="/wiki/Scaled_Composites_Tier_One" title="Scaled Composites Tier One">Scaled Composites Tier One</a> in 2004, the commercial astronaut category was created.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="FAA_Commercial_Astronaut_rating">FAA Commercial Astronaut rating</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Commercial_astronaut&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: FAA Commercial Astronaut rating">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:US_-_FAA_Astronaut_Wings.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="649" data-file-width="2435" decoding="async" height="59" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/US_-_FAA_Astronaut_Wings.png/220px-US_-_FAA_Astronaut_Wings.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/US_-_FAA_Astronaut_Wings.png/330px-US_-_FAA_Astronaut_Wings.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/US_-_FAA_Astronaut_Wings.png/440px-US_-_FAA_Astronaut_Wings.png 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:US_-_FAA_Astronaut_Wings.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>FAA Commercial Astronaut Wings</div></div></div><p>With the advent of private commercial space flight ventures in the U.S., the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Aviation_Administration" title="Federal Aviation Administration">FAA</a> has been faced with the task of developing a certification process for the pilots of commercial spacecraft. The Commercial Space Launch Act of 1984 established the FAA\'s <a href="/wiki/Office_of_Commercial_Space_Transportation" title="Office of Commercial Space Transportation">Office of Commercial Space Transportation</a> and required companies to obtain a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Launch_license" title="Launch license">launch license</a> for vehicles, but at the time manned commercial flight - and the licensing of crewmembers - was not considered. The Commercial Space Launch Amendments Act has led to the issuance of draft guidelines by the FAA in February 2005 for the administration of vehicle and crew certifications.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Currently, the FAA has not issued formal regulatory guidance for the issuance of a Commercial Astronaut Certificate, but as an interim measure, has established the practice of awarding "Commercial Astronaut Wings" to commercial pilots who have demonstrated the requisite proficiency. The content of 14 CFR Part 460 implies that an instrument rating and second-class medical certificate issued within the 12 months prior to the proposed qualifying flight will be included as a minimum standard.\n</p><h2><span class="mw-headline" id="List_of_Commercial_Astronauts">List of Commercial Astronauts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Commercial_astronaut&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: List of Commercial Astronauts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>FAA Commercial Astronaut Wings have been awarded to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scaled_Composites_SpaceShipOne" title="Scaled Composites SpaceShipOne">SpaceShipOne</a> pilots <a href="/wiki/Mike_Melvill" title="Mike Melvill">Mike Melvill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brian_Binnie" title="Brian Binnie">Brian Binnie</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Virgin_Galactic_Unity" title="Virgin Galactic Unity">Virgin Galactic Unity</a> pilots <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mark_Stucky" title="Mark Stucky">Mark Stucky</a> and <a href="/wiki/Frederick_W._Sturckow" title="Frederick W.
