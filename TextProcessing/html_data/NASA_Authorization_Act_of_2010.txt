id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>\n<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>\n<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>\n<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>NASA Authorization Act of 2010</b> is a U.S. law authorizing <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a> appropriations for <a href="/wiki/Fiscal_year" title="Fiscal year">fiscal years</a> 2011, 2012, 2013 with the same top-line budget values as requested by <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">US President</a> <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a>. It resulted from the <a href="/wiki/Review_of_United_States_Human_Space_Flight_Plans_Committee" title="Review of United States Human Space Flight Plans Committee">Augustine Commission</a>\'s review of then-current manned space flight plans.\n</p><p>The law supports an overall growth in <a href="/wiki/Science" title="Science">science</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aeronautics" title="Aeronautics">aeronautics</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space_technology" title="Space technology">space technology</a> and defines a long-term goal for <a href="/wiki/Human_spaceflight" title="Human spaceflight">human spaceflight</a> to expand a permanent human presence <a href="/wiki/Flexible_path" title="Flexible path">beyond low Earth orbit</a>. Key objectives of this goal include full utilization of the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a> (ISS), determining the ability of humans to live in <a href="/wiki/Outer_space" title="Outer space">outer space</a> for extended periods of time, maximizing the role of <a href="/wiki/Space_exploration" title="Space exploration">space exploration</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space_technology" title="Space technology">space technology</a> in current and future missions, advancing knowledge and inspiring young people into <a href="/wiki/Higher_education" title="Higher education">higher education</a>, and building upon international partnerships.\n</p><p>The act was signed by President Obama on October&#160;11, 2010. A total of $58 billion in funding is called for, spread across three years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spacenews_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-spacenews-1">[1]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="toc" class="tocnumber">10</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Human_spaceflight">Human spaceflight</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=NASA_Authorization_Act_of_2010&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Human spaceflight">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In regard to <a href="/wiki/Human_spaceflight" title="Human spaceflight">human spaceflight</a>, the law is intended to:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Couple human spaceflight efforts to national and global needs and challenges.</li>\n<li>Provide a sustainable <a href="/wiki/Space_exploration" title="Space exploration">space exploration</a> program to incorporate new technologies and in-space capabilities.</li>\n<li>Require immediate development of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heavy_lift_launch_vehicle" title="Heavy lift launch vehicle">heavy lift launch vehicle</a> and continued support of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crew_exploration_vehicle" title="Crew exploration vehicle">crew exploration vehicle</a> to be capable of supporting missions beyond <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low_earth_orbit" title="Low earth orbit">low earth orbit</a> starting in 2016.</li>\n<li>Support a sound performance and cost framework by maximizing use, where possible, of the <a href="/wiki/Workforce" title="Workforce">workforce</a>, assets, and capabilities of the <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle" title="Space Shuttle">Space Shuttle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Constellation_program" title="Constellation program">Constellation program</a>, and other NASA programs.</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Space_technology">Space technology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=NASA_Authorization_Act_of_2010&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Space technology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The law will invest in space technologies and <a href="/wiki/Robotics" title="Robotics">robotics</a> capabilities that are tied to the overall <a href="/wiki/Space_exploration" title="Space exploration">space exploration</a> framework and support U.S. innovation and competitiveness.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_cargo_and_crew">Commercial cargo and crew</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=NASA_Authorization_Act_of_2010&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Commercial cargo and crew">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In regard to commercial cargo and crew, the law will:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Continue to support commercial cargo development and provide additional funds to meet launch infrastructure requirements and accelerate development activity.</li>\n<li>Expand the <a href="/wiki/Commercial_Crew_Development" title="Commercial Crew Development">Commercial Crew Development</a> Program in 2011 for concept development and supporting activities, while requiring a number of studies to ensure effective oversight of the potential initiation of a commercial crew capability procurement program no earlier than 2012.</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_Space_Station">International Space Station</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=NASA_Authorization_Act_of_2010&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: International Space Station">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In regard to the International Space Station (ISS), the law will:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Extend the ISS to at least 2020 to support international and commercial collaboration and growth, research, and technology development to maximize the scientific return on the significant investment in the ISS.</li>\n<li>Establish an independent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Non-profit" title="Non-profit">non-profit</a> to work with NASA to fully develop the ISS U.S. segment as a National Laboratory.</li>\n<li>Require an assessment of ISS requirements for parts and equipment needed to ensure its full functionality through 2020.</li></ul>\n<h2><span id="Additional_.22launch_on_need.22_Shuttle_mission"></span><span class="mw-headline" id=\'Additional_"launch_on_need"_Shuttle_mission\'>Additional "launch on need" Shuttle mission</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=NASA_Authorization_Act_of_2010&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title=\'Edit section: Additional "launch on need" Shuttle mission\'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The law authorizes an additional Space Shuttle flight (<a href="/wiki/STS-135" title="STS-135">STS-135</a>), contingent on a safety review, to provide necessary support for the extension of the ISS.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Science_and_aeronautics">Science and aeronautics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=NASA_Authorization_Act_of_2010&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Science and aeronautics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The law protects a balanced portfolio for NASA, including full funding of <a href="/wiki/Aeronautics" title="Aeronautics">aeronautics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Earth_science" title="Earth science">Earth science</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space_science" title="Space science">space science</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=NASA_Authorization_Act_of_2010&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In regard to education, the law will:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Support new education initiatives, such as teacher training programs, to reinforce NASA&#8217;s role in developing a <a href="/wiki/Workforce" title="Workforce">workforce</a> with strong <a href="/wiki/Science" title="Science">science</a>, <a href="/wiki/Technology" title="Technology">technology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">engineering</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a> skills.</li>\n<li>Increase the investment in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=NASA_EPSCoR&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="NASA EPSCoR (page does not exist)">NASA EPSCoR</a> (Experimental Program to Stimulate Competitive Research) and NASA Space Grant program.</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rescoping_and_revitalizing_institutional_capabilities">Rescoping and revitalizing institutional capabilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=NASA_Authorization_Act_of_2010&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Rescoping and revitalizing institutional capabilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The law requires NASA to examine alternative <a href="/wiki/Management" title="Management">management</a> models for NASA&#8217;s <a href="/wiki/Workforce" title="Workforce">workforce</a>, centers, and capabilities, while enforcing short-term prohibitions on major center displacements and reductions-in-force until the study is completed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-senate_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-senate-2">[2]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
