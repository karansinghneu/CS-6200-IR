href="/w/index.php?title=Human_outpost&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: In fiction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Human outposts in other worlds are a common motif in science fiction, whether established and occupied solely by humans or in cooperation &#8211; or competition &#8211; with <a href="/wiki/Extraterrestrials_in_fiction" title="Extraterrestrials in fiction">alien species</a>.  The setting may be another planet, Earth-like or otherwise; or a <a href="/wiki/Spacecraft" title="Spacecraft">spaceship</a> large enough to house a city.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reality:_Scientific_advances">Reality: Scientific advances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Human_outpost&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reality: Scientific advances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Under_sea">Under sea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Human_outpost&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Under sea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a> currently trains <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Astronauts" title="Astronauts">astronauts</a> in an <a href="/wiki/Underwater_habitat" title="Underwater habitat">underwater habitat</a>, to simulate living and working in the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a>. They conduct scientific research on the human body and coral reefs, and build undersea structures to simulate space station assembly spacewalk tasks. The program is also being used to study how isolation affects human behaviour, to prepare for the first human outposts on the Moon and Mars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Outer_space">Outer space</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Human_outpost&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Outer space">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Salyut_1" title="Salyut 1">Salyut 1</a> <a href="/wiki/Space_station" title="Space station">space station</a> in <a href="/wiki/Low_Earth_orbit" title="Low Earth orbit">low Earth orbit</a> was the first human outpost in space.  The only current human outposts in space are the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a> and China\'s <a href="/wiki/Tiangong-2" title="Tiangong-2">Tiangong-2</a>.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/NanoRacks" title="NanoRacks">NanoRacks</a>, after finalizing its contract with NASA, and after winning NextSTEPs Phase II award, is now developing its concept Independence-1 (previously known as Ixion), the first \'outpost\' in NanoRacks\' Space Outpost Program, which would turn spent rocket tanks into a habitable living area, often known as a <a href="/wiki/Wet_workshop" title="Wet workshop">wet workshop</a>.\n</p><p>Planning and design for <a href="/wiki/Colonization_of_the_Moon" title="Colonization of the Moon">Lunar</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NASA_goes_lunar:_Robot_craft,_human_outpost_plans_(USA_TODAY)_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-NASA_goes_lunar:_Robot_craft,_human_outpost_plans_(USA_TODAY)-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Colonization_of_Mars" title="Colonization of Mars">Martian</a> outposts is underway.<sup class="reference"
