href="/wiki/Cape_Disappointment_(Antarctica)" title="Cape Disappointment (Antarctica)">Disappointment Bay</a>, Antarctica, early 1840</div></div></div>\n<p>The <b>United States Exploring Expedition</b> of 1838&#8211;1842 was an exploring and surveying expedition of the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a> and surrounding lands conducted by the United States. The original appointed commanding officer was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commodore_(USN)#History" title="Commodore (USN)">Commodore</a> <a href="/wiki/Thomas_ap_Catesby_Jones" title="Thomas ap Catesby Jones">Thomas ap Catesby Jones</a>. Funding for the original expedition was requested by President <a href="/wiki/John_Quincy_Adams" title="John Quincy Adams">John Quincy Adams</a> in 1828, however, Congress would not implement funding until eight years later. In May 1836, the oceanic exploration voyage was finally authorized by <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">Congress</a> and created by President <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Jackson" title="Andrew Jackson">Andrew Jackson</a>.\n</p><p>The expedition is sometimes called the "U.S. Ex.&#160;Ex." for short, or the "Wilkes Expedition" in honor of its next appointed commanding officer, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> Lieutenant <a href="/wiki/Charles_Wilkes" title="Charles Wilkes">Charles Wilkes</a>. The expedition was of major importance to the growth of science in the United States, in particular the then-young field of <a href="/wiki/Oceanography" title="Oceanography">oceanography</a>. During the event, armed conflict between Pacific islanders and the expedition was common and dozens of natives were killed in action, as well as a few Americans.\n</p>\n<div class="toc" href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_Exploring_Expedition&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Through the <a href="/wiki/Lobbying_in_the_United_States" title="Lobbying in the United States">lobbying efforts</a> of <a href="/wiki/Jeremiah_N._Reynolds" title="Jeremiah N. Reynolds">Jeremiah N. Reynolds</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives" title="United States House of Representatives">United States House of Representatives</a> passed a resolution on May 21, 1828, requesting President <a href="/wiki/John_Quincy_Adams" title="John Quincy Adams">John Quincy Adams</a> to send a ship to explore the Pacific. Adams was keen on the resolution and ordered his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Secretary_of_the_Navy" title="Secretary of the Navy">Secretary of the Navy</a> to ready a ship, the <a href="/wiki/USS_Peacock_(1813)" title="USS Peacock (1813)"><i>Peacock</i></a>, while the House voted an <a href="/wiki/Appropriation_(law)" title="Appropriation (law)">appropriation</a> in December Yet, the bill stalled in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Senate" title="US Senate">US Senate</a> in February 1829. However, under President <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Jackson" title="Andrew Jackson">Andrew Jackson</a>, Congress passed legislation in 1836 approving the exploration mission. Yet again, the effort stalled under Secretary of the Navy <a href="/wiki/Mahlon_Dickerson" title="Mahlon Dickerson">Mahlon Dickerson</a> until President <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Van_Buren" title="Van Buren">Van Buren</a> assumed office and pushed the effort forward.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEStanton197513&#8211;17_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStanton197513&#8211;17-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEDupree198859&#8211;65_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDupree198859&#8211;65-2">[2]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Originally, the expedition was under the command <a href="/wiki/Thomas_ap_Catesby_Jones" title="Thomas ap Catesby Jones">Commodore Jones</a>, but he resigned in November 1837, frustrated with all of the procrastination. <a href="/wiki/United_States_Secretary_of_War" title="United States Secretary of War">Secretary of War</a> <a href="/wiki/Joel_Roberts_Poinsett" title="Joel Roberts Poinsett">Joel Roberts Poinsett</a>, in April 1838, then assigned command to Wilkes, after more senior officers refused the command. Wilkes had a reputation for <a href="/wiki/Hydrography" title="Hydrography">hydrography</a>, <a href="/wiki/Geodesy" title="Geodesy">geodesy</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Magnetism" title="Magnetism">magnetism</a>. Additionally, Wilkes had received mathematics training from <a href="/wiki/Nathaniel_Bowditch" title="Nathaniel Bowditch">Nathaniel Bowditch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Triangulation" title="Triangulation">triangulation</a> methods from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ferdinand_Hassler" title="Ferdinand Hassler">Ferdinand Hassler</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geomagnetism" title="Geomagnetism">geomagnetism</a> from <a href="/wiki/James_Renwick_(physicist)" title="James Renwick (physicist)">James Renwick</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEStanton197519,_35,_56&#8211;61_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStanton197519,_35,_56&#8211;61-3">[3]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Personnel included <a href="/wiki/Natural_history" title="Natural history">naturalists</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Botanist" title="Botanist">botanists</a>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mineralogist" title="Mineralogist">mineralogist</a>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taxidermist" title="Taxidermist">taxidermist</a>, and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philologist" title="Philologist">philologist</a>. They were carried aboard the <a href="/wiki/Sloop-of-war" title="Sloop-of-war">sloops-of-war</a> <a href="/wiki/USS_Vincennes_(1826)" title="USS Vincennes (1826)">USS&#160;<i>Vincennes</i></a> (780&#160;tons), and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USS_Peacock_(1828)" title="USS Peacock (1828)">USS&#160;<i>Peacock</i></a> (650&#160;tons), the <a href="/wiki/Brig" title="Brig">brig</a> <a href="/wiki/USS_Porpoise_(1836)" title="USS Porpoise (1836)">USS&#160;<i>Porpoise</i></a> (230&#160;tons), the <a href="/wiki/Full-rigged_ship" title="Full-rigged ship">full-rigged ship</a> <a href="/wiki/USS_Relief_(1836)" title="USS Relief (1836)"><i>Relief</i></a>, which served as a <a href="/wiki/Auxiliary_ship" title="Auxiliary ship">store-ship</a>, and two <a href="/wiki/Schooner" title="Schooner">schooners</a>, <a href="/wiki/USS_Sea_Gull_(1838)" title="USS Sea Gull (1838)"><i>Sea Gull</i></a> (110&#160;tons) and <a href="/wiki/USS_Flying_Fish_(1838)" title="USS Flying Fish (1838)">USS&#160;<i>Flying Fish</i></a> (96&#160;tons), which served as <a href="/wiki/Ship%27s_tender" title="Ship\'s tender">tenders</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEStanton197543,_63&#8211;68,_73&#8211;76_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStanton197543,_63&#8211;68,_73&#8211;76-4">[4]</a></sup>\n</p><p>On the afternoon of August 18, 1838, the vessels weighed anchor and set to sea under full sail. By 0730 the next morning, they had passed the <a href="/wiki/List_of_lightships_of_the_United_States" title="List of lightships of the United States">lightship</a> off <a href="/wiki/Willoughby_Spit" title="Willoughby Spit">Willoughby Spit</a> and discharged the <a href="/wiki/Maritime_pilot" title="Maritime pilot">pilot</a>. The fleet then headed to <a href="/wiki/Madeira" title="Madeira">Madeira</a>, taking advantage of the prevailing winds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEStanton197571&#8211;76_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStanton197571&#8211;76-5">[5]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Coincidentally, <a href="/wiki/Commodore_(United_States)" title="Commodore (United States)">Commodore</a> <a href="/wiki/George_C._Read" title="George C. Read">George C. Read</a> in command of the <a href="/wiki/East_India_Squadron" title="East India Squadron">East India Squadron</a> aboard the <a href="/wiki/Flagship" title="Flagship">flagship</a> frigate <a href="/wiki/USS_Columbia_(1836)" title="USS Columbia (1836)">USS <i>Columbia</i></a>, together with the frigate <a href="/wiki/USS_John_Adams_(1799)" title="USS John Adams (1799)">USS <i>John Adams</i></a>, were at the time in the process of circumnavigating the globe when the ships paused for the <a href="/wiki/Second_Sumatran_expedition" title="Second Sumatran expedition">second Sumatran punitive expedition</a>, which required no detour.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ships_and_personnel">Ships and personnel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_Exploring_Expedition&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ships and personnel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The expedition consisted of a great many people, a lot of whom were not assigned to any specific vessel. The squadron arrived in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Madeira_Islands" title="Madeira Islands">Madeira Islands</a> on September 16, 1838, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Porto_Praya" title="Porto Praya">Porto Praya</a> on October 6.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEStanton197586&#8211;87_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStanton197586&#8211;87-7">[7]</a></sup> The <i>Peacock</i> arrived at Rio de Janeiro on November 21, and the <i>Vincennes</i> with brigs and schooners on November 24. However, the <i>Relief</i> did not arrive until the November 27, setting a record for slowness, 100 days. While there, they used Enxados Island in <a href="/wiki/Guanabara_Bay" title="Guanabara Bay">Guanabara Bay</a> for an observatory and naval yard for repair and refitting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEStanton197588&#8211;89_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStanton197588&#8211;89-8">[8]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:422px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Charles_wilkes_part1.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1070" data-file-width="2570" decoding="async" height="175" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Charles_wilkes_part1.png/420px-Charles_wilkes_part1.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Charles_wilkes_part1.png/630px-Charles_wilkes_part1.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Charles_wilkes_part1.png/840px-Charles_wilkes_part1.png 2x" width="420"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Charles_wilkes_part1.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Voyage route:&#160;1.&#160;<a href="/wiki/Hampton_Roads" title="Hampton Roads">Hampton Roads</a>&#160;&#8211; 2.&#160;<a href="/wiki/Madeira" title="Madeira">Madeira</a>&#160;&#8211; 3.&#160;<a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>&#160;&#8211; 4.&#160;<a href="/wiki/Tierra_del_Fuego" title="Tierra del Fuego">Tierra del Fuego</a>&#160;&#8211; 5.&#160;<a href="/wiki/Valpara%C3%ADso" title="Valpara&#237;so">Valpara&#237;so</a>&#160;&#8211; 6.&#160;<a href="/wiki/Callao" title="Callao">Callao</a>&#160;&#8211; 7.&#160;<a href="/wiki/Samoa" title="Samoa">Samoa</a>&#160;&#8211; 8.&#160;<a href="/wiki/Fiji" title="Fiji">Fiji</a>&#160;&#8211; 9.&#160;<a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>&#160;&#8211; 10.&#160;<a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctica</a>&#160;&#8211; 11.&#160;<a href="/wiki/Hawaiian_Islands" title="Hawaiian Islands">Sandwich Islands</a></div></div></div>\n<p>The Squadron did not leave Rio de Janeiro until January 6, 1839, arriving at the mouth of the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Negro_(Argentina)" title="R&#237;o Negro (Argentina)">R&#237;o Negro</a> on January 25. On February 19, the squadron joined the <i>Relief</i>, <i>Flying Fish</i>, and <i>Sea Gull</i> in Orange Harbor, <a href="/wiki/Hoste_Island" title="Hoste Island">Hoste Island</a>, after passing through <a href="/wiki/Le_Maire_Strait" title="Le Maire Strait">Le Maire Strait</a>. While there, the expedition came in contact with the <a href="/wiki/Fuegians" title="Fuegians">Fuegians</a>. Wilkes sent an expedition south in an attempt to exceed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Captain_Cook" title="Captain Cook">Captain Cook</a>\'s farthest point south, 71&#176;10\'.\n</p><p>The <i>Flying Fish</i> reached 70&#176; on March 22, in the area about 100 miles north of <a href="/wiki/Thurston_Island" title="Thurston Island">Thurston Island</a>, and what is now called <a href="/wiki/Cape_Flying_Fish" title="Cape Flying Fish">Cape Flying Fish</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Walker_Mountains" title="Walker Mountains">Walker Mountains</a>. The squadron joined the <i>Peacock</i> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Valparaiso" title="Valparaiso">Valparaiso</a> on May 10, but the <i>Sea Gull</i> was reported missing. On June 6, the squadron arrived <a href="/wiki/San_Lorenzo_Island_(Peru)" title="San Lorenzo Island (Peru)">San Lorenzo</a>, off <a href="/wiki/Callao" title="Callao">Callao</a> for repair and provisioning, while Wilkes dispatched the <i>Relief</i> homewards on June 21.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEStanton197591&#8211;96,_103&#8211;111_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStanton197591&#8211;96,_103&#8211;111-9">[9]</a></sup> Leaving South America on July 12, the expedition reached <a href="/wiki/Reao" title="Reao">Reao</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tuamotu" title="Tuamotu">Tuamotu</a> Group on August 13, and <a href="/wiki/Tahiti" title="Tahiti">Tahiti</a> on September 11. They departed Tahiti on October 10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEStanton1975114&#8211;116,_123&#8211;131_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStanton1975114&#8211;116,_123&#8211;131-10">[10]</a></sup>\n</p><p>The expedition then visited <a href="/wiki/Samoa" title="Samoa">Samoa</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">New South Wales</a>, Australia. In December 1839, the expedition sailed from Sydney into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antarctic_Ocean" title="Antarctic Ocean">Antarctic Ocean</a> and reported the discovery of the <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctic continent</a> on January 16, 1840, when <a href="/wiki/Henry_Eld" title="Henry Eld">Henry Eld</a> and <a href="/wiki/William_Reynolds_(naval_officer)" title="William Reynolds (naval officer)">William Reynolds</a> aboard the <i>Peacock</i> sighted <a href="/wiki/Eld_Peak" title="Eld Peak">Eld Peak</a> and <a href="/wiki/Reynolds_Peak" title="Reynolds Peak">Reynolds Peak</a> along the <a href="/wiki/George_V_Coast" title="George V Coast">George V Coast</a>. On the January 19, Reynolds spotted <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_Hudson" title="Cape Hudson">Cape Hudson</a>. On January 25, the <i>Vincennes</i> sighted the mountains behind the <a href="/wiki/Cook_Ice_Shelf" title="Cook Ice Shelf">Cook Ice Shelf</a>, similar peaks at <a href="/wiki/Piner_Bay" title="Piner Bay">Piner Bay</a> on January 30, and had covered 800 miles of coastline by February 12, from 140&#176; 30\' E. to 112&#176; 16\' 12"E., when Wilkes acknowledged they had "discovered the Antarctic Continent." Named <a href="/wiki/Wilkes_Land" title="Wilkes Land">Wilkes Land</a>, it includes <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Claire_Land" title="Claire Land">Claire Land</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Banzare_Land" title="Banzare Land">Banzare Land</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sabrina_Land" title="Sabrina Land">Sabrina Land</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Budd_Land" title="Budd Land">Budd Land</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Knox_Land" title="Knox Land">Knox Land</a>. They reached a westward goal of 105&#176; E., the edge of <a href="/wiki/Queen_Mary_Land" title="Queen Mary Land">Queen Mary Land</a>, before departing to the north again on February 21.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEStanton1975132,_142&#8211;149,_155&#8211;159,_171&#8211;175_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStanton1975132,_142&#8211;149,_155&#8211;159,_171&#8211;175-11">[11]</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <i>Porpoise</i> came across the French expedition of <a href="/wiki/Jules_Dumont_d%27Urville" title="Jules Dumont d\'Urville">Jules Dumont d\'Urville</a> on January 30. However, due to a misunderstanding of each other\'s intentions, the Porpoise and <a href="/wiki/French_ship_Astrolabe_(1811)" title="French ship Astrolabe (1811)">Astrolabe</a> were unable to communicate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEStanton1975176&#8211;177_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStanton1975176&#8211;177-12">[12]</a></sup> In February 1840, some of the expedition were present at the initial signing of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Waitangi" title="Treaty of Waitangi">Treaty of Waitangi</a> in New Zealand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWilkes1845376_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWilkes1845376-13">[13]</a></sup> Some of the squadron then proceeded back to Sydney for repairs, while the rest visited the <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Islands" title="Bay of Islands">Bay of Islands</a>, before arriving in <a href="/wiki/Tonga" title="Tonga">Tonga</a> in April. At <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nuku%27alofa" title="Nuku\'alofa">Nuku\'alofa</a> the met King Josiah (Aleamotu\'a), and the George (Taufa\'ahau), chief of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ha%27apai" title="Ha\'apai">Ha\'apai</a>, before proceeding onwards to <a href="/wiki/Fiji" title="Fiji">Fiji</a> on May 4. The <i>Porpoise</i> surveyed the Low Archipelago, while the <i>Vincennes</i> and <i>Peacock</i> proceeded onwards to <a href="/wiki/Ovalau_(Fiji)" title="Ovalau (Fiji)">Ovalau</a>, where they signed a commercial treaty with <a href="/wiki/Tanoa_Visawaqa" title="Tanoa Visawaqa">Tanoa Visawaqa</a> in <a href="/wiki/Levuka" title="Levuka">Levuka</a>. <a href="/wiki/Edward_Belcher" title="Edward Belcher">Edward Belcher</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/HMS_Starling_(1829)" title="HMS Starling (1829)"><i>HMS Starling</i></a> visited Ovalau at the same time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEStanton1975180&#8211;195_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStanton1975180&#8211;195-14">[14]</a></sup> Hudson was able to capture Vendovi, after holding his brothers Cocanauto, Qaraniqio, and Tui Dreketi (<a href="/wiki/Roko_Tui_Dreketi" title="Roko Tui Dreketi">Roko Tui Dreketi</a> or King of <a href="/wiki/Rewa_Province" title="Rewa Province">Rewa Province</a>) hostage. Vendovi was deemed responsible for the attack against US sailors on <a href="/wiki/Ono_Island_(Fiji)" title="Ono Island (Fiji)">Ono Island</a> in 1836. Vendovi was to be taken back to the US after a brief trial on board.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEStanton1975199&#8211;201_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStanton1975199&#8211;201-15">[15]</a></sup>\n</p><p>In July 1840, two members of the party, Lieutenant Underwood and Wilkes\' nephew, <a href="/wiki/Midshipman" title="Midshipman">Midshipman</a> Wilkes Henry, were killed while bartering for food in western <a href="/wiki/Fiji" title="Fiji">Fiji</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Malolo" title="Malolo">Malolo</a> Island. The cause of this event remains equivocal. Immediately prior to their deaths, the son of the local chief, who was being held as a hostage by the Americans, escaped by jumping out of the boat and running through the shallow water for shore. The Americans fired over his head. According to members of the expedition party on the boat, his escape was intended as a prearranged signal by the Fijians to attack. According to those on shore, the shooting actually precipitated the attack on the ground. The Americans landed sixty sailors to attack the hostile natives. Close to eighty Fijians were killed in the resulting American reprisal and two villages were burned to the ground.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-glory_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-glory-16">[16]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_route">Return route</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_Exploring_Expedition&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Return route">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:412px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Charles_Wilkes_part2.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="983" data-file-width="2394" decoding="async" height="168" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Charles_Wilkes_part2.png/410px-Charles_Wilkes_part2.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Charles_Wilkes_part2.png/615px-Charles_Wilkes_part2.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Charles_Wilkes_part2.png/820px-Charles_Wilkes_part2.png 2x" width="410"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Charles_Wilkes_part2.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Return route:&#160;1.&#160;<a href="/wiki/Puget_Sound" title="Puget Sound">Puget Sound</a>&#160;&#8211; 2.&#160;<a href="/wiki/Columbia_River" title="Columbia River">Columbia River</a>&#160;&#8211; 3.&#160;<a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San&#160;Francisco</a>&#160;&#8211; 4.&#160;<a href="/wiki/Polynesia" title="Polynesia">Polynesia</a>&#160;&#8211; 5.&#160;<a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>&#160;&#8211; 6.&#160;<a href="/wiki/Borneo" title="Borneo">Borneo</a>&#160;&#8211; 7.&#160;<a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>&#160;&#8211; 8.&#160;<a href="/wiki/Cape_of_Good_Hope" title="Cape of Good Hope">Cape of Good Hope</a>&#160;&#8211; 9.&#160;<a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a></div></div></div>\n<p>On August 9, after three months of surveying, the squadron met off <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Macuata" title="Macuata">Macuata</a>. The <i>Vincennes</i> and <i>Peacock</i> proceeded onwards to the Sandwich Islands, with the <i>Flying Fish</i> and <i>Porpoise</i> to meet them in <a href="/wiki/Oahu" title="Oahu">Oahu</a> by October. Along the way, Wilkes named the <a href="/wiki/Phoenix_Islands" title="Phoenix Islands">Phoenix Group</a> and made a stop at the <a href="/wiki/Palmyra_Atoll" title="Palmyra Atoll">Palmyra Atoll</a>, making their group the first scientific expedition in history to visit Palmyra.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> While in Hawaii, the officers were welcomed by Governor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kekuanaoa" title="Kekuanaoa">Kekuanaoa</a>, King <a href="/wiki/Kamehameha_III" title="Kamehameha III">Kamehameha III</a>, his aide <a href="/wiki/William_Richards_(Hawaii)" title="William Richards (Hawaii)">William Richards</a>, and the journalist <a href="/wiki/James_Jackson_Jarves" title="James Jackson Jarves">James Jackson Jarves</a>. The expedition surveyed <a href="/wiki/Kauai" title="Kauai">Kauai</a>, Oahu, Hawaii, and the peak of <a href="/wiki/Mauna_Loa" title="Mauna Loa">Mauna Loa</a>. The <i>Porpoise</i> was dispatched in November to survey several of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tuamotu" title="Tuamotu">Tuamotus</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Aratika" title="Aratika">Aratika</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kauehi" title="Kauehi">Kauehi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Raraka" title="Raraka">Raraka</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Katiu" title="Katiu">Katiu</a>, before proceeding onwards to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Penrhyn_(atoll)" title="Penrhyn (atoll)">Penrhyn</a> and returning to Oahu on 24 March.\n</p><p>On April 5, 1841, the squadron departed Honolulu, the <i>Porpoise</i> and <i>Vincennes</i> for the Pacific Northwest, the <i>Peacock</i> and <i>Flying Fish</i> to resurvey Samoa, before rejoining the squadron. Along the way, the <i>Peacock</i> and <i>Flying Fish</i> surveyed <a href="/wiki/Jarvis_Island" title="Jarvis Island">Jarvis Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Enderbury_Island" title="Enderbury Island">Enderbury Island</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Tokelau" title="Tokelau">Tokelau</a> Islands, and <a href="/wiki/Fakaofo" title="Fakaofo">Fakaofo</a>. The <i>Peacock</i> followed this with surveys of the <a href="/wiki/Tuvalu" title="Tuvalu">Tuvalu</a> islands of <a href="/wiki/Nukufetau" title="Nukufetau">Nukufetau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vaitupu" title="Vaitupu">Vaitupu</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nanumanga" title="Nanumanga">Nanumanga</a> in March, followed by <a href="/wiki/Tabiteuea" title="Tabiteuea">Tabiteuea</a> in April. Also in April, the <i>Peacock</i> surveyed the <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Islands" title="Gilbert Islands">Gilbert Islands</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nonouti" title="Nonouti">Nonouti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aranuka" title="Aranuka">Aranuka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maiana" title="Maiana">Maiana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Abemama" title="Abemama">Abemama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kuria_(islands)" title="Kuria (islands)">Kuria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tarawa" title="Tarawa">Tarawa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marakei" title="Marakei">Marakei</a>, <a href="/wiki/Butaritari" title="Butaritari">Butaritari</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Makin_(islands)" title="Makin (islands)">Makin</a>, before returning to Ohau on June 13. The <i>Peacock</i> and <i>Flying Fish</i> then left for the <a href="/wiki/Columbia_River" title="Columbia River">Columbia River</a> on June 21.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEStanton1975212,_217,_219&#8211;221,_224&#8211;237,_240,_245&#8211;246_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStanton1975212,_217,_219&#8211;221,_224&#8211;237,_240,_245&#8211;246-18">[18]</a></sup>\n</p><p>In April 1841, USS <i>Peacock</i>, under Lieutenant <a href="/wiki/William_L._Hudson" title="William L. Hudson">William L. Hudson</a>, and USS <i>Flying Fish</i>, surveyed <a href="/wiki/Tabiteuea" title="Tabiteuea">Drummond\'s Island</a>, which was named for an American of the expedition. Lieutenant Hudson heard from a member of his crew that a ship had wrecked off the island and her crew massacred by the <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Islands" title="Gilbert Islands">Gilbertese</a>. A woman and her child were said to be the only survivors, so Hudson decided to land a small force of marines and sailors, under <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=William_M._Walker&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="William M. Walker (page does not exist)">William M. Walker</a>, to search the island. Initially, the natives were peaceful and the Americans were able to explore the island, without results. It was when the party was returning to their ship that Hudson noticed a member of his crew was missing.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:312px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Map_of_the_Oregon_Territory_by_the_U._S._Ex._Ex.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="4843" data-file-width="7000" decoding="async" height="214" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Map_of_the_Oregon_Territory_by_the_U._S._Ex._Ex.png/310px-Map_of_the_Oregon_Territory_by_the_U._S._Ex._Ex.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Map_of_the_Oregon_Territory_by_the_U._S._Ex._Ex.png/465px-Map_of_the_Oregon_Territory_by_the_U._S._Ex._Ex.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Map_of_the_Oregon_Territory_by_the_U._S._Ex._Ex.png/620px-Map_of_the_Oregon_Territory_by_the_U._S._Ex._Ex.png 2x" width="310"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Map_of_the_Oregon_Territory_by_the_U._S._Ex._Ex.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>An 1841 map of the <a href="/wiki/Oregon_Territory" title="Oregon Territory">Oregon Territory</a>. <i>Narrative of the United States Exploring Expedition</i>.</div></div></div>\n<p>After making another search, the man was not found and the natives began arming themselves. Lieutenant Walker returned his force to the ship, to converse with Hudson, who ordered Walker to return to shore and demand the return of the sailor. Walker then reboarded his boats with his landing party and headed to shore. Walker shouted his demand and the natives charged for him, forcing the boats to turn back to the ships. It was decided on the next day that the Americans would <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Drummond%27s_Island" title="Battle of Drummond\'s Island">bombard</a> the hostiles and land again. While doing this, a force of around 700 Gilbertese warriors opposed the American assault, but were defeated after a long battle. No Americans were hurt, but twelve natives were killed and others were wounded, and two villages were also destroyed. A similar episode occurred two months before in February when the <i>Peacock</i> and the <i>Flying Fish</i> briefly <a href="/wiki/Bombardment_of_Upolu" title="Bombardment of Upolu">bombarded</a> the island of <a href="/wiki/Upolu" title="Upolu">Upolu</a>, Samoa following the death of an American merchant sailor on the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEEllsworth1974172&#8211;174_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEEllsworth1974172&#8211;174-19">[19]</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <i>Vincennes</i> and <i>Porpoise</i> reached <a href="/wiki/Cape_Disappointment_(Washington)" title="Cape Disappointment (Washington)">Cape Disappointment</a> on April 28, 1841, but then headed north to the <a href="/wiki/Strait_of_Juan_de_Fuca" title="Strait of Juan de Fuca">Strait of Juan de Fuca</a>, <a href="/wiki/Port_Discovery,_Washington" title="Port Discovery, Washington">Port Discovery</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Fort_Nisqually" title="Fort Nisqually">Fort Nisqually</a>, where they were welcomed by <a href="/wiki/William_Henry_McNeill" title="William Henry McNeill">William Henry McNeill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Caulfield_Anderson" title="Alexander Caulfield Anderson">Alexander Caulfield Anderson</a>. The <i>Porpoise</i> surveyed the <a href="/wiki/Admiralty_Inlet" title="Admiralty Inlet">Admiralty Inlet</a>, while boats from the <i>Vincennes</i> surveyed <a href="/wiki/Hood_Canal" title="Hood Canal">Hood Canal</a>, and the coast northwards to the <a href="/wiki/Fraser_River" title="Fraser River">Fraser River</a>. Wilkes visited <a href="/wiki/Fort_Clatsop" title="Fort Clatsop">Fort Clatsop</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_McLoughlin" title="John McLoughlin">John McLoughlin</a> at <a href="/wiki/Fort_Vancouver" title="Fort Vancouver">Fort Vancouver</a>, and <a href="/wiki/William_Cannon_(pioneer)" title="William Cannon (pioneer)">William Cannon</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Willamette_River" title="Willamette River">Willamette River</a>, while he sent Lt. Johnson on an expedition to <a href="/wiki/Fort_Okanogan" title="Fort Okanogan">Fort Okanogan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fort_Colvile" title="Fort Colvile">Fort Colvile</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fort_Nez_Perces" title="Fort Nez Perces">Fort Nez Perces</a>, where they met <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Whitman" title="Marcus Whitman">Marcus Whitman</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEStanton1975253&#8211;256_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStanton1975253&#8211;256-20">[20]</a></sup> Like his predecessor, <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Great_Britain" title="Kingdom of Great Britain">British</a> explorer <a href="/wiki/George_Vancouver" title="George Vancouver">George Vancouver</a>, Wilkes spent a good deal of time near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bainbridge_Island" title="Bainbridge Island">Bainbridge Island</a>. He noted the bird-like shape of the harbor at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Winslow,_Washington" title="Winslow, Washington">Winslow</a> and named it <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eagle_Harbor_(harbor)" title="Eagle Harbor (harbor)">Eagle Harbor</a>. Continuing his fascination with bird names, he named Bill Point and Wing Point. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Port_Madison,_Washington" title="Port Madison, Washington">Port Madison, Washington</a> and Points Monroe and Jefferson were named in honor of former United States presidents. <a href="/wiki/Port_Ludlow,_Washington" title="Port Ludlow, Washington">Port Ludlow</a> was assigned to honor Lieutenant <a href="/wiki/Augustus_Ludlow" title="Augustus Ludlow">Augustus Ludlow</a>, who lost his life during the <a href="/wiki/War_of_1812" title="War of 1812">War of 1812</a>.\n</p><p>The <i>Peacock</i> and <i>Flying Fish</i> arrived off Cape Disappointment on July 17. However, the <i>Peacock</i> went <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aground" title="Aground">aground</a> while attempting to enter the Columbia River and was soon lost, though with no loss of life. The crew was able to lower six boats and get everyone into Baker\'s Bay, along with their journals, surveys, the chronometers, and some of Agate\'s sketches. A one-eyed Indian named George then guided the <i>Flying Fish</i> into the same bay.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:312px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Peruvian_Andes2.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3235" data-file-width="4001" decoding="async" height="251" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Peruvian_Andes2.jpg/310px-Peruvian_Andes2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Peruvian_Andes2.jpg/465px-Peruvian_Andes2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Peruvian_Andes2.jpg/620px-Peruvian_Andes2.jpg 2x" width="310"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Peruvian_Andes2.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i>"Andes near Alparmarca, Peru: Sketched from an Elevation of 16,000 Feet"</i> &#8211; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alfred_Agate" title="Alfred Agate">Alfred Agate</a></div></div></div>\n<p>There, the crew set up "Peacockville", assisted by <a href="/wiki/James_Birnie" title="James Birnie">James Birnie</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Hudson%27s_Bay_Company" title="Hudson\'s Bay Company">Hudson\'s Bay Company</a>, and the American Methodist Mission at Point Adams. They also traded with the local <a href="/wiki/Clatsop" title="Clatsop">Clatsop</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chinookan" title="Chinookan">Chinookan</a> Indians over the next three weeks, while surveying the channel, before journeying to <a href="/wiki/Fort_Astoria" title="Fort Astoria">Fort George</a> and a reunion with the rest of the squadron. This prompted Wilkes to send the <i>Vincennes</i> to San Francisco Bay, while he continued to survey <a href="/wiki/Grays_Harbor" title="Grays Harbor">Grays Harbor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEStanton1975247&#8211;253,_259_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStanton1975247&#8211;253,_259-21">[21]</a></sup>\n</p><p>From the area of modern-day <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Oregon" title="Portland, Oregon">Portland</a>, Wilkes sent an overland party of 39 southwards, led by Emmons, but guided by <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Meek" title="Joseph Meek">Joseph Meek</a>. The group included Agate, Eld, Colvocoresses, Brackenridge, Rich, Peale, Stearns, and Dana, and proceeded along an inland route to <a href="/wiki/Fort_Umpqua" title="Fort Umpqua">Fort Umpqua</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mount_Shasta" title="Mount Shasta">Mount Shasta</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Sacramento_River" title="Sacramento River">Sacramento River</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Sutter" title="John Sutter">John Sutter</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/New_Helvetia" title="New Helvetia">New Helvetia</a>, and then onwards to <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Bay" title="San Francisco Bay">San Francisco Bay</a>. They departed September 7, and arrived aboard the <i>Vincennes</i> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sausalito" title="Sausalito">Sausalito</a> on October 23, having traveled along the <a href="/wiki/Siskiyou_Trail" title="Siskiyou Trail">Siskiyou Trail</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEStanton1975259&#8211;265_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStanton1975259&#8211;265-22">[22]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Wilkes arrived with the <i>Porpoise</i> and <i>Oregon</i>, while the <i>Flying Fish</i> was to rendezvous with the squadron in Honolulu.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEStanton1975267_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStanton1975267-23">[23]</a></sup> The squadron surveyed San Francisco and its tributaries, and later produced a map of "Upper California".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> The expedition then headed back out on October 31, arriving Honolulu on November 17, and departing on November 28.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEStanton1975269&#8211;272_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStanton1975269&#8211;272-25">[25]</a></sup> They included a visit to <a href="/wiki/Wake_Island" title="Wake Island">Wake Island</a>, and returned by way of the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Borneo" title="Borneo">Borneo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Polynesia" title="Polynesia">Polynesia</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Cape_of_Good_Hope" title="Cape of Good Hope">Cape of Good Hope</a>, reaching New York on June 10, 1842.\n</p><p>The expedition was plagued by poor relationships between Wilkes and his subordinate officers throughout. Wilkes\' self-proclaimed status as captain and commodore, accompanied by the flying of the requisite pennant and the wearing of a captain\'s uniform while being commissioned only as a Lieutenant, rankled heavily with other members of the expedition of similar real rank. His apparent mistreatment of many of his subordinates, and indulgence in punishments such as "<a href="/wiki/Cat_o%27_nine_tails#Flogging_round_the_fleet" title="Cat o\' nine tails">flogging round the fleet</a>" resulted in a major controversy on his return to America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-glory_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-glory-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEStanton1975219&#8211;220_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStanton1975219&#8211;220-26">[26]</a></sup> Wilkes was <a href="/wiki/Court-martial" title="Court-martial">court-martialled</a> on his return, but was acquitted on all charges except that of illegally punishing men in his squadron.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Significance">Significance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_Exploring_Expedition&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Significance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Wilkes Expedition played a major role in the development of 19th-century science, particularly in the growth of the American scientific establishment. Many of the species and other items found by the expedition helped form the basis of collections at the new <a href="/wiki/Smithsonian_Institution" title="Smithsonian Institution">Smithsonian Institution</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Nukufetauman1831.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="149" data-file-width="150" decoding="async" height="149" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/80/Nukufetauman1831.jpg" width="150"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Nukufetauman1831.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Nukufetau" title="Nukufetau">Nukufetau</a> man by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alfred_Agate" title="Alfred Agate">Alfred Agate</a>, 1841</div></div></div>\n<p>With the help of the expedition\'s scientists, derisively called "<i>clam digger</i>s" and "<i>bug catchers</i>" by navy crew members, 280 islands, mostly in the Pacific, were explored, and over 800 miles of <a href="/wiki/Oregon" title="Oregon">Oregon</a> were mapped. Of no less importance, over 60,000 plant and bird specimens were collected. A staggering amount of data and specimens were collected during the expedition, including the seeds of 648 species, which were later traded, planted, and sent throughout the country. Dried specimens were sent to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_National_Herbarium" title="United States National Herbarium">National Herbarium</a>, now a part of the Smithsonian Institution. There were also 254 live plants, which mostly came from the home stretch of the journey, that were placed in a newly constructed greenhouse in 1850, which later became the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Botanic_Garden" title="United States Botanic Garden">United States Botanic Garden</a>.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Alfred_Thomas_Agate" title="Alfred Thomas Agate">Alfred Thomas Agate</a>, engraver and illustrator, created an enduring record of traditional cultures such as the illustrations made of the dress and tattoo patterns of natives of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ellice_Islands" title="Ellice Islands">Ellice Islands</a> (now <a href="/wiki/Tuvalu" title="Tuvalu">Tuvalu</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup>\n</p><p>\nA collection of artifacts from the expedition also went to the <a href="/wiki/National_Institute_for_the_Promotion_of_Science" title="National Institute for the Promotion of Science">National Institute for the Promotion of Science</a>, a precursor of the Smithsonian Institution. These joined artifacts from American history as the first artifacts in the Smithsonian collection.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p><div style="clear:both;"></div>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Published_works">Published works</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_Exploring_Expedition&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Published works">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>For a short time Wilkes was attached to the <a href="/wiki/Office_of_Coast_Survey" title="Office of Coast Survey">Office of Coast Survey</a>, but from 1844 to 1861 he was chiefly engaged in preparing the expedition report. Twenty-eight volumes were planned, but only nineteen were published.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Of these, Wilkes wrote the multi-volume <i>Narrative of the United States exploring expedition, during 1838, 1839, 1840, 1841, 1842</i>, <i>Hydrography</i>, and <i>Meteorology</i>. \n</p><p>The <i>Narrative</i> concerns the customs, political and economic conditions of many places then little-known. Other contributions were three reports by <a href="/wiki/James_Dwight_Dana" title="James Dwight Dana">James Dwight Dana</a> on <i>Zoophytes</i>, <i>Geology</i>, and <i>Crustacea</i>. In addition to shorter articles and reports, Wilkes published <i>Western America, including California and Oregon</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> and <i>Theory of the Winds</i>. The <a href="/wiki/Smithsonian_Institution" title="Smithsonian Institution">Smithsonian Institution</a> digitized the five volume narrative and the accompanying scientific volumes. The mismanagement that plagued the expedition prior to its departure continued after its completion. By June 1848, many of the specimens had been lost or damaged and many remained unidentified. <a href="/wiki/Asa_Gray" title="Asa Gray">Asa Gray</a> was hired to work on the botanical specimens.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEDupree1988185&#8211;195_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDupree1988185&#8211;195-32">[32]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_Exploring_Expedition&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 20em;
