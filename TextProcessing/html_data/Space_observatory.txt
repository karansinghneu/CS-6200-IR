Space Telescope">Hubble Space Telescope</a>, one of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Observatories_program" title="Great Observatories program">Great Observatories</a></div></div></div>\n<p>A <b>space telescope</b> or <b>space observatory</b> is an instrument located in <a href="/wiki/Outer_space" title="Outer space">outer space</a> to observe distant <a href="/wiki/Planet" title="Planet">planets</a>, <a href="/wiki/Galaxy" title="Galaxy">galaxies</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Astronomical_object" title="Astronomical object">astronomical objects</a>. Space telescopes avoid many of the problems of <a href="/wiki/Observatory#Ground-based_observatories" title="Observatory">ground-based observatories</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Light_pollution" title="Light pollution">light pollution</a> and distortion of <a href="/wiki/Electromagnetic_radiation" title="Electromagnetic radiation">electromagnetic radiation</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scintillation_(astronomy)" title="Scintillation (astronomy)">scintillation</a>). In addition, <a href="/wiki/Ultraviolet" title="Ultraviolet">ultraviolet</a> frequencies, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/X-rays" title="X-rays">X-rays</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gamma_rays" title="Gamma rays">gamma rays</a> are blocked by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Earth%27s_atmosphere" title="Earth\'s atmosphere">Earth\'s atmosphere</a>, so they can only be observed from space.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Theorized by <a href="/wiki/Lyman_Spitzer" title="Lyman Spitzer">Lyman Spitzer</a> in 1946, the first operational space telescopes were the American <a href="/wiki/Orbiting_Astronomical_Observatory" title="Orbiting Astronomical Observatory">Orbiting Astronomical Observatory</a> OAO-2 launched in 1968 and the Soviet <a href="/wiki/Orion_(space_telescope)" title="Orion (space telescope)">Orion 1 ultraviolet telescope</a> aboard space station <a href="/wiki/Salyut_1" title="Salyut 1">Salyut 1</a> in 1971.\n</p><p>Space telescopes are distinct from other imaging satellites pointed toward Earth for purposes of <a href="/wiki/Reconnaissance_satellite" title="Reconnaissance satellite">espionage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Weather_satellite" title="Weather satellite">weather analysis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Earth_observation_satellite" title="Earth observation satellite">other types of information gathering</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc"><input class="toctogglecheckbox" id="toctogglecheckbox" role="button" style="display:none" type="checkbox"/><div class="toctitle" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Space_telescope&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Spitzer,_Hubble_and_XMM-Newton.tif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="4300" data-file-width="2200" decoding="async" height="293" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/Spitzer%2C_Hubble_and_XMM-Newton.tif/lossy-page1-150px-Spitzer%2C_Hubble_and_XMM-Newton.tif.jpg" class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Spitzer,_Hubble_and_XMM-Newton.tif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Spitzer, Hubble and XMM with their most important parts depicted</div></div></div>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Wilhelm_Beer" title="Wilhelm Beer">Wilhelm Beer</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Johann_Heinrich_M%C3%A4dler" title="Johann Heinrich M&#228;dler">Johann Heinrich M&#228;dler</a> in 1837 discussed the advantages of an observatory on the Moon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-galaxy196506_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-galaxy196506-2">[2]</a></sup> In 1946, American theoretical astrophysicist <a href="/wiki/Lyman_Spitzer" title="Lyman Spitzer">Lyman Spitzer</a> proposed a telescope in space, 11 years before the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> launched the first satellite, <i><a href="/wiki/Sputnik_1" title="Sputnik 1">Sputnik 1</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Spitzer\'s proposal called for a large telescope that would not be hindered by Earth\'s atmosphere. After lobbying in the 1960s and 70s for such a system to be built, Spitzer\'s vision ultimately materialized into the <a href="/wiki/Hubble_Space_Telescope" title="Hubble Space Telescope">Hubble Space Telescope</a>, which was launched on April 24, 1990 by the <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Discovery" title="Space Shuttle Discovery">Space Shuttle <i>Discovery</i></a> (STS-31).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Advantages">Advantages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Space_telescope&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Advantages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Performing <a href="/wiki/Astronomy" title="Astronomy">astronomy</a> from <a href="/wiki/Observatory#Ground-based_observatories" title="Observatory">ground-based observatories</a> on Earth is limited by the filtering and distortion of <a href="/wiki/Electromagnetic_radiation" title="Electromagnetic radiation">electromagnetic radiation</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scintillation_(astronomy)" title="Scintillation (astronomy)">scintillation</a> or twinkling) due to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Earth%27s_atmosphere" title="Earth\'s atmosphere">atmosphere</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-galaxy196506_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-galaxy196506-2">[2]</a></sup> Some terrestrial telescopes can reduce atmospheric effects with <a href="/wiki/Adaptive_optics" title="Adaptive optics">adaptive optics</a>. A telescope orbiting Earth outside the atmosphere is subject neither to twinkling nor to <a href="/wiki/Light_pollution" title="Light pollution">light pollution</a> from artificial light sources on Earth. As a result, the <a href="/wiki/Angular_resolution" title="Angular resolution">angular resolution</a> of space telescopes is often much smaller than a ground-based telescope with a similar <a href="/wiki/Aperture" title="Aperture">aperture</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ill-2_O3.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1320" data-file-width="2636" decoding="async" height="110" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Ill-2_O3.jpg/220px-Ill-2_O3.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Ill-2_O3.jpg/330px-Ill-2_O3.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Ill-2_O3.jpg/440px-Ill-2_O3.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ill-2_O3.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Space and ground observatories\' wavelength working ranges compared against atmospheric transparency windows</div></div></div>\n<p>Space-based astronomy is even more important for frequency ranges which are outside the <a href="/wiki/Optical_window" title="Optical window">optical window</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Radio_window" title="Radio window">radio window</a>, the only two wavelength ranges of the <a href="/wiki/Electromagnetic_spectrum" title="Electromagnetic spectrum">electromagnetic spectrum</a> that are not severely attenuated by the atmosphere. For example, <a href="/wiki/X-ray_astronomy" title="X-ray astronomy">X-ray astronomy</a> is nearly impossible when done from Earth, and has reached its current importance in astronomy only due to orbiting X-ray telescopes such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chandra_observatory" title="Chandra observatory">Chandra observatory</a> and the <a href="/wiki/XMM-Newton" title="XMM-Newton">XMM-Newton observatory</a>. <a href="/wiki/Infrared" title="Infrared">Infrared</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ultraviolet" title="Ultraviolet">ultraviolet</a> are also largely blocked.\n</p><p>However, all these advantages do come with a price. Space telescopes are much more expensive to build than ground-based telescopes. Due to their location, space telescopes are also extremely difficult to maintain. The Hubble Space Telescope was serviced by the <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle" title="Space Shuttle">Space Shuttle</a> while many other space telescopes cannot be serviced at all.\n</p><p>Space observatories can generally be divided into two classes: missions which map the entire sky (surveys), and observatories which focus on selected <a href="/wiki/Astronomical_object" title="Astronomical object">astronomical objects</a> or parts of the sky.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Future_of_space_observatories">Future of space observatories</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span However, the availability of space telescopes and observatories in the future is threatened due to delays and budget cuts.
