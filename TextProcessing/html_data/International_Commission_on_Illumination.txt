The colours depicted depend on the <a href="/wiki/Color_space" title="Color space">colour space</a> of the device on which the image is viewed.</div></div></div>\n<p>The <b>International Commission on Illumination</b> (usually abbreviated <b>CIE</b> for its French name, <b>Commission internationale de l\'&#233;clairage</b>) is the international authority on <a href="/wiki/Light" title="Light">light</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lighting" title="Lighting">illumination</a>, <a href="/wiki/Color" title="Color">colour</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Color_space" title="Color space">colour spaces</a>. It was established in 1913 as a successor to the Commission Internationale de Photom&#233;trie and is today based in <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna, Austria</a>. class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Commission_on_Illumination&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Organization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The CIE has eight divisions, each of which establishes technical committees to carry out its program under the supervision of the division\'s director:\n</p>\n<ol><li>Vision and Colour</li>\n<li>Measurement of Light and Radiation</li>\n<li>Interior Environment and Lighting Design</li>\n<li>Lighting and Signalling for Transport</li>\n<li>Exterior Lighting and Other Applications</li>\n<li>Photobiology and Photochemistry</li>\n<li>General Aspects of Lighting (Inactive)</li>\n<li>Image Technology</li></ol>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Milestones">Milestones</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Commission_on_Illumination&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Milestones">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li>In 1924 it established the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Standard_photopic_observer&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Standard photopic observer (page does not exist)">standard photopic observer</a> defined by the spectral <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luminous_efficiency_function" title="Luminous efficiency function">luminous efficiency function</a> <i>V</i>(<i>&#955;</i>), followed in 1951 by the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Standard_scotopic_observer&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Standard scotopic observer (page does not exist)">standard scotopic observer</a> defined by the function <i>V</i>&#8217;(<i>&#955;</i>).</li>\n<li>Building on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Optical_Society_of_America" title="Optical Society of America">Optical Society of America</a>\'s report on <a href="/wiki/Colorimetry" title="Colorimetry">colorimetry</a> in 1922,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Troland1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Troland1-2">[2]</a></sup> the CIE convened its eighth session in 1931, with the intention of establishing an international agreement on colorimetric specifications and updating the OSA\'s 1922 recommendations based on the developments during the past decade.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The meeting, held in <a href="/wiki/Cambridge" title="Cambridge">Cambridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, concluded with the formalization of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CIE_1931_colour_space" title="CIE 1931 colour space">CIE 1931 XYZ colour space</a> and definitions of the 1931 CIE 2&#176; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CIE_1931_colour_space#CIE_standard_observer" title="CIE 1931 colour space">standard observer</a> with the corresponding <a href="/wiki/CIE_1931_color_space#Color_matching_functions" title="CIE 1931 color space">colour matching functions</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Standard_illuminant" title="Standard illuminant">standard illuminants</a> A, B, and C.</li></ul>\n<ul><li>In 1964 the 10&#176; CIE standard observer and its corresponding colour matching functions as well as the new standard daylight <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Illuminant&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Illuminant (page does not exist)">illuminant</a> D6500 were added, as well as a method for calculating daylight illuminants at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Correlated_colour_temperature" title="Correlated colour temperature">correlated colour temperatures</a> other than 6500 <a href="/wiki/Kelvin" title="Kelvin">kelvins</a>.</li>\n<li>In 1976, the commission developed the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CIELAB" title="CIELAB">CIELAB</a> and <a href="/wiki/CIELUV" title="CIELUV">CIELUV</a> colour spaces, which are widely used today.</li>\n<li>Based on CIELAB, colour difference formulas CIEDE94 and CIEDE2000 were recommended in the corresponding years.</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
