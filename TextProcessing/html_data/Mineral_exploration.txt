mine</a> with <a href="/wiki/Haul_truck" title="Haul truck">haul truck</a> in foreground</div></div></div>\n<p><b>Mining engineering</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">engineering</a> discipline that applies science and technology to the extraction of <a href="/wiki/Mineral" title="Mineral">minerals</a> from the earth. Mining engineering is associated with many other disciplines, such as <a href="/wiki/Mineral_processing" title="Mineral processing">mineral processing</a>, Exploration, Excavation, <a href="/wiki/Geology" title="Geology">geology</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Metallurgy" title="Metallurgy">metallurgy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Geotechnical_engineering" title="Geotechnical engineering">geotechnical engineering</a> and <a href="/wiki/Surveying" title="Surveying">surveying</a>. A mining engineer may manage any phase of mining operations &#8211; from exploration and discovery of the mineral resource, through <a href="/wiki/Feasibility_study" title="Feasibility study">feasibility study</a>, mine design, development of plans, <a href="/wiki/Production_(economics)" title="Production (economics)">production</a> and operations to <a href="/wiki/Mine_closure" title="Mine closure">mine closure</a>.\n</p><p>With the process of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mineral_extraction" title="Mineral extraction">Mineral extraction</a>, some amount of <a href="/wiki/Waste" title="Waste">waste</a> and uneconomic material are generated which are the primary source of <a href="/wiki/Pollution" title="Pollution">pollution</a> in the vicinity of mines. Mining activities by their nature cause a disturbance of the <a href="/wiki/Natural_environment" title="Natural environment">natural environment</a> in and around which the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minerals" title="Minerals">minerals</a> are located. <a href="/wiki/Mining" title="Mining">Mining</a> engineers must therefore be concerned not only with the production and processing of mineral <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commodities" title="Commodities">commodities</a>, but also with the mitigation of damage to the environment both during and after mining as a result of the change in the mining area.\n</p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc"><input class="toctogglecheckbox" id="toctogglecheckbox" role="button" style="display:none" type="checkbox"/><div class="toctitle" dir="ltr" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mining_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History of mining engineering">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>From <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prehistoric" title="Prehistoric">prehistoric</a> times to the present, <a href="/wiki/Mining" title="Mining">mining</a> has played a significant role in the existence of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Human_race" title="Human race">human race</a>. Since the beginning of civilization people have used <a href="/wiki/Rock_(geology)" title="Rock (geology)">stone</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ceramic" title="Ceramic">ceramics</a> and, later, <a href="/wiki/Metal" title="Metal">metals</a> found on or close to the <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth\'s</a> surface. These were used to manufacture early <a href="/wiki/Tool" title="Tool">tools</a> and <a href="/wiki/Weapon" title="Weapon">weapons</a>. For example, high quality <a href="/wiki/Flint" title="Flint">flint</a> found in northern <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> and southern <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> were used to set <a href="/wiki/Fire" title="Fire">fire</a> and break rock.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Flint mines have been found in <a href="/wiki/Chalk" title="Chalk">chalk</a> areas where seams of the stone were followed underground by shafts and galleries. The oldest known mine on archaeological record is the "Lion Cave" in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swaziland" title="Swaziland">Swaziland</a>. At this site, which <a href="/wiki/Radiocarbon_dating" title="Radiocarbon dating">radiocarbon dating</a> indicates to be about 43,000 years old, <a href="/wiki/Paleolithic" title="Paleolithic">paleolithic</a> humans mined mineral <a href="/wiki/Hematite" title="Hematite">hematite</a>, which contained <a href="/wiki/Iron" title="Iron">iron</a> and was ground to produce the red <a href="/wiki/Pigment" title="Pigment">pigment</a> <a href="/wiki/Ochre" title="Ochre">ochre</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">ancient Romans</a> were innovators of <a href="/wiki/Mining" title="Mining">mining</a> engineering. They developed large scale mining methods, such as the use of large volumes of water brought to the minehead by numerous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aqueduct_(watercourse)" title="Aqueduct (watercourse)">aqueducts</a> for <a href="/wiki/Hydraulic_mining" title="Hydraulic mining">hydraulic mining</a>. The exposed rock was then attacked by <a href="/wiki/Fire-setting" title="Fire-setting">fire-setting</a> where fires were used to heat the rock, which would be quenched with a stream of water. The <a href="/wiki/Thermal_shock" title="Thermal shock">thermal shock</a> cracked the rock, enabling it to be removed. In some mines the Romans utilized water-powered machinery such as <a href="/wiki/Reverse_overshot_water-wheel" title="Reverse overshot water-wheel">reverse overshot water-wheels</a>. These were used extensively in the <a href="/wiki/Copper" title="Copper">copper</a> mines at <a href="/wiki/Rio_Tinto_(river)" title="Rio Tinto (river)">Rio Tinto</a> in Spain, where one sequence comprised 16 such wheels arranged in pairs, lifting water about 80 feet (24&#160;m).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>\n</p><p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gun_powder" title="Gun powder">Black powder</a> was first used in mining in <a href="/wiki/Bansk%C3%A1_%C5%A0tiavnica" title="Bansk&#225; &#352;tiavnica">Bansk&#225; &#352;tiavnica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hungary" title="Kingdom of Hungary">Kingdom of Hungary</a> (present-day <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovakia</a>) in 1627.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>  This allowed blasting of rock and earth to loosen and reveal ore veins, which was much faster than fire-setting. The <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Revolution" title="Industrial Revolution">Industrial Revolution</a> saw further advances in mining technologies, including improved explosives and <a href="/wiki/Steam_engine" title="Steam engine">steam-powered</a> pumps, lifts, and drills as long as they remained safe.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mining_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Coloradosm.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="800" data-file-width="1200" decoding="async" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/Coloradosm.png/220px-Coloradosm.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/Coloradosm.png/330px-Coloradosm.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/Coloradosm.png/440px-Coloradosm.png 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Coloradosm.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Students outside <a href="/wiki/Colorado_School_of_Mines" title="Colorado School of Mines">Colorado School of Mines</a> campus</div></div></div>\n<p>There are many ways to become a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mining_Engineer" title="Mining Engineer">Mining Engineer</a> but all include a university degree in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mining_Engineering" title="Mining Engineering">Mining Engineering</a>. Primarily, training includes a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Engineering" title="Bachelor of Engineering">Bachelor of Engineering</a> (B.Eng. or B.E.), <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Science" title="Bachelor of Science">Bachelor of Science</a> (B.Sc. or B.S.), <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Technology" title="Bachelor of Technology">Bachelor of Technology</a> (B.Tech.) or <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Applied_Science" title="Bachelor of Applied Science">Bachelor of Applied Science</a> (B.A.Sc.) in Mining Engineering. Depending on the country and jurisdiction, to be licensed as a mining engineer a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Master%27s" title="Master\'s">Master\'s</a> degree; <a disciplines e.g. from engineering fields like <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mechanical_Engineering" title="Mechanical Engineering">Mechanical Engineering</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Physics" title="Physics">Physics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Geomatics" title="Geomatics">Geomatics</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Earth_Science" title="Earth Science">Earth Science</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">Mathematics</a>, However, this path requires taking a <a href="/wiki/Graduate_diploma" title="Graduate diploma">graduate</a> degree such as M.Eng, M.S., M.Sc. or M.A.Sc. in Mining Engineering after graduating from a different <a href="/wiki/Quantitative_research" title="Quantitative research">quantitative</a> <a class="mw-redirect" class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Mining salaries are usually determined by the level of skill required, where the position is, and what kind of organization the engineer is working for.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs C. Wyeth">N. C. Wyeth</a>, 1906</div></div></div>\n<p>Mineral exploration is the process of finding <a href="/wiki/Ore" title="Ore">ores</a> (commercially viable concentrations of minerals) to <a href="/wiki/Mining" title="Mining">mine</a>. Mineral exploration is a much more intensive, organized and professional form of mineral <a href="/wiki/Prospecting" title="Prospecting">prospecting</a> and, though it frequently uses the services of prospecting, the process of mineral exploration on the whole is much more involved.\n</p><p>The foremost stage of mining starts with the process of finding and exploration of the mineral deposit. In the initial process of mineral exploration, however, the role of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geologists" title="Geologists">geologists</a> and <a href="/wiki/Surveying" title="Surveying">surveyors</a> is prominent in the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pre-feasibility_study&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pre-feasibility study (page does not exist)">pre-feasibility study</a> of the future mining operation. Mineral exploration and estimation of reserve through various prospecting methods are done to determine the method and type of mining in addition to profitability condition.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mineral_discovery">Mineral discovery</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mining_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Mineral discovery">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Once a mineral discovery has been made, and has been determined to be of sufficient economic quality to mine, mining engineers will then work on developing a plan to mine this effectively and efficiently.\n</p><p>The discovery can be made from research of mineral maps, academic geological reports or local, state, and national geological reports. Other sources of information include property assays, well drilling logs, and local word of mouth. Mineral research may also include satellite and airborne photographs. Unless the mineral exploration is done on public property, the owners of the property may play a significant role in the exploration process, and may be the original discoverer of the mineral deposit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mineral_determination">Mineral determination</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mining_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Mineral determination">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>After a prospective mineral is located, the mining geologist and/or mining engineer then determines the ore properties. This may involve chemical analysis of the ore to determine the composition of the sample. Once the mineral properties are identified, the next step is determining the quantity of the ore. This involves determining the extent of the deposit as well as the purity of the ore.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The geologist drills additional core samples to find the limits of the deposit or seam and calculates the quantity of valuable material present in the deposit.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Feasibility_study">Feasibility study</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mining_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Feasibility study">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mining_feasibility_study" title="Mining feasibility study">Mining feasibility study</a></div>\n<p>Once the mineral identification and reserve amount is reasonably determined, the next step is to determine the <a href="/wiki/Feasibility_study" title="Feasibility study">feasibility</a> of recovering the mineral deposit. A preliminary study shortly after the discovery of the deposit examines the market conditions such as the supply and demand of the mineral, the amount of ore needed to be moved to recover a certain quantity of that mineral as well as analysis of the cost associated with the operation. This pre-feasibility study determines whether the mining project is likely to be profitable; if it is then a more in-depth analysis of the deposit is undertaken. After the full extent of the ore body is known and has been examined by engineers, the feasibility study examines the cost of initial capital investment, methods of extraction, the cost of operation, an estimated length of time to payback, the gross revenue and net <a href="/wiki/Profit_margin" title="Profit margin">profit margin</a>, any possible resale price of the land, the total life of the reserve, the total value of the reserve, investment in future projects, and the property owner or owners\' contract. In addition, environmental impact, reclamation, possible legal ramifications and all government permitting are considered.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> These steps of analysis determine whether the mine company should proceed with the extraction of the minerals or whether the project should be abandoned. The mining company may decide to sell the rights to the reserve to a third party rather than develop it themselves, or the decision to proceed with extraction may be postponed indefinitely until market conditions become favorable.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mining_operation">Mining operation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mining_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Mining operation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Mining engineers working in an established mine may work as an engineer for operations improvement, further <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mineral_exploration" title="Mineral exploration">mineral exploration</a>, and operation capitalization by determining where in the mine to add equipment and personnel. The engineer may also work in supervision and management, or as an equipment and mineral salesperson. In addition to engineering and operations, the mining engineer may work as an environmental, health and safety manager or design engineer.\n</p><p>The act of mining required different methods of extraction depending on the <a href="/wiki/Mineralogy" title="Mineralogy">mineralogy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Geology" title="Geology">geology</a>, and location of the resources. Characteristics such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mineral_hardness" title="Mineral hardness">mineral hardness</a>, the mineral <a href="/wiki/Stratum" title="Stratum">stratification</a>, and access to that mineral will determine the method of extraction.\n</p><p>Generally, mining is either done from the surface or underground. Mining can also occur with both surface and underground operations taking place on the same reserve. Mining activity varies as to what method is employed to remove the mineral.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Surface_mining">Surface mining</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mining_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Surface mining">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Surface_mining" title="Surface mining">Surface mining</a> comprises 90% of the world\'s mineral tonnage output. Also called <a href="/wiki/Open-pit_mining" title="Open-pit mining">open pit mining</a>, surface mining is removing minerals in formations that are at or near the surface. Ore retrieval is done by material removal from the land in its natural state. Surface mining often alters the land characteristics, shape, <a href="/wiki/Topography" title="Topography">topography</a>, and geological make-up.\n</p><p>Surface mining involves quarrying which is excavating minerals by means of machinery such as cutting, cleaving, and breaking. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Explosives" title="Explosives">Explosives</a> are usually used to facilitate breakage. Hard rocks such as limestone, sand, gravel, and slate are generally quarried into a series of benches.\n</p><p>Strip mining is done on softer minerals such as clays and phosphate are removed through use of mechanical shovels, track dozers, and front end loaders. Softer Coal seams can also be extracted this way.\n</p><p>With <a href="/wiki/Placer_mining" title="Placer mining">placer mining</a>, minerals can also be removed from the bottoms of lakes, rivers, streams, and even the ocean by dredge mining. In addition, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/In-situ" title="In-situ">in-situ</a> mining can be done from the surface using dissolving agents on the ore body and retrieving the ore via pumping. The pumped material is then set to leach for further processing. Hydraulic mining is utilized in forms of water jets to wash away either overburden or the ore itself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mining_process">Mining process</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mining_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Mining process">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Blasting:<br/>\n<a href="/wiki/Explosive" title="Explosive">Explosives</a> are used to break up a rock formation and aid in the collection of <a href="/wiki/Ore" title="Ore">ore</a> in a process called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rock_blasting" title="Rock blasting">blasting</a>.  Blasting utilizes the heat and immense pressure of the detonated explosives to shatter and fracture a rock mass. The type of explosives used in mining are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Explosive_material#High_explosives" title="Explosive material">high explosives</a> which vary in composition and performance properties.  The mining engineer is responsible for the selection and proper placement of these explosives, in order to maximize efficiency and safety.  Blasting occurs in many phases of the mining process, such as development of infrastructure as well as production of the ore.\n</p><p>Leaching:<br/>\n<a href="/wiki/Leaching_(metallurgy)" title="Leaching (metallurgy)">Leaching</a> is the loss or extraction of certain materials from a carrier into a liquid (usually, but not always a solvent). Mostly used in rare-earth metals extraction.\n</p><p>Flotation:<br/>\n<a href="/wiki/Froth_flotation#Mining" title="Froth flotation">Flotation</a> (also spelled floatation) involves phenomena related to the relative buoyancy of minerals.It is the most widely used metal separate method.\n</p><p>Electrostatic separation:<br/>\n<a href="/wiki/Electrostatic_separator" title="Electrostatic separator">Separating</a> minerals by electro-characteristic differences.\n</p><p>Gravity separation:<br/>\n<a href="/wiki/Gravity_separation" title="Gravity separation">Gravity separation</a> is an industrial method of separating two components, either a suspension, or dry granular mixture where separating the components with gravity is sufficiently practical.\n</p><p>Magnetic separation:<br/>\n<a href="/wiki/Magnetic_separation" title="Magnetic separation">Magnetic separation</a> is a process in which magnetically susceptible material is extracted from a mixture using a magnetic force. \n</p><p>Hydraulic separation:<br/>\n<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hydraulic_separation&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hydraulic separation (page does not exist)">Hydraulic separation</a> is a process that using the density difference to separate minerals. Before hydraulic separation, minerals were crushed into uniform size; because minerals have uniform size and different density will have different settling velocities in water, and that can be used to separate target minerals.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mining_health_and_safety">Mining health and safety</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a When a mining engineer works at an active mine he or she is subject to the same rights, violations, mandatory health and safety regulations, and mandatory training as any other worker at the mine. The mining engineer can be legally identified as a "miner."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Act establishes the rights of miners. The miner may report at any time a hazardous condition and request an inspection. The miners may elect a miners\' representative to participate during an inspection, pre-inspection meeting, and post-inspection conference. The miners and miners\' representative shall be paid for their time during all inspections and investigations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a
