title="Enlarge"></a></div>Gubernatorskaya street, Ferghana, 1913</div></div></div>\n<p>The fertile <a href="/wiki/Fergana_Valley" title="Fergana Valley">Fergana Valley</a> was an important conduit on the <a href="/wiki/Silk_Road" title="Silk Road">Silk Roads</a> (more precisely the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Silk_Road" title="North Silk Road">North Silk Road</a>), which connected the ancient Chinese capital of <a href="/wiki/Xi%27an" title="Xi\'an">Xi\'an</a> to the west over the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wushao_Ling_Mountain" title="Wushao Ling Mountain">Wushao Ling Mountain</a> Pass to <a href="/wiki/Wuwei,_Gansu" title="Wuwei, Gansu">Wuwei</a> and emerging in <a href="/wiki/Kashgar" title="Kashgar">Kashgar</a> before linking to ancient <a href="/wiki/Parthia" title="Parthia">Parthia</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> or on to the north of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aral_sea" title="Aral sea">Aral</a> and <a href="/wiki/Caspian_Sea" title="Caspian Sea">Caspian Seas</a> to ports on the <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black Sea</a>.\n</p><p>It used to be called ferghana, during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kushan_empire" title="Kushan empire">Kushan empire</a>. The ancient kingdom referred to as <a href="/wiki/Dayuan" title="Dayuan">Dayuan</a> (&#22823;&#23451;, "Great Yuan", literally "Great Ionians") in the Chinese chronicles is now generally accepted as being in the Ferghana Valley. It is sometimes, though less commonly, written as Dawan (&#22823;&#23451;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>). <a href="/wiki/Dayuan" title="Dayuan">Dayuan</a> were Greeks, the descendants of the Greek colonists that were settled by <a href="/wiki/Alexander_the_Great" title="Alexander the Great">Alexander the Great</a> in Ferghana in 329 BCE, and prospered within the Hellenistic realm of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seleucids" title="Seleucids">Seleucids</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greco-Bactrians" title="Greco-Bactrians">Greco-Bactrians</a>, until they were isolated by the migrations of the <a href="/wiki/Yuezhi" title="Yuezhi">Yuezhi</a> around 160 BCE. It has been suggested that the name "Yuan" was simply a transliteration of the words &#8220;<a href="/wiki/Yona" title="Yona">Yona</a>&#8221;, or &#8220;<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yavana" title="Yavana">Yavana</a>&#8221;, used throughout antiquity in Asia to designate Greeks (&#8220;<a href="/wiki/Ionians" title="Ionians">Ionians</a>&#8221;). Their capital was <a href="/wiki/Alexandria_Eschate" title="Alexandria Eschate">Alexandria Eschate</a>.\n</p><p>The earliest Chinese visitor was the ambassador <a href="/wiki/Zhang_Qian" title="Zhang Qian">Zhang Qian</a>, who passed through on his way to secure a military alliance with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Da_Yuezhi" title="Da Yuezhi">Da Yuezhi</a> or \'Great Yuezhi\' <a href="/wiki/Han%E2%80%93Xiongnu_War" title="Han&#8211;Xiongnu War">against the Xiongnu</a>, c. 127/126 BCE. The <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shiji" title="Shiji">Shiji</a></i>, Chap. 123 says:\n</p>\n<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r856303468">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p><a href="/wiki/Dayuan" title="Dayuan">Dayuan</a> lies southwest of the territory of the Xiongnu, some 10,000 li [4,158 km] directly west of China. The people are also settled on the land, plowing the fields and growing rice and wheat. They also make wine out of grapes. The region has many fine horses which sweat blood;[apparently due to skin parasites which caused sores] their forebears are supposed to have been foaled from heavenly horses. The people live in houses in fortified cities, there being some seventy or more cities of various\n</p><p>sizes in the region. The population numbers several hundred thousand. The people fight with bows and spears and can shoot from horseback. Dayuan is bordered on the north by <a href="/wiki/Kangju" title="Kangju">Kangju</a>, on the west by the kingdom of the Great Yuezhi, on the southwest by <a href="/wiki/Daxia" title="Daxia">Daxia</a> (<a href="/wiki/Bactria" title="Bactria">Bactria</a>), on the northeast by the land of the <a href="/wiki/Wusun" title="Wusun">Wusun</a>, and on the east by Yumi (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Keriya_County" title="Keriya County">Keriya</a>) and Yutian (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Khotan" title="Khotan">Khotan</a>)."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>\n</p>\n</blockquote>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:282px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Negotiations_with_basmachs_Fergana,_1921.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1268" data-file-width="1923" decoding="async" height="185" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Negotiations_with_basmachs_Fergana%2C_1921.jpg/280px-Negotiations_with_basmachs_Fergana%2C_1921.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Negotiations_with_basmachs_Fergana%2C_1921.jpg/420px-Negotiations_with_basmachs_Fergana%2C_1921.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Negotiations_with_basmachs_Fergana%2C_1921.jpg/560px-Negotiations_with_basmachs_Fergana%2C_1921.jpg 2x" width="280"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Negotiations_with_basmachs_Fergana,_1921.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Soviet negotiations with <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Basmachi" title="Basmachi">basmachi</a></i>, Fergana, 1921</div></div></div>\n<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Da_Yuan" title="Da Yuan">Da Yuan</a> appears as a powerful state in both the <i>Shiji</i> and the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hanshu" title="Hanshu">Hanshu</a></i>. However, after Xian, king of <a href="/wiki/Yarkant_County" title="Yarkant County">Yarkand</a>, conquered it about the middle of the 1st century CE, it gradually lost importance. The <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hou_Hanshu" title="Hou Hanshu">Hou Hanshu</a></i> adds that Da Yuan sent tribute and offerings to the Chinese court in 130 CE along with <a href="/wiki/Kashgar" title="Kashgar">Kashgar</a> and Yarkand. After that, it is referred to as Liyi &#26647;&#24331; (preferably read Suyi &#31903;&#24331;), and is specifically stated to be a dependency of <a href="/wiki/Kangju" title="Kangju">Kangju</a>.\n</p><p>By the time of the <i><a href="/wiki/Weil%C3%BCe" title="Weil&#252;e">Weil&#252;e</a></i> (in the 3rd century CE), the old capital, <a href="/wiki/Alexandria_Eschate" title="Alexandria Eschate">Alexandria Eschate</a> (modern <a href="/wiki/Khujand" title="Khujand">Khujand</a>), had become a separate kingdom called \'Northern Wuyi.\'<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Zoroastrianism" title="Zoroastrianism">Zoroastrian</a> literature identifies the area as the <a href="/wiki/Zoroastrianism" title="Zoroastrianism">Zoroastrian</a> homeland. It was known as "&#214;zkent" during <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Karakhanid" title="Karakhanid">Karakhanid</a> rule. Fergana also played a central role in the history of the <a href="/wiki/Mughal_Empire" title="Mughal Empire">Mughal</a> dynasty of <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South Asia</a> in that Omar Sheikh Mirza, chieftain of Farghana, was the father of Zahiruddin Muhammad <a href="/wiki/Babur" title="Babur">Babur</a> (1483&#8211;1530), founder of the Mughal dynasty in <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>. At Mirza\'s death in 1498, Babur became chief, although he was still a minor.\n</p><p>During the expansion of <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> in the nineteenth century the Russians invaded <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turkistan" title="Turkistan">Turkistan</a>, gradually taking it over between 1855 and 1884. They took the capital of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kokand_Khanate" title="Kokand Khanate">Kokand Khanate</a> in 1873 and included it within what was named the Fergana Province of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_empire" title="Russian empire">Russian empire</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Modern_history">Modern history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fergana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Modern history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Modern Fergana city was founded in 1876 as a <a href="/wiki/Garrison" title="Garrison">garrison</a> town and colonial appendage to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Margelan" title="Margelan">Margelan</a> (13.5 miles to the northwest) by the Russians. It was initially named <b>New Margelan</b> (&#1053;&#1086;&#1074;&#1099;&#1081; &#1052;&#1072;&#1088;&#1075;&#1077;&#1083;&#1072;&#1085;), then renamed <a href="/wiki/Mikhail_Skobelev" title="Mikhail Skobelev">Skobelev</a> (&#1057;&#1082;&#1086;&#1073;&#1077;&#1083;&#1077;&#1074;) in 1907 after the first Russian military governor of Fergana Valley. In 1924, after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bolshevik" title="Bolshevik">Bolshevik</a> reconquest of the region from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Basmachi" title="Basmachi">basmachi</a> rebels, the name was changed to <b>Fergana</b>, after the province of which it was the centre.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>\n</p><p>The industrial base of Fergana was developed in the 20th century.  Industry in the city included textile manufacturing and a nitric fertiliser plant.  Some of the industrial development was a result of <a href="/wiki/Evacuation_in_the_Soviet_Union" title="Evacuation in the Soviet Union">Evacuation in the Soviet Union</a> during World War II.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BishkekAndFerghana_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-BishkekAndFerghana-6">[6]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:242px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:FerganaAirport.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1312" data-file-width="2080" decoding="async" height="151" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/FerganaAirport.jpg/240px-FerganaAirport.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/FerganaAirport.jpg/360px-FerganaAirport.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/FerganaAirport.jpg/480px-FerganaAirport.jpg 2x" width="240"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:FerganaAirport.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fergana_Airport" title="Fergana Airport">Fergana Airport</a>.</div></div></div>\n<p>Fergana has been a center for oil production in the Fergana Valley since the region\'s first <a href="/wiki/Oil_refinery" title="Oil refinery">oil refinery</a> was built near the city in 1908. Since then, more refineries have been added, and Fergana is one of the most important centers of oil refining in Uzbekistan. <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">Natural gas</a> from western Uzbekistan is transported by <a href="/wiki/Pipeline_transport" title="Pipeline transport">pipeline</a> to the valley, where it is used to manufacture <a href="/wiki/Fertilizer" title="Fertilizer">fertilizer</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Great_Fergana_Canal" title="Great Fergana Canal">Great Fergana Canal</a>, built almost entirely by hand during the 1930s, passes through the northern part of the city and completed in 1939. During its construction, the canal and the city was widely photographed by the noted photographer <a href="/wiki/Max_Penson" title="Max Penson">Max Penson</a>. With a western loan Fergana is able to modernize its refinery and also reduce <a href="/wiki/Air_pollution" title="Air pollution">air pollution</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> emissions.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fergana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Fergana has a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cold_desert_climate" title="Cold desert climate">cold desert climate</a> (<a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="K&#246;ppen climate classification">K&#246;ppen climate classification</a> <i>BWk</i>). Winters are cool and short, with a daily average low temperature of &#8722;2.8&#160;&#176;C (27&#160;&#176;F) and a daily average high of 4.6&#160;&#176;C (40&#160;&#176;F) in January; summers are hot, with an average low temperature of 20.3&#160;&#176;C (69&#160;&#176;F) and an average high of 34.7&#160;&#176;C (94&#160;&#176;F) in July.
