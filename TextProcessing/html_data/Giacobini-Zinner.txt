href="#cite_note-NK2191-3">[3]</a></sup></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Comet Giacobini&#8211;Zinner</b> (official designation: <b>21P/Giacobini&#8211;Zinner</b>) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Periodic_comet" title="Periodic comet">periodic comet</a> in the Solar System.\n</p><p>It was discovered by <a href="/wiki/Michel_Giacobini" title="Michel Giacobini">Michel Giacobini</a> (from <a href="/wiki/Nice" title="Nice">Nice</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>), who observed the comet in the <a href="/wiki/Constellation" title="Constellation">constellation</a>  of <a href="/wiki/Aquarius_(constellation)" title="Aquarius (constellation)">Aquarius</a> on December 20, 1900. It was recovered two passages later by <a href="/wiki/Ernst_Zinner" title="Ernst Zinner">Ernst Zinner</a> (from <a href="/wiki/Bamberg" title="Bamberg">Bamberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>) while observing <a href="/wiki/Variable_star" title="Variable star">variable stars</a> near <a href="/wiki/Beta_Scuti" title="Beta Scuti">Beta Scuti</a> on October 23, 1913.\n</p><p>During its apparitions, Giacobini&#8211;Zinner can reach about the 7-8th <a href="/wiki/Apparent_magnitude" title="Apparent magnitude">magnitude</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> but in 1946 it underwent a series of outbursts that made it as bright as 5th magnitude. It is the parent body of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Giacobinids" title="Giacobinids">Giacobinids</a> <a href="/wiki/Meteor_shower" title="Meteor shower">meteor shower</a> (also known as the <a href="/wiki/Draconids" title="Draconids">Draconids</a>). The comet currently has an Earth-<a href="/wiki/Minimum_orbit_intersection_distance" title="Minimum orbit intersection distance">MOID</a> of 0.035&#160;<a href="/wiki/Astronomical_unit" title="Astronomical unit">AU</a> (5,200,000&#160;<a href="/wiki/Kilometre" title="Kilometre">km</a>; 3,300,000&#160;<a href="/wiki/Mile" title="Mile">mi</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jpldata_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-jpldata-1">[1]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Giacobini&#8211;Zinner was the target of the <a href="/wiki/International_Cometary_Explorer" title="International Cometary Explorer">International Cometary Explorer</a> spacecraft, which passed through its plasma tail on September 11, 1985. In addition, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> space officials considered redirecting the <a href="/wiki/Sakigake" title="Sakigake">Sakigake</a> interplanetary probe toward a 1998 encounter with Giacobini&#8211;Zinner, but that probe lacked the propellant for the necessary maneuvers and the project was abandoned.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Comet_nucleus" title="Comet nucleus">comet nucleus</a> is estimated to be 2.0 kilometers in diameter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jpldata_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-jpldata-1">[1]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="thumb
