(47&#160;/sq&#160;mi)</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Tuamotus</b>, also referred to in English as the <b>Tuamotu Archipelago</b> or the <b>Tuamotu Islands</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <i lang="fr">&#206;les Tuamotu</i></b>, officially <b><i lang="fr" title="French language text">Archipel des Tuamotu</i></b>), are a <a href="/wiki/French_Polynesia" title="French Polynesia">French Polynesian</a> chain of almost 80 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Islands" title="Islands">islands</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atolls" title="Atolls">atolls</a> forming the largest chain of atolls in the world. This <a href="/wiki/Archipelago" title="Archipelago">archipelago</a> in the southern <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a> stretches from the northwest to the southeast over an area roughly the size of <a href="/wiki/Western_Europe" title="Western Europe">Western Europe</a>. The total area of land within this chain is 850 square kilometres (328 square miles), with its major islands being <a href="/wiki/Anaa" title="Anaa">Anaa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fakarava" title="Fakarava">Fakarava</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hao_(French_Polynesia)" title="Hao (French Polynesia)">Hao</a> and <a href="/wiki/Makemo" title="Makemo">Makemo</a>.\n</p><p>The Tuamotus have approximately 16,000 inhabitants. The islands were initially settled by <a href="/wiki/Polynesians" title="Polynesians">Polynesians</a>, and from them, modern Tuamotuans share a common <a href="/wiki/Polynesian_culture" title="Polynesian culture">culture</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Tuamotuan_language" title="Tuamotuan language">Tuamotuan language</a>. \n</p><p>The Tuamotus are a <a href="/wiki/Overseas_collectivity" title="Overseas collectivity">French overseas collectivity</a>. The people of <a href="/wiki/Tahiti" title="Tahiti">Tahiti</a> originally referred to the islands with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exonym" title="Exonym">exonym</a> of the <i>Paumotus</i>, which means the "Subservient Islands", until a delegation from the island<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Avoid_weasel_words" title="Wikipedia:Avoid weasel words"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too vague attribution or weasel words. (November 2017)">which?</span></a></i>]</sup> convinced the French authorities to change it to <i>Tuamotus</i>, which means the "Distant Islands".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. <a href="/wiki/Takaroa" title="Takaroa">Takaroa</a>; <a href="/wiki/Tatakoto" title="Tatakoto">Tatakoto</a>; and <a href="/wiki/Tureia" title="Tureia">Tureia</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Electoral_divisions">Electoral divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tuamotus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Electoral divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:TuamotuPearlFarm.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="683" data-file-width="1280" decoding="async" height="117" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/TuamotuPearlFarm.jpg/220px-TuamotuPearlFarm.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/TuamotuPearlFarm.jpg/330px-TuamotuPearlFarm.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/TuamotuPearlFarm.jpg/440px-TuamotuPearlFarm.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:TuamotuPearlFarm.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Pearl farm in the Tuamotus</div></div></div>\n<p>The communes on Tuamotu are part of two different electoral districts (<i>circonscriptions &#233;lectorales</i>) represented in the <a href="/wiki/Assembly_of_French_Polynesia" title="Assembly of French Polynesia">Assembly of French Polynesia</a>. The <i>&#206;les Gambier et Tuamotu Est</i> electoral district comprises the commune of Gambier and eleven communes in eastern Tuamotu: Anaa; Fangatau; Hao; Hikueru; Makemo; Napuka; Nukutavake; Pukapuka; Reao; Tatakoto; and Tureia. The other five communes in western Tuamotu &#8211; Arutua; Fakarava; Manihi; Rangiroa; and Takaroa &#8211; form the <i>&#206;les Tuamotu Ouest</i> electoral district.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demography">Demography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tuamotus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Demography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:TuamotuWaterside.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="960" data-file-width="1280" decoding="async" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/04/TuamotuWaterside.jpg/220px-TuamotuWaterside.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/04/TuamotuWaterside.jpg/330px-TuamotuWaterside.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/04/TuamotuWaterside.jpg/440px-TuamotuWaterside.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:TuamotuWaterside.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Typical waterside view in the Tuamotus</div></div></div>\n<p>At the 2007 census, the Tuamotus (including the Gambier Islands) had a population of 18,317 inhabitants (15,862 in 2002, 8,100 in 1983). Of these, 769 inhabitants live in a 215-<a href="/wiki/Nautical_mile" title="Nautical mile">nautical-mile</a> (400&#160;km; 250&#160;mi) radius around <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mururoa" title="Mururoa">Mururoa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fangataufa" title="Fangataufa">Fangataufa</a>, the sites of former French nuclear tests.\n</p><p>The common language spoken in the Tuamotus is <a href="/wiki/Tuamotuan_language" title="Tuamotuan language">Tuamotuan</a>, except in Puka-Puka which uses the <a href="/wiki/Marquesan_language" title="Marquesan language">Marquesan language</a>. The Gambier Islands use  <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mangarevan_language" title="Mangarevan language">Mangarevan</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tuamotus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The islands\' economy is predominantly composed of <a href="/wiki/Subsistence_agriculture" title="Subsistence agriculture">subsistence agriculture</a>. The most important sources of additional income are the cultivation of <a href="/wiki/Tahitian_pearl" title="Tahitian pearl">black pearls</a> and the preparation of <a href="/wiki/Copra" title="Copra">copra</a>. <a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">Tourism</a>-related income remains meager, especially by comparison to the tourism industry of the neighboring <a href="/wiki/Society_Islands" title="Society Islands">Society Islands</a>. Modest tourism infrastructure is found on the atolls of <a href="/wiki/Rangiroa" title="Rangiroa">Rangiroa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Manihi" title="Manihi">Manihi</a> which have recreational <a href="/wiki/Scuba_diving" title="Scuba diving">scuba diving</a> and <a href="/wiki/Snorkeling" title="Snorkeling">snorkeling</a> destinations.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tuamotus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Despite the vast spread of the archipelago, it covers a total land area of only about 885&#160;km<sup>2</sup> (<span class="nowrap">345 sq mi</span>). The climate is <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Warm_Pool" title="Tropical Warm Pool">warm tropical</a>, without pronounced seasons. The annual average temperature is a relatively continuous 26&#160;&#176;C (79&#160;&#176;F). Water sources such as lakes or rivers are absent, leaving catchments of rain as the only source of fresh water. The annual average rainfall is 1400&#160; mm (about 55 in). Rainfall is not markedly different throughout the year, although it is lowest during the months of September and November.\n</p><p>Geological stability of the archipelago is high, as it was created by the weakly active <a href="/wiki/Easter_Fracture_Zone" title="Easter Fracture Zone">Easter Fracture Zone</a>. No volcanic eruptions have been recorded historically.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Flora_and_fauna">Flora and fauna</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tuamotus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Flora and fauna">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tuamotu_9612a.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2218" data-file-width="3409" decoding="async" height="143" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f3/Tuamotu_9612a.jpg/220px-Tuamotu_9612a.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f3/Tuamotu_9612a.jpg/330px-Tuamotu_9612a.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f3/Tuamotu_9612a.jpg/440px-Tuamotu_9612a.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tuamotu_9612a.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Coconut palms, <a href="/wiki/Takapoto" title="Takapoto">Takapoto</a></div></div></div>\n<p>The sparse soil of the coral islands does not permit diverse vegetation. The <a href="/wiki/Coconut" title="Coconut">coconut</a> palm, which forms the basis for <a href="/wiki/Copra" title="Copra">copra</a> production, is of special economic importance. On a few islands, <a href="/wiki/Vanilla" title="Vanilla">vanilla</a> is also cultivated. Agriculture is generally otherwise limited to simple subsistence. Fruit and vegetable staples include <a href="/wiki/Yam_(vegetable)" title="Yam (vegetable)">yams</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taro" title="Taro">taro</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Breadfruit" title="Breadfruit">breadfruit</a>, as well as a wide range of other tropical fruits. <a href="/wiki/Pandanus" title="Pandanus">Pandanus</a> leaves are traditionally woven together as a roof <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thatch" title="Thatch">thatch</a> (although nowadays there are a great number of corrugated sheet-metal roofs instead), as well as for other items, such as mats and hats.\n</p><p>The species-rich reefs are home to a diverse range of underwater fauna. Surface creatures are primarily <a href="/wiki/Seabird" title="Seabird">seabirds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lizard" title="Lizard">lizards</a>. The Tuamotus have only <a href="/wiki/List_of_birds_of_the_Tuamotus" title="List of birds of the Tuamotus">57 species of birds</a>, but ten of these are <a href="/wiki/Endemism" title="Endemism">endemic</a>, including the <a href="/wiki/Tuamotu_kingfisher" title="Tuamotu kingfisher">Tuamotu kingfisher</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Tuamotu_reed_warbler" title="Tuamotu reed warbler">Tuamotu reed warbler</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Tuamotu_sandpiper" title="Tuamotu sandpiper">Tuamotu sandpiper</a>. Thirteen species are globally threatened and one is extinct.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geology">Geology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tuamotus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Geology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>All of the islands of the Tuamotus are coral "low islands": essentially high sand bars built upon coral <a href="/wiki/Reef" title="Reef">reefs</a>. <a href="/wiki/Makatea" title="Makatea">Makatea</a>, southwest of the Palliser Islands, is one of three great <a href="/wiki/Phosphate" title="Phosphate">phosphate</a> rocks in the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>. The others are <a href="/wiki/Banaba_Island" title="Banaba Island">Banaba</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kiribati" title="Kiribati">Kiribati</a>, and the island nation of <a href="/wiki/Nauru" title="Nauru">Nauru</a>. Although <a href="/wiki/Geography" title="Geography">geographically</a> part of the Tuamotus, the Gambier Islands, at the southeastern extreme of the <a href="/wiki/Archipelago" title="Archipelago">archipelago</a>, are <a href="/wiki/Geology" title="Geology">geologically</a> and <a href="/wiki/Culture" title="Culture">culturally</a> distinct.\n</p><p>In the northwest of the archipelago, the ring-shaped atoll <a href="/wiki/Taiaro" title="Taiaro">Taiaro</a> provides a rare example of a coral reef with a fully enclosed lagoon. The atoll was officially designated as a <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biosphere_reserve" title="Biosphere reserve">biosphere reserve</a> in 1977.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tuamotus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The early history of the Tuamotu islands is generally shrouded in mystery. Archaeological findings lead to the conclusion that the western Tuamotus were settled from the <a href="/wiki/Society_Islands" title="Society Islands">Society Islands</a> by c. 700. On the islands of <a href="/wiki/Rangiroa" title="Rangiroa">Rangiroa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manihi" title="Manihi">Manihi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mataiva" title="Mataiva">Mataiva</a>, there are flat ceremonial platforms (called <i><a href="/wiki/Marae" title="Marae">marae</a></i>) made of coral blocks, although their exact age is unknown.\n</p><p>European encounters with the Tuamotus began with that of Portuguese sailor <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_Magellan" title="Ferdinand Magellan">Ferdinand Magellan</a>, during his circumglobal voyage in 1521 sailing in the service of the Spanish Crown. His visit was followed by:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Portuguese sailor <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Fernandes_de_Queir%C3%B3s" title="Pedro Fernandes de Queir&#243;s">Pedro Fernandes de Queir&#243;s</a> in 1606, sailing in the service of the Spanish Crown;</li>\n<li>Dutch mariners <a href="/wiki/Willem_Schouten" title="Willem Schouten">Willem Schouten</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jacob_Le_Maire" title="Jacob Le Maire">Jacob Le Maire</a> in 1616;</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Jacob_Roggeveen" title="Jacob Roggeveen">Jacob Roggeveen</a> (who also first sighted <a href="/wiki/Easter_Island" title="Easter Island">Easter Island</a>) in 1722;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Anne_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Anne-3">[3]</a></sup></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/John_Byron" title="John Byron">John Byron</a> in 1765;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Anne_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Anne-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>37</span></sup></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Louis_Antoine_de_Bougainville" title="Louis Antoine de Bougainville">Louis Antoine de Bougainville</a> in 1768;</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/James_Cook" title="James Cook">James Cook</a> during his first voyage in 1769;</li>\n<li>Spanish navigator <a href="/wiki/Domingo_de_Bonechea" title="Domingo de Bonechea">Domingo de Bonechea</a> in 1774 and</li>\n<li>Russian expedition of <a href="/wiki/Otto_von_Kotzebue" title="Otto von Kotzebue">Otto von Kotzebue</a> in 1815.</li></ul>\n<p>None of these visits were of political consequence, as the islands were in the sphere of influence of the <a href="/wiki/P%C5%8Dmare_Dynasty" title="P&#333;mare Dynasty">P&#333;mare Dynasty</a> of <a href="/wiki/Tahiti" title="Tahiti">Tahiti</a>.\n</p><p>It was not until the beginning of the 19th century that the first Christian <a href="/wiki/Missionary" title="Missionary">missionaries</a> arrived. Traders took the islands\' <a href="/wiki/Pearl" title="Pearl">pearls</a> to the European markets by the late 19th century, making them coveted possessions. France forced the abdication of King <a href="/wiki/P%C5%8Dmare_V" title="P&#333;mare V">P&#333;mare V</a> of Tahiti and claimed the islands without ever having formally annexed them.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Robert_Louis_Stevenson" title="Robert Louis Stevenson">Robert Louis Stevenson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fanny_Stevenson" title="Fanny Stevenson">Fanny Stevenson</a> travelled among the Paumotus on the Yacht <i>Casco</i> in 1888; an account of their journey was published as <i>In the South Seas</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jack_London" title="Jack London">Jack London</a> wrote a story, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Seed_of_McCoy" title="The Seed of McCoy">The Seed of McCoy</a>", based on an incident in 1900 when a burning ship, the <i>Pyrenees</i>, was safely beached on <a href="/wiki/Mangareva" title="Mangareva">Mangareva</a>. In the story, London has the ship sail past Mangareva and all through the Tuamotus before beaching on <a href="/wiki/Fakarava" title="Fakarava">Fakarava</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Tuamotus made headlines around the world in 1947, when Norwegian ethnographer <a href="/wiki/Thor_Heyerdahl" title="Thor Heyerdahl">Thor Heyerdahl</a>, sailing from South America with a crew of five others, reached <a href="/wiki/Raroia" title="Raroia">Raroia</a> on his raft <i><a href="/wiki/Kon-Tiki_expedition" title="Kon-Tiki expedition">Kon-Tiki</a></i>. More recently the islands have been the subject of headlines as the site of French <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_weapons_testing" title="Nuclear weapons testing">nuclear weapons testing</a> on the atolls of <a href="/wiki/Moruroa" title="Moruroa">Moruroa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fangataufa" title="Fangataufa">Fangataufa</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Island_groups">Island groups</span><span
